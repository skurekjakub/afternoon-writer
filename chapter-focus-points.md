# Chapter Focus Points: Intimate & Explicit Scene Expansion

## Relationship to Editor Guide

All rules from the editor guide (1-editor-guide.md, sections 1–18) remain in force. The Limited Third Absolute applies. No emotional labeling, no subtext translation, no narrator editorializing. These focus points are **additive** — they describe what to expand and deepen when writing intimate/explicit content, not what to loosen.

The editing philosophy flips for these scenes: where description and connective tissue get trimmed for pacing, intimate and explicit content gets expanded for immersion. The body is the text. Every physical detail earns its place.

---

## Core Principle: The Scene Must Be Lived

The reader should feel like they are IN the scene — not watching it, not reading a summary of it. Every intimate or explicit scene must be expanded to its full sensory depth. The goal is not to mention that something happened. The goal is to put the reader inside the body experiencing it.

**The test**: If a reader could skim a passage and still know what happened, the scene isn't detailed enough. The detail should be the experience, not a description of the experience.

## Scene-Type Expansion Rules

### Fluids (Watersports, Cum, Squirting, Mixed)

**Principle**: The fluid is a character. It has temperature, weight, behavior on skin. It goes somewhere and does something when it arrives.

## The Expansion Decision

When editing, apply this test to every intimate/explicit passage, beat, or scene:

1. **Is the physical act shown moment-by-moment?** If not, expand.
2. **Can I feel what the POV character feels?** If not, add sensory grounding — temperature, pressure, texture, internal sensation.
3. **Do the characters react distinctly?** If Hasera and Yanna respond the same way to the same touch, something is wrong.
4. **Is the receiver active?** If the receiver is just "lying there receiving," add their participation — reaching, pressing, adjusting, demanding.
5. **Does the context matter?** If you could transplant this scene to a different setting with different characters and change nothing, the scene isn't specific enough.
6. **Is there variation?** If the same motion is described three times identically, find what changes on the second and third iteration.

When the answer to all six is yes, the scene is expanded enough. If not, continue rewriting.

## What This Is Not

This is not permission to:
- Add narrator editorializing ("it was the most intense sensation she'd ever felt")
- Label emotions ("her face showed pure ecstasy")
- Break limited third-person POV
- Use clinical vocabulary ("refractory period," "erogenous zone")
- Pad with repeated synonyms

The detail must be **physical, specific, and from inside the body**. More words, but every word earned.
