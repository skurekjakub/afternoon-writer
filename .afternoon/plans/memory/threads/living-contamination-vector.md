# How did the contamination get into living bodies?

## Chapter 2-4 state

This thread is resolved in Chapter 4, but it is resolved narrowly and should stay narrow in future memory use. The answer is not that the whole plague is understood. The answer is that Jaina finally proves how it enters living bodies in practice: through tainted grain becoming bread and being taken in meal by meal until the body starts helping the process along.

What matters about the resolution is how carefully the book earns it. Chapter 2 established contamination in land, water, and flesh. Chapter 3 made grain a serious suspicion without permission to lock it down. Chapter 4 is where Jaina can finally put living tissue, diet pattern, spared households, and a tainted loaf into one chain of proof. That keeps the payoff fair.

Future chapters should remember the distinction. The ingestion vector is solved. The larger questions are not. Who made the treatment, where it is being prepared, how broad the network is, and who commands it remain open elsewhere.
