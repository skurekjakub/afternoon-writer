# Can the Hearthglen road and Arthas become the outside force the field team now needs?

Chapter 14 advances this thread by making clear that the force did launch and that launch did not stabilize anything. Hearthglen nearly fails again after dark. Uther remains on the field. Arthas has already moved on. The question is no longer whether he can be activated. It is what his speed is going to do to the next city.

This keeps the thread painfully balanced. Arthas is not useless. He is already moving toward the right place. But the same overdriven certainty Uther warned about remains intact, which means every mile he gains on the protagonists also increases the chance that he will arrive at Stratholme in the wrong state of mind.

By chapter end the thread points straight ahead. Hearthglen is behind the crisis. Stratholme is the live fuse. The protagonists are not catching up to a stable ally. They are chasing a prince and a catastrophe at the same time.
