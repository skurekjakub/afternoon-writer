# How is the contamination entering living bodies before death?

Chapter 4 resolves this question cleanly. The answer is ingestion through tainted grain. Jaina earns it by reading the sick in motion rather than by retroactive certainty: gut-first concentration, heavier cases among villagers living on bread, lighter traces among people on oats or separate stores, and then the final proof in the granary itself.

The important continuity note is that the story does not collapse all prior clues into premature certainty. Millhaven and the earlier road evidence were precursor signs. This chapter is where the mechanism becomes canon. From here on, characters should not behave as if grain is still only a maybe.
