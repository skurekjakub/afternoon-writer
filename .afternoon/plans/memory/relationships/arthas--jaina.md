# Arthas Menethil / Jaina Proudmoore

## Chapter 6 position
Chapter 6 frames this relationship as a wound that has learned public manners. Arthas is courteous, almost warm for a moment, and still completely capable of refusing the thing Jaina crossed half the kingdom to tell him. Jaina answers without title and without illusion. The old intimacy remains visible, but it no longer gives her leverage. If anything, it makes the refusal hurt more cleanly.

The scene matters because it refuses to flatten them into simple melodrama. Arthas is not dismissing her because he never cared. Jaina is not furious only because he once mattered. The chapter insists on something harsher: he listens, understands enough, and still chooses visible war first. Their private history sharpens the exchange, but the real tragedy is institutional.

## What chapter 6 establishes
For continuity, this pair should now be remembered as proof that personal access does not equal political power. Jaina can still reach Arthas as Arthas. She cannot make Prince Arthas act. Future chapters should carry forward both the old emotional charge and the newer operational truth that he has already heard the warning and chosen otherwise.
