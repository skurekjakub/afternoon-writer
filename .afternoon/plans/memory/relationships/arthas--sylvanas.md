# Arthas Menethil / Sylvanas Windrunner

## Chapter 6 position
This is a first-contact relationship built almost entirely out of command pressure. Arthas meets Sylvanas as the Ranger-General of another power arriving in his war camp with unwelcome information. Sylvanas meets Arthas as a prince already committed to the wrong battle. Their exchange never becomes emotional, but it is sharp from the start because neither mistakes courtesy for surrender.

What stands out is how quickly they measure each other. Arthas gives rank recognition. Sylvanas gives him no aid he has not earned. Once the warning stops being useful, she reduces him to 'the prince' and moves on. That tells you the shape of the relationship better than any longer argument would.

## What chapter 6 establishes
Future continuity should remember this pair as mutually legible but not aligned. Arthas understands that Sylvanas is real authority, not escort ornament. Sylvanas understands that Arthas's courtesy will not outrun his priorities. Their first meeting ends with clean nonalignment and no illusion on either side.


## Chapter 11 update

Chapter 11 upgrades this relationship from mutual nonalignment to dangerous usefulness. Arthas finally takes Sylvanas's warning seriously enough to move, which means she gets the army she came for. It does not become trust. It becomes a colder problem: a commander she can activate but cannot trust to stop in the right place.
