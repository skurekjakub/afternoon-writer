# Arthas Menethil

Chapter 6 introduces Arthas as a man whose courtesy and certainty arrive in the room together. He is not careless. He listens, reads the papers, follows the route logic, and understands exactly what Jaina is trying to tell him. The problem is not failure to hear. The problem is that his world is organized around the war he can see.

That makes him more dangerous than a simple fool would be. He is persuasive even when he is wrong for this particular crisis. He sounds like a commander protecting the living villages in front of him, and in a narrow sense he is. The tragedy is that the narrower truth wins.

Write him with warmth under discipline, never as a sneering caricature. His refusal lands because it is courteous, reasoned, and still catastrophic.
