# Arthas Menethil

Arthas never appears directly in chapter 14, but the chapter tightens his shape through relay traffic and consequence. Hearthglen nearly fails again after dark. Uther is still on the field. Arthas has not stayed to consolidate. He has taken the main body south-east at speed toward Stratholme.

That is exactly who this version of Arthas is becoming: a man who can identify the right fire and then throw himself at it with a velocity that may solve one disaster only by arriving early enough for the next. Chapter 11 made that risk legible. Chapter 14 accelerates it. He is now ahead of the protagonists, ahead of repair, ahead of any chance for slower minds to temper him.

The chapter therefore preserves the tragedy of him rather than flattening him into villainy. The move makes sense. Hearthglen is failing. Bodies are still answering after death. Stratholme is the next city on the grain line. But the speed itself is ominous. Every relay makes him feel more like a prince trying to outrun catastrophe by becoming its fastest moving piece.

By chapter end, Arthas is no longer a possible audience. He is the live fuse south of them.
