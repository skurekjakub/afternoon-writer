# Antonidas

Chapter 14 brings Antonidas back into the story not as remote archmage, but as frightened mentor speaking through long-distance magic while the field outruns him again. His questions are exact because they have to be. His plea to Jaina to come home is personal because he cannot make it anything else for long.

What the chapter preserves carefully is his double role. He is still the Grand Magus of Dalaran, still a man who can promise to move what he is able on the strength of a partial report. But the emotional center of the exchange is not political command. It is fear. He wants the fuller version because the danger is larger than he feared. He wants her home because this is still his apprentice, still the brilliant child moving faster than caution, and the road ahead has already become Stratholme.

His dry impatience returning near the end of the call matters almost as much as the plea. It proves the old relationship is still alive under the crisis. Antonidas is not reduced to institutional drag here. He is too far away, yes, and still too slow for the field. But he is emotionally present, strategically useful, and painfully aware that Jaina is carrying more than she can tell him.

He ends the chapter where this story increasingly leaves its institutions: behind the road, trying to move in time, and knowing he probably will not. The difference is that Antonidas feels that lag as a man as well as an office.
