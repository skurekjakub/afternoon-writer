# Aldous

## Chapter 4 state
Aldous is an ordinary grain keeper turned accidental witness. The flour on his apron and the sleeve he cannot stop worrying matter because they keep him rooted in routine right up until routine becomes evidence. He does not bring theory. He brings dates, deliveries, and the simple terrible fact that the first shipment is still stacked in the back half of the granary.

That is his value to continuity. Aldous shows how the plague hides inside boring competence. He is not malicious, only late to understand that his ordinary work has been folded into someone else's design.
