# Paret Dane

## Chapter 3 state

Paret Dane arrives like ordinary road life stubbornly refusing to understand that Millhaven is already over. Old horse, empty wagon, brown wool, white hair at the temples, practical trade goods mostly gone because Harbrook cleaned him out. His usefulness lies in how untheatrical he is. He does not stride in with secrets. He reins in, sees armed strangers in his own village, and realizes by degrees that the place is dead.

Once the shock hits, he becomes valuable fast. He can name the next settlements. He can explain the younger trader undercutting older sellers. He can say what the local arguments were about and why hard seasons make people accept cheap sacks they would question otherwise. His knowledge is economic and geographic rather than magical, which is exactly why it matters.

The chapter also gives him a humane touch through Mira. He knows her by sight. He softens to sweetheart and little pip at once. That softness makes the rest of his practicality sharper, not gentler. He is the one who says, plainly, that people should burn what gets back up. He reads as an old road man who has understood in seconds what sentiment will cost if the plague reaches the next village.
