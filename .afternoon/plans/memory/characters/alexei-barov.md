# Alexei Barov

## Core read

Alexei Barov is aristocratic insulation made flesh. He is not the loudest or cleverest person in chapter 10. He is the smoothest. The great hall, the warmed stone, the ready tea, the still hands — all of it says the same thing: this is a man used to letting wealth turn crisis into atmosphere.

## What he does

He receives Jaina without surprise, which is the first warning sign. He has a script for grain suspicion ready before she finishes asking the question. He does not deny that there is a problem. He reframes it: rumor, loss, panic, scale attracting blame. That register matters. Alexei's instinct is always to convert danger into something administrative enough to survive.

The moment the conversation becomes truly scholarly, he yields the field to Jandice. That tells you nearly everything worth knowing about him. He is the political shell of the Barov arrangement, not its intellectual engine.

## Current chapter-end state

At chapter end, Alexei remains above the real action, which is exactly how he wants it. Jaina is no longer his immediate problem. She has been handed to the scholarly daughter, the curated institution, and the deeper authority beneath both. That deflection is not incidental. It is his central talent.
