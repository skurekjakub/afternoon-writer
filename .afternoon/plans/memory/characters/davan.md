# Davan

## Chapter 4 state
Davan is the chapter's clearest proof that the plague network has live hands inside it. He is barely more than a boy, dusty from hiding in the granary, panicked enough to answer the wrong question first, and still disciplined enough to reach for fire before surrender. He is not written as a mastermind. He is written as a frightened expendable piece.

That is why his death matters. The poison in his sleeve turns him from a local culprit into evidence of structure. Somebody expected capture. Somebody prepared a village watcher for silence. Davan's body tells Jaina and Sylvanas more about the size and coldness of the network than his words ever would have.
