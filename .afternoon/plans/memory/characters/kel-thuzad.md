# Kel'Thuzad

Kel'Thuzad is dead by the time chapter 14 opens, but the chapter is where his death stops being a private horror and becomes field intelligence. Jaina carries the report out of Scholomance and says plainly what chapter 12 and 13 already implied: he expected death to be temporary. His calm was not courage. It was continuity planning.

That matters because chapter 14 changes who knows what. Sylvanas now hears the truth in operational language. Antonidas hears enough of it to understand that the threat did not end cleanly in the sanctum. The road party is no longer reacting only to the man they killed, but to the system he built for surviving beyond the body.

The result is that Kel'Thuzad's influence expands even in death. He no longer occupies the room, but he occupies their timetable, their strategy, and their understanding of what counts as an ending. The chapter strips away any comforting version of the victory at Scholomance.

He is gone. His theorem is not.
