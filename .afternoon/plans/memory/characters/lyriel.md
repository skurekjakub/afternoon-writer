# Lyriel

## Core read

Lyriel has always been the ranger of edges: the woman who notices the quiet fork, the practical escape fact, the second body problem. Chapter 9 gives her a little more direct speech than before and uses it well. Her lines are dry, useful, and exactly as untheatrical as her job.

## Chapter 9 ending

Then the chapter kills her in a way that matches the story's logic. There is no heroic monologue. No lingering body. The corridor flashes, the air cracks, and the right flank becomes empty space. That abruptness matters. It makes Lyriel less a lesson than a cost suddenly made physical.

## What remains

What survives of her in continuity is not a final statement. It is changed behavior: Cyndia checking right twice, Sylvanas pricing speed differently, Jaina measuring analysis against time and lives. Lyriel becomes the absence that rewrites the team.

## Current goals

Active goal: None — Lyriel is killed instantly by the compound ward before the mission can continue.

Underlying drive: She dies still doing right-flank work for the team.

## Emotional state

Not applicable. Lyriel dies in the trap corridor before any end-of-chapter emotional state can replace action.

## Arc position

Chapter 9 ends Lyriel's arc in the instant her disciplined edge-reading meets terrain built specifically to kill disciplined scouts. After that, her absence becomes part of everyone else's movement.


## Chapter 11 update

Chapter 11 confirms Lyriel's continuity role after death. She is not remembered through speeches or relics. She is remembered through changed formation. Cyndia checks the empty right twice. Sylvanas finally looks straight at what that means. The team still moves as if the missing ranger ought to be there, and that is how the story keeps her alive in the file.
