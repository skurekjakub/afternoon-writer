# Sylvanas Windrunner

## Chapter 7 state
Chapter 7 is where Sylvanas finally stops framing Jaina as support. The shift is not sentimental. It is structural. Andorhal only opens once she lets the work split: Jaina takes the magical and bureaucratic layers Sylvanas cannot force with fieldcraft, while Sylvanas takes the streets, the false labor, and the live targets.

The city suits her immediately. She reads guards, wagon jams, hidden lanes, supervisor sight lines, and the kind of stillness that belongs to trained men pretending to be workers. By the time Havel finishes his district sheets, Sylvanas already has the covered passage, the off-ledger building, and the lodging house in her head as attack geometry.

Write her cold, exact, and load-bearing. The chapter-end realization is simple: Jaina is no longer help she brought along. She is part of the job itself.
