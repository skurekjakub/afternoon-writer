# Miri

Miri is the one living human remainder inside Millhaven. She is small, dehydrated, bruised, and so frightened that memory only comes out in shards, but those shards change the whole chapter. Through her the disaster stops being an environmental read and becomes a lived civilian catastrophe: sweetness in the bread, sickness house by house, the dead walking, some of them coming back for the not-yet-dead, the north road, the newer grain man.

She is important partly because she survives and partly because she does not suddenly become articulate just because rescuers arrive. The chapter is careful about that. Miri gives images, not an orderly report. Jaina and Sylvanas have to work around the breaks in her memory rather than smoothing them into exposition.

Write her as frightened, exhausted, and still responsive to practical kindness. Neral's calm is what gets her moving. Her testimony should continue to feel fragmentary if recalled later, because that is what makes it believable.
