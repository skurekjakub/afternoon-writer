# Village Militia Captain

## Chapter 4 state
The militia captain is a good example of what this chapter means by ordinary denial. He is not a fool and not a coward. He is a man trying to keep a village functioning long enough for breakfast and the next argument over a cart wheel, which means he reaches for the word fever because fever is still governable. Sylvanas breaks that defense by naming Millhaven, and once the lie fails he does not cling to pride. He asks what Jaina is worth, gets his answer, and starts doing what he can still do.

By the end of the chapter he has been stripped down to the hard local essentials: seal the grain, pull the bread if any can still be saved, get another mage here, keep the village upright. He matters as the face of how long an ordinary settlement can stay half inside disbelief while the plague is already in its food.
