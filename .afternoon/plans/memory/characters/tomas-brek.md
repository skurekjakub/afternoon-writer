# Tomas Brek

## Chapter 4 state
Tomas Brek is the village medic held together by no sleep and refusal to quit. The improvised infirmary tells you almost everything about him: fever pallets in a meeting room, herbs beside a butcher's knife, cold broth gone untouched, and a man who has kept going because stopping would mean watching people die faster. He does not waste time defending himself when Jaina arrives. He gives symptoms, patient histories, and practical answers because that is the work in front of him.

He is useful precisely because he is not romanticized. Brek has been calling it fever because he lacks the language for anything worse, not because he is stupid. Once Jaina gives him a better model, he adapts immediately. He is the local human version of the chapter's larger lesson: the right pattern matters more than wounded pride.
