# Aldric Havel

## Chapter 8 state
If chapter 7 made Havel the man who finally got believed, chapter 8 makes him the man whose local knowledge keeps belief alive long enough to matter. He does not merely provide a suspect or a sketch. He gives the trap its body, the escape its shape, and the aftermath its practical logic.

The chapter also sharpens his limitations without diminishing him. Havel suspected a hidden network inside the district. He did not yet understand that the district had rehearsed losing a man. Once the witness drops and the streets start closing, Havel is the first person in the cellar to name the response for what it is: not a search, a net. That distinction matters because it tells everyone what kind of enemy they are standing inside.

By chapter end he does not become an honorary member of the traveling unit. The continuity point is the opposite. Havel stays where his knowledge is strongest. He remains Andorhal's local ally, the captain who can split caches, salt false trails, and make the city harder to clean while the main hunt widens toward Eastweald.
