# Jaina Proudmoore

Jaina enters chapter 14 carrying two separate burdens at once: the answer she won in Scholomance, and the room she still cannot explain without sounding guilty even to herself. The chalk on her fingers survives lake water, canteen water, and distance. So does the damage. She rides like someone holding the line between usefulness and collapse by force of will alone.

The chapter locks in a new version of her voice. She can brief the plague with brutal clarity now — grain as shell, the body learning the deeper answer, death releasing what has already been taught — but the emotional truth of the sanctum remains withheld. That is the chapter's central wound. Jaina is no longer omitting by accident or delay. She chooses the partial truth on purpose because the road cannot survive the whole one yet.

What saves her from becoming purely evasive is that she keeps converting knowledge into action. By the time the south-track rider reports Hearthglen failing again and Arthas racing Stratholme, Jaina is already mapping granaries, bakehouses, and holding yards in her head. She still does not have a cure. What she has is narrower and uglier: a chance to cut the next dose before it enters enough bodies to finish the city.

Her relationship to Antonidas sharpens too. He gets almost all of the truth, enough to act and enough to fear for her, but not the room itself. Her line — “He wasn't mad.” — reveals the part of the wound she still cannot safely unpack: the horror was never stupidity, only brilliance without ethics. By chapter end, Jaina remains ashamed and guarded, but she is no longer inert inside that shame. She is riding toward Stratholme with an answer that might still matter.
