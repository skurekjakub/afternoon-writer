# Captain Havel

## Chapter 7 state
Havel is a street-level officer who has been solving the wrong scale of problem correctly for months. He cannot name the necromancy. He can name the district, the crews, the false shifts, the new lock, the rear lane, and the men who never drink with the others.

His authority comes from lived ground. He works the district on foot, keeps windows in sight while he talks, and makes his case with dates and doors instead of rhetoric. The chapter's key fact about him is that his instincts are right even when his framework is incomplete.

Write him as practical, suspicious, and immediately more useful than polished. He is not a grand captain. He is the man who kept looking after everyone above him stopped.
