# Neral

Neral arrives in chapter 3 as the rear rider most likely to become a courier and leaves it having proven she is more than that. The big new fact about her is how she handles fear. In the cellar she crouches instead of looming, keeps her hands empty, and talks to Miri with the same practical steadiness she uses on nervous horses. Later she gets the girl into the saddle with the same tone: calm, specific, almost matter-of-factly kind.

That makes Sylvanas's choice feel earned. Neral is already the best dispatch rider in the party; now she is also the one adult the child can actually bear to go with. Her northbound ride is not a retreat from the story's danger. It is the chapter's first real act of protection.

Write Neral as calm without softness draining out of her competence. She is not sentimental. She is useful in exactly the way a frightened child and an urgent warning both need.
