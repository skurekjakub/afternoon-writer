# Cyndia

Cyndia's role in chapter 14 is small in volume and enormous in function. She holds the left, carries the relay stone, notices the silence, and refuses to interfere with it. That refusal is not passivity. It is discipline. Cyndia understands that the line does not need commentary from her; it needs the next message delivered cleanly and the next threat intercepted before it strikes the formation broadside.

The chapter sharpens her into a relay-and-response specialist. She passes Lor'themar's recall forward without noise, slides her mare across the road when the south-track rider bursts from the lane, and has her bow up before anyone else has finished deciding what the interruption means. Later, when Jaina asks how far to the next relay, Cyndia gives the answer in the exact number of words required and no more.

Lyriel's absence still lives in her body. The empty-right check remains. But chapter 14 shows what that grief has become: not hesitation, not confession, but habit redirected into competence. She is the surviving witness to the damaged formation and the practical reason it still works.

By chapter end Cyndia is still doing what she has increasingly done since chapter 9: holding the exterior thread so the women at the center can afford to carry heavier things. She cannot mend the crack between Jaina and Sylvanas. She can make sure the crack does not cost them the next city.
