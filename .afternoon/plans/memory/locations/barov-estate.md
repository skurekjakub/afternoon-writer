# Barov Estate

The Barov estate is the chapter's first great correction. After weeks of plague sites that looked stripped, abandoned, or hastily cleaned, this place is alive. Trimmed ditches, worked orchards, pale walls, stable traffic, servants who know how to keep their faces smooth under scrutiny. It is prosperous enough to feel offensive.

That prosperity is the point. The estate is not the monster's ruined lair. It is the respectable front door. Alexei's great hall, the tea waiting in advance, the enchanted warmth instead of visible fire — every choice tells the same story: House Barov intends to remain legible as civilization for as long as possible.

Use this location whenever the story needs wealth as camouflage. The danger here does not smell like panic. It smells like expensive wool, old stone, and the certainty that everyone in the room has practiced this conversation before.


## Chapter 11 update

Chapter 11 reclassifies the estate from suspicious host site to confirmed threshold. The relays can now say, cleanly, that Jaina entered under mage authority and crossed on from there. The estate is no longer just where the trail pointed. It is part of the chain that swallowed her.
