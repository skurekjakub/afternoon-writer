# Caer Darrow

## Current read
Island walls, slick black stone at the waterline, an upper window left open a hand's width, and
old fortress stone carrying institutional traffic instead of noble domestic life. Mist-thinned
quiet outside, ward hum through the walls, and then the building's own interior signals passing
downward once the breach is made. Cold wet masonry outside, then preserving salts, dust, and old
blood from the service levels within. Seams between lake-slick stones, wet mortar beneath the
hands, and a keep whose outer wall already feels like part of the academy below it. A fortress
that can still be infiltrated, but only through the neglected edge rather than the face it shows
visitors.

## Chapter 13
Sylvanas breaches Caer Darrow through the east-face window while Cyndia holds the boat and exit
line below. The island releases Sylvanas and Jaina only after the sanctum fight, with Cyndia
taking them back across before the south road claims them.

## Established facts
The east face of Caer Darrow carries less traffic than the public landing and can be breached
through an upper service window when watched routes are too costly. The keep's outer shell,
upper service rooms, and lower Scholomance core operate as one layered defensive environment.
