# Unnamed south-road village

This village matters because it is still alive enough to look normal. There are wagons in the square, bread on sale, arguments about work, and enough routine left that the locals can still pretend they are dealing with a hard fever season. That surface normality is the location's defining trait. Millhaven was aftermath. This place is active infection hiding inside habit.

Everything in chapter 4 turns on that contrast. Jaina reads the sickness in living bodies while Sylvanas reads the fear, stores, and delivery pattern underneath the village's ordinary face. The common house and granary become the chapter's paired interiors: one room where time is being bought, one room where the mechanism is exposed. By the end of the chapter the village is no longer a mystery site. It is a wounded place left behind with instructions while the investigation rides on toward the source.
