# Corin's Crossing

Corin's Crossing enters the story as a report before it enters as a scene. The important continuity fact is that it is still alive when the party leaves Millhaven. The old grain seller places it four days back as a functioning market town with a cough moving through it but not yet enough collapse to stop trade. That makes it the next pressure point on the road and the next test of whether the pattern can still be caught before total village death.

Write Corin's Crossing from this chapter's residue as a place where ordinary life is still strong enough to hide danger. Markets still work. Riders can still cross the sound bridge at the mill fork. People can still mistake plague conditions for seasonal illness and supply trouble. The horror there should therefore feel delayed rather than absent.
