# Eastweald

Chapter 14 finally lets Eastweald look ordinary again, which is exactly what makes it frightening. Harvested fields, orchard rows, wagons groaning north, dogs behind hedges, windows kindling for supper — the road still carries the visual grammar of a kingdom that thinks evening meals are the thing that matters. That apparent normalcy is now inseparable from what Jaina knows about the grain.

The location therefore shifts from occupied countryside to endangered normalcy. Earlier chapters established Eastweald as plague-administered rural space. Chapter 14 sharpens the emotional contrast: life is still visibly happening here even while the riders are already speaking in terms that belong to city collapse. The bells matter too. One rings behind them for evening meal. Another answers farther south in alarm. That is the chapter's geography in miniature.

Eastweald also becomes a briefing space. The road itself carries the larger war frame, the omitted truth, the institutional refusals, and the relay that turns Hearthglen from crisis into failed buffer. By the end of the chapter, Eastweald is the last stretch of Lordaeron that still looks salvageable before Stratholme begins to prove otherwise.
