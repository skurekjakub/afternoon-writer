# Strahnbrad

Strahnbrad in chapter 6 is not a village first. It is a military zone built on top of village farmland. Banners, rope lines, pickets, quartermasters, healers, forge noise, and muddy lanes have eaten the edges of the fields. Even the ground has changed from farmland to command space.

That matters because this is where visible war lives. The place makes Arthas's refusal legible. Strahnbrad is full of soldiers, wounded men, supply carts, and immediate problems. The plague comes into that environment as a second war without a front line, and the camp rejects it almost by instinct.

Write Strahnbrad as organized pressure: competent, busy, and exactly the sort of place that would make an invisible threat lose the argument.
