# Kel'Thuzad's Sanctum

## Current read
A cold cup by Jaina's place, boards worked in two hands, a desk, specimen jars, white ice panes
across the exits, and later blood dark down Kel'Thuzad's robe behind the desk. Conversation
stopping into stillness, bowstring note under cold air, cracking ice lattice, and then the
abrupt silence after the final line is spoken. Chalk, old paper, preservative, cold tea, and
fresh blood over the room's earlier dry scholar-calm. Chalk dust on skin and wood, floor
vibration under ward pulse, and cold air made sharper by Jaina's containment magic. A scholar's
office that becomes a wound. The room is easy to read, easy to misread, and brutal precisely
because it stays so lucid while the damage lands.

## Chapter 13
Jaina turns the exits into containment, Sylvanas takes the northeast lane, and Kel'Thuzad falls
behind the desk after the throat shot. Jaina gives Sylvanas the road-useful mechanism summary
here before both women leave the rest unsaid and climb back out.

## Established facts
From the doorway, the sanctum presents enough honest external evidence to support a damaging
misread: cold tea, active boards, chalk on Jaina, and no visible coercion. The sanctum's
existing room geometry can be turned into a short lethal cage once Jaina starts sealing exits
and Sylvanas exploits the lane she leaves open. Kel'Thuzad dies here in chapter 13, and the
wards continue cycling for a time after his fall before they begin to slip.
