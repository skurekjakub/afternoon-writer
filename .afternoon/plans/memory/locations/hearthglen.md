# Hearthglen

Hearthglen never comes back on stage directly in chapter 14, but the chapter sharpens it into something more precise and more frightening than the earlier relay version. The town held. Then it nearly failed again after dark. Priests are burning the wounded before families finish the words over them because bodies keep answering after the lull. That is no longer a stable military fallback. It is a fortification losing its argument with the plague in real time.

The important continuity shift is that Hearthglen stops functioning as destination and starts functioning as failing buffer. Arthas has already moved on. Uther remains tied to the field. The protagonists are now riding toward the city beyond the city, which means Hearthglen's real role is to show how little time remains and how badly visible defense is already being outpaced.

In chapter 14 the place becomes memorable less as architecture than as condition: a furnace with gates, still standing, still manned, and no longer enough.
