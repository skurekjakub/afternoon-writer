# Stratholme

Stratholme enters memory in chapter 14 before the protagonists ever reach its walls. It arrives first as relay destination, then as Jaina's mental map: granaries, bakehouses, warehouse lanes, service roads, doors still open because ordinary life has not yet accepted what the grain has become. The chapter makes the city vivid through anticipation rather than direct scene work.

That anticipation is functional. Stratholme is not merely the next famous place on the route. It is the first city where Jaina can finally turn the completed mechanism into a narrow operational answer. If they can still do anything, it will happen at the city's feed points — where stock remains stock long enough to break, burn, freeze, or deny before it disappears into bodies.

The location therefore enters the ledger already split between civic normalcy and imminent atrocity. Arthas is racing toward it. The protagonists are chasing him. And chapter 14 makes clear that once they arrive, the city may already be too far inside the process for warning alone to matter.
