# Magic

The major magical advance in chapter 14 is not new theory. It is applied logic. Jaina already knows how the plague works. What the chapter adds is the first convincing translation of that knowledge into city intervention: if the contaminated shell can still be hit at granaries, bakehouses, or holding yards before it disappears into bodies, then a narrow answer may still exist.

That answer remains brutally limited. Cold or fire may destroy stock. Neither cures people already conditioned by repeated exposure. The chapter therefore preserves the scale and cruelty of the problem even while making the mechanism more useful.

Chapter 14 also spreads one more magical truth outward: Kel'Thuzad's expected return after death is no longer private sanctum knowledge. It is now part of the road party's strategic understanding of what kind of enemy they are really facing.
