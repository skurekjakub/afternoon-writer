# Geography

Chapter 14 adds an important final pre-catastrophe layer to the book's geography. The road out of Darrowmere and down through Eastweald still looks like Lordaeron at supper time: harvested fields, orchards, hedges, wagons, lit windows. That matters because the protagonists now know the road is also a plague corridor feeding the next city.

The chapter also clarifies the map logic between Darrowmere, Hearthglen, and Stratholme. These are not isolated dramatic nodes. They are connected by ordinary agricultural and service routes, which is why Jaina can think about granaries, bakehouses, and holding yards before she ever sees Stratholme's walls.

Geography in chapter 14 therefore stops being scenic backdrop and becomes one continuous chain of ordinary land carrying extraordinary danger. The closer the riders get to Stratholme, the less room remains between farmland and catastrophe.
