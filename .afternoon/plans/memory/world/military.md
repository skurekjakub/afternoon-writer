# Military

Chapter 14 makes formation itself meaningful. The line is now stable in its damage: Cyndia left, Jaina center, Sylvanas forward-right by half a step. That spacing is tactical, emotional, and load-bearing all at once.

The chapter also sharpens what Hearthglen means militarily. The town held, but only provisionally. The dead still answer after short lulls. The wounded are burned early. Arthas has already taken the main body south-east toward Stratholme. In other words, the military line is functioning, but it is functioning in retreat from stability.

Relay speed becomes part of doctrine here as well. Once Jaina produces a usable narrow plan, Sylvanas immediately halves the interval to the next station. That small command choice matters because it shows how thoroughly the story has shifted from investigation to war-tempo response.
