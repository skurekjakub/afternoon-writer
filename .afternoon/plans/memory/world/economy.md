# Economy

Chapter 14 keeps insisting that the plague works because normal economic life keeps functioning. Wagons still move. People still hurry home for supper. Cities still rely on granaries, bakehouses, and holding yards. Ordinary throughput remains the enemy's best weapon.

What changes in this chapter is that the protagonists can finally name the choke points. The food economy is no longer just the means of spread; it is also the last place where intervention might still matter. That is why Stratholme becomes immediately legible to Jaina as a map of supply nodes rather than merely a city on the horizon.

The chapter therefore reframes economic continuity as both doom and opportunity. The same routines feeding Lordaeron are what make Lordaeron destroy itself. But if those routines can still be interrupted early enough, they may also become the narrow margin by which part of a city survives.
