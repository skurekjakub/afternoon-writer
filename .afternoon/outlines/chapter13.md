# Chapter 13: The Arrival

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 12. Opens with Sylvanas and Cyndia reaching Caer Darrow while Jaina is still in Kel'Thuzad's sanctum, and ends on the road south after the clean exit and the first Hearthglen relay.
**Open location:** Darrowmere shoreline opposite Caer Darrow.
**Transport:** Mounted approach before open; rowboat across Darrowmere; on foot for the breach, descent, sanctum fight, and exit.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Immediate objective:** Get into Scholomance, find Jaina, and get her back out before whatever runs the place closes around her.

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** Get inside, read the room, and pull Jaina out before the building eats the trail.
- **Surface objective:** Breach Caer Darrow, find Jaina, and extract her with the intelligence intact.
- **Pressure source:** She is arriving late to an arcane room with one ranger outside, one dead wing behind her, and no access to the invisible fight that may already have happened inside.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** She trusts visible behavior completely - posture, spacing, tools in hand, tone through a door - and once Jandice gives her a first read, she treats the coming tableau as something her eyes can settle cleanly.
- **Chapter test:** The sanctum gives her a perfect external picture and none of the internal context that would explain it.
- **Forced choice:** Either refuse to trust the evidence in front of her, or trust it and carry Jaina out as a partner with a newly discovered failure mode.
- **End-state shift:** She gets Jaina out and kills Kel'Thuzad, but trust has moved into the confirmation step.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 14 opens with Sylvanas half a step ahead, closer than before, checking Jaina instead of moving level with her.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** The missing partner inside the room, holding the crucial intelligence in her head and too much evidence of an unseen conversation on her hands.
- **POV misread at open:** Jaina is below as someone who needs extraction, not as someone who has already spent hours engaged with the man at the table.
- **Correction earned here:** The chalk, the boards, the tea, the posture, and Kel'Thuzad's tone all tell Sylvanas she is looking at engagement rather than simple captivity.
- **Interaction rule:** After the door opens, keep every exchange short. The real damage lands through silence, spacing, and what each woman now watches in the other.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Split team. Cyndia holds the outside thread while Sylvanas goes in alone to retrieve the missing half of the pair.
- **Operational change this chapter:** They still kill like two women who know each other's hands, but after the sanctum both of them start checking before they trust.
- **Naming/attitude rule:** Keep everything cold and professional. No romance bleed, no shouting, no jealous overread.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** This is the first crack, not the breakup. The silence is the event.

## Scene 1: The Approach

**Scene function:** Get Sylvanas and Cyndia to the lake, confirm that Jaina crossed to Caer Darrow, and force a covert island breach that still leaves Sylvanas to go below alone.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Knowledge at scene start:** Jaina went in and did not come out; the estate is occupied and the real source lies across the lake.

### Beat 1 - Scene: The Shore Read
- **Goal:** Reach the Darrowmere access point and confirm where Jaina actually went.
- **Conflict:** The Barov estate is only the front half of the problem. Jaina's trail runs through the house and out to the lake, where the real source sits on Caer Darrow behind mist and an outer ward line.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas confirms Jaina crossed to the island, but any obvious approach over the water will be seen.
- **Value shift:** mounted approach -> island extraction
- **New on-page information:**
  - The deepest source is on Caer Darrow, not in the manor surface alone
  - Jaina reached the shore, took a boat, and left no return line behind her
  - The lake crossing itself is part of the estate's security, not just the road to the island
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What is holding the island together from below
  - Whether Jaina is trapped, negotiating, or already dead
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Mist sitting low over green-black water
  - An outer ward shimmer hanging over the lake like a skin
  - Rowboats pulled half out of the mud beside an abandoned landing
- **Transition intent:** Once the shore read is clear, the team has to decide how to cross without announcing Sylvanas before she ever reaches the wall.

### Beat 2 - Scene: The Split
- **Goal:** Set the crossing and preserve one surviving route back out of the island.
- **Conflict:** The team is down to three women and cannot afford a loud breach; one ranger has to hold the boat and the window exit or Sylvanas loses her only clean way back out.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas gets a covert crossing and an east-face window breach, but taking it means leaving Cyndia on the outside thread while she goes below alone.
- **Value shift:** island extraction -> covert entry
- **New on-page information:**
  - The outer ward can be crossed more quietly by hugging the east wall in a low boat than by taking the public landing
  - The east-face window gives Sylvanas an entry route that bypasses the front approach and the curated interior path Jaina used
  - Cyndia will hold the boat, the breach point, and the relay line instead of following below
  - Lyriel's absence is now built into the shape of every decision the team makes
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What waits on the island's upper levels
  - Whether Sylvanas will find Jaina quickly enough for the crossing to hold
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Oarlocks wrapped in cloth so the crossing stays quiet
  - Mist closing behind the boat as soon as they push off
  - A brief softening in the ward pressure near the east wall below the window
  - Cyndia's automatic right-side check before she settles in place
  - Wet stone under Sylvanas's hands as she takes the window climb
- **Transition intent:** The crossing gets Sylvanas onto the island, but she still has to get through the upper levels without using the route built for invited scholars.
- **Planted thread:** Cyndia's right-side check carries Lyriel's absence forward into the reformed three-woman formation at close.

## Scene 2: The Descent

**Scene function:** Make Sylvanas earn the breach - side window, upper-service movement, Jandice ambush, then the older crypt route down to the sanctum.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jandice
**Knowledge at scene start:** Sylvanas is on the island alone and any use of the public route will light her up to the whole place.

### Beat 3 - Scene: The Window
- **Goal:** Get through the east-face breach, stay off the public approach, and keep Jaina's trail alive.
- **Conflict:** The island route was never meant for intruders. Sylvanas has to come in through a side window, cut through service rooms and upper archives, and avoid the corridors built to funnel guests and students where the building can watch them. The off-route rooms immediately show her what the public path was designed to hide.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Sylvanas gets inside clean, and the route immediately tells her the deeper defense is still waiting farther below.
- **Value shift:** covert entry -> the building shows its true shape
- **New on-page information:**
  - The east-face breach leads into service, archive, and prep space above the public route, not straight into the main halls
  - Scholomance's upper levels still carry the bones of the Barov estate under their necromantic conversion
  - Side rooms hold peasant bodies in different stages of conversion - some still breathing, some dead and moving, some reduced to parts that still answer ward pulses
  - Reanimated hands and other stripped parts are being worked as separate test stock rather than only as whole corpses
  - Jaina's trail keeps going down toward older crypt architecture and never turns back
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who designed the deeper defenses
  - What condition Jaina is in below the upper levels
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Lake water dripping from the sill inside the breach window
  - Blue-white fungi pulsing brighter when Sylvanas moves past
  - A peasant hand scratching uselessly at a table edge without the body it belonged to
  - Dusty archive air turning to preservative cold as she moves inward
  - Chemical preservation over a sweeter old-rot smell and fresh-cut flesh
  - Cold that feels like heat is being pulled out of the stone, not merely absent
- **Transition intent:** A living mage moving where she should not be gives Sylvanas a source instead of only architecture.

### Beat 4 - Scene: The Wounded Pride
- **Goal:** Neutralize the living mage in the upper levels and get something useful out of her before going lower.
- **Conflict:** Jandice is angry at being dismissed, not frightened enough to stay quiet, and her jealousy makes every detail she spits out both valuable and dangerous. She also treats the rooms Sylvanas just crossed as ordinary stock space, which tells Sylvanas exactly what kind of place Scholomance really is.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas secures the source and gets real information, but the information poisons the way she will read the room below.
- **Value shift:** the building shows its true shape -> poisoned first read
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jandice was sent away so Kel'Thuzad could keep talking to Jaina alone
  - Jandice heard enough to know Jaina was asking real questions and being answered in full
  - Kel'Thuzad valued the exchange enough to dismiss his own student from it
  - Jandice makes plain that Jaina was given the polished route while the filthier work stayed off that tour
  - The older crypt stair below the upper research levels is the fast way down if Sylvanas wants the sanctum without taking the guest route
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How much of Jandice's framing is jealousy and how much is plain fact
  - What the sanctum itself will show once Sylvanas reaches it
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jandice's reagent-burned hands under Sylvanas's grip
  - Fungi-light catching chalk dust in the seams of a scholar's sleeve and dried blood at the cuff edge
  - Anger in Jandice's voice that sounds insulted before it sounds afraid
- **Transition intent:** Sylvanas takes the older crypt route down with Jandice's complaint already sitting in her head.
- **Disclosure provenance:** Jandice's wounded complaint is the first on-page source for Sylvanas's belief that Jaina and Kel'Thuzad were engaged as peers; it is emotionally loaded complaint, not neutral debrief.

### Beat 5 - Scene: The Catacomb Route
- **Goal:** Reach the sanctum without taking the public corridor and learn what kind of room waits beyond the last turn.
- **Conflict:** The old crypt stair is tighter, colder, and more tightly watched than the upper levels, but it keeps Sylvanas off the route built for invited scholars. It also runs past the real waste of the institution - failed stock, butchered remains, and the parts of the work no polite tour would name. By the time she reaches the final approach, the voices inside have already confirmed Jandice's story.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the catacomb route gets Sylvanas to the sanctum unseen, and the voices make the coming tableau worse before she even crosses the threshold.
- **Value shift:** poisoned first read -> certainty at the door
- **New on-page information:**
  - The fastest covert route to the sanctum runs through older crypt architecture repurposed for lower-level research traffic
  - The lower route passes discard rooms and holding spaces that make the clean sanctum above them feel like a lie told on top of the work
  - Jaina and Kel'Thuzad are speaking in a real two-directional academic cadence
  - The sanctum door is standing open because neither person inside was guarding against interruption
  - Kel'Thuzad still sounds calm and fully in control of the room
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What the room itself will show once Sylvanas enters
  - Whether Jaina's face will match the voice Sylvanas is hearing
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Bone-dry dust on the older stair under newer ward-ripple light
  - A jaw still moving in a bucket of preservative beside the wall
  - Kel'Thuzad's lecture cadence carrying through the stone
  - Jaina's lower, more analytical register answering it
  - Corridor cold tightening Sylvanas's bow hand by a hair
- **Transition intent:** Once Sylvanas steps through the door, the chapter becomes a room-read instead of an approach.

## Scene 3: The Room

**Scene function:** Put Sylvanas in front of a tableau that any perfect external reader would misread the same way.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Knowledge at scene start:** Jandice's complaint and the voices through the door both say the conversation was real.
**Arc pressure:** Everything visible in this room points one way. The only evidence against that read happened inside Jaina and is unavailable to Sylvanas.

### Beat 6 - Scene: The Tableau
- **Goal:** Cross the threshold and assess the sanctum fast enough to act.
- **Conflict:** Every visible detail reinforces the same read - Kel'Thuzad seated and satisfied, Jaina near the boards instead of the exits, chalk on her hands, two different hands written across the room.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Sylvanas gets the room in one sweep, and the sweep confirms every poisoned assumption she was carrying down the corridor.
- **Value shift:** certainty at the door -> the tableau lands
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad is seated, unhurried, and not behaving like a cornered man
  - Jaina is positioned in engagement posture, not defensive posture
  - Jaina has chalk dust on her fingers and evidence of participation on the notation boards
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the notation in Jaina's hand is counterargument, collaboration, or both
  - What happened in the hours before Sylvanas reached the room
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Chalk dust on Jaina's left hand
  - Cold tea on a side table near Jaina rather than near Kel'Thuzad
  - Fungi-light flattening the room into a scholar's office instead of a lair
- **Transition intent:** Seeing the tableau is not enough; Sylvanas now has to file what it means and act on that file.

### Beat 7 - Sequel: The Room-Read
- **Emotion:** Cold professional certainty
- **Dilemma:** Trust the external evidence completely, or hold back from the one thing her training has always been built to do - file the room and move.
- **Decision:** File Jaina as a trusted partner with a newly discovered vulnerability that now has to be watched.
- **Value shift:** the tableau lands -> the judgment locks
- **New on-page information:**
  - The conversation lasted long enough to cover boards, burn through chalk, and cool a cup on the side table
  - The evidence points to active two-way intellectual engagement, not simple coercion
  - Kel'Thuzad valued the exchange enough that Jandice envied it
  - After the holding rooms and discard work above, the sanctum's clean office reads as refinement rather than contradiction
  - The critical missing fact lives inside Jaina, not anywhere Sylvanas can see from the doorway
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - That Jaina fought the argument for hours before the posture in the room settled where it did
  - That the chalk marks in Jaina's hand were part of a counterargument rather than surrender
  - Whether Jaina will be able to challenge this read without making it worse
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - The cold cup near Jaina's place at the table
  - Two hands visible on the boards even from the door
  - Sylvanas's body going still in the particular way it does before a new file is sealed
- **Transition intent:** Kel'Thuzad only has to speak one honest line now to harden that judgment into damage.

### Beat 8 - Sequel: The Recalculation
- **Emotion:** Cold shock held under discipline
- **Dilemma:** Treat Kel'Thuzad's praise as manipulation and discount it, or hear the sincerity in it and accept what that sincerity does to the room.
- **Decision:** Carry that judgment forward as real, finish the kill, and leave the explanation for later even though later may never come.
- **Value shift:** the judgment locks -> the crack opens
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad openly and sincerely values Jaina's mind
  - Sylvanas moves Jaina from instinctively trusted to someone she now has to watch
  - Jaina sees the shift happen and knows any defense she could offer would sound like confirmation
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any later explanation can narrow the gap that just opened
  - How much this new watchfulness will alter their next chapter on the road
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Kel'Thuzad's calm voice turning a compliment into a wound
  - Sylvanas's total stillness rather than any flash of anger
  - Silence settling over the sanctum harder than the lecture ever did
- **Transition intent:** The silence only lasts until muscle memory takes over and the two women move on the same target.
- **Dialogue guidance:** Kel'Thuzad's compliment must land as genuine scholarly truth, not villain needling. The damage comes from honesty.

## Scene 4: The Partnership Activates

**Scene function:** Prove the partnership still works below the level where the crack just opened, then let Kel'Thuzad die exactly as the scholar he has been all chapter.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Knowledge at scene start:** Sylvanas has filed the misread and Jaina knows it.

### Beat 9 - Scene: The Seamless Mechanism
- **Goal:** End Kel'Thuzad before he can leave the sanctum or turn the room again.
- **Conflict:** The fight takes seconds, and the terrible proof is that the partnership's muscle memory still works even while its trust layer is being poisoned in real time.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Kel'Thuzad is trapped and lined up for the shot, but the tiny delays inside the exchange prove the crack is already affecting how both women move.
- **Value shift:** the crack opens -> functional damage
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina turns the ward layout into containment instead of trying to overpower Kel'Thuzad directly
  - Sylvanas takes the northeast lane Jaina leaves open and uses it for the throat shot
  - Both women confirm before they commit in ways they did not need to earlier in the partnership
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the fight's clean finish can keep this new watchfulness from following them out of the room
  - What Kel'Thuzad will do with his final breath
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Ice-lattice barriers collapsing the exits in hard white planes
  - Bowstring note lowered by the cold
  - The fraction of extra space Jaina leaves in the barrier pattern for Sylvanas to choose from
- **Transition intent:** The shot lands, but Kel'Thuzad still has one last observation to give them before the room lets go of him.

### Beat 10 - Scene: The Dying Data Point
- **Goal:** Finish Kel'Thuzad and leave the sanctum with the target down.
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad does not die like a cornered villain. He dies like a scholar completing a thought, and the calm of that death adds one more line of evidence to Sylvanas's filed read.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the target is eliminated, but his final line and his lack of fear make the room harder to forget rather than easier to leave.
- **Value shift:** functional damage -> objective completed
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad's final spoken data point is the northeastern corridor's concentration rather than any plea or threat
  - He expected death and priced it into the continuity of his work
  - Sylvanas notices the calm and files it as evidence even though she does not yet have the full resurrection context Jaina has
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How useful Kel'Thuzad's last line will be once they are back on the road
  - Whether Jaina can carry both the intelligence and the room's damage out without dropping one
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - The arrow in Kel'Thuzad's throat arriving like a period
  - Kel'Thuzad's hands staying on the table instead of going to the wound
  - Fungi-light pulsing once and then settling lower
- **Transition intent:** With the target down, the only thing left is the climb back out and the shape the formation takes once daylight finds them again.

## Scene 5: The Ascent

**Scene function:** Get them out of Scholomance, re-form the three-woman line, and show the crack as spacing, timing, and silence rather than argument.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia
**Knowledge at scene start:** Kel'Thuzad is dead, Jaina has the intelligence, and neither woman has said the thing that matters.

### Beat 11 - Scene: The Ascent
- **Goal:** Leave the sanctum and get back to Cyndia without losing the route or the intelligence.
- **Conflict:** The exit is tactically clean, but the spacing has changed - Sylvanas runs closer, Jaina lets herself be escorted, and the whole climb turns into a field report neither woman speaks aloud.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they get out clean, but the changed spacing makes the crack visible in movement.
- **Value shift:** objective completed -> extraction in progress
- **New on-page information:**
  - Sylvanas now ranges closer to Jaina than she did before the sanctum
  - Jaina reads the escort spacing and does not challenge it
  - Jandice is already gone from the upper levels by the time they climb out
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Sylvanas will ever share how much Jandice's complaint shaped the room-read
  - Whether Jaina will try to explain the sanctum before they reach the next crisis
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Fungi-light dimmer on the way up than it was on the way down
  - Pressure in the walls shifting as the building redistributes around Kel'Thuzad's absence
  - Jaina's silence carrying more weight than her footfalls
- **Transition intent:** Once they break back into daylight, the formation has to take Cyndia back in and carry the next crisis south at once.

### Beat 12 - Sequel: The Formation Reconstituted
- **Emotion:** Damaged efficiency
- **Dilemma:** Stop and force the conversation now, or keep moving because Hearthglen is already burning and the road south will not wait for private repair.
- **Decision:** Keep moving. Carry the silence. Let the briefing happen before the explanation that will not come.
- **Value shift:** extraction in progress -> formation reconstituted, damaged
- **New on-page information:**
  - Cyndia's relay says Hearthglen is overwhelmed and Arthas is already moving toward Stratholme
  - The re-formed line is three women now, with Lyriel's absence on the right and Sylvanas's half-step oversight in the middle
  - Jaina will give the intelligence product in Chapter 14 without giving the sanctum's internal truth
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the crack can be repaired before the next catastrophe forces them deeper together again
  - Whether any institution will hear the intelligence in time to matter
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Late light on contaminated Eastweald soil
  - Cyndia's second right-side check when she falls back into line
  - Sylvanas's half-step forward changing the whole feel of the road
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 14 picks up on this same road with Jaina briefing while they walk.
