# Chapter 15: The Prince

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 14 on the road south. Opens on the approach to Hearthglen and closes with Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia riding ahead of Arthas's main column toward Stratholme to try to kill the plague at its source before the city fully turns.
**Open location:** South road into Hearthglen's blighted outskirts.
**Transport:** Horseback approach from the Eastweald; on foot through Hearthglen and Arthas's camp; back to horseback for the ride toward Stratholme.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia.
**Immediate objective:** Reach Arthas, hand over the full plague picture, and decide whether there is still any move left before Stratholme goes under.

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** Accurate intelligence should let her steer the prince toward useful force.
- **Surface objective:** Hand Arthas everything he needs and keep ahead of the plague's pace.
- **Pressure source:** Hearthglen's ruin, Uther's unease, the partnership scar beside her, and a prince with an army she does not command.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas still wants to treat Arthas like a lever she can aim instead of a man whose speed has already outrun caution.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether intelligence creates control here or only creates a faster disaster.
- **Forced choice:** Trust Arthas's main march alone, or ride behind Jaina's smaller, riskier source plan.
- **End-state shift:** Sylvanas still reads Arthas as a fuse, but Jaina stops reading as a passive source and starts reading as a woman trying to use the theory before it is too late.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 16 opens on the head-start ride and the source attempt under a shrinking city clock.

### Arthas through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** Prince, commander, and hard center of the camp's pace.
- **POV misread at open:** Sylvanas wants to read him as ally first and danger second.
- **Correction earned here:** Everything about Hearthglen and the war camp says he converts bad news straight into motion.
- **Interaction rule:** No interiority. Use armor damage, titles, hands on maps, interruptions, and what he does when given time-sensitive options.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Damaged pair inside someone else's war machine, with Cyndia on the left.
- **Change this chapter:** Jaina does more than brief. She immediately tries to turn Kel'Thuzad's teaching into a rescue plan, and Sylvanas has to reckon with that in real time.
- **Naming/attitude rule:** Keep the public competence clean and the private scar in the margins.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** This is not repair by speech. It is repair by who rides where when the clock starts.

## Scene 1: The Held City

**Scene function:** Show that Hearthglen survived only by brute force against a spreading, self-feeding outbreak, then let Uther confirm that the dead are now coming in numbers that no single kill can end.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, refugees, local soldiers, Uther
**Knowledge at scene start:** Arthas is ahead, Hearthglen has already taken the blow, and the partnership crack is still riding with them.
**Arc pressure:** Sylvanas put a prince on this road. Hearthglen shows what that road now looks like under full load.

### Beat 1 - Scene: The Road South
- **Goal:** Reach Hearthglen and read what the plague has done to the country around it.
- **Conflict:** The farther south they ride, the less of the land can pretend nothing happened. Refugees clog the northbound side of the road, orchards are black at the tips, grain stands dead in fields nobody dared reap, and the old three-rider crack is still present even in the saddle.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they make the miles they need, but every mile turns the plague from mapped intelligence into bodies, exhaustion, and land already lost.
- **Value shift:** southbound urgency -> human cost on the road
- **New on-page information:**
  - The plague had time to settle here in a way the earlier chapters only warned about
  - Refugee movement is slow, exhausted, and too large for any local response to absorb cleanly
  - Jaina is already straining under the ambient necromantic pressure before Hearthglen comes into view
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What shape Arthas is in after fighting through Hearthglen
  - Whether the city is merely scarred or still actively losing ground
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - A child's shoe half sunk in road mud
  - Blackened fruit still hanging in an orchard because no one stayed to pick it
  - Wagon wheels dragging under too much family weight and too little horse strength
- **Transition intent:** The road widens into a city that is still working to stay alive.

### Beat 2 - Scene: Hearthglen Still Fighting
- **Goal:** Assess Hearthglen's state and determine what kind of battle was actually fought here.
- **Conflict:** Hearthglen held, but the city is not quiet after the win. Hospital tents are full, pyres are hot, and fresh cries keep rising whenever another body on another stretcher starts moving before the priests can get to it. The dead are not arriving in neat waves. The outbreak is feeding itself.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas confirms the defense was competent, but the field picture says the plague is now attacking in full force and picking up speed.
- **Value shift:** human cost on the road -> outbreak now running hot
- **New on-page information:**
  - Hearthglen's grain had already activated inside a living town before anyone understood the full mechanism
  - Newly dead townsfolk and soldiers are still rejoining the attack if the field misses them for even minutes
  - The self-mustering choir effect is now strong enough that the dead keep coming without Kel'Thuzad standing over them personally
  - The Cult's orders are still being carried out in the field despite Scholomance's loss
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How much of this is blind choir behavior and how much is guided by living handlers
  - Whether Stratholme is already farther gone than Hearthglen was
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Holy-fire scoring on stone near the gate
  - Men hauling a body toward the pyres before it can sit up
  - Bells ringing again before the smoke from the last alarm is gone
- **Transition intent:** The first clean reading does not come from the prince. It comes from the man trying to keep the wounded from joining the next wave.

### Beat 3 - Scene: The Healer's Hands
- **Goal:** Get Uther's reading of Hearthglen and Arthas before entering the war camp.
- **Conflict:** Uther is healing, not commanding, when Sylvanas finds him. The Light in his hands is warm, ordinary, and openly good. The words he chooses around Arthas are not. Hearthglen held because the defenders kept killing the newly dead faster than the choir could fold them back in.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas gets the firsthand read she needs, but Uther's visible goodness only sharpens how bad the prince's road may already be.
- **Value shift:** outbreak now running hot -> warning in human form
- **New on-page information:**
  - Uther is still a field healer first, not only a title
  - The city was saved by speed, holy fire, and constant reclearing, not by breaking the plague cycle
  - Uther notices the three-rider formation and its scar before formal introductions do any work
  - He already fears what Arthas will do in a city larger than this one
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Uther thinks the prince can still be checked
  - Whether Arthas will hear anything except urgency once the next city is named
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Golden warmth rising off Uther's scarred hands
  - A wounded soldier breathing easier under the Light before Uther even steps back
  - The older hammer at Uther's side, heavier and plainer than the prince's blessed weapon
- **Transition intent:** With Hearthglen priced, the chapter has to enter the tent where price turns into orders.

## Scene 2: The Tent

**Scene function:** Put the plague picture on Arthas's table, then let Jaina turn that picture into a narrow source plan before the room can collapse into fatalism.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, staff officers
**Knowledge at scene start:** Hearthglen proves the outbreak is accelerating and Stratholme is the next obvious city.
**Arc pressure:** If Arthas hears only doom, he will march with only steel. If Jaina can still name one narrow path, it must be named now.

### Beat 4 - Scene: Lady Proudmoore
- **Goal:** Meet Arthas in person and get the first direct read on what he has become.
- **Conflict:** Arthas is visibly changed before he says anything useful: damaged plate left unrepaired, eyes fixed too hard on the map, and the formal "Lady Proudmoore" landing on Jaina instead of her name. Sylvanas can see the history as a live wire without yet knowing its full shape.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Sylvanas confirms Arthas is still an ally in form, but he is already moving like a commander whose answers come too fast.
- **Value shift:** warning in human form -> personal fault lines visible
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas has stopped spending time on anything that does not move him toward the next decision
  - The title "Lady Proudmoore" is a loaded choice, not neutral etiquette
  - Uther stands behind the prince carrying concern in his grip rather than in his voice
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Arthas can still hear a plan that buys time rather than spends bodies
  - The full shape of the Jaina-Arthas history gap
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - A cracked shoulder guard splitting the Menethil crest
  - Dried blood in polished grooves that were never cleaned out
  - Jaina's posture tightening for a fraction before she answers him professionally
- **Transition intent:** Once the room is set, the plague picture has to be put down in the order the prince can act on it.

### Beat 5 - Scene: The Full Picture
- **Goal:** Deliver the full Sylvanas-and-Jaina briefing to Arthas's command.
- **Conflict:** The partnership still carries the crack, but the room does not have time for private damage. Sylvanas lays down the road, the cult trail, the witness chain, and Hearthglen's field read. Jaina adds the resonance mechanism, the ward mismatch, Kel'Thuzad's death, and the larger chain behind him.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the full picture lands, but Arthas immediately treats it as movement fuel.
- **Value shift:** personal fault lines visible -> full danger on the table
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas now has the full plague picture: Cult routes, resonance, ward failure, Lich King chain, and Kel'Thuzad's death
  - Uther recognizes how cleanly Sylvanas and Jaina still work under pressure
  - Arthas interrupts only for timing, roads, and where the next city will fail first
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the room contains any answer other than immediate movement
  - Whether the theory can do more than explain why cities are dying
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Daggers pinning the map corners instead of proper weights
  - Sylvanas's road marks crossing Jaina's finer arcane notes
  - Arthas's eyes settling on Stratholme and not leaving it
- **Transition intent:** The room needs either a brake or a narrower path, and Jaina is the only person there who can try to name one.

### Beat 6 - Scene: The Source Plan
- **Goal:** Put a practical plague answer on the table before Arthas commits the whole room to steel.
- **Conflict:** Jaina does not ask for time to think. She asks for time to ride. The theory can still matter if they reach Stratholme's grain stores, bakeries, and main holding points before the city finishes seeding itself. It is not a full answer. It is a narrow one, ugly and late, but real.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the room gets a practical plan, but it is a shrinking-window plan and Arthas refuses to wait on it.
- **Value shift:** full danger on the table -> one narrow path still open
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina's countermeasure can be used against source nodes if she gets to them early enough
  - The target is not "the city" in the abstract but specific grain stores, bakehouses, and flow points
  - Jaina is trying to spend Kel'Thuzad's teaching against the plague at once instead of hoarding it
  - Sylvanas sees that attempt and cannot fold it cleanly into her old sanctum read
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Stratholme is already too far seeded for the source plan to matter
  - Whether Arthas hears "head start" as opportunity or as delay
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina's hand flattening on one quarter of the city map instead of the whole thing
  - Chalk marking grain nodes rather than theory circles
  - Uther looking at the plan like a man hearing one last door open
- **Transition intent:** Once a narrow path exists, Arthas has to choose whether he waits for it or marches beside it.

### Beat 7 - Scene: The March
- **Goal:** Fix the relationship between Jaina's source plan and Arthas's army before the room breaks.
- **Conflict:** Arthas refuses to bet Stratholme on waiting. He marches the main column at once. Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia can ride ahead and try the granaries, but the army is moving now and the city will be judged on the road, not in a library.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina gets her attempt, but only under the shadow of an army already on the move.
- **Value shift:** one narrow path still open -> double-track race
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas's answer to uncertainty is still motion
  - Uther stays with the main column because the break has not happened yet
  - The source plan and the army march now run in parallel against the same clock
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the source team can buy enough time to matter
  - What Arthas will do if Jaina says "too late" at the city itself
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Arthas's gauntlet striking the map beside Stratholme
  - Staff officers already moving before the last words in the room are done
  - Falric turning toward the tent flap on the first clear order
- **Transition intent:** The head start becomes real in the departure rush, where the old farewell surfaces again in the most ordinary possible place.

### Beat 8 - Scene: The Departure Table
- **Goal:** Secure fresh horses, route clearance, and immediate departure ahead of the main column.
- **Conflict:** The camp is already turning over too fast. Quartermasters are shouting, fresh tack is coming late, and every minute spent here is a minute the city keeps eating its own bread.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the advance team gets the head start it needs, but the farewell pattern crosses from background oddity into something Sylvanas can no longer dismiss.
- **Value shift:** double-track race -> pattern back in play
- **New on-page information:**
  - The camp has enough discipline to throw a fast rider team out ahead of the army within minutes
  - Cyndia hears the fourth farewell from an ordinary aide handling departure work
  - The same exact phrase now sits in Sylvanas's awareness as a pattern rather than a curiosity
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the pattern matters to the road they are about to take
  - Whether the head start is already too small
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Fresh reins still stiff from the tack chest
  - A route packet slapped onto a crate and pinned under a knife
  - "May the stars keep your road" landing in the middle of shouted departure orders
- **Transition intent:** Once the horses are under them, the chapter stops being a briefing chapter and becomes a race chapter.

## Scene 3: The Head Start

**Scene function:** Put Jaina's source plan on the road and let Sylvanas recognize, without any speech about it, that Jaina is trying to use the terrible knowledge the right way.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia
**Knowledge at scene start:** The main army is marching behind them and the city clock is already running.
**Arc pressure:** Sylvanas still carries the sanctum misread, but the road now gives her new evidence she cannot ignore.

### Beat 9 - Sequel: The Ride Ahead
- **Emotion:** Hard urgency with no room left for the old silence to stay empty.
- **Dilemma:** Treat Jaina's source plan as one more piece of theory that may fail in the field, or treat the speed and shape of the attempt as evidence about who Jaina is when lives are actually on the line.
- **Decision:** Ride with it. Test it in the field. Let the city decide the rest.
- **Value shift:** pattern back in play -> the theory is being spent
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina is already breaking the city into real nodes she can reach, not abstract theory she can admire
  - Sylvanas is willing to put her own speed and authority behind that attempt even while the crack remains
  - The partnership now has two clocks on it at once: the city's and the one between them
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the source plan can beat the city's spread
  - Whether success or failure will change the sanctum read enough to matter
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Three horses stretching into a faster line than the road wants to give them
  - Jaina shouting granary names and district order over the wind instead of speaking in theorem language
  - Cyndia riding the left shoulder of the road while the main column's dust starts rising behind them
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 16 opens on this same hard ride and the first attempt to kill the plague before it can finish the city.
