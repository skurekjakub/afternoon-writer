# Chapter 15: The Prince

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 14 on the south road. Opens on the approach to Hearthglen and closes with Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia riding ahead of Arthas's main column toward Stratholme.
**Where:** South road into Hearthglen and Arthas's war camp.
**Travel:** Horseback approach from the Eastweald, on foot through Hearthglen and the camp, then back to horseback for the ride toward Stratholme.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric.
**Goal:** Reach Arthas, give him the full plague picture, and decide whether there is still one move left before Stratholme goes under.

## Scene 1: Hearthglen

**The action:** The road into Hearthglen shows what the plague looks like once it is fully awake, and Uther confirms the dead are now coming fast enough that simple battlefield killing will not solve it.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, refugees, local soldiers, Uther.
**Starting point:** Arthas is ahead, Hearthglen has already taken the blow, and the crack in the riding line is still there.

### Beat 1
- The country south of Eastweald no longer looks wounded. It looks lost piece by piece. Refugees crowd the northbound side of the road, orchards blacken on the branch, and fields stand dead where no one dared finish the harvest.
- The plague had time to settle here in a way the earlier road only warned about, refugee movement is too large for local response to absorb, and Jaina is already straining under the necromantic pressure before the city comes into view.
- They do not know what shape Arthas is in after Hearthglen or whether the city is merely scarred or still actively losing ground.
- Ground the approach: a child's shoe half sunk in mud, black fruit still hanging in an orchard, wagon wheels dragging under too much family weight.
- The road turns the plague from theory back into bodies.

### Beat 2
- Hearthglen held, but it is still fighting after the win. Hospital tents are full, pyres burn hot, and every fresh death risks standing back up before the priests can reach it.
- Hearthglen's grain already activated inside a living town, the newly dead keep rejoining the attack if the field misses them for even minutes, the dead are still coming without Kel'Thuzad standing over them, and the cult's work is still moving in the field despite Scholomance's loss.
- They do not know how much of this is blind plague behavior and how much is still guided or whether Stratholme is already farther gone than Hearthglen was.
- Ground the city edge: holy fire scored into stone near the gate, men dragging a body to the pyres before it can sit up, bells ringing again before the last smoke clears.
- Sylvanas sees the plague is now feeding itself.

### Beat 3
- Uther is healing when they find him, not commanding. The Light in his hands is warm and ordinary and fully good. His words about Arthas are not. Hearthglen held because the defenders kept killing the newly dead faster than the outbreak could fold them back into the fight.
- Uther is still a field healer first, not only a title, speed and holy fire saved the city rather than any break in the plague cycle, he notices the three-rider line and its scar at once, and he already fears what Arthas will do in a city larger than this one.
- They do not know whether Uther thinks the prince can still be checked or whether Arthas will hear anything except urgency once the next city is named.
- Ground the meeting: golden warmth lifting from Uther's scarred hands, a wounded soldier breathing easier before Uther even steps back, the older hammer at his side heavier and plainer than Arthas's weapon.
- With Hearthglen priced, they have to enter the tent where price turns into orders.

## Scene 2: The War Tent

**The action:** Sylvanas and Jaina put the plague picture on Arthas's table, and Jaina turns that picture into one narrow source plan before the room can collapse into steel and fatalism.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, staff officers.
**Starting point:** Hearthglen proves the outbreak is accelerating and Stratholme is the next obvious city.

### Beat 4
- Arthas looks wrong before he says anything useful. His armor is damaged and unrepaired, his eyes stay fixed on the map, and he calls Jaina Lady Proudmoore instead of her name.
- Arthas has stopped spending time on anything that does not move him toward the next decision, the title is a choice rather than neutral etiquette, and Uther stands behind him carrying concern in his grip rather than his voice.
- They do not know whether Arthas can still hear a plan that buys time instead of spending bodies or the full shape of the history between him and Jaina.
- Ground the tent: a cracked shoulder guard splitting the Menethil crest, dried blood left in polished grooves, Jaina tightening for a fraction before answering him professionally.
- Sylvanas sees an ally in form and a danger in motion.

### Beat 5
- Sylvanas and Jaina give the full briefing together. Sylvanas lays down the roads, the cult trail, the witness chain, and Hearthglen's field read. Jaina adds resonance, ward failure, Kel'Thuzad's death, and the larger chain behind him.
- Arthas now has the full plague picture, Uther sees how cleanly Sylvanas and Jaina still work under pressure, and Arthas interrupts only for timing, roads, and where the next city will fail first.
- They do not know whether the room contains any answer other than immediate movement or whether the theory can do more than explain why cities are dying.
- Ground the briefing: daggers pinning map corners instead of proper weights, Sylvanas's road marks crossing Jaina's finer arcane notes, Arthas's eyes settling on Stratholme and not leaving it.
- The room needs either a brake or a narrower path.

### Beat 6
- Jaina provides the narrower path herself. If they reach Stratholme's grain stores, bakehouses, and main feed points before the city finishes spreading the plague through itself, the theory can still matter.
- Jaina's countermeasure can be used against concentrated source nodes if she gets there early enough, the targets are specific stores and flow points rather than the city in the abstract, Jaina is trying to spend Kel'Thuzad's teaching against the plague at once, and Sylvanas sees that attempt and cannot fit it cleanly into her old read of the sanctum.
- They do not know whether Stratholme is already too far seeded for the source plan to matter or whether Arthas hears head start as opportunity or delay.
- Ground the proposal: Jaina's hand flattening on one quarter of the city map instead of the whole thing, chalk marking grain nodes rather than theory circles, Uther looking at the plan like a man hearing one last door open.
- Once the plan exists, Arthas has to decide whether he waits for it or marches beside it.

### Beat 7
- Arthas refuses to bet Stratholme on waiting. He marches the main column at once. Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia can ride ahead and try the granaries, but the army moves now.
- Arthas's answer to uncertainty is still motion, Uther stays with the main column because the break has not happened yet, and the source plan and the army march now run in parallel against the same clock.
- They do not know whether the source team can buy enough time to matter or what Arthas will do if Jaina says the city is already too late.
- Ground the order: Arthas's gauntlet striking the map beside Stratholme, staff officers moving before the last words are finished, Falric turning for the tent flap on the first clear command.
- The chapter stops being a briefing and becomes a race.

### Beat 8
- The camp turns over fast. Quartermasters shout, tack comes late, and every minute spent here is another minute the city keeps eating its own bread. In the middle of that rush, an ordinary aide sends them off with the same farewell again.
- The camp can throw a fast rider team out ahead of the army within minutes, Cyndia hears the fourth farewell clearly, and the phrase is now a pattern Sylvanas cannot dismiss as coincidence.
- They do not know whether the pattern matters to the road ahead or whether the head start is already too small.
- Ground the departure: fresh reins still stiff from the tack chest, a route packet slapped onto a crate and pinned under a knife, May the stars keep your road landing in the middle of shouted orders.
- Once they mount, there is no room left for talk.

## Scene 3: The Head Start

**The action:** The road gives Sylvanas her first hard proof that Jaina is trying to use the dangerous knowledge the right way.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia.
**Starting point:** The main army is marching behind them and the city clock is already running.

### Beat 9
- The three women ride hard for Stratholme. Jaina is already breaking the city into real targets she can reach, not abstract theory she can admire. Sylvanas decides to ride with the attempt and let the city prove the rest.
- Jaina is spending the theory instead of sitting with it, Sylvanas puts her own speed and authority behind that attempt even while the crack remains, and the partnership now runs under the city's clock as well as its own.
- They do not know whether the source plan can beat the city's spread or whether success or failure will change Sylvanas's read of the sanctum enough to matter.
- Ground the ride: three horses stretching into a faster line than the road wants to give them, Jaina shouting granary names and district order into the wind, the main column's dust starting to rise behind them.
- Chapter 16 opens on this same ride and the first attempt to kill the plague before it can finish the city.
