# Chapter 11: The Fuse

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Runs parallel to Chapter 12. While Jaina is already inside Scholomance after Chapter 10's descent, Sylvanas and Cyndia ride south from the Chapter 9 split to brief Arthas, then turn north again when Jaina's trail goes dark.
**Where:** Eastweald road south toward Hearthglen and Arthas's field camp.
**Travel:** Mounted on Thalassian horses south through the Eastweald to Arthas's camp, then hard north again toward Caer Darrow.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Goal:** Force proof of the plague through human military and political machinery fast enough to matter, then decide whether Arthas is help or a loaded weapon.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- She opens hoping one clean handoff to a prince and an army can make the plague someone else's problem.
- Instead she gets dragged through the arena she hates most: protocol, witnesses, holy caution, and commanders who only hear danger once it sounds like normal war.
- Without Jaina present, she has to argue with partial truth, field evidence, and judgment.
- She wins in the ugliest way possible: by giving Arthas a war he knows how to want.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Jaina is missing both as magical proof and as the one person who could have made this briefing legible without translation.
- Without her, Sylvanas can sell routes, grain, sickness, and tempo, but not the deepest dark logic underneath it.
- Jaina stays off-page and simultaneous: missing proof, missing backup, and a risk Sylvanas cannot stop thinking about.

### Partnership
- The two trails are split, leaving both badly understrength.
- The separation traps each woman inside the arena she hates most: Jaina alone inside pure magic, Sylvanas inside human politics.
- Jaina's absence and Lyriel's ghost show up in wrong translations, forced guesswork, and changed formation habits, not speeches.

## Scene 1: The Road Alone

**The action:** The broken formation rides through a country already half lost and toward the human machinery Sylvanas hates needing.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Starting point:** Jaina is alone on the Barov line, and the southbound ride has to deliver military force fast enough to matter.

### Beat 1
- The absence of Jaina and Lyriel is a practical problem before it is an emotional one. Sylvanas has to choose between slowing down for safety or keeping speed without Jaina's magical read. She chooses speed.
- The formation is broken, Sylvanas is working without Jaina's sweep, and political backing is already fraying in the messages riding with her.
- They do not know how much of the road south is already compromised or whether Arthas is still where the dispatch says he is.
- Ground the ride: bow leather creaking at Sylvanas's back, hoofbeats too sparse for the old formation, cold air biting at her healing wound, warm dispatch parchment in her coat.
- The country starts answering what kind of war this really is.

### Beat 2
- They ride hard south through territory already shaped by cult control. Villages are silent, harvests are ruined, and hidden watchers do not even bother to engage because the land is already working for them.
- Normal cavalry cannot read the full damage here, and the cult does not need open battle to control ground. Treated grain has already reached the roads and granaries.
- They do not know how far south the sickness has spread or whether Arthas's camp is already eating from compromised stores.
- Ground the country: grain rotting on the stalk, a farm dog too sick to bark, the sweet chemical smell of tainted storage rolling off the road.
- Sylvanas has to admit two scouts cannot fix a kingdom.

### Beat 3
- Sylvanas turns grief into utility. If this must become a human war, she will choose where the human sword lands. Lyriel's death hardens her case for scale, and the Hearthglen dispatch becomes a way to get authority moving.
- The dispatch is the lead, but authority is the real target. The old border-defense story is dead, and Arthas may become a useful weapon or a catastrophic one.
- They do not know which version of Arthas they are riding toward or whether the army can pivot fast enough to matter.
- Ground the decision: a dark road flattening under hard pace, Cyndia riding tighter than a full formation would allow, the ugly calm of a necessary decision.
- The road delivers them to the prince's camp.

## Scene 2: The Prince's Camp

**The action:** Sylvanas has to push plague truth through a camp and command structure built for the wrong war.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Cyndia, Arthas, Captain Falric, Lordaeron troops, Silver Hand presence.
**Starting point:** The plague picture is finally clear enough to lay before the prince, but the human leadership is not built to absorb it.

### Beat 4
- Sylvanas reads the camp and sees the real problem at once: not lack of force, but force aimed in the wrong direction. Everything here is built for visible war while the plague moves through food, water, and quartermaster records.
- Healers are already treating sickness the Light does not answer cleanly, supply people are defensive, and Jaina's absence hurts because Sylvanas has to translate magic into logistics.
- They do not know whether command will let them past ceremony in time or how much sickness the camp is already hiding.
- Ground the camp: field-forge hammering, healers' incense against cavalry stink, mud, banners, and wagons that look strong until you see what they miss.
- Before she can brief Arthas, Sylvanas has to beat the camp's machinery.

### Beat 5
- Falric, healers, and supply officers all stand between urgency and action. Sylvanas gets through only by refusing to soften the field facts and by forcing the argument into logistics: routes, granaries, wagon tempo, sickness clusters, and the Hearthglen dispatch.
- Grain is the accusation that shakes the room. Falric is a barrier of rank and ceremony, and nobody moves until the threat sounds like administration and war instead of magic.
- They do not know whether the audience will produce action or just another layer of caution, or what Arthas has already heard.
- Ground the blockade: Falric's plate at the checkpoint, healers' tents beyond it, a quartermaster going still when grain becomes blame, Sylvanas swallowing the urge to solve ceremony with an arrow.
- The barrier drops, and the real fight starts inside the tent.

### Beat 6
- Sylvanas presents the plague in the only language the room can hear. She does not have Jaina's deep proof, so she briefs with maps, supply lines, compromised grain, hidden distribution, and the fact that visible war has been chasing the wrong thing.
- Arthas hears target and motion. Uther hears danger and incompleteness. The rest of the room hears risk to grain, nobles, and public order. Sylvanas withholds Kel'Thuzad's name and Jaina's position because she cannot prove one and will not endanger the other.
- They do not know whether Arthas will act with discipline or whether Uther can slow him if he does not.
- Ground the briefing: a campaign map filling with plague marks over war marks, Arthas leaning forward when the threat becomes marchable, Uther going still for all the wrong reasons.
- Sylvanas wins the room by giving Arthas a war he knows how to want.

## Scene 3: The Conversion

**The action:** Sylvanas gets the march she wanted and learns immediately why wanting it was dangerous.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Arthas, Uther, Cyndia, Lordaeron command staff.
**Starting point:** Arthas has the right target at last, but none of the deeper pacing or magical sense behind it.

### Beat 7
- Arthas does not need steering once the pattern clicks. He turns certainty into motion before the briefing is even finished. Uther names the flaw almost at once: Arthas cannot bear a battle he thinks he must win, and he will drive through any caution that slows him.
- Arthas will move faster than nuance, Uther sees the danger clearly, and Sylvanas has armed a speed she does not trust.
- They do not know whether the march saves anyone or only sharpens the disaster, or whether Arthas will listen once he is moving.
- Ground the shift: Arthas's gauntleted hand taking the map, Uther's tired warning outside the tent, warhorses turning policy into dust and motion.
- The gamble goes live, and the political cost arrives before the column is out of sight.

## Scene 4: The Call North

**The action:** Political support collapses, then Jaina's silence cuts through it.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider messengers.
**Starting point:** Arthas is in motion, and the wider response to the plague has been handed off to human armies.

### Beat 8
- Lor'themar's latest dispatch lands like a second enemy. Sites are being scrubbed, evidence is vanishing, and recall pressure from Silvermoon is turning from irritation into command.
- Political backing is actively collapsing, and evidence erasure is not incidental. Arthas's march will continue without Sylvanas's hand on it if she lets it.
- They do not know whether staying with the army buys anything or how long before recall pressure becomes a direct order.
- Ground the pressure: Lor'themar's tight angry handwriting, warm dispatch parchment, the humiliation of being attacked by process instead of blades.
- Then Jaina's line goes silent, and politics loses the argument.

### Beat 9
- Sylvanas gets word that Jaina entered Caer Darrow and never came back out. The Arthas march is now open war, but the missing partner becomes the only field truth that matters.
- Sylvanas hands the kingdom off to the prince she just armed. She and Cyndia turn north at once, and Chapter 13 begins from this redirect.
- They do not know whether Jaina is trapped, compromised, or already in the hands of something older than the local cult face.
- Ground the turn: the familiar weight of the bow in Sylvanas's hand as the direction changes, Thalassian horses turning north while Arthas's dust still hangs to the south, Cyndia reading the order from posture alone.
- Chapter 13 opens when Sylvanas reaches the room.
