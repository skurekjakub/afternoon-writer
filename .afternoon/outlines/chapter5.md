# Chapter 5: The Grain Road

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly after Chapter 4, with the party riding southwest from the sick village toward the source.
**Where:** Lordaeron trade roads between village depots and the routes feeding into Andorhal.
**Travel:** Mounted on Thalassian horses.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Goal:** Work backward along the grain trail until they find the road that holds the whole plague network together.

**Sylvanas's starting position:**
- Grain is carrying the sickness.
- Someone organized the wagons and is killing loose ends.
- The trail points southwest toward Andorhal.
- Most towns are only in the early stage of the sickness, but Millhaven was hit hard.
- Jaina can read contamination before other people know a place is sick.
- The old seller from the Millhaven road and the survivor girl's newer grain man still feel connected, even if Sylvanas cannot prove it.

**Still in the dark about:**
- Who is really running the trade route.
- Whether the Barovs built the plague, helped cover it, or know nothing.
- Whether the old seller matters or just looks suspicious in hindsight.

**Hold back:**
- No certainty that the Barovs are guilty.
- No Mal'Ganis naming.
- No sudden warmth between Sylvanas and Jaina. Their trust stays work-first.
- No proof that the old seller is the creature behind the plague.

**End of chapter state:**
- The party is on the southern road toward Andorhal, passing straight through Arthas's military zone near Strahnbrad.
- The Barov name keeps showing up in the grain records, but their guilt remains unproven.
- The old seller clue has upgraded from a stray memory to a real pattern.

### Sylvanas
- She starts the ride treating this like road work: map the depots, read the wagons, find the break in the trail.
- The records get too big and too strange for tracking alone, and she has to lean on Jaina's magic to keep the hunt moving.
- By the end, Sylvanas is no longer just tolerating Jaina's help. She is using Jaina's read to choose the road ahead.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Sylvanas opens still half-expecting theory and softness from Jaina.
- Jaina keeps matching the work: fast reads, quick corrections, no pride when the facts change.
- Sylvanas's respect turns practical. She stops thinking the mage and starts thinking Jaina.

### Partnership
- They start out doing the same job from different angles, still sharp with each other.
- The miles force a clean split of labor: Jaina finds the invisible trail, Sylvanas follows the paper and the road.
- By the end, they are still not gentle, but they are finally trusting the same hunt.

## Scene 1: The Paper Trail

**The action:** Sylvanas and Jaina turn scattered sickness into a road map and realize they are tracking a network, not one bad village.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, depot clerks, innkeepers, merchants.
**Starting point:** They know grain is the carrier and are riding southwest along the trade route.

### Beat 1
- They stop treating the plague like a rumor and start working it like freight. Ledgers, manifests, inn books, and Jaina's readings only make sense when they lay them together.
- Millhaven's grain route matches dozens of other towns, and Jaina can tell which places are deep in exposure and which are only starting to turn. Their methods finally build one map instead of two competing guesses.
- They do not know who is directing the route or where the center of it sits.
- Ground the work: dusty account books, ink-stained fingers, horses tethered outside hot little offices.
- The hunt stops looking local.

### Beat 2
- Each new ledger makes the picture worse. The poisoned grain is moving through a whole regional network, not one village line.
- Dozens of settlements are touched, and Lordaeron's trade roads are being used like a weapon. The center is still buried under too many routes.
- They do not know how far the spread already goes or which depot matters most.
- Ground the tracking: repeated town names, layered notes, Jaina's marks and Sylvanas's route lines turning one table into a mess.
- They push for the regional depot that should finally narrow the map.

### Beat 3
- By evening Sylvanas sees the uglier truth. They are not studying an old crime. They are chasing people who are still moving grain and cleaning the road ahead of them.
- The network is active, and they are arriving after each move rather than before it. Someone may already know they are coming.
- They do not know whether the next depot will still have records when they reach it.
- Ground the realization: damp horses, dark tree lines, Cyndia and Lyriel watching the road while Sylvanas and Jaina talk over movement.
- The next stop becomes a race.

## Scene 2: The Cleaned Depot

**The action:** The next depot has been wiped clean, and the hunt shifts from records to active killers.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, depot staff, dead overseer.
**Starting point:** They are chasing a live network, not just mapping old damage.

### Beat 4
- They reach the depot in time to learn they were already late. The books are gone, the grain is gone, and the warehouse is too clean to trust.
- Someone is erasing evidence ahead of them, which means the road is being watched. The enemy understands exactly what matters in this building.
- They do not know how they were tracked or whether any witness is still alive.
- Ground the emptiness: blank shelves, swept floors, a warehouse quiet that feels made rather than natural.
- They stop looking for books and start looking for the keeper.

### Beat 5
- Sylvanas finds the overseer dead at his desk. It looks natural until Jaina catches the same cold necromantic trace they found on other silenced witnesses.
- The enemy is using precise magical killings to protect the grain route. The one man who could have named the drivers is gone, but Jaina can see what Sylvanas would otherwise have had to write off as illness.
- They do not know who cast the killing spell or how close the killer still is.
- Ground the body: a heavy form folded over wood, a frost-sharp trace in still air, Jaina reading a crime scene no ordinary guard would see.
- They tear the office apart for scraps.

### Beat 6
- Sylvanas spends hours piecing together torn slips and broken seals until one pattern finally holds. The routes pinch toward Andorhal, and the Barov name is all over the paper.
- Barov paperwork sits inside the grain network, which makes Andorhal the road hub they need. It is proof of connection, not proof of guilt, and Sylvanas refuses to blur that line.
- They do not know whether the Barovs are running the plague or being used by it.
- Ground the search: torn parchment edges, hard wax chips, a dead overseer in the corner while Sylvanas works the table.
- They have a destination and need to choose the final road in.

## Scene 3: The South Road

**The action:** They lock the route to Andorhal, Sylvanas fully accepts Jaina as a working partner, and the old seller clue comes back.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Starting point:** They have the Barov name, Andorhal as the hub, and a road being cleaned ahead of them.

### Beat 7
- At a midnight stop they finally fuse the paper trail with Jaina's magical map. The work is clean enough that Sylvanas stops holding Jaina at arm's length inside her own head.
- Jaina gives hard, useful answers without needing to be handled. Sylvanas stops thinking of her as just the mage and starts using Jaina's read to choose the road.
- They do not know who designed the plague or what the Barov connection really means.
- Ground the work: one candle over ledgers, Cyndia and Lyriel outside the perimeter, quiet concentration instead of another argument.
- The road pattern snaps into place.

### Beat 8
- Once the network looks deliberate, the old grain seller from the Millhaven road stops feeling like a roadside oddity and starts feeling wrong in a way Sylvanas cannot shake.
- The empty grain wagon encounter no longer looks random because the whole operation is too controlled to ignore. Sylvanas carries the clue forward without pretending it is proof.
- They do not know why the seller keeps bothering her or who he really was.
- Ground the memory: grain dust on old wagon boards, the echo of a polite flat voice, the irritation of missing something important.
- The old seller becomes a live thread instead of background noise.

### Beat 9
- They avoid the main highway and choose the southern supply road into Andorhal. It is the best path, but it runs straight through Arthas's war near Strahnbrad.
- The southern road is the only workable approach, and it forces them through human military traffic. An encounter with Arthas is now unavoidable.
- They do not know whether Arthas will listen, help, or get in their way.
- Ground the choice: a knife pinning the map at Strahnbrad, cold pre-dawn air, horses turning south into distant army dust.
- Chapter 6 opens with the ride into the military zone.
