# Chapter 12: The Necromancer

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Runs parallel to Chapter 11. It opens after Jaina's descent at the end of Chapter 10 and ends when Sylvanas's approach reaches the deep levels of Scholomance.
**Where:** Kel'Thuzad's inner sanctum beneath Scholomance, deep below Caer Darrow.
**Travel:** On foot through the lower crypts before open; seated in the sanctum at chapter start.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad, Jandice at the threshold only.
**Goal:** Pull the missing spell out of Kel'Thuzad without yielding moral or intellectual ground.

**Jaina's starting position:**
- She reached the lower levels alone by using Jandice's vanity as the door.
- The Barov estate and Scholomance are live cult strongholds, not rumor or ruins.
- Her field read gave her binding rules but not the awakening spell. That missing magic is why she came down here.
- She already knows the treated grain is only the carrier and that the body is part of the process, but not how the dead rise after death.
- Jandice named resonance as the missing link.
- The Kirin Tor is too slow to help at the pace this crisis is moving.
- Sylvanas and Cyndia took the Arthas track, so Jaina has no combat backup.

**Still in the dark about:**
- The true awakening spell.
- Whether Kel'Thuzad wants to recruit her, test her, or simply teach her.
- Who or what Kel'Thuzad answers to.
- Whether Sylvanas is still far south or already turning north.
- How she leaves with what she learns if the room turns against her.

**Hold back:**
- No Jaina agreement or moral conversion.
- No theatrical villain register from Kel'Thuzad.
- No rescue already inside the room.
- No resolution of the old seller and repeated-farewell clue.
- No repair of the split partnership before Chapter 13.

**End of chapter state:**
- Jaina leaves with the complete awakening spell, including the difference between crude ghouls and preserved thinking dead.
- She learns necromancy grows directly out of Kirin Tor method rather than alien magic.
- She learns the plague serves the Lich King and that the Burning Legion stands above that chain.
- Kel'Thuzad remains alive in the sanctum, and Jandice remains above it.
- Sylvanas is descending toward the room.

## Scene 1: The Sanctum

**The action:** Kel'Thuzad receives Jaina in a study that feels like Dalaran turned inside out and begins walking her toward the missing spell.
**Cast:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad, Jandice briefly.
**Starting point:** Jaina descended because the one answer she still lacks is here.

### Beat 1
- Jaina reaches the deepest room and finds not a throne room but a study. The scholarship is recognizably Kirin Tor-shaped even here, and Jandice is dismissed like a student, not a partner.
- Kel'Thuzad's sanctum is a working study, Jandice's authority stops at him, and Kel'Thuzad notices the stolen grimoire at once without asking for it back.
- They do not know why Kel'Thuzad is willing to receive her, or whether this is a trap, a test, or a real exchange.
- Ground the room: cold dry air, green fungi light, Antonidas-style margin notes, the door sealing softly after Jandice leaves.
- Jaina has reached the room that holds the answer.

### Beat 2
- Kel'Thuzad does not simply lecture. He makes Jaina finish the line of reasoning with him. The treated grain is only the carrier. Conditioned tissue keeps patterned potential. Resonance wakes answerability.
- Most village dead only rise as crude ghouls, and preserving a thinking self takes an extra deliberate layer. Jaina's fractured notes finally make sense, and Kel'Thuzad frames necromancy as an extension of accepted arcane work on persistence and binding.
- They do not know the final waking trigger or what Kel'Thuzad wants from Jaina beyond comprehension.
- Ground the exchange: chalk on slate, elegant notation that looks like Antonidas pushed into forbidden territory, Jaina realizing the equations are sound before she decides what they mean.
- Jaina understands enough to challenge him instead of dismissing him.

## Scene 2: The Argument

**The action:** Jaina drags the conversation from elegant theory back to mass death, and Kel'Thuzad answers with a logic that is worse because it is coherent.
**Cast:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad.
**Starting point:** The process is sound enough that Jaina cannot write it off as fraud.

### Beat 3
- Jaina forces the discussion back to villages, grain, and bodies. Kel'Thuzad answers by separating magical validity from Kirin Tor law.
- Dalaran banned necromancy because it destroys consent, personhood, and civic trust, not because the arcane logic fails. Kel'Thuzad sees the campaign and the research as one act, and he answers seriously instead of theatrically.
- They do not know whether shared Kirin Tor standards can still reach him, or whether he is teaching Jaina, testing her, or recruiting her.
- Ground the argument: specimen jars turning from background into accusation, Kel'Thuzad's still hands, the room staying ordinary while the content turns unbearable.
- Jaina has to argue ethics because the magic itself holds.

### Beat 4
- Jaina shifts to consent and coercion. If the process preserves pattern without permission, the violation begins before any elegant theory can save it. Kel'Thuzad hears the point and answers it cleanly, which only makes the room worse.
- Jaina's strongest ground is about people, not models. Kel'Thuzad will acknowledge a strong argument when he hears one, and Jaina notices how easily her own body has slipped into scholar habits in his room.
- They do not know whether that ethical foothold changes anything or how much of Jaina's resistance he can route around.
- Ground the pressure: chalk still in her hand after she notices it, wards humming under the floor like a second conversation, silence used as pressure instead of mercy.
- Kel'Thuzad names the likeness between them.

### Beat 5
- Kel'Thuzad points out that the same institution made them both: distrust of delay, trust in personal analysis, and a willingness to cross lines when institutions fail.
- The difference between them is direction and cost, not method. Kel'Thuzad sees Jaina as evidence, not just an enemy, and the parallel cannot be dismissed cleanly.
- They do not know how Jaina keeps the mirror from widening once it has been named, or what motive is worth in a framework that distrusts anything it cannot measure.
- Ground the mirror: Kirin Tor texts on the shelves, Kel'Thuzad's gentleness making the claim land harder, Jaina recognizing the pattern before she can reject it.
- Jaina follows the logic far enough to see the terrible beauty in it.

## Scene 3: The Mirror

**The action:** Jaina sees exactly why this reasoning is dangerous to her and why Sylvanas mattered more than she admitted.
**Cast:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad.
**Starting point:** The mirror has been named, and Jaina cannot dismiss it without lying to herself.

### Beat 6
- Kel'Thuzad moves from mechanics to implication: continuity of pattern, continuity of learned structure, and the arcane line between function and self. Jaina does not agree with him, but she follows the logic far enough to see its beauty for one heartbeat and recoils from that fact.
- Necromancy is not alien to her education, and the ban had to be moral because the math can keep working past the point where it should. Jaina can recognize beauty in atrocity without consenting to it.
- They do not know what she does with the fact that her mind followed the path or whether this changes anything practical before the room breaks open.
- Ground the recoil: Jaina's hands flattening on the table, a ward ripple moving through the sanctum like a gasp, silence dense enough to count as an event.
- She realizes Sylvanas was not only physical protection but mental friction.

### Beat 7
- Jaina finally sees what Sylvanas's absence costs in this room. No one is here to cut the lecture short, ask what the theory does to living people, or stop her from following an elegant line too far.
- The partnership was not only field safety. It was moral and intellectual friction. Understanding is not agreement, but it is not harmless either.
- They do not know how she hunts with this knowledge later or how Dalaran would hear it without mistaking understanding for agreement.
- Ground the absence: breath control used for composure instead of spellwork, the room's banality making everything worse, research notes lying open as if none of this were personal.
- Kel'Thuzad presses on to the scale above him.

### Beat 8
- Kel'Thuzad's read of Antonidas is accurate enough to hurt. He names a generous teacher, a limited institution, and a tradition that could train brilliant minds without preparing them for necromantic war.
- Antonidas trained both Jaina and Kel'Thuzad in the same intellectual tradition, and institutional caution helped leave the kingdom unready. Jaina's anger at Dalaran and fear of herself can coexist.
- They do not know what Jaina can safely tell Antonidas later or whether his caution was failure, mercy, or both.
- Ground the wound: warmth entering Kel'Thuzad's voice only when he says Antonidas's name, Antonidas's notation on every surface, Jaina feeling her teacher present in both the monster's handwriting and her own habits.
- The conversation moves beyond Kel'Thuzad himself.

## Scene 4: The Scale

**The action:** Kel'Thuzad widens the frame from plague mechanics to the powers above him, and Jaina finally gets the whole shape of the threat.
**Cast:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad.
**Starting point:** Jaina understands the awakening spell and the mirror it opened.

### Beat 9
- Kel'Thuzad names the Lich King: an ancient mind in Northrend, not just another necromancer. The plague is not a side experiment. It is a supply line.
- Kel'Thuzad sees the relationship as patron and scholar, not master and servant, and key parts of the final awakening architecture came from beyond anything Dalaran could have taught.
- They do not know what created the Lich King or whether anything stands above it.
- Ground the widening: older, colder ward signatures in the walls, Kel'Thuzad sounding grateful instead of afraid, the room feeling more like an instrument than a shelter.
- The scale widens again.

### Beat 10
- Kel'Thuzad names the Burning Legion. Jaina now has the full chain: carrier, resonance, waking trigger, and the larger purpose behind the plague.
- The Scourge is preparation for invasion, not the final threat. Jaina now understands the difference between crude village dead and preserved thinking dead, and Kel'Thuzad expects his own continuity to continue past death.
- They do not know which institutions can respond to a threat at this scale or whether any countermeasure Jaina designs will matter in time.
- Ground the revelation: Jaina's fingers whitening where they lock together, specimen jars shrinking into insignificance beside what they imply, Kel'Thuzad discussing his own death with the calm of a lecturer.
- The debate ends. What remains is the silence after it.

## Scene 5: The Sound

**The action:** The knowledge settles for one beat, then the sanctum begins answering Sylvanas's approach.
**Cast:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad.
**Starting point:** Jaina has everything she came for and no safe way to file what it cost.

### Beat 11
- The room goes still. Jaina now has the complete process, the cosmic frame, and the knowledge of her own dangerous susceptibility to elegant logic. Kel'Thuzad is satisfied because he was understood, not because he won.
- No moral conversion has happened, but Jaina cannot unknow what she followed. Kel'Thuzad remains calm and in control of the room.
- They do not know how an outsider will read this room when they arrive or how Jaina will report any of this later.
- Ground the stillness: silence deep enough to feel physical, research logs sitting on the desk as if they belonged there all along, Jaina in a receded kind of shock instead of relief.
- The stillness breaks because the building answers someone else's movement.

### Beat 12
- Kel'Thuzad notices the disturbance first through the wards. Jaina feels it a beat later and knows the movement pattern before she says the name: Sylvanas is coming down through Scholomance.
- Sylvanas is already descending toward the sanctum, and Kel'Thuzad reads the change on Jaina's face without understanding the partnership behind it. Chapter 13 begins at entry, not aftermath.
- They do not know how Sylvanas will read the tableau when she arrives or whether Chapter 13 opens on rescue, confrontation, or both.
- Ground the approach: defensive wards waking in layered ripples, relentless room-clearing motion with no wasted spellwork, Kel'Thuzad's attention turning upward with amused curiosity.
- Chapter 13 begins at the door.
