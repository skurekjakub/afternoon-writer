# Chapter 1: The Border

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Story start. No prior chapter.
**Where:** Southeastern Eversong Woods, on Farstrider patrol near the Amani frontier.
**Travel:** Thalassian horses for Sylvanas, Lor'themar, and the mounted patrol.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Lor'themar, unnamed Farstrider patrol.
**Goal:** Finish the troll sweep, dodge the Convocation budget session, and stay outdoors one more day.

- **Worldbuilding references:** [Silvermoon & Quel'Thalas](stories/the-plague-road/locations/silvermoon-and-quelthalas.md), [Thalassian Horses](stories/the-plague-road/locations/thalassian-horses.md)
- **Character references:** [Sylvanas profile](stories/the-plague-road/characters/sylvanas.md), [Sylvanas voice](stories/the-plague-road/characters/voice-sheets/sylvanas-voice.md), [Lor'themar profile](stories/the-plague-road/characters/lorthemar.md), [Jaina profile](stories/the-plague-road/characters/jaina.md), [Jaina voice](stories/the-plague-road/characters/voice-sheets/jaina-voice.md), [Cyndia profile](stories/the-plague-road/characters/cyndia.md)

**Sylvanas's starting position:**
- Routine Amani pressure exists on the southeastern frontier.
- The ward network looks stable in every formal report.
- The Farstriders keep files on powerful human mages near the border.
- Jaina Proudmoore is one of the names worth watching.

**Still in the dark about:**
- Anything about the plague.
- Anything about leyline corruption.
- That a human mage has already crossed the ward boundary.
- Why Jaina is in Quel'Thalas.

**Hold back:**
- No trust between Sylvanas and Jaina.
- No proof that this is more than a human trespass.
- No Jaina dialogue before the chapter boundary.

**End of chapter state:**
- Jaina is in Farstrider custody and has to make her case directly to Sylvanas.
- Sylvanas knows the prisoner is Jaina Proudmoore, not a random trespasser.
- The breach has turned from patrol annoyance into a serious interrogation.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- She opens treating anything south of the wards as someone else's problem unless it touches her stones, roads, or riders.
- She wants to finish the troll work, stay on her horse, and avoid the Convocation budget session.
- The report of a human mage inside the wards turns an easy patrol day into her problem.
- By the end, the border mess belongs to her, and the prisoner is waiting in her own holding space.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Before Sylvanas sees her, Jaina is just the human mage who ruined an easy day.
- Sylvanas expects a polished Kirin Tor problem: spy, diplomat, or both.
- What she finds instead is a road-worn young mage who looks exhausted, furious, and very far from court.
- Even then, Jaina is still only the mage, the human, or the prisoner inside Sylvanas's head.

### Partnership
- There is no partnership here. One woman is in custody, and the other decides how hard the door stays shut.
- Jaina shifts from paperwork headache to the one person Sylvanas has to hear out.
- The chapter ends on their first hard face-to-face with no warmth and no softening.

## Scene 1: The Hunt

**The action:** Show Sylvanas in her element before politics ruins the day.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Lor'themar, mounted patrol, Amani raiders.
**Starting point:** Routine frontier work. Trolls are the only threat on the board.

### Beat 1
- Sylvanas finishes the sweep, dropping the last Amani axethrower cleanly as he breaks from cover.
- She reads the woods effortlessly—footing, range, openings. This is where she belongs, doing normal border work.
- They have no idea anything worse than trolls is out there.
- Ground the aftermath: a wet arrow impact, crushed ferns, troll blood in the sweet Eversong air, birdsong returning.
- The clean kill gives them a breather and proves exactly why Sylvanas would rather stay out here than ride back to the city.

### Beat 2
- In the quiet after the fight, Sylvanas tries to stall. She wants more time on the road, not a Convocation budget session.
- She uses checking the ward-stones as an excuse to stretch the patrol south. Lor'themar sees right through it—the politics are already dragging at her.
- They don't actually know if the stones need checking.
- Ground the moment: wiping steel clean on a fern, cooling forest air, horses settling under riders who know them perfectly.
- They steal one more easy stretch of road before duty catches up.

### Beat 3
- They ride south in easy company. Lor'themar gently needles her about the court business she keeps dodging.
- He is the only one who can absorb her irritation without slowing her down. Not even Cyndia expects a real threat from the south.
- They don't know the day is about to crack open.
- Ground the ride: amber light through the canopy, a low ward-hum under the horses' strides, a pace they both know by heart.
- An urgent rider comes hard from the south, cutting the easy rhythm in half.

## Scene 2: The Messenger

**The action:** Bad news from the border lands, forcing Sylvanas to split up from Lor'themar.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Lor'themar, Farstrider runner, patrol.
**Starting point:** As far as Sylvanas knows, this is just an annoying border day.

### Beat 4
- A Farstrider runner arrives from Cyndia's post at full speed with the kind of report that ruins the whole patrol.
- A human mage crossed the first ward ring before dawn, got caught studying the stones without credentials, and claims the leylines are wrong.
- The scouts don't know the mage's identity, who sent her, or if her warning means anything.
- Ground the arrival: lather on the runner's horse, foam at the bit, clipped patrol shorthand from tired breathing.
- Sylvanas loses the rest of her easy day and has to treat this border breach as a serious threat.

### Beat 5
- She turns irritation into command. Lor'themar has to take the patrol north to handle the politicians, while she judges the prisoner herself.
- An unauthorized human mage near the wards is too big to hand off to someone else. Lor'themar peels away, and the runner stays with Sylvanas.
- She has no idea if this is a diplomatic failure, a Kirin Tor spy, or something much worse.
- Ground the split: horses separating at the fork, the shift from the softer northern road to the hard southern track, the sudden gap when Lor'themar leaves.
- Without Lor'themar, there's no one to absorb Sylvanas's temper as she rides hard south.

### Beat 6
- Riding fast for the Pass, Sylvanas turns the problem over and grows certain it actually matters.
- A mage talking about wrong leylines doesn't fit a standard spy box. The shift from deep woods to border stone changes the entire ride. She's heading into a problem she doesn't grasp yet.
- She doesn't know she's riding to meet Jaina Proudmoore. She just doesn't know if the intruder is hostile, reckless, or drowning in a problem of her own making.
- Ground the ride: warmer forest air thinning into the cold mountain bite, ward-stones pulsing faster, gravel and wind under the horses.
- She hits the border post and demands Cyndia's report directly.

## Scene 3: The Prisoner

**The action:** A generic border arrest becomes a high-stakes problem that locks them both into the next chapter.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Cyndia, border rangers, Jaina.
**Starting point:** A human mage is in a holding cell. No verified identity. No trusted warning.

### Beat 7
- Cyndia gives the raw facts of the arrest. Instead of clearing things up, the facts make the mage stranger.
- The mage arrived before dawn, hit the second ring, and instead of bolting, argued to stay and work. The ward-stones read as if they were malfunctioning because of something inside them. Her staff and gear sit in lockdown.
- Cyndia and Sylvanas have no clue if this human is arrogant, terrified, or completely correct.
- Ground the room: heavy stone walls, bright ward-light, rangers standing in exhausting, stiff alert posture.
- The report is weird enough that Sylvanas decides to look at the prisoner right now.

### Beat 8
- Sylvanas pulls back the door. The human in the chair completely shatters her read of a pampered, court-trained nuisance.
- She expects a silver-tongued Kirin Tor diplomat. She gets a young mage in practical travel gear, worn boots, frost-burned knuckles, and ink stains, furious that nobody believes her.
- Cyndia finally puts a name to the human: Jaina Proudmoore, Antonidas's apprentice.
- They still don't know why Antonidas let her walk out alone, or if he even knows where she is.
- Ground the reveal: cold ward-light reflecting off Jaina's face, her staff propped uselessly against the weapons rack, the raw exhausted anger she's projecting.
- This isn't just a border trespass anymore. It's a high-stakes diplomatic and magical incident.

### Beat 9
- Sylvanas shoves down her temper. She realizes that throwing Jaina out or letting her wait are both worse than just hearing her right now.
- Releasing a trespasser like this is completely impossible, but stalling the interrogation feels equally risky. The next conversation changes everything they thought they knew about the border.
- They don't know if Jaina is telling the truth, and they definitely don't have any reason to trust her.
- Ground the standoff: rangers instinctively clearing a path for the Ranger-General, blue ward-light hitting Sylvanas's boots, a suffocating silence right before someone breaks it.
- Sylvanas hits her with the opening demand for chapter two: "Start talking."
