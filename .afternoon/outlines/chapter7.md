# Chapter 7: Andorhal

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 6. The party reaches Andorhal after Arthas's dismissal and enters before the city understands it is being investigated.
**Open location:** Andorhal's gates and market district, with inspection offices, granaries, and the mage quarter beyond.
**Transport:** Mounted arrival on Thalassian horses; on foot once inside the city.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Immediate objective:** Enter Andorhal under cover, identify how the plague is being embedded at hub level, and map the human infrastructure carrying it.

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** She can still solve this like a deep field hunt if she gets inside Andorhal and reads it harder than the humans who live there.
- **Surface objective:** Turn Andorhal from rumor and road logic into a usable picture of who stores the grain, who moves it, and where the city is weakest.
- **Pressure source:** The city is alive, bureaucratic, and layered. Clerks, guards, mages, and civic rhythms are carrying the plague inside ordinary motion.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas keeps looking for the decisive field angle - the weak gate, the loose watch, the flank she can turn - as if sharper scouting can solve the whole thing.
- **Chapter test:** Andorhal only opens when the work splits. Jaina handles the arcane and civic doors Sylvanas cannot force with fieldcraft alone.
- **Forced choice:** Keep treating Jaina's side as support to Sylvanas's command, or let the mission stand on work Sylvanas cannot personally watch line by line.
- **End-state shift:** By chapter close Sylvanas stops thinking of Jaina as help she brought along and starts treating her read as part of the job itself.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 8 opens with Sylvanas ready to divide labor by competence and trust Jaina to close her half without hand-holding.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** Contamination analyst, city reader, and the one who can move through Andorhal's magical and bureaucratic layers without losing the thread.
- **POV misread at open:** Proven useful on the road, but still easier to trust when Sylvanas can see the work happening.
- **Correction earned here:** Jaina keeps producing answers Sylvanas cannot reach just by watching the street and the room, and she does it without grandstanding. That kills the old asset framing.
- **Interaction rule:** Sylvanas can think Jaina's name plainly now. The distance is no longer contempt. It lives in caution, workload, and the city's pressure.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Functional cooperation with growing reliance, still run on Sylvanas's command tempo.
- **Operational change this chapter:** The work truly splits here. Each woman has to trust the other to finish the part she cannot stand over and supervise.
- **Naming/attitude rule:** Respect is practical and matter-of-fact now. Do not write it as sentiment.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** They are not softened. They are simply harder to separate from the mission.

## Scene 1: The Infiltration

**Scene function:** Get the party inside Andorhal under plausible cover and add the two local allies who let the investigation operate in layers instead of brute force.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, city guards, clerks, Corin, Captain Havel
**Knowledge at scene start:** Grain is the vector; Andorhal is the hub; the city still functions outwardly

### Beat 1 — Scene: Supply inspectors at the gate
- **Goal:** Enter Andorhal without advertising that the investigation has reached the city itself
- **Conflict:** The city is functioning well enough that officials still think in terms of paperwork, jurisdictions, and routine inspections rather than an active plague conspiracy
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — the cover holds and gets them through the gate, but the ordinary civic rhythm makes the scale feel worse rather than safer
- **Value shift:** external target -> living system carrying hidden contamination
- **New on-page information:**
  - Andorhal is still operating as a market city rather than a ruin
  - Jaina can navigate inspection protocol and bureaucratic tone well enough to make the cover believable
  - Sylvanas immediately starts reading guards, exits, choke points, and granary traffic
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How the grain is being treated inside the city
  - Which officials are merely bureaucrats and which belong to the network
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Hooves on stone at the gate
  - Grain wagons and market chatter moving past people who think their city is normal
  - Jaina's false paperwork beside Sylvanas's real threat assessment
- **Transition intent:** Once inside, they need someone local who has been seeing the pattern without being believed

### Beat 2 — Scene: Corin, the ignored report
- **Goal:** Find a magical contact who can get Jaina into the right rooms and translate what Andorhal's mages have been missing
- **Conflict:** Corin is competent but junior, frightened, and already trained by experience to expect his correct reports to vanish into Dalaran procedure
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Corin becomes an ally and opens institutional doors, but his history proves the bureaucracy has already been absorbing and discarding warning signs
- **Value shift:** living system carrying hidden contamination -> local ally inside failed institutions
- **New on-page information:**
  - Corin has been tracking grain-distribution irregularities for weeks
  - His reports disappeared into the same kind of bureaucracy Jaina has always trusted
  - The magical layer of the city has been producing warnings long before the field party arrived
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Corin has seen the mechanism itself or only the symptoms around it
  - Whether any part of Dalaran's silence is incompetence rather than compromise
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Ink-stained hands and sleepless eyes
  - Stacks of ignored notices and copied route marks
  - Corin trying not to sound relieved when somebody finally takes him seriously
- **Transition intent:** Corin can open the mage-side doors, but Sylvanas still needs someone who reads the city from the street up

### Beat 3 — Scene: Captain Havel's instincts
- **Goal:** Add a ground-level local read on Andorhal's granary district and human routines
- **Conflict:** Havel is suspicious of outsiders and has only pattern, not proof: sick workers, new grain routes, and a district full of people who behave like civilians until they don't
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Havel gives them the physical city they need, but his evidence is still instinct and repetition rather than a case anyone above him would honor
- **Value shift:** local ally inside failed institutions -> the city has two reliable witnesses who were ignored for different reasons
- **New on-page information:**
  - Havel has been seeing sickness cluster around the grain system for months
  - A new supplier pattern entered the city roughly three months ago
  - Certain workers in the granary district keep military-grade discipline under civilian cover
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who directs those workers
  - Whether the city knows it is hosting enemy infrastructure or merely living atop it
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Militia leather carrying street dust instead of parade polish
  - Havel's short, exact answers once he decides to answer at all
  - Names and routes spoken like patrol notes rather than theory
- **Transition intent:** With Corin and Havel in place, the investigation can split along the exact seam Sylvanas and Jaina have been building toward

### Beat 4 — Sequel: Divide the labor
- **Emotion:** Professional recognition stripped of ceremony
- **Dilemma:** Keep the team together for safety, or split Jaina and Sylvanas so each can work at full competency in the layer only she can read
- **Decision:** Jaina takes Corin and the mage-side access to study the contamination; Sylvanas takes Havel and the granary district to map the human structure
- **Value shift:** two local witnesses -> the partnership becomes the investigation's operating system
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina can function unsupervised in institutional terrain Sylvanas would only slow down
  - Sylvanas can read embedded operatives and supply discipline in ways Jaina cannot
  - Cyndia and Lyriel anchor the physical security layer while the leads split
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the city is already reacting to them
  - Which half of the investigation will reach the real danger first
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Havel unrolling district notes while Corin fumbles for keys and access seals
  - The four-woman field unit redistributing itself without discussion
  - Sylvanas realizing the split feels efficient, not reckless
- **Transition intent:** The city now yields two separate halves of the same answer

## Scene 2: The Mechanism Revealed

**Scene function:** Let Jaina solve the how and Sylvanas solve the who until the halves lock together into one infrastructure picture.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Corin, Sylvanas, Havel, Cyndia, Lyriel, granary workers, alchemical staff
**Knowledge at scene start:** Andorhal has both magical and logistical witnesses; the city is hiding a system, not an accident

### Beat 5 — Scene: Jaina's half of the answer
- **Goal:** Determine exactly how contaminated grain is being treated inside a working distribution hub
- **Conflict:** The mechanism is buried inside routine packaging work, meaning Jaina has to read active alchemical traces without alerting everyone standing around them
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — she isolates the mechanism cleanly, but the sophistication tells her the enemy is closer to institutional research than hedge sabotage
- **Value shift:** the partnership becomes the investigation's operating system -> mechanism resolved, threat escalated
- **New on-page information:**
  - The grain is treated during packaging with a necromantic carrier layer rather than fouled at harvest
  - Milling, moisture, and baking carry that outer shell deeper into ordinary use instead of cleansing it away
  - The strongest signature in the grain still looks like only the carrier half of the plague, implying the host body finishes the work later
  - The work shows institutional-grade alchemy and disciplined magical methodology
  - Jaina's answer lands cleanly even without Sylvanas standing beside her
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who designed the process
  - Whether Andorhal is the top of the system or merely a mid-chain hub
  - How the conditioned host becomes undead after death
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Dusty grain bags carrying a magical wrongness too fine for normal senses
  - Corin holding his breath while Jaina works through the contamination model
  - Alchemical residue hidden in places any ordinary inspector would miss
- **Transition intent:** Jaina now has the mechanism but needs the human map Sylvanas is building elsewhere in the city

### Beat 6 — Scene: Sylvanas's half of the answer
- **Goal:** Identify how many living operatives are embedded in the granary district and how they are structured
- **Conflict:** The city is full of workers, porters, guards, and clerks; the enemy survives by looking like one more layer of ordinary labor
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Sylvanas maps the local structure and hidden supply behavior, but the top command node stays out of reach
- **Value shift:** mechanism resolved, threat escalated -> embedded organization exposed
- **New on-page information:**
  - At least eight operatives are embedded in the district
  - The cell has a two-tier internal hierarchy rather than random zealots
  - Off-ledger caches and movement patterns prove the conspiracy has been operating here for months
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who sits above the local hierarchy
  - Whether the city's official leadership is penetrated or merely blind
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Workers whose stillness reads like trained waiting, not fatigue
  - Havel noticing the same people never drink with the rest after shift
  - Hidden route marks and storage behavior that make no sense on honest books
- **Transition intent:** The two investigations now need to be put on the same table before the city moves

### Beat 7 — Sequel: The city-level picture
- **Emotion:** Cold professional recognition
- **Dilemma:** Treat the two findings as adjacent leads, or admit that each woman solved only half a machine neither could have seen alone
- **Decision:** Put the data together immediately and build the next move from the combined picture
- **Value shift:** embedded organization exposed -> city-scale conspiracy seen whole
- **New on-page information:**
  - Andorhal is a functioning distribution center weaponized from the inside
  - Mage-level contamination work and disciplined human logistics are inseparable parts of one system
  - Jaina has the carrier-side answer and Sylvanas the living-network answer, but the host-side trigger still has to be earned elsewhere
  - Corin and Havel can support the operation, but neither can replace one of the two leads
  - Sylvanas cannot honestly describe Jaina's work as support anymore; without that half, there is no case against the city at all
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the city already suspects them
  - Whether the network has already completed its wider rollout
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina's notes and Sylvanas's route marks overlaying each other
  - Corin and Havel watching two different forms of expertise arrive at one answer
  - The room going quiet when the scale stops being theoretical
- **Transition intent:** Once the whole machine is visible, the next move becomes a choice between tracing material and taking a live witness

## Scene 3: The Load-Bearing Partnership

**Scene function:** Turn the Andorhal breakthrough into an operational plan and make Sylvanas admit, privately, that the partnership is no longer optional.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Corin, Havel
**Knowledge at scene start:** The mechanism and the local organization are both in hand, but the command layer remains unnamed

### Beat 8 — Scene: Trace versus capture
- **Goal:** Decide the next move before the enemy realizes Andorhal has been breached
- **Conflict:** Jaina wants the alchemical trail back to its source; Sylvanas wants a living operative who can name the command structure; either move alone leaves half the gap open
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — the disagreement resolves into a joint plan instead of a split plan, but that only works because each woman trusts the other to handle the half she cannot
- **Value shift:** city-scale conspiracy seen whole -> two-part operational plan
- **New on-page information:**
  - A live capture is possible tonight because the network still feels secure in its own city
  - Jaina's trace work can validate or break whatever a prisoner says
  - Cyndia and Lyriel are strong enough on perimeter to let the leads commit fully
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the city has already marked Corin or Havel as leaks
  - How fast the network can erase itself once it senses contact
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Candlelit maps weighted by knives, keys, and chalk
  - Havel's fallback routes and Corin's access notes spreading across the same table
  - Jaina and Sylvanas disagreeing without losing alignment
- **Transition intent:** Once the plan exists, the chapter only needs the private recognition of what that plan depends on

### Beat 9 — Sequel: The word that dies
- **Emotion:** Unspectacular dependence
- **Dilemma:** Keep thinking of Jaina as an attached resource, or admit that removing her now would collapse the investigation she helped make possible
- **Decision:** Admit privately that the investigation requires both of them and act from that truth
- **Value shift:** split work -> one shared job
- **New on-page information:**
  - Sylvanas no longer frames Jaina as an asset in her own thinking
  - The mission's real structure is now complementary competence, not custody or convenience
  - Corin and Havel are valuable, but the core engine remains the same four-woman field unit plus the two leads
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who will break first once the capture starts
  - What the city will do when it realizes it is being hunted from inside
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Midnight quiet with Jaina still writing in the next room
  - Perimeter turns outside handled by Cyndia and Lyriel without fuss
  - Sylvanas hearing the scratch of a quill and understanding the sound as operational reassurance
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 8 opens with the capture operation and the moment the hunters discover the city has been hunting back
