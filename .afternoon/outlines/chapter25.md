# Chapter 25: The Stinger

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore in Scene 1, Sylvanas Windrunner in Scene 2 (both limited third, absolute)
**When:** Opens the night after Chapter 24's verdict, with the pair still in adjacent dungeon cells beneath a palace holding an unheld Frostmourne still tethered north.
**Where:** Capital City palace dungeons, two adjacent cells divided by two feet of stone.
**Travel:** Static imprisonment only. Motion belongs to the blade's pulse, the voices through the wall, and the visitor who ignores the wall entirely.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, the visitor, Lor'themar and Vereesa off-page pressure only.
**Goal:** Survive the cell-night, move the blade intelligence through stone, and hold onto the partnership long enough for it to remain useful.

### Jaina
- She opens with the answer to Frostmourne and nowhere to put it but through a wall.
- The last scraps of her old lie still whisper that clear enough thinking should have been enough on its own.

- By the end, Jaina is still imprisoned, but she is no longer mistaken about what actually solved the first book.

### Sylvanas
- She enters Scene 2 still doing what she always does: reading, assessing, and looking for whatever can still be used.
- The chapter widens the board without giving her any new freedom, which is exactly the kind of pressure she hates.
- She has to survive the visitation, listen hard enough to steal something useful from it, and keep hold of the pattern once it breaks open.

### Partnership
- The pair opens reduced to voices, breath, and counting through masonry.
- This chapter turns the partnership from a proven method into the thematic answer of the book.
- Let the wall-talk, the pulse counting, and the final recognition carry that change.

### The visitor's pressure
- The blade is already the active danger at open, but the chapter has to prove there is something older standing behind even that danger.
- The visitor should feel easy where everyone else has been strained: easy through stone, easy through fear, easy through scale.
- The point is not a lore dump. The point is that the women realize their victory was real and still too local.
- By the end, the new enemy is not solved, only named through pattern.

## Scene 1: Through the Wall

**The action:** Jaina tracks the blade, names the real lesson of the book, and turns the wall back into a working channel before the larger enemy arrives.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas through the wall.
**Starting point:** The verdict is delivered, the cells are shut, and Frostmourne is still active above them.

### Beat 1
- Jaina keeps counting because the blade's pulse is the only thing in the night she can still measure.
- The cycle is shortening from pulse to pulse, the blade is not broadcasting blindly anymore but narrowing toward individual minds, and Jaina can now distinguish the difference between ambient hunger and targeted interest.
- They do not know which mind the blade lands on first or whether the palace wards fail before a person does.
- Ground the pulse: wrong heartbeat through stone above her, a guard-mind brushing the blade's radius and catching on it, bread-taste and pulse collapsing into the same rhythm inside Jaina's body.
- The blade becomes the real night.

### Beat 2
- Through the wall, Jaina finally says the thing the whole book has been driving toward: she was never enough alone.
- Jaina can now name border work and grain work and Scholomance and Northrend and the throne room as partnership achievements rather than solitary insights, Kel'Thuzad becomes the warning version of solitary brilliance rather than only the enemy teacher, and Sylvanas answers from the wall by sharpening the truth into something usable instead of softening it.
- They do not know how this truth changes the next investigation once the door opens or whether the world outside the cells ever learns the same lesson.
- Ground the exchange: consonants blunted by two feet of stone, long pauses that still feel full because the other voice is listening, one sentence crossing the wall harder than the verdict did.
- The book's truth is named.

### Beat 3
- Jaina turns immediately back to work. If she cannot touch the blade, she can still package the counter for whoever gets a surface first.
- Jaina can already describe likely vulnerable targets in practical terms, the counter remains valid even outside the throne room but only if delivered in time, and the pair's wall-language is now strong enough to carry technical threat reporting.
- They do not know whether any outside ally can receive and act on the intelligence before the blade chooses or whether the next wielder is already in the palace or only being shaped toward it.
- Ground the channel: fingertip taps marking cycle length between spoken lines, stone making every sentence cost extra effort, the blade spiking harder every time Jaina says something true about it.
- The partnership becomes a working channel again.

## Scene 2: The Visitor

**The action:** The chapter widens the scale of the story, confirms that Frostmourne is still only one part of the problem, and resolves the five-face thread into a sequel hunt.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina through the wall, the visitor.
**Starting point:** The partnership is still working through stone, and the blade is seeking above them.

### Beat 4
- Something enters the cells that does not belong to any category Sylvanas has been using so far.
- The blade's cold is not the only cold in the prison now, Jaina's fear through the wall is qualitatively different from anything before it, and the darkness itself changes before the visitor fully appears.
- They do not know what the visitor is or whether the blade and the visitor are allied, parallel, or nested.
- Ground the entrance: torch flame shrinking inward, air gaining weight before shape, Jaina's sharp inhale through the wall.
- The room stops belonging to known threats.

### Beat 5
- The visitor does not deny their victory. It simply places that victory on a board so large it almost erases the comfort of it.
- Arthas's death is a setback to the larger enemy rather than a decisive blow, the plague and Cult and Scourge apparatus already accomplished more than the pair understood while they were stopping the prince, and the blade will find another hand is spoken with maker-level familiarity rather than rumor-level fear.
- They do not know exactly where this being sits relative to the Lich King and the plague apparatus or who the next hand might be.
- Ground the scale: a voice too calm for what it is saying, scale conveyed through boredom instead of explanation, Jaina's steadying breath through the wall when the blade line lands.
- The board is redefined.

### Beat 6
- The visitor leaves as easily as it came, and its final farewell in ancient Kaldorei is more disturbing than its arrival.
- The visitor never treated the cell as confinement in the first place, the original farewell is older than any living polity now standing, and the repeated Common phrase from earlier chapters was always an echo of something older.
- They do not know whether the visitor meant the clue as mockery, sloppiness, or bait or how much of the investigation it steered from behind those other faces.
- Ground the exit: a body passing through solid stone without noise or strain, one impossible phrase hanging in the cell after the shape is gone, torchlight only recovering after the room belongs to ordinary dark again.
- Impossible recognition becomes possible.

### Beat 7
- Sylvanas's memory locks into place all at once. Five earlier faces collapse into one operative line.
- The grain trader and the Andorhal contact and the retreating figure and the military aide and the Stratholme-road merchant all resolve into the same enemy, the repeated star-road farewell stops being a curiosity and becomes a tell, and the new investigation lane is not only the blade but the disguised manipulator moving through the kingdom behind it.
- They do not know how many other faces the same being wore without being caught or whether the tell remains usable now that the being knows it has been noticed.
- Ground the recognition: five remembered farewells striking in sequence behind the ancient original, one dead language relighting five old scenes at once, Frostmourne pulsing above them while the pattern locks into place below.
- The sequel gets its first real hunting tool.
