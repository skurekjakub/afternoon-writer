# Chapter 17: The Gate

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 16 at Stratholme's gates. Opens on the moment after Jaina answers Arthas honestly and closes after the Culling, with Arthas emerging from the gate beneath the wall — clean hands, wrong eyes.
**Open location:** Stratholme's main gate and inner approach.
**Transport:** On foot at the gates and through the service lane; short climb to the inner wall; static witness position once the Culling begins.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers.
**Immediate objective:** Survive Arthas's answer to "too late," get a workable read on the city once the source plan fails, and keep Jaina moving inside a decision neither of them controls.

## Knowledge ledger

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- Jaina's source plan was real, practical, and too late at city scale.
- At least one granary could be broken, which proves the method. The problem is not theory now. The problem is time and mouths.
- The city is both seeded and partly steered: the crude choir can self-muster, but living Cult handlers are still hardening and directing pieces of the dead.
- Arthas arrived at the gate needing a yes-or-no answer and received no.
- Uther heard the same answer.

**What Sylvanas does NOT know at open**
- How far Arthas will carry "too late."
- Whether Uther will oppose him in the moment or only afterward.
- How long Jaina can stay upright once the prince turns the answer into policy.
- What the fifth farewell will attach itself to if it appears again.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No clean emotional reconciliation.
- No explicit Mal'Ganis reveal.
- No interiority for Arthas, Jaina, or Uther.
- No framing that makes the Culling tactically easy just because it is tactically legible.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Arthas answers Jaina's "too late" with the Culling.
- Uther breaks with him at the gate.
- Jaina refuses the order and stands outside the command chain with Sylvanas.
- The fifth farewell surfaces in the service lane from a fleeing grain carter who also provides useful route information to the wall.
- From the wall, Sylvanas reads the Culling as deliberate, methodical, and effective at preventing a full city-wide turn while still remaining an atrocity.
- The crack from Scholomance is functionally welded by shared witness before either woman says anything about it.
- Arthas emerges from the gate afterward with clean hands and the look of someone who has already moved on to the next necessity.

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** Arthas may still be a usable ally if his answer stays inside hard containment rather than crossing into something worse.
- **Surface objective:** Keep a clear read on the city, keep Jaina on her feet, and decide where to stand once the prince chooses.
- **Pressure source:** Jaina's failed source plan, Arthas's speed, Uther's looming response, and the fact that Sylvanas lit this prince's road back in Chapter 11.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas still wants to believe there may be a version of this decision that remains brutal but contained.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can watch a tactically coherent atrocity and refuse to let coherence excuse it.
- **Forced choice:** Stay close enough to read and survive, or step away far enough to keep her own hands clean and risk losing the truth of what happened here.
- **End-state shift:** Arthas stops reading as a hard ally and starts reading as a man who has crossed a line fast enough that only the aftermath remains to track.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 18 opens with Jaina on the wall after the screaming stops and Arthas walks away.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** Witness, failed rescuer, and the person whose honest answer made the next order possible without causing it.
- **POV correction:** The source attempt gives Sylvanas the field proof she needed. Whatever Jaina did in Kel'Thuzad's room, she spent the knowledge here trying to save strangers.
- **Interaction rule:** No interiority. Show Jaina through silence, posture, spell residue, and where she fixes her eyes once the gate order lands.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Damaged but synchronized.
- **Change this chapter:** Shared witness finishes what the source attempt started. The crack is not talked through. It is welded by where they stand and what they keep seeing.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** No spoken absolution. No emotional summary language. Let the weld live in placement, timing, and who stays beside whom when leaving would be easier.

## Cast and handoff rules

- **Arthas:** Exterior only. Use commands, posture, pace, and what he prioritizes once the answer is no.
- **Uther:** Must read as a moral break happening in real time, not a delayed disagreement.
- **Cyndia:** Route sense, flank awareness, and practical movement support through the gate chaos.
- **Falric:** The prince's working edge. Use him to show how fast command becomes action.
- **Grain carter:** Ordinary enough to be forgettable, specific enough to matter. He delivers route intelligence and the fifth farewell, nothing more.

**Chapter handoff target:** Chapter 18 opens on the wall after the Culling ends, with Jaina immobilized by what she has understood and Sylvanas beside her.

## Scene 1: The Gate

**Scene function:** Turn Jaina's honest answer into Arthas's order and force the moral split into the open.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers
**Knowledge at scene start:** The source plan failed because it was late, not because it was wrong.
**Arc pressure:** Once Arthas hears "too late," the chapter stops being about options and starts being about what he thinks necessity permits.

### Beat 1 - Scene: The Prince's Answer
- **Goal:** Learn what Arthas does with Jaina's answer before the city finishes tipping on its own.
- **Conflict:** Arthas does not ask for nuance twice. If the city cannot be saved cleanly, he treats that as the decision already made. He gives the order to purge the city before the dead can claim it.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the uncertainty ends, but it ends in the worst clean answer the gate could have produced.
- **Value shift:** failed rescue -> atrocity named as policy
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas converts "too late" directly into the Culling
  - He is now moving faster than anyone else in the gate group can morally follow
  - Falric and the command edge are already orienting around execution rather than debate
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Uther will refuse in words only or in action
  - Where Sylvanas and Jaina can stand that keeps them clear enough to witness and alive enough to leave
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Arthas's voice flattening instead of rising
  - Gate guards stiffening before the last words land
  - Jaina's flour-gray hands still dirty from the failed stores while the prince gives a clean order
- **Transition intent:** The next beat must break the old command chain in public.

### Beat 2 - Scene: Uther Breaks
- **Goal:** Read the moral line in the room and decide where to stand when it snaps.
- **Conflict:** Uther refuses the order. Jaina refuses it too, though she does not waste breath pretending the city can still be saved by the plan that already failed. Arthas does not bend. The split happens in titles, commands, and who turns away from whom.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the gate loses its last moderating weight, and Sylvanas is left with a prince unbraked by the only man in the room who could have slowed him.
- **Value shift:** atrocity named as policy -> command chain ruptured
- **New on-page information:**
  - Uther's break is immediate and public
  - Jaina stands outside Arthas's order, which means the old bond between them is functionally over in the only way that matters now
  - Sylvanas is no longer watching a hard ally make a hard call; she is watching a man step past his own checks
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the city can still be read from anywhere but inside the purge line
  - What the prince will look like once the city is done
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Uther's hammer held low but final
  - The scrape of armored men shifting when loyalties become physical
  - Arthas turning away from both mentor and former lover without losing pace
- **Transition intent:** Sylvanas needs a place to stand that keeps the whole picture visible. The service lane gives her one.

## Scene 2: The Service Lane

**Scene function:** Move the trio off the main gate line, surface the farewell a fifth time, and let the city become legible as a military operation unfolding under horror.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, fleeing civilians, grain carter, scattered soldiers
**Knowledge at scene start:** The Culling is underway. Witness now matters as much as survival.
**Arc pressure:** If they leave entirely, they lose the truth. If they stay at the gate, they get caught in the prince's machine.

### Beat 3 - Scene: Off the Main Gate
- **Goal:** Reach a defensible vantage before the gate becomes unusable.
- **Conflict:** The main approach is collapsing into panic, soldiers, and people who still do not understand what the prince just ordered. Sylvanas has to move Jaina and Cyndia through the edge of that chaos without getting trapped in it.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they break away toward the service lane, but doing so means accepting that they are not stopping what is coming.
- **Value shift:** command chain ruptured -> witness chosen over intervention
- **New on-page information:**
  - Sylvanas actively chooses witness instead of futile participation
  - Jaina keeps moving only because motion is simpler than standing at the main gate and listening
  - The service route offers access to the inner wall without putting them under Arthas's immediate command
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How much of the city they can still see from the wall
  - Whether the wall will make the Culling feel cleaner or worse
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - A side lane clogged with handcarts and dropped sacks
  - Cyndia using her horse as moving cover long enough to clear the turn
  - Jaina stumbling once and correcting without speaking
- **Transition intent:** The route to the wall arrives through a stranger who knows too much about grain routes and stairs.

### Beat 4 - Scene: The Fifth Farewell
- **Goal:** Get route intelligence to the wall and keep clear of the worst crush in the service lane.
- **Conflict:** A grain carter with an empty wagon is trying to get out before the lane seals up. He knows which wall stair is still open, which service yard has already gone sick, and which route will put them above the inner district instead of into it. He should not know enough to be this useful.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they get precise route information and a workable path to the wall, but the farewell lands for the fifth time and locks the pattern in Sylvanas's mind.
- **Value shift:** witness chosen over intervention -> pattern fused to witness
- **New on-page information:**
  - The city's service routes make the wall reachable without reentering the main gate crush
  - An apparently ordinary grain carter has precise, useful knowledge at exactly the right moment
  - "May the stars keep your road" surfaces a fifth time under pressure and cannot be dismissed as coincidence any longer
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the pattern ties to the plague, the route, or only to these women being watched
  - What the city will look like once viewed from above
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Empty wagon bed dusted with flour
  - A man talking too fast because fear is finally catching him
  - The farewell spoken like habit, not performance
- **Transition intent:** The climb begins, and the chapter has to change from lane pressure to wall witness.

### Beat 5 - Scene: First District
- **Goal:** Reach the wall before the first full district goes under.
- **Conflict:** They climb while the city starts breaking below them. Bells, fire, and disciplined troop movement make the prince's answer legible one street at a time. From halfway up the stair, Sylvanas can already tell this is method, not riot.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - they reach the wall, but they do so in time to watch the machine start working as intended.
- **Value shift:** pattern fused to witness -> atrocity now visible at scale
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas's purge is being run district by district, not as blind slaughter
  - That method is what will keep the dead from claiming the city in one mass turn
  - Tactical coherence does not make the thing below them any less monstrous
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How long Arthas can keep the pattern controlled
  - What Jaina is doing with herself beside Sylvanas while the city starts dying under a correct pattern
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Torch lines cutting across a street grid
  - Smoke rising in separate columns instead of one general burn
  - The sound of doors being broken in rhythm rather than panic
- **Transition intent:** Once on the wall, Sylvanas has to read the whole city as both military event and human ruin.

## Scene 3: The Wall

**Scene function:** Deliver the operational witness to the Culling and end on the clean-hands image that hands the inner reckoning to Chapter 18.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas's forces below
**Knowledge at scene start:** The source attempt failed, the Culling is underway, and the city is now a process they cannot stop.
**Arc pressure:** Sylvanas must read clearly without letting clarity turn into absolution.

### Beat 6 - Scene: The Method
- **Goal:** Understand what Arthas is doing well enough to know what comes next.
- **Conflict:** From the wall, the prince's pattern is horrible and plain. He is cutting the city into manageable pieces, denying the plague one mass activation by getting to the living first. The numbers below are flattening in the wrong direction all at once: living population, rescue chance, moral distance, and any remaining illusion that there was a cleaner answer once Jaina's window closed.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Sylvanas gets the full military read, but the read only proves that atrocity can be efficient.
- **Value shift:** atrocity now visible at scale -> method without mercy
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas is tactically preventing a full city-wide turn by killing the city in controlled sections
  - The same method that makes the operation effective is what makes it unforgivable
  - Jaina stays on the wall beside Sylvanas instead of breaking away, which matters more than either woman saying anything
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What this will leave in Arthas when it is over
  - Whether Sylvanas can ever reduce what she is seeing back into report language
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - District fires stepping outward in sequence
  - Soldiers rotating lines with the discipline of a siege instead of a panic
  - Jaina so still beside the parapet that Sylvanas starts tracking her the way she tracks her own bow hand
- **Transition intent:** The chapter must end not on the broad shape but on the one image that turns the military read into something personal and irreversible.

### Beat 7 - Scene: Clean Hands
- **Goal:** Catch the final read on Arthas when the city is done.
- **Conflict:** When the screaming stops and the last organized movement comes back toward the gate, Arthas emerges below them. He looks up once. His hands are clean. That is worse than blood would have been.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the city is contained, but the man who contained it is no longer someone Sylvanas can file under prince, ally, or necessary force without changing the meaning of those words.
- **Value shift:** method without mercy -> irreversible crossing
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas had time, or made time, to wash before stepping back into view
  - He knows the wall holds witnesses and prepares for that fact
  - Sylvanas now has an image she can carry south that is cleaner and more damning than battlefield gore
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What Jaina is making of the same image from inside her own history with him
  - What direction the pursuit takes once the city is behind them
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Pale hands catching firelight with no blood on them
  - Arthas's face emptied out instead of inflamed
  - The city below gone quiet except for burning timber
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 18 opens on this same wall after Arthas walks away, with Jaina carrying the image from the inside.
