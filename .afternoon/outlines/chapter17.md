# Chapter 17: The Gate

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 16 at Stratholme's gates. Opens the moment after Jaina answers Arthas honestly and closes after the Culling, with Arthas emerging below the wall with clean hands and wrong eyes.
**Where:** Stratholme's main gate, service lane, and inner wall.
**Travel:** On foot at the gates and through the service lane, a short climb to the inner wall, then a static witness position once the Culling begins.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers.
**Goal:** Survive Arthas's answer to too late, get a clear read on the city once the source plan fails, and keep Jaina moving inside a decision neither of them controls.

**Sylvanas's starting position:**
- Jaina's source plan was real, practical, and too late at city scale.
- At least one granary could be broken, which proves the method. The problem now is time and mouths rather than theory.
- The city is both seeded and partly steered: the crude choir can self-muster, but living cult handlers are still directing parts of the dead.
- Arthas came to the gate needing a yes-or-no answer and got no.
- Uther heard the same answer.

**Still in the dark about:**
- How far Arthas will carry too late.
- Whether Uther will oppose him in the moment or only afterward.
- How long Jaina can stay upright once the prince turns the answer into policy.
- What the fifth farewell will attach itself to if it appears again.
- Where she and Jaina can stand that keeps them clear enough to witness and alive enough to leave.

**Hold back:**
- No clean emotional reconciliation.
- No explicit Mal'Ganis reveal.
- No interiority for Arthas, Jaina, or Uther.
- No framing that makes the Culling easy just because it is tactically legible.
- No Northrend pivot yet.

**End of chapter state:**
- Arthas answers Jaina's too late with the Culling.
- Uther breaks with him at the gate.
- Jaina refuses the order and stands outside the command chain with Sylvanas.
- The fifth farewell surfaces in the service lane from a fleeing grain carter who also gives them the route to the wall.
- From the wall, Sylvanas reads the Culling as deliberate, methodical, and effective at preventing a full city-wide turn while still remaining an atrocity.
- The crack from Scholomance closes in practice through shared witness before either woman says anything about it.
- Arthas emerges from the gate afterward with clean hands and the look of someone already moving on to the next necessity.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- She opens still hoping Arthas may stay inside hard containment instead of crossing into something worse.
- The gate proves he will turn necessity into license the moment he thinks the cleaner answer is gone.
- She has to watch a tactically coherent atrocity and refuse to let coherence excuse it.
- By the end, Arthas no longer reads as a hard ally. He reads as a man who crossed a line so fast only the aftermath is left to track.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Jaina is the failed rescuer and the witness whose honest answer made the next order possible without causing it.
- The source attempt gives Sylvanas the field proof she needed: whatever happened in Kel'Thuzad's room, Jaina spent the knowledge here trying to save strangers.
- Keep Jaina exterior. Show her through silence, posture, spell residue, and where she fixes her eyes once the gate order lands.

### Partnership
- The pair opens damaged but synchronized.
- Shared witness finishes what the source attempt started.
- The crack is not talked through. It closes in placement, timing, and who stays beside whom when leaving would be easier.
- Do not give them spoken absolution.

## Scene 1: The Gate

**The action:** Jaina's honest answer becomes Arthas's order, and the moral split breaks into the open.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers.
**Starting point:** The source plan failed because it was late, not because it was wrong.

### Beat 1
- Arthas does not ask for nuance twice. If the city cannot be saved cleanly, he treats that as the decision already made and orders the city purged before the dead can claim it.
- Arthas converts too late straight into the Culling, he is now moving faster than anyone else at the gate can morally follow, and Falric and the command edge are already orienting around execution rather than debate.
- They do not know whether Uther will refuse in words only or in action or where Sylvanas and Jaina can stand that keeps them clear enough to witness and alive enough to leave.
- Ground the order: Arthas's voice flattening instead of rising, gate guards stiffening before the last words land, Jaina's flour-gray hands still dirty from the failed stores while the prince gives a clean command.
- The uncertainty ends in the worst clean answer the gate could have produced.

### Beat 2
- Uther refuses the order. Jaina refuses it too, though she does not waste breath pretending the city can still be saved by the plan that already failed. Arthas does not bend.
- Uther's break is immediate and public, Jaina stands outside Arthas's order and closes that bond in the only way that matters now, and Sylvanas is no longer watching a hard ally make a hard call but a man stepping past his last checks.
- They do not know whether the city can still be read from anywhere but inside the purge line or what the prince will look like once the city is done.
- Ground the split: Uther's hammer held low but final, the scrape of armored men shifting when loyalties become physical, Arthas turning away from mentor and former lover without losing pace.
- The main gate stops being a place to argue and becomes a place to survive.

## Scene 2: The Service Lane

**The action:** The three women leave the main gate, the farewell appears a fifth time, and the city becomes legible as a military operation unfolding inside horror.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, fleeing civilians, grain carter, scattered soldiers.
**Starting point:** The Culling is underway. Witness now matters as much as survival.

### Beat 3
- The main approach is collapsing into panic, soldiers, and people who still do not understand what the prince has ordered. Sylvanas gets Jaina and Cyndia out through the edge of that crush before it seals.
- Sylvanas chooses witness over futile participation, Jaina keeps moving only because motion is easier than standing at the gate and listening, and the service route offers access to the wall without putting them under Arthas's immediate command.
- They do not know how much of the city they can still see from the wall or whether the wall will make the Culling feel cleaner or worse.
- Ground the escape: a side lane clogged with handcarts and dropped sacks, Cyndia using her horse as moving cover long enough to clear the turn, Jaina stumbling once and correcting without speaking.
- They leave the gate line knowing they are not stopping what is coming.

### Beat 4
- A grain carter with an empty wagon is trying to get out before the lane seals up. He knows which wall stair is still open, which service yard has already gone sick, and which route will put them above the inner district instead of inside it. He should not be this useful.
- The service routes make the wall reachable without reentering the main crush, an apparently ordinary grain carter has precise and timely knowledge, and May the stars keep your road surfaces a fifth time and cannot be dismissed as coincidence anymore.
- They do not know whether the pattern ties to the plague, the route, or only to these women being watched or what the city will look like once viewed from above.
- Ground the clue: an empty wagon bed dusted with flour, a man talking too fast because fear is finally catching him, the farewell spoken like habit instead of performance.
- The pattern locks harder in Sylvanas's mind even as the wall route opens.

### Beat 5
- They climb while the city starts breaking below them. Bells, fire, and disciplined troop movement make Arthas's answer legible one street at a time. From halfway up the stair, Sylvanas can already tell this is method rather than riot.
- Arthas's purge is being run district by district rather than as blind slaughter, that method is what will keep the dead from claiming the city in one mass turn, and tactical coherence does not make what is happening less monstrous.
- They do not know how long Arthas can keep the pattern controlled or what Jaina is doing with herself beside Sylvanas while the city starts dying under a correct pattern.
- Ground the climb: torch lines cutting across a street grid, smoke rising in separate columns instead of one general burn, doors being broken in rhythm instead of panic.
- They reach the wall in time to watch the machine start working exactly as intended.

## Scene 3: The Wall

**The action:** Sylvanas gets the operational read on the Culling, then sees the one image that turns that read into something personal and irreversible.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas's forces below.
**Starting point:** The source attempt failed, the Culling is underway, and the city is now a process they cannot stop.

### Beat 6
- From the wall, Arthas's pattern is horrible and plain. He is cutting the city into manageable pieces, denying the plague one mass activation by getting to the living first. The same method that makes it effective is what makes it unforgivable.
- Arthas is tactically preventing a full city-wide turn by killing the city in controlled sections, efficiency does not reduce the atrocity, and Jaina stays on the wall beside Sylvanas instead of breaking away, which matters more than anything either woman says.
- They do not know what this will leave in Arthas when it is over or whether Sylvanas can ever reduce what she is seeing back into report language.
- Ground the witness: district fires stepping outward in sequence, soldiers rotating lines with the discipline of a siege instead of a panic, Jaina so still beside the parapet that Sylvanas starts tracking her the way she tracks her own bow hand.
- Sylvanas gets the full military read and hates what the clarity proves.

### Beat 7
- When the screaming stops and the last organized movement comes back toward the gate, Arthas emerges below them. He looks up once. His hands are clean. That is worse than blood would have been.
- Arthas had time, or made time, to wash before stepping back into view, he knows the wall holds witnesses and prepares for that fact, and Sylvanas now has an image she can carry south that is cleaner and more damning than battlefield gore.
- They do not know what Jaina is making of the same image from inside her own history with him or what direction the pursuit takes once the city is behind them.
- Ground the image: pale hands catching firelight with no blood on them, Arthas's face emptied out instead of inflamed, the city below gone quiet except for burning timber.
- Arthas stops reading as prince, ally, or necessary force and becomes something worse.
