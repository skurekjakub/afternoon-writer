# Mal'Ganis

- Role: hidden primary antagonist, counterintelligence chief, Book 2 reveal that recontextualizes Book 1.
- Core: he is not the plague's inventor or the Cult's builder. He is the thing making sure no one reaches either in time.

## Job
- Front 1: sanitize sites, remove witnesses, redirect investigations.
- Front 2: keep Arthas busy with the wrong war until the timing is right.
- He is the reason good investigation keeps arriving one step late.

## Relevant capabilities
- Perfect humanoid disguise.
- Short-range telepathy good enough to read intent in conversation.
- Ward-focus that flattens his demonic aura so Jaina reads him as nothing at all.
- True-form power stays offstage until the reveal.

## The two tells
- Physical: the dark-stone ward-focus that makes him magically boring.
- Verbal: the repeated farewell, "May the stars keep your road," which turns out to be an ancient Kaldorei fossil he no longer notices he is using.

## Confirmed face trail
- Ch 3: old trader.
- Ch 6: grain trader.
- Ch 9: retreating figure.
- Ch 15: military aide.
- Ch 17: refugee-road merchant.
- On first read these should feel like unrelated helpers. On second read they are the same ancient thing walking beside the investigation.

## True self
- Patient, amused, professionally contemptuous.
- His single indulgence is the late gloat.
- He does not explain everything at the reveal; he gives just enough for Sylvanas and Jaina to start reconstructing the larger enemy.

## Book 2 function
- Sylvanas brings the linguistic tell.
- Jaina brings the ward logic.
- The partnership becomes the only way to hunt him.
