# Antonidas

- Role: Jaina's mentor, Archmage, the voice on the stone.
- Core: the best of the Kirin Tor and still too slow. He believes Jaina from the start. That is what makes the failure hurt.

## Look
- Seventy-one, thin, white-haired, long beard, scholar's posture, administrator's hands stained by ink instead of reagents.
- Dalaran violet worn as office, not costume.

## Voice
- Measured, paternal, precise.
- Complete thoughts, careful qualifiers, silences that say more than the words.
- Calls Jaina "child" when the father in him slips past the Archmage.

## What matters
- He trained Jaina to trust her mind and then could not move the institution fast enough to back her.
- He trained beside Kel'Thuzad and failed to stop him before procedure ran out of usefulness.
- He mirrors Lor'themar: one good man inside a system built to delay.

## Stone progression
- Ch 2: calm mentor voice. He wants her home and already knows she will refuse.
- Ch 7: the institution proves it still has value by sending useful Cult intel.
- Ch 8: Kel'Thuzad's name strips the softness out of him.
- Ch 12: longest silence in the book after Jaina reports the full scale.
- Ch 18: last real plea before Northrend.
- Ch 25: he arrives, argues, asks for time, and still cannot save her.

## Carry-forward
- Keep him warm but never safe.
- His tragedy is not disbelief. It is belief without speed.
- Book 2 inherits two things from him: the coming death and the question of whether the Kirin Tor can be worth saving after failing this badly.
