# Vereesa Windrunner

- Role: recurring supporting; the family bond the Convocation turns into a weapon.
- Core: the sister who carries orders Sylvanas cannot bear to receive from anyone else.

## Look
- Clear Windrunner resemblance, lighter build than Sylvanas, scout rather than command-ranger presence.
- Wide, more readable face; emotions show faster than Sylvanas's do.

## Voice
- Warmer, longer, more openly emotional.
- Under stress she goes fast and precise where Sylvanas goes still.
- Childhood or family Thalassian slips out when the fight turns personal.

## What matters
- The Convocation sends her because blood can do what bureaucracy cannot: make the order hurt.
- She knows she is being used and comes anyway because she would rather be the blade than let someone colder carry it.
- She is the stay-home Windrunner, the one who still believes home can hold if enough people keep serving it.

## Sylvanas line
- Their argument is not really about the recall. It is about whether duty to the wider truth is worth losing the family that still exists.
- Sylvanas keeps answering as commander. Vereesa keeps speaking as sister. That mismatch is the wound.

## Chapter use
- Ch 19: carries the recall and Lor'themar's worst report.
- Ch 25: carries the disavowal and makes the family cost public.

## Carry-forward
- Book 2 inherits her as someone whose anger has shifted away from Sylvanas and toward the institution that used her to wound her own blood.
