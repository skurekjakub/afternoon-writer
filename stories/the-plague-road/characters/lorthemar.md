# Lor'themar Theron

- Role: Sylvanas's second-in-command and the Silvermoon political war made human.
- Core: he fights the same battle Antonidas fights, only with votes and favors instead of archives and councils.

## Look
- Ranger-built, red-gold hair, old field scar on the jaw, armor that starts neat and gets progressively less cared for.
- Three snapshots matter:
  - Ch 1: dry, sharp, at ease.
  - Ch 9: tired, gaunt, first real visible wear.
  - Ch 19 via Vereesa: hollowed out, humor gone, still standing.

## Voice
- Field voice with Sylvanas: dry shorthand, old-command efficiency, wit used like a knife.
- Convocation voice: quiet, precise, controlled, built to land one sentence at a time.
- Track the humor as a barometer. When the humor dies, he is nearly spent.

## What matters
- He knows Sylvanas is right and spends twenty-five chapters trying to make Silvermoon act like it.
- He counts votes the way a ranger counts angles.
- He spends every favor he has to buy time, then a scout company, then finally full mobilization.
- He wins too late.

## Key ties
- Sylvanas: the only person he can challenge privately and the commander whose freedom he keeps alive by doing politics for her.
- Antonidas: explicit mirror. Two good advocates, two failing systems, same wall.

## Chapter use
- Ch 1: baseline ease.
- Ch 3-8: runner messages shift from routine to active political combat.
- Ch 9: brief appearance to show the cost in his body.
- Ch 17-18: partial win with the scout company.
- Ch 19: Vereesa reports the man the Convocation has ground nearly flat.
- Ch 23-25: full force finally moves and still misses the one room that mattered.

## Carry-forward
- Book 2 inherits him as the man who finally won the argument and burned his whole political life doing it.
