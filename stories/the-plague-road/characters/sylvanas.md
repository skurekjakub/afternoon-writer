# Sylvanas Windrunner

- Role: protagonist, odd-chapter POV.
- Core: the border's finest defender forced to learn the border is a lie.

## Background
- Ranger-General, middle Windrunner, living and pre-undeath.
- Alleria is gone, Lirath is dead, Vereesa is what family remains close at hand.
- Duty is not one part of her identity. It is the frame holding the rest together.

## Look
- Tall, silver-blonde, sharp-featured, Farstrider armor worn like second skin.
- Beauty is present and irrelevant to her except as another fact the room can react to.
- Moves like someone who has spent centuries choosing where to stand before anyone else knows a choice exists.

## Voice
- Dry, exact, economical.
- Questions often function as orders.
- Humor is deadpan and usually wasted on humans.
- Inward voice is tactical: rooms, people, roads, silences, all read for use or threat.

## Core shape
- Strength: battlefield intelligence, command authority, refusal to panic.
- Flaw: arrogance about what her lens can and cannot see.
- Lie: what happens beyond the border belongs to other people.
- Truth: the threat that ignores the wall ignores the politics that built the wall.

## Key ties
- Jaina: starts as dangerous trespasser, becomes necessary specialist, then the partner Sylvanas would not trade even after a lost fight.
- Lor'themar: second-in-command, political translator, oldest working trust in the book.
- Vereesa: sister she cannot stop treating partly like a subordinate.
- Arthas: a commander she reads correctly and still cannot control.

## Arc line
- Ch 1: she is still entirely a border creature.
- Ch 2-8: the mage becomes Jaina and the investigation drags her far past the frame she trusts.
- Ch 9: her tactical mistake kills one of her own and teaches her to wait for what she cannot read alone.
- Ch 13-14: the Scholomance crack damages trust but does not kill the team.
- Ch 17-18: Stratholme burns away the remaining distance.
- Ch 19: she refuses recall for the first time in her life.
- Ch 21-23: Northrend and the throne room prove the partnership is not convenience but structure.
- Ch 25: prison strips away every institution except the one bond she built herself.

## Writer rules
- Keep her self-aware, not self-explanatory.
- She does not monologue about her feelings. She shows them in choices, timing, and what she refuses to leave alone.
