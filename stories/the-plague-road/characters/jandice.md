# Jandice Barov

**Role:** Jaina's guide into Scholomance, peer mirror, the dark road taken
**Species:** Human (she/her)
**Age:** Early twenties (roughly Jaina's age)
**Chapters:** 10 (primary), referenced in Ch 12 (Kel'Thuzad conversation)
**Tier:** Supporting (single-chapter primary, Book 2 potential)

---

## The Concept — The Fork Taken

Kel'Thuzad is the mirror across a lifetime. Jandice is the mirror across a hallway.

Where Kel'Thuzad (Ch 12) shows Jaina what she could become in decades of unchecked conviction — the slow patience of corruption, each compromise small enough to rationalize — Jandice shows the choice made NOW. A woman Jaina's age. Same training methods. Same intellectual hunger. Same willingness to pursue knowledge beyond what her teachers approved. Same hands, scarred by the same kind of work. The difference: Jandice walked into Scholomance and decided the methodology was worth more than the ethics. Not in the abstract. Not someday. In the same building where Jaina is standing. With the same textbooks on the same shelves.

The three mirrors, each operating at a different timescale:
- **Kel'Thuzad:** The destination — what happens to brilliant conviction across a lifetime without friction.
- **Arthas:** The velocity — what happens to brilliant conviction in weeks, without methodology.
- **Jandice:** The threshold — the moment of choice, frozen in amber. She is standing at the fork Jaina already passed, and she chose the other path.

Jandice is terrifying to Jaina not because she is monstrous, but because she is RECOGNIZABLE. The monstrous part of Kel'Thuzad allows Jaina the comfort of distance — "I would never go that far." Jandice allows no such comfort. Jandice is smart, disciplined, passionate about knowledge, critical of systems that suppress inquiry, and convinced that the work speaks for itself. Jaina is all of those things. The gap between them is a single decision that Jandice made with the same certainty Jaina brings to every border she crosses.

## Appearance

Dark hair pulled back in a style that is half noblewoman, half scholar — practical, severe, designed to keep hair out of ritual work. Not vanity — function married to a noble upbringing that won't let her look unkempt even in a laboratory. Her robes are cut like Kirin Tor academic dress but dyed in Barov burgundy rather than Dalaran violet. The colors tell the story before she opens her mouth: she was trained in one tradition and chose another.

She carries no staff — unusual for a practicing mage of her caliber. The omission is deliberate. Scholomance's methods do not require a focus; the work is done through inscription, catalysis, and direct manipulation. A staff would mark her as a Kirin Tor product. She is not.

Her hands are the telling detail. Calloused in the same places as Jaina's — the pen callus, the instrument grip, the pattern of someone who holds tools for hours. Reagent burns between the second and third fingers of the right hand. The flattened pad on the left thumb from holding ritual chalk. Fine scarring on both palms from inscription work. They are a peer's hands. A colleague's hands. But Jandice's scarring has a darker cast — the bruise-purple discoloration that comes from working with necromantic reagents, materials that leave traces in living tissue because the materials interact with life itself. The stains don't wash out. They are a record of what she has handled, written on her skin.

Her face is composed, controlled — a noblewoman's social mask over a scholar's restless intelligence. The composure is trained, not natural. Her eyes give her away: they assess Jaina with the same speed and precision Jaina uses to assess everything. The mutual recognition is immediate. Two people who operate at the same intellectual speed, reading each other at the pace the world usually can't follow.

### The Physical Echo

The Jaina-Jandice parallel must be visible in the prose. Same height (within an inch). Same scholar's posture — the forward lean of people who spend hours over desks. Same habit of speaking with their hands when excited about methodology (Jandice catches herself and stills hers; the self-control reveals the passion). Same ink stains at the right thumb and forefinger. Same reagent burns, different colors. If they stood side by side in Dalaran robes, a casual observer might take them for classmates. That is the horror.

## Voice Guide

The voice of old money married to new education — polished social courtesy with flashes of genuine scholarly passion that break through the composure. Her sentences are controlled when she is performing (the guide, the noblewoman, the Scholomance representative), and they lengthen and complexify when she forgets to perform (the scholar, the true believer, the mind that can't help following a thought to its conclusion).

### Key Patterns

- **The curated sentence:** When guiding, her language is measured, each phrase chosen to present Scholomance in its best light while avoiding specifics that would alarm. "Our research program covers the full spectrum of post-mortem magical theory." Translation: "We study necromancy, but I'm not going to say that word until you do."

- **The scholarly break:** When her pride is engaged — when Jaina asks a question that touches the work Jandice loves — the curation fails. The sentences lengthen. Her vocabulary shifts from diplomatic to precise. She starts using terminology Jaina recognizes from Kirin Tor advanced seminars. Her hands come up, tracing shapes in the air that map the conceptual territory she's describing. She catches herself. Stills her hands. Too late — the passion has been visible for three seconds. The break is the crack Jaina reads.

- **The validation hunger:** She does not ask for approval. She asks for recognition. The distinction is everything. "You see the method" (what she wants to hear) vs. "The method is moral" (what she does NOT need to hear). She wants Jaina to look at the work and admit that it is rigorous, innovative, and brilliant. Whether it is ethical is, to Jandice, a separate conversation — and one she believes she has already settled.

- **The Kirin Tor citation:** She has read the Kirin Tor literature. All of it. She cites papers with specific, accurate references — authors, dates, the conclusions they reached and the conclusions they should have reached. She has formed opinions about Dalaran's published work the way a rival academic forms opinions: with respect for the effort and contempt for the timidity. She knows the Kirin Tor's research better than most Kirin Tor mages. She has had years to study it without the constraints of what Dalaran considers acceptable conclusions.

### Dialogue Samples

**Curated mode (early in the tour):**
"The estate has served as a center of learning since Lord Barov's generous endowment. Our students come from across Lordaeron — promising minds who sought opportunities the traditional academies could not provide."

**The scholarly break (when Jaina asks about the carrier treatment):**
"The binding coefficient — you see it? The preparation doesn't force the transformation. It settles the outer layer and lets the body finish the answer. The tissue wants to fall into the taught pattern. We found that three years ago. Mellar's paper in Dalaran still assumes you batter dead matter from the outside. That's the crude version. Why force the change when living tissue can be taught to keep it for you?"

**Validation hunger (reading Jaina's face):**
"You see it. You understand the elegance of it." (Not a question. A statement. She needs to say it aloud to make it real.)

**The noble mask returning:**
"Shall we continue? The library is quite extensive. I think you'll find our collection... comprehensive."

**The Kirin Tor citation (when challenged):**
"Hartley published the standard binding theory in — when, 642? The model assumes unidirectional transfer. Your own Archmage refined it twelve years later, and even then the directional assumption held. We disproved it in the first semester. Not theoretically — experimentally. The tissue samples are in the third archive if you'd care to verify."

### Distinction from the Other Academic Voices

- **Antonidas** speaks *to* his listener — adjusting, softening, meeting them where they are. The mentor.
- **Kel'Thuzad** speaks *at* his listener — offering the material at the level it demands, without accommodation. The lecturer.
- **Jandice** speaks *for* her listener — she needs Jaina to see the quality, because recognition from a mind that can follow the work at speed is the only recognition that counts. The peer seeking acknowledgment.

The three voices share Kirin Tor vocabulary and training, making them sound similar in word choice. The orientation — toward, at, for — is what distinguishes them. Prose writers should key on this: Jandice's sentences lean forward. They reach. They want something from the listener that Antonidas never asks for and Kel'Thuzad would never lower himself to need.

## Background: The Barov Choice

Lord Alexei Barov made a deal. His daughter made a choice. The distinction matters.

Alexei Barov's arrangement with Kel'Thuzad was transactional: the estate, the grain network, the distribution channels — offered in exchange for protection and the promise that the Barov family would be among the survivors. (See `characters/supporting.md`, Lord Alexei Barov section.) Alexei did not believe in the methodology. He believed in the odds. He read the situation the way a man of his class reads any crisis: with a ledger, calculating which side to back.

Jandice's choice was not transactional. No one promised her survival. No one offered her a deal. She walked into Scholomance's lecture halls because she heard what was being taught there, and she recognized brilliance.

She had been trained in basic arcane theory — the education available to a Lordaeron noblewoman with aptitude. Not Kirin Tor-caliber training (Dalaran accepts few, and the Barovs are provincial nobility, not Dalaran-connected), but enough to understand what she was looking at when Kel'Thuzad's curriculum crossed her path. Enough to recognize that the methodology was more rigorous, more ambitious, and more intellectually honest than anything the regional academies offered.

The word "honest" is doing the heavy lifting. In Jandice's framework, the Kirin Tor's prohibition on necromantic research is intellectual cowardice — a powerful body of scholars drawing a line not because the research is wrong but because the conclusions are uncomfortable. Kel'Thuzad crossed that line. The Kirin Tor expelled him for it. Jandice's reading: they punished the best mind they had for following the evidence. That is the betrayal of everything scholarship is supposed to mean. Scholomance is the correction.

She does not see genocide. She sees methodology. The distinction is not stupidity — it is the selective attention of a mind that has decided what it wants to look at. The plague is "applied research." The reanimation protocols are "practical application." The human cost is "in another department." She has built a conceptual wall between the work she loves and the work's consequences, and the wall is maintained by the same intellectual rigor she applies to everything else. The wall is brilliantly constructed. That is why it will be so devastating when reality demolishes it.

## The Chess Game — Chapter 10 Deployment

Chapter 10 is a chess game disguised as a guided tour. Both players know the other is playing. Neither can admit it. The tension is not whether Jaina will see something dangerous — it is how much Jandice will accidentally reveal because her pride cannot tolerate a Kirin Tor prodigy walking through Scholomance and failing to appreciate the work.

### Jandice's Dual Agenda

**Agenda 1 — Contain:** Kel'Thuzad authorized the tour. His calculus: the mage has already entered the building. Better to control what she sees than to let her wander. Jandice is the guide because she is young, articulate, and presents the best face for a visitor from Dalaran. She is supposed to keep Jaina on the curated path — the library, the lecture halls, the presentable research. NOT the production levels. Not the plague refinement labs. Not the reanimation chambers.

**Agenda 2 — Impress:** Jandice WANTS Jaina to see the work. Not the worst of it — the best of it. The theoretical frameworks, the cross-school synthesis, the research methodology that surpasses anything Dalaran produces. She wants the Kirin Tor's star pupil to walk through Scholomance and think: *they were right to come here.* This agenda is not sanctioned by Kel'Thuzad. It is Jandice's own, driven by the validation hunger that is her deepest vulnerability.

### The Chess Mechanics

The game has three phases:

**Opening (curated, controlled):** Jandice shows the library, the lecture halls, the research archive. She narrates in curated mode — polished, careful, presenting Scholomance as an ambitious independent academy. Jaina plays the impressed guest while mapping the building's layout, ward placements, and security patterns.

**Middle game (the pride crack):** Jaina asks a question that engages Jandice's scholarly pride — something about methodology, about the carrier treatment and host response, about the cross-school synthesis work. The question must be genuine (Jaina IS interested, and the interest is not entirely feigned — the work IS brilliant). Jandice's curation fails. She starts showing more than the script allows. Not the production levels — but the research that leads to them. The theoretical foundations. The work she loves.

**Endgame (the reveal too far):** Jandice's pride carries her past the containment line. She shows Jaina something she should not have — a research document, a demonstration, a room that was not on the tour. Not the genocide. Not the plague lab. Something ADJACENT — something that, combined with what Jaina already knows from the investigation, lets Jaina make a connection Jandice didn't intend. The reveal is not about carelessness. It is about the specific vulnerability of a brilliant person whose need for recognition overrides their operational discipline.

### What Jandice Does NOT Know

She does not know:
- The full scope of the plague deployment (she knows the research, not the logistics)
- That Jaina is investigating the Cult of the Damned (she has been told Jaina is a Kirin Tor emissary pursuing academic exchange — Kel'Thuzad's cover story)
- That Sylvanas is waiting outside with a Farstrider escort planning a combat extraction if Jaina doesn't return by dawn
- The extent to which the "applied research" she dismisses as another department has already ended thousands of lives

She DOES know:
- The full Scholomance curriculum (academic content, not operational deployment)
- The layout of every floor she has access to (she does not have access to the deepest production levels)
- Kel'Thuzad's research methodology in exquisite detail
- The ward placements and their maintenance schedule
- The identities and capabilities of the other students and faculty
- The Barov family's relationship to Kel'Thuzad (her father's deal, which she considers beneath discussion)

### The Handwriting Mirror

Among the tour's stopping points is a research archive where Kel'Thuzad's notes are stored alongside student work. Jaina recognizes the notation system — the same system Antonidas taught her, the same system Kel'Thuzad learned at the Kirin Tor decades before either of them was born. The handwriting echoes become unbearable here: Jandice's notes beside Kel'Thuzad's notes beside what looks like Jaina's own handwriting. Three generations of the same training, the same margins, the same methodical progression from hypothesis to proof. Different conclusions. Same hand.

## Relationship to Kel'Thuzad

Admiration that borders on reverence. Not personal — scholarly.

He is the finest mind she has encountered. His willingness to cross the lines the Kirin Tor drew is, to Jandice, intellectual courage rather than ethical failure. She has absorbed his conceptual approach: the treatment of death as transformation rather than cessation, the insistence that moral squeamishness is the enemy of understanding, the framework that reframes prohibition as cowardice.

She does not worship him. She studies under him. The distinction matters for her voice: she does not quote him the way a disciple quotes a prophet. She cites him the way a graduate student cites their thesis advisor — with respect, with specific references to specific works, and with the occasional disagreement that proves she has done her own thinking within his tradition.

The relationship's most telling feature: Kel'Thuzad trusts her enough to put her in front of a Kirin Tor prodigy. He chose her specifically because he recognized that her scholarly passion — the genuine article, not performance — would be more convincing than any rehearsed presentation. He weaponized her authenticity. She does not know this. She believes she was chosen because she is the best student. She is. That is precisely why she is dangerous as a guide.

## What She Wants / What She Fears

**What she wants:** Recognition. The Kirin Tor expelled Kel'Thuzad. The academic establishment rejected the methodology. Jandice has spent years in a school that the outside world considers an abomination, studying under a genius the Kirin Tor cast out, and she believes the work speaks for itself. Jaina Proudmoore — Antonidas's protegee, the prodigy everyone in Dalaran whispers about — is standing in her foyer. If JAINA sees the quality...

**What she fears:** Irrelevance. That the Kirin Tor is right and Scholomance is a footnote. That the work she's devoted herself to is, at the end of the day, the intellectual hobby of a noble girl playing at scholarship while the real mages study in Dalaran. The fear makes her NEED Jaina's engagement. It's the crack Jaina can exploit — and does, without meaning to, simply by being genuinely interested in the methodology.

## Arc Seeds (Book 2)

### The Wall Falls

When the dead march. When the plague Jandice categorized as "applied research" produces an army of corpses walking in formation toward the living. When the methodology she revered produces its intended result and the result is not transformation but extinction.

Jandice's conceptual wall — the partition between brilliant work and monstrous consequence — cannot survive contact with the consequence walking through the gates. The question is not WHETHER the wall falls but HOW she responds when it does. Three possibilities, each offering a different Book 2 path:

**Possibility 1: The Turncoat.** The wall falls and she sees. Horror. Revision. The dawning understanding that she was not a scholar — she was a weapon's maintenance crew, keeping the methodology sharp while someone else aimed it. She becomes a reluctant intelligence source: the only living person who understands Scholomance's remaining workings from the inside. The price: returning to the building where she made her choice, in the company of the woman whose recognition she craved and whose judgment she now fears.

**Possibility 2: The Rationalist.** The wall falls and she REBUILDS it. New rationalization: the deployment was a perversion of the research. Kel'Thuzad's methodology was sound; the application was someone else's corruption. The genius was right; the genocide was an aberration. This version of Jandice is more dangerous — she retains her capabilities and her framework, and she is actively constructing a narrative that lets her continue the work. She becomes not a turncoat but a competitor: someone who wants to inherit Scholomance's legacy while rejecting its worst applications.

**Possibility 3: The Shattered.** The wall falls and there is nothing underneath it. She built her identity on the work. The work built an army of corpses. Identity without foundation. This version is the most tragic — a brilliant mind with nowhere to stand, the scholarly certainty that defined her dissolved by its own conclusions. She becomes not a source or a competitor but a cautionary tale — the person Jaina would have been if the partnership hadn't given her somewhere to stand when her own certainty failed.

### The Nathrezim Journals

Kel'Thuzad's journals in Scholomance (documented in `characters/kelthuzad.md` under "The Journals — Book 2 Seed") contain the most comprehensive mortal scholarship on dreadlord capabilities. Jandice may know where the private study is. She may know the ward sequences. She may be the only person who can guide someone through Scholomance's defenses to reach the one room that matters. The price: returning to the place where she made her choice, walking the same halls she walked as a true believer, with the true belief gone.

### The Recognition Reversed

In Ch 10, Jandice wanted Jaina to recognize the quality of Scholomance's work. In Book 2, the recognition reverses: Jaina needs Jandice to recognize the COST of that work. The peer mirror inverts. The woman who wanted validation must now give testimony. The brilliant scholar who said "you see the method" must now answer: "What did the method produce?"

## Prose-Writer Notes

### The Trap

Jandice must be LIKEABLE. Not despite her choice — because of who she is around the choice. She is smart, passionate, funny when her guard drops, and genuinely excited about ideas. If the reader cannot see why Jaina finds her compelling — cannot feel the magnetic pull of a mind operating at the same speed — then the mirror fails. The reader must think, for at least one page: "If I were Jaina, I would like this person." The horror arrives when the reader realizes what the person they like has been studying.

### The Tour Is Not Exposition

The Ch 10 tour is a two-person scene with the tension of a knife fight conducted with courtesy. Both women are reading each other. Both are performing. Both keep dropping the performance when their scholarly selves break through. The prose must carry TWO chess games: Jaina's (mapping the building, reading the security, looking for intelligence) and Jandice's (managing the reveal, containing her pride, measuring whether Jaina is impressed). The reader should feel both.

### Voice Traps to Avoid

- **Do not make her a zealot.** She does not preach. She does not monologue about the righteousness of her cause. She is a scholar who believes the work speaks for itself. Her conviction is quiet, ambient, the background hum of someone who has settled a question and moved on to more interesting problems.
- **Do not make her naive.** She is not stupid. She CHOSE not to look at certain things. The selective attention is sophisticated, not childish. She has reasons for each boundary she maintains. The reasons are good. The conclusions they support are catastrophic.
- **Do not make her a villain.** She is a person. She has a father she considers embarrassing (the deal was beneath the family name). She has favorite passages in the Kirin Tor canon. She has a preferred tea. She gets cold hands in Scholomance's drafty halls and tucks them into her sleeves the same way Jaina tucks hers into her robes in the Dalaran library. The humanity is the blade.
