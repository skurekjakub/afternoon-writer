# Kel'Thuzad

- Role: primary antagonist of the investigation and Scholomance block; Jaina's long-form dark mirror.
- Core: right about almost everything except the one thing that matters.

## Look
- Gaunt, former-Council silhouette still visible in the robes, scholar's hands marked by ugly work.
- He should look like a man who never stopped being an academic, only changed the subject matter.

## Voice
- Lecturer's cadence, not villain theater.
- Imperatives of engagement: "consider," "observe," "you see it now."
- He is warm when intellectually pleased, which makes him worse.
- Distinction:
  - Antonidas speaks to.
  - Kel'Thuzad speaks at.
  - Jandice speaks for.

## What matters
- Former Council member, expelled too late, went north willingly.
- Built the Cult and Scholomance with the same rigor Dalaran taught him.
- He is Jaina's mirror because the method is shared even when the ethics are not.
- The horror of Ch 12 is that he is compelling for the right reasons: intelligence, coherence, delight in being fully understood.

## Key ties
- Antonidas: colleague he outgrew or believes he outgrew.
- Jaina: first mind in years he thinks is worth teaching at full speed.
- Lich King: patron he reads as a greater library, not as a master.

## Chapter use
- Ch 7-10: present through the machine he built.
- Ch 12: the conversation, not the duel, is the main event.
- Ch 13: dies calm, as if finishing a theorem.

## Book 2 hook
- His journals on the Nathrezim are real treasure.
- The monster's library may be the only place with the intelligence needed to fight Mal'Ganis.
