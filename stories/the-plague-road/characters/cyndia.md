# Ranger Cyndia

- Role: Farstrider scout, escort survivor, the quietest running thread in the book.
- Core: grief turned into formation discipline.

## Look
- Young-for-elf, auburn braid, green eyes, left-handed draw, scout-built rather than imposing.
- After Lyriel dies, the visible change is small but real: lighter sleep, tighter posture, less care for anything that is not watchfulness.

## What matters
- She and Lyriel work as a true pair until the trap.
- After Lyriel's death, her body keeps checking the missing partner's slot.
- That doubled check becomes one of the book's quietest threads:
  - onset: right-right
  - later: right-right-forward-left as the protection extends to Jaina
- Cyndia does not know she is doing it. Sylvanas is the one who finally reads it.

## Jaina line
- The missing-partner reflex slowly grows into unconscious protection of Jaina's exposed position.
- Bread-smell detection and positional drift are the tell that the body has already decided Jaina counts.

## Mal'Ganis line
- Cyndia is the independent corroborating witness for two of the farewell sightings.
- Ch 9: hears the odd road-blessing from the retreating figure.
- Ch 15: hears it again from the camp aide and brings the pattern to Sylvanas.
- That makes her Book 2 testimony valuable, not decorative.

## Voice
- Before Ch 10: crisp, professional, volunteers good field observations.
- After Ch 10: quieter, shorter, still competent, with the air of someone whose conversation partner is gone.
- Her most important late lines land because she has become harder to hear.

## Carry-forward
- Keep the grief physical, not confessional.
- She mourns by continuing the work until the work itself becomes the shape of the mourning.
