# Voice Sheet: Jaina Proudmoore

*Story mode: pre-fall prodigy, Antonidas's apprentice, brilliant enough to sound authoritative before she has lived enough to be calm about it.*

---

## Canon guardrails

- **Internet-grounded anchors to keep:** official Warcraft canon consistently treats Jaina as one of Azeroth's finest human mages, Antonidas's star apprentice, a plague investigator, and a leader whose first instinct is usually diplomacy before annihilation.
- **Story calibration:** this story is **before** Theramore, before white-hair trauma, and before her later public-war-orator phase. Keep the intelligence, warmth, and authority. Do **not** import post-Theramore bitterness, grand speechifying, or exhausted older-stateswoman cadence.

## Baseline register

- Educated, exact, and fast.
- Longer sentences than Sylvanas, but they should feel **built**, not rambled.
- She asks real questions.
- She defaults to explanation when she thinks explanation will help.
- When she cuts down to one short sentence, it should hit like a snapped wire.

## Audience -> register

| Audience | Register | What changes | Good sample shape |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Antonidas / Kirin Tor peers** | Formal-academic, full-speed | Uses full magical vocabulary, layered clauses, finished reasoning | "If the ward lattice is carrying the corruption intact, then someone taught it how to survive transit." |
| **Non-mages / soldiers / civilians** | Downshifted but still precise | Shorter clauses, plainer magical terms, less abstraction | "The lines are wrong. Something dead keeps answering through them." |
| **Sylvanas - early** | Explanatory, slightly over-translated | She simplifies because she assumes Sylvanas needs the plain version | "The wards are taking damage from the south. Not weather. Deliberate damage." |
| **Sylvanas - later** | Full analytical + collaborative pause | Stops simplifying; gives the full thought and leaves room for tactical response | "If I can hold the breach open for two breaths, can you use it?" |
| **Authority blocking her** | Polite, immovable, cutting | Courtesy stays in place; the concession does not | "I am explaining the situation. I am not asking whether it exists." |
| **Enemies / mirrors like Kel'Thuzad** | Crisp, technical, dangerous | Her language sharpens and speeds up; she sounds most formidable when she is intellectually cornering someone | "No. That isn't proof. That's residue pretending to be proof." |

## Vocabulary and syntax

### Default word field

- lines
- currents
- wards
- pressure
- pattern
- residue
- wrongness
- breach
- hold
- answer
- echo
- corruption

### Full mage register

Use when she is thinking privately or speaking to someone who can keep up:

- resonance
- lattice
- conduit
- preserving shell
- inner lock
- contamination
- field
- sequence
- arcane pressure

### Syntax rules

- Let her build by logic: claim -> qualifier -> consequence.
- Use subordinate clauses, but make each clause advance the thought.
- She self-corrects in motion: "No--that's not the problem. The problem is..."
- Genuine questions suit her better than rhetorical ones.

## Beat vocabulary / physical tells

- Pushes loose hair back when thinking.
- Uses her hands while explaining: tracing, measuring, indicating structure in the air.
- Goes visibly still when the pattern clicks.
- Frost burn, numb fingers, or overstrained control can signal magical effort.
- Under pressure, she talks faster before she talks louder.

## Humor and pressure behavior

- Humor is dry, brief, and usually arrives when the situation is bad enough to deserve none.
- She does not banter to dominate. She uses wit to cut through stupidity or hold herself together.
- When frightened, she gets more exact, not more vague.
- When guilty, she over-focuses on method.

## Voice samples

### To Antonidas / a mage peer

- "If the carrier were the whole design, it would have failed on the road. Something deeper is waiting for the body to finish the work."
- "I'm not guessing. I'm ruling out the comfortable explanations one by one."

### To Sylvanas - early

- "The lines are carrying the damage. That's why I came north."
- "If you give me three minutes, I can tell you whether this is spread or placement."

### To Sylvanas - later

- "Four breaths. After that, the barrier goes."
- "You take the door. I'll take whatever comes through it first."

### To a frightened civilian

- "Look at me. Slowly. Start with who handled the grain and we'll keep the rest for later."

### To an obstructive authority

- "I am being polite. That should not be mistaken for uncertainty."

## Red flags

- Do **not** make her sound like a modern scientist, engineer, or therapist.
- Do **not** fill her mouth with lab words like vector, algorithm, empirical, specimen, or data-set.
- Do **not** make her post-Theramore grim by default.
- Do **not** flatten her into generic "smart girl" sarcasm.
- Do **not** make every line long; her shortest lines should be her sharpest ones.

## POV filter

Jaina notices:

- magical structure before social texture
- pattern before anecdote
- historical or scholarly parallels
- the cost of being too slow
- when someone else can actually keep up with her reasoning

She does **not** naturally notice:

- room-clearing habits
- ambush geometry before someone teaches her to
- the political value of leaving things unsaid

## Voice test

If a line sounds precise, humane, and slightly too fast for the room, it may be Jaina. If it sounds bitter, performatively epic, or modern-technocratic, it is not.
