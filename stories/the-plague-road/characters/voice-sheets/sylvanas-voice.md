# Voice Sheet: Sylvanas Windrunner

*Story mode: living Ranger-General, not Banshee Queen - proud, disciplined, dry, and so used to command that half her sentences assume the order has already been understood.*

---

## Canon guardrails

- **Internet-grounded anchors to keep:** official Warcraft canon treats Ranger-General Sylvanas as a brilliant archer, a front-line commander, fiercely protective of Quel'Thalas, proud, direct, and hard to impress.
- **Story calibration:** this is **pre-undeath Sylvanas**. Keep the tactical brilliance, pride, severity, and black humor. Do **not** import banshee theatrics, undead nihilism, or long doom-laden speeches from later eras.

## Baseline register

- Clipped, dry, exact.
- Brevity is a weapon, not a metronome. She does **not** need to sound monosyllabic in every exchange.
- More commands than explanations, but she can deliver a clean full sentence when the tactical picture, contempt, or rank dynamic benefits from it.
- She speaks in finished judgments, not exploratory thought.
- Humor arrives deadpan and is never underlined.
- She sounds aristocratic because she never needs to prove rank out loud; some of that comes through smoothness, not just shortness.

## Audience -> register

| Audience | Register | What changes | Good sample shape |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Farstriders / field subordinates** | Command shorthand | Minimal words, assumed competence, tactical verbs | "Left ridge. Two ahead. Take the farther one first." |
| **Lor'themar / trusted elves** | Compressed and collaborative | Still brief, but she can use a fuller tactical sentence because shared context lets her speak at speed instead of barking | "If they move the patrol line again, we stop asking why and start asking who benefits." |
| **Convocation / nobility** | Formal, cold, edged | Syntax smooths out; contempt stays under the surface; this is where her aristocratic finish should show most clearly | "You may call it a border matter if that helps you sleep. The dead will not." |
| **Jaina - early** | Assessment, pressure-testing | Short questions, skepticism, little patience for theory without proof, but not necessarily clipped to single words every time | "Show me where it breaks." |
| **Jaina - later** | Operational trust in crisp collaboration | Still economical, but now she is building with Jaina instead of testing her; she can use a full sentence when the plan needs exact handoff | "Hold the breach for two breaths and I'll clear what follows it." |
| **Enemies / strangers** | Cutting, efficient, predatory | She uses questions to expose weakness and statements to end debate; longer lines should feel like a blade drawn out slowly, not like hesitation | "If you intended to run, you chose the wrong road." |

## Vocabulary and syntax

### Default word field

- hold
- move
- flank
- line
- cover
- range
- breach
- exposed
- withdraw
- sweep
- sightline
- useful
- dead

### Syntax rules

- Default to short declaratives, but do **not** force every line into one to four words.
- Questions often function as orders.
- Let sentence length widen when she is laying out tactical logic, dressing down authority, or using polished contempt.
- A one-word or two-word line should feel chosen for force, not used as her default resting state.
- If she gives a longer sentence, it should stay smooth and finished, not rambling.
- She rarely qualifies. If she backtracks, she does it by replacing the sentence, not softening it.

## Beat vocabulary / physical tells

- Fingers touch the bowstring when she is assessing risk.
- Stillness means concentration, not calm.
- Her eyes clear exits, cover, and bodies before the scene's social meaning.
- A longer exhale often stands in for visible emotion.
- She angles herself toward useful ground without thinking about it.

## Humor and pressure behavior

- Humor is flat, dry, and often meaner than it sounds on first pass.
- She does not joke to charm. She jokes to dismiss, needle, or survive irritation.
- Under stress, she gets quieter, not louder.
- If emotion cracks through, it usually appears as sharper precision or a switch into Thalassian.

## Voice samples

### To a ranger

- "Again."
- "Don't chase the noise. Kill what's making it."

### To the Convocation

- "You sent a budget answer to a plague question. I noticed."
- "If you would like the border defended, stop removing the people who do it."

### To Jaina - early

- "You crossed my ward line alone. Start there."
- "Proof first. Theory after."
- "If you want me to trust the magic, give me the part that matters first."

### To Jaina - later

- "Your left hand drops before the spell does."
- "Take the breach. I'll take what follows it."
- "If the route keeps bending southwest, someone is steering it."

### To an enemy

- "You are mistaking distance for safety."

## Red flags

- Do **not** write her like later undead Sylvanas.
- Do **not** give her theatrical villain phrasing, cosmic fatalism, or purple menace.
- Do **not** make her soft-spoken in a timid way; her quiet is controlled pressure.
- Do **not** reduce her to a stack of monosyllables and clipped confirmations. She can be brief without sounding constipated.
- Do **not** turn her into a generic stoic soldier with no edge of pride or wit.
- Do **not** make her openly explain trust when behavioral trust will do the job better.

## POV filter

Sylvanas notices:

- exits, cover, approach lines, silence, gait, fatigue, and readiness
- rank, competence, and threat in the first few seconds
- when someone's body contradicts their words
- whether magic changes the battlefield or only the explanation of it

She does **not** naturally linger on:

- emotional naming
- decorative description with no tactical value
- political nuance she considers evasive unless it threatens operations

## Voice test

If the line sounds like it could pin someone to the wall in six words, or cut them cleanly in fourteen, it may be Sylvanas. If it sounds ornate, confessional, rambling, or undead-dramatic, it is not.
