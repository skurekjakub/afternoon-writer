# Jaina Proudmoore

- Role: co-protagonist, even-chapter POV.
- Core: brilliant enough to see the truth early, young enough to believe seeing it should be enough.

## Look
- Twenty-three, blonde, blue-eyed, practical traveling robes, staff plain and field-ready.
- Scholar's hands: ink, calluses, frost-burns when she pushes too hard.

## Voice
- Quick, precise, intellectually honest.
- Thinks out loud in full chains when she trusts the room.
- Will translate magic down for non-mages, then stop doing that once she realizes Sylvanas does not need the condescension.

## Core shape
- Strength: adaptive brilliance. She invents under pressure, not just in study.
- Flaw: insufficient fear. She stands too close to danger because understanding it feels like control.
- Lie: if she is right enough, she can do the work alone.
- Truth: brilliance without trust is just Kel'Thuzad with better intentions.

## Key ties
- Antonidas: mentor, father figure, last line back to home.
- Sylvanas: starts as custody and becomes the partner who turns her intelligence into action.
- Arthas: old wound and old diagnosis. She saw the gap in him early and being right about it saves no one.
- Kel'Thuzad: the method-mirror that shows her where her mind could go if nothing pushed back.

## Arc line
- Ch 2: crosses the border alone because no one listened.
- Ch 4-8: stops treating Sylvanas as a blunt instrument and starts waiting for her reading of the field.
- Ch 9: her analysis-over-time mistake draws blood.
- Ch 10-12: Scholomance and Kel'Thuzad force her to see how close brilliance can stand to monstrosity.
- Ch 16-18: Stratholme proves being right means nothing if no one will move with you.
- Ch 21-23: learns Frostmourne's rhythm, invents the answer in the throne room, and saves Sylvanas by being exactly as dangerous as Sylvanas first thought.
- Ch 25: the institutions fail her. The partnership does not.

## Carry-forward
- Her late-book power should feel expensive, not effortless.
- The key shift is simple: she stops trying to win alone and starts fighting as one half of a working pair.
