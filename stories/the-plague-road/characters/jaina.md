# Jaina Proudmoore — Character Profile

**Role:** Co-protagonist / POV character (even chapters)
**Species:** Human (she/her)
**Title:** Protégée of Archmage Antonidas (operating without Kirin Tor authorization)
**Age:** Early twenties (23)
**Status in this story:** Pre-Theramore, pre-Arthas's fall. She sensed necromantic wrongness in the ambient leylines on a northbound courier road, followed it north without Kirin Tor backing or authorization, and crossed into elven territory alone when the Thalassian ward-stones began carrying the same damage. The Kirin Tor doesn't know she's here. Nobody believed her readings. She came alone because nobody would come with her.

---

## Appearance

Average height for a human woman — five-seven, five-eight. Blonde hair, golden, usually tied back in a practical braid that comes loose when she's working. Blue eyes with a quality of focus that borders on unsettling — the look of someone who sees the underlying structure of things. Her face is expressive in spite of herself: she tries for academic neutrality and achieves it except when she's working, when her concentration breaks through in furrowed brows, bitten lips, muttered incantations. Her hands are her most distinctive feature — callused from staff-work, ink-stained from notation, and occasionally frost-burned from her own ice magic when she pushes too hard. She dresses practically: travelling robes adapted for fieldwork, leather boots, a belt of reagent pouches. Her staff is unornamented — a length of crystallized arcane focus wood that hums faintly when she's concentrating.

---

## Voice and Register

Precise, rapid, intellectually honest. Jaina thinks out loud — not chattering, but processing, her words mapping her analytical chain in real time. She speaks to colleagues as equals regardless of rank. She speaks to authority with the politeness of someone who learned young that you don't need to be rude to be immovable. She speaks to people in distress with a gentleness that surprises anyone who's only seen the analytical machine.

**Dialogue patterns:**
- Longer sentences than Sylvanas, with subordinate clauses — she builds complex thoughts in real time.
- Technical precision when discussing magic, but she can translate down fast when the audience is not magical: lines, currents, wards, wrongness, pressure, pattern. With trained mages she can climb back into resonance, corruption, lattice, and conduit talk. She should not sound like a modern scientist or engineer.
- Questions that are genuine, not rhetorical. She asks because she wants to know.
- Moments of stark honesty that cut through evasion: she'll say the thing everyone is avoiding, not from cruelty but from the inability to function around unacknowledged facts.
- Occasional dark humor that arrives unexpectedly — the laugh of someone who's young enough to be surprised by how bad things can get.

**She does NOT:**
- Defer to authority she hasn't evaluated.
- Soften conclusions to make them palatable.
- Apologize for being the smartest person in the room. She doesn't announce it either — she simply operates at her level and lets people catch up.

---

## Personality

- **Core trait:** Intellectual honesty. Jaina follows the evidence wherever it leads, including places she doesn't want to go. This is her greatest strength and her deepest source of pain — she can't un-know things, can't unsee patterns, can't pretend the math isn't what it is.
- **Strength:** Adaptive brilliance. She doesn't just know magic — she invents it on the fly, recombining principles under pressure in ways that aren't in the textbooks because she's the one who'll write them.
- **Flaw:** Insufficient fear. She walks into danger because the data is there, because the analysis requires proximity, because understanding the threat means standing close enough to touch it. This isn't bravery — it's the researcher's blindness, the assumption that comprehension equals safety.
- **Fear:** Being too slow. Not intellectually — operationally. She can see what's coming (the plague, Arthas's descent, the political paralysis of the Kirin Tor), but seeing it and stopping it are different things, and the gap between them is where people die.

## Key Relationships (in this story)

**Sylvanas Windrunner:** Begins as the escort — a blunt instrument, a soldier assigned by politics. Jaina addresses her in simplified register (shorter words, fewer clauses) the way academics address non-specialists. The shift: Sylvanas's combat in Millhaven forces Jaina to see the intelligence behind the violence. The grid-formation convergence (both reaching the same conclusion through different methods) cracks the intellectual snobbery. By Andorhal, Jaina delivers conclusions at full complexity and waits for tactical implications — a collaborative pause replacing a pedagogical one. After Jaina's mistake (the Cult ambush) costs Sylvanas a wound, Jaina starts asking "how long?" before committing to analysis — acknowledging that time is a tactical resource. The throne room is Jaina's apex: she invents a three-school spell in two seconds to penetrate Frostmourne's field and save a downed Sylvanas, proving she's not just a researcher who tags along with soldiers but a combat mage who can stand beside centuries of martial experience and contribute equally. See `plans/partnership-arc.md` for full phase breakdown.

**Antonidas:** Her mentor, her father figure, the man who saw what she could be before she knew herself. Their relationship is complicated by the fact that Jaina has outgrown his ability to teach her and neither of them has acknowledged it. His caution frustrates her. His political awareness is something she lacks and needs. His voice on the sending stone is the last thread connecting her to a world that makes sense.

**Arthas Menethil:** They had a brief romantic relationship when Jaina was 18-19 — genuine, roughly six to eight months. Jaina ended it when she saw the gap between his charm and his judgment. The breakup's political shrapnel (Terenas wanted a Lordaeron-Kul Tiras alliance, Daelin applied pressure) is still falling. Arthas carries suppressed bitterness hidden under courtesy. Jaina doesn't hate him — she saw the gap years ago, she's right about it, and being right changes nothing. When he reappears at Strahnbrad (Ch 6) chasing the wrong threat, the personal history makes the dismissal cut deeper. When he descends through Stratholme and Northrend, she watches the person she warned everyone about do exactly what she predicted — and the gap between charm and judgment becomes literal in Frostmourne's glow.

## Arc in This Story

**The Lie:** "Being right is enough — I can do this alone because I'm the one who sees clearly." She left Dalaran because nobody believed her leyline readings. She crossed the border alone. Her intelligence IS her authority, and if the proof is strong enough, people will listen. She doesn't need institutional backing, doesn't need partners, doesn't need permission — she needs data.

**The Truth:** "Intelligence without trust is just Kel'Thuzad with better intentions." Being right means nothing alone. The thing that separates Jaina from Kel'Thuzad isn't brilliance, conviction, or methodology — they share all three. It's that Jaina found someone who said "Show me" before the proof was complete. A partner who challenges her, covers her blind spots, forces her to operate within operational reality rather than pure analysis. Without Sylvanas, Jaina's trajectory is Kel'Thuzad's: brilliant conviction spiraling inward without friction until it crosses a line it can't see.

**Want:** Prove the plague is real and stop it. Map it, understand the mechanism, develop countermeasures. The scientist's impulse: comprehend before acting.

**Need:** Learn that intelligence without trust is powerless — that the partnership (someone who acts on your analysis before it's proven) is what separates her from Kel'Thuzad.

**The three-way mirror:**
- Kel'Thuzad: brilliant conviction → acted alone (expelled by the Kirin Tor, enabled by the Lich King) → crossed ethical lines → genocide
- Arthas: brilliant conviction → had partners (Uther, Jaina) → lost them all (Jaina left him years ago, Uther bailed at Stratholme when the moral framework broke) → alone, his certainty escalated without friction → Stratholme → Northrend → Frostmourne → patricide. His journey repeats Kel'Thuzad's origin: the same arc of conviction without counterweight.
- Jaina: brilliant conviction → started alone → found Sylvanas → the partnership is the difference. Every time her Lie would have led her deeper into Kel'Thuzad's pattern, the partnership absorbs the cost.

**Arc progression:**
1. Ch 2: "Show me" — the moment that separates her path from Kel'Thuzad's, but she doesn't know it yet. She reads it as Sylvanas being pragmatic, not as trust. The interrogation → custody → Millhaven investigation. Her leyline analysis and Sylvanas's tactical read converge independently.
2. Ch 4-6: Expanding the investigation, register-shifting to "simplify" for the soldier → the Strahnbrad encounter with Arthas (personal history, the dismissal that cuts, being right about him changes nothing) → first crack in intellectual snobbery as Sylvanas's military intelligence asks questions she didn't think to ask.
3. Ch 7-8: Antonidas's institution delivers useful intel — a crack in "I don't need them." The Lie wobbles. Then Kel'Thuzad's name surfaces and the mirror begins: Antonidas's colleague, the one who also crossed institutional lines.
4. Ch 9-10: Her mistake — insisting on analysis during a Cult ambush, prioritizing data over operational time. The cost is paid by Sylvanas's blood. The Lie in action. Then the physical mirror: Kel'Thuzad's research notes look like her handwriting. Same notational system, same margin habits.
5. Ch 12: Kel'Thuzad dies with scholarly calm. The mirror breaks, the reflection stays. Jaina can never fully condemn his method without condemning her own.
6. Ch 14: She chooses Sylvanas over every institutional alternative — the Truth arriving before she can name it. Not "I'm staying because I'm right" but "I'm staying because we're right together."
7. Ch 16: Stratholme — being right about Arthas changes nothing because nobody listened. The Lie's ultimate failure. Sylvanas anchors her: "South. We go south."
8. Ch 18: Defies Antonidas to go to Northrend. The parallel with Arthas's defiance is structural — same action, opposite consequences. Her defiance preserves the investigation; his defiance destroys his army.
9. Ch 22: The throne room. The kill works ONLY because Sylvanas is there for the shot. Intelligence (three-school spell) + trust (Sylvanas rising) = victory. Intelligence alone = Kel'Thuzad. Conviction alone = Arthas. All three personal threads converge: she kills a man her mentor's institution created, using methodology that mirrors what she condemned in Kel'Thuzad, against the person who embodies the foil she's been running from.
10. Ch 24: The institutions won't save her. Only the partnership survives. Through the wall, the Truth: they stopped him because they were right *together*.
