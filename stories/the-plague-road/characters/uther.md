# Uther the Lightbringer

- Role: Arthas's mentor, the Silver Hand's moral center, tragic witness.
- Core: a father-figure who sees the trajectory and still cannot stop it.

## Look
- Broad, scarred, weathered, older knight's body inside paladin authority.
- The hands matter: huge, calloused, built equally for healing and killing.

## Voice
- Grave, deliberate, chosen words.
- Short when commanding, longer only when the moral ground is slipping under him.
- The shift from "Prince Arthas" to "Arthas" to "boy" tracks the break.

## What matters
- First paladin, decades of service, faith proven in practice rather than theology.
- He trained Arthas well enough to make the fall hurt more.
- He saw the prince's speed problem long before Stratholme, but hope kept him in the teacher's role too long.

## Jaina line
- Uther understands why she left Arthas better than either of them can admit.
- He sees the Sylvanas/Jaina partnership and instantly recognizes the kind of corrective friction Arthas no longer has.

## Chapter use
- Ch 15: watching and afraid. The Light still answers Arthas, which is its own horror.
- Ch 17: Stratholme break. "Boy" is not insult; it is paternal anguish stripped raw.
- Ch 25: tells the truth at the tribunal and is dismissed anyway.

## Writer rules
- He does not lose faith. He runs headfirst into its limits.
- Keep him solid, honest, and plain. The tragedy comes from how little room there is between truth and useless truth.
