# Arthas Menethil

- Role: tragic antagonist.
- Core: a hero-shaped man destroyed by certainty.
- Never POV. Read him from the outside through behavior, voice, and what other people lose around him.

## Look
- Mid-twenties, golden, broad-shouldered, princely, made to be trusted on sight.
- Silver Hand plate, Menethil crest, Light's Vengeance in hand until he throws it away for Frostmourne.
- He looks like a hero the whole way down.

## Voice line
- Strahnbrad: warm, confident, charismatic.
- Hearthglen: formal and tightening.
- Stratholme: clipped, declarative, no room left for doubt.
- Northrend: mechanical, stripped to orders.
- Throne room: almost silent. "Succeeding you, Father" and then the blade speaks for him.

## Core shape
- Strength: courage, charisma, real care for his people at the start.
- Flaw: he experiences uncertainty as failure and reaches for the first strong answer hard enough to make it feel righteous.
- He does not fall because he is stupid. He falls because true facts hit a mind that cannot bear not to act.

## Key ties
- Uther taught him conscience and command.
- Muradin taught him to fight and warns him off Frostmourne.
- Jaina saw the gap in him years before the story and left because of it.
- Sylvanas reads him as a military asset whose tempo keeps turning more dangerous.

## Story beats
- Ch 6: golden prince chasing the wrong war.
- Ch 15: absorbs weeks of plague work in hours and turns it into acceleration.
- Ch 17: Stratholme proves the math can be sound and the act still monstrous.
- Ch 19-21: burned fleet, Frostmourne, abandonment of Muradin.
- Ch 23: first arrow should kill him and does not.
- Ch 23 end: Jaina finds the seam, Sylvanas lands the second shot, and the prince dies too late to save anyone who matters.

## Writer rules
- Do not soften him into pure victim or flatten him into a cartoon tyrant.
- The horror is that part of his reasoning is always understandable right up until the point it becomes unforgivable.
