# Scholomance & Cult of the Damned — Location Reference

## Caer Darrow Island

**Role in story:** Chapter 5 (Kel'Thuzad's sanctum). The converted estate near Scholomance where Sylvanas and Jaina confront the necromancer.

### History
Caer Darrow is an island fortress in Darrowmere Lake (the Eastweald). Built on the site of a high elven runestone — a detail Sylvanas might notice, a faint echo of Thalassian magic perverted. The runestone was shattered during the Second War when the Horde stormed the island; fragments were used by orc warlocks to create the Altars of Storms.

After the Second War, the noble Barov family restored Caer Darrow. In exchange for promised immortality, they gave their fortress to Kel'Thuzad. He used the crypts beneath the keep to establish Scholomance — a secret necromantic academy.

### Layout
- **Surface:** Ruined village structures. A keep, outbuildings, what was once a functioning settlement. By the time of the story, the surface appears abandoned — empty buildings, overgrown paths. The population is dead, their spirits trapped.
- **Beneath:** The Scholomance proper. Connected crypts, lecture halls (necromantic instruction), laboratories, and the Barov family vault. The air smells of old stone, preservative chemicals, and something else — the sweet-rot stink of undeath.
- **Approach:** By boat across Darrowmere Lake. The island sits in mist. The water around it is wrong — too still, too dark, and Jaina can detect the necromantic saturation from the shoreline.

### Sensory Anchors
- The mist on the lake. Cold, clinging, with a chemical taint.
- The silence of the island — no birds, no insects, no wind in the grass. A silence that's active, as if something is listening.
- Underground: torch-lit stone corridors. The echo of footsteps on ancient flagstone. The distant sound of chanting (Cult acolytes). The smell of embalming compounds and old blood.
- Kel'Thuzad's study: books, scrolls, alchemical apparatus. Clean, organized, the workspace of a meticulous researcher. The horror is in the neatness — this is not a madman's lair, it's a university office.

## Dalaran

**Role in story:** Referenced throughout as Jaina's home institution. Antonidas communicates via sending stone from the Violet Citadel. The Kirin Tor's institutional inertia is a recurring frustration.

### Pre-Destruction Layout
Located near Lordamere Lake in the Eastern Kingdoms. A city of towering spires and glittering streets, radiating ambient magical energy. Protected by formidable ward-systems — both mundane and arcane. The streets are clean, orderly, with magical lanterns and enchanted decorations.

### The Violet Citadel
The seat of the Kirin Tor, towering above the city center. Roofed with thick lapis-lazuli-colored slate, flanked by cone-shaped towers. Inside: vast assembly halls, the Grand Library, research laboratories, private quarters for the Council of Six. Deep beneath: the Arcane Vault, housing the most dangerous magical artifacts.

At its pinnacle: a magical focus rumored to be an arcane weapon capable of repelling great threats. The Violet Hold (the city's magical prison) is separate but linked to the Citadel's security systems.

### The Kirin Tor
Governed by the Council of Six. A meritocratic hierarchy of arcane practitioners — human and high elven. Dalaran's politics are internal, factional, and agonizingly slow. The institution values consensus, caution, and rigorous verification. These are virtues in peacetime and fatal flaws in crisis. Antonidas's frustration in Chapters 7-12 is that he BELIEVES Jaina but cannot move the Council fast enough to act on her intelligence.

### Atmosphere
Dalaran is intellectual, cosmopolitan, slightly decadent. Mages from across Azeroth study here. The air smells of ink, reagents, and ozone. Conversations in doorways run to arcane theory. It's the only human city where a high elf might feel genuinely welcome — the shared pursuit of magical knowledge bridges the cultural divide.

### Sensory Anchors (for sending-stone scenes)
- Jaina hears Dalaran through the stone: the hum of wards, the murmur of scholarly argument, Antonidas's measured voice against the backdrop of the Citadel's ambient resonance.
- The quality of the connection degrades with distance and interference — by Northrend, the stone barely functions, and Antonidas's voice comes through fragmented, layered with static that sounds like whispering.
