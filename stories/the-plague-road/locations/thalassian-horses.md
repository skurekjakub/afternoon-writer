# Thalassian Horses — Quick Notes

- Fine-boned, high-headed, bred for endurance, responsiveness, and disciplined road work rather than brute charge weight.
- Keep coat colors believable: white, grey, chestnut, bay, black. Favor striking lines without making every horse magical.

## Tack
- Light, fitted, quiet, well-kept.
- Good leather, minimal jangle, gear that reads expensive because it is maintained, not because it is showy.

## Training and feel
- These mounts know ward-stones, arcane hum, patrol rhythm, and sudden terrain changes.
- They should settle fast under a trained rider and tolerate the magical background as routine.

## Useful cues
- Hoofbeats under canopy.
- Quiet leather creak.
- Hot breath, foam, snort after a hard push.
- Reins tightening at a check.
- A sidestep near ward-stone vibration, then calm because the animal has done this before.
