# Thalassian Horses — Mount Reference

**Role in story:** Border patrol, escort travel, dispatch riding, and the early southbound investigation arc.

## Canon-first summary

If the chapter needs a mount image at first glance, use **horses**, not hawkstriders. That is the safest fit for high-elf / pre-Third-War Quel'Thalas framing.

Older Warcraft lore and lore summaries keep a horse baseline around Quel'Thalas, while later blood-elf visual identity leans hard toward hawkstriders. Unicorn or horned-horse imagery does exist around high-elf symbolism and prestige mounts, but it reads better as a rarer, more ceremonial or elite-adjacent variant than as the default Farstrider patrol animal. For this first pass, "Thalassian horse" is the correct everyday shorthand.

## Default look

- Elegant rather than bulky: fine-boned, high-headed, bred for endurance and responsiveness more than brute draft strength.
- Clean, controlled movement. These are patrol and road horses, not tournament chargers.
- Coat colors should stay believable at a glance: white, grey, chestnut, black, bay. If the prose wants an especially elven silhouette, favor pale or striking coats without making every horse feel magical.
- They should read as carefully bred and expensive, but still field-used: sweat, mud at the fetlocks, road dust on the lower legs, foam after a hard ride.

## Tack and handling

- Tack should feel lighter and more refined than heavy human war-gear.
- Think fitted saddles, good leather, quiet buckles, disciplined kit, nothing jangling unless a scene specifically wants noise.
- Farstrider mounts are trained around ward-stones, arcane hum, and fast changes in terrain; they should read steady under magic and quick to settle when a trained rider cues them.

## Sound and motion cues

- Hoofbeats under forest canopy
- Leather creak kept to a minimum
- Snort, foam, hot breath after a push
- Reins tightening at a sudden stop
- A horse sidestepping ward-stone vibration, then settling because it knows the routine

## On horned / unicorn-like variants

High-elf culture does have a unicorn / horned-horse association in lore-adjacent material and symbolic imagery. If a scene near Silvermoon or among nobility wants that silhouette, a horned or unicorn-bred Thalassian steed is fine. But it should read as a distinguished variant, not the automatic default for every ranger on the road.

For the working chapter plans:

- **Default:** say horse, mare, gelding, patrol mount, field horse.
- **Optional flourish:** mention a horn only when the moment benefits from the image.
- **Avoid:** making the entire escort read like a stable of fantasy unicorns unless the chapter is explicitly staging ceremony, status, or mythic pageantry.

## Prose shorthand

When in doubt, use:

- "Thalassian field horse"
- "Farstrider patrol horse"
- "Silvermoon-bred horse"
- "mounted escort"
