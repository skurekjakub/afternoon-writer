# Silvermoon and Quel'Thalas — Location Notes

- Use this for Sylvanas's home ground and for the political world Lor'themar is fighting to move.
- The place should feel old, finished, and very sure of itself.

## Silvermoon
- Golden spires, white stone, curved lines, controlled water, ambient magical hum.
- Nothing looks temporary. That permanence is part of the problem.
- Key feel: clean, radiant, self-maintaining, almost eerily quiet by human standards.

### Districts that matter
- Sunfury Spire: power, Convocation chambers, formal decisions Sylvanas hates having to hear in person.
- Court of the Sun: ceremony, arrivals, speeches, civic display.
- Bazaar: living commercial center, color and movement without human grime.
- Walk of Elders: grand approach built to remind arrivals who built this place and how long they expect it to last.

## Eversong Woods
- Golden canopy, curated forest, old water, Farstrider routes hidden inside something that looks untouched.
- Before the Scourge this land is whole. No Dead Scar yet.
- Windrunner ground should stay mostly geographic in Sylvanas's POV: seen, not indulged.

## Windrunner sites
- Windrunner Spire: family estate and history container, best used as a place Sylvanas passes without stopping.
- Windrunner Village: rebuilt home under older grief.
- Farstrider retreats: practical, elevated, supply-light, known by heart to Sylvanas.

## Thalassian Pass
- The real edge of home.
- Border hum fading into human air is the main transition cue.
- Ward-stones matter physically: warmth, bass vibration, old runes, a defense grid horses and riders already know how to live around.
- Jaina at the stone in Ch 1 should feel like an intrusion into a living border system, not just a person standing near a monument.

## The Convocation
- Not villainous. Slow, certain, and protected by the fact that much of what it believes is narrowly true.
- Main split is not hawk vs dove but whether the wards answering the narrow question should decide the larger one.

### Named seats that matter
- Vandellor: brilliant ward-mind, defends his life's work by mistaking border security for full strategic safety.
- Dawnstar: question-asker and hinge vote, useful because she can be moved but only by evidence that always arrives too late.
- Sunsorrow: precedent and cultural continuity, never likely to break.

## Writer rule
- We do not cut to Silvermoon for full scenes. The place is mostly felt through Sylvanas's memory, Lor'themar's messages, and Vereesa's reports.
