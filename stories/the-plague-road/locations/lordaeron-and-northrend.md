# Lordaeron and Northrend — Location Notes

- Use this as the fast geography sheet for the human-side locations and the Northrend run.
- Lordaeron is still whole, fed, and prosperous on the surface. The horror is that the ordinary life is real right up until it isn't.

## Lordaeron at a glance
- Northern Eastern Kingdoms: fertile lowlands, wet temperate climate, heavy grain economy.
- Main roads tie Capital City, Andorhal, Hillsbrad, Alterac, and the northern settlements together.
- The grain network matters more than any single city wall. It is how the plague travels.

## Border country
- Human land just south of Quel'Thalas: mud, grey-green woods, livestock, woodsmoke, autumn harvest.
- The contrast with Eversong should be immediate: rougher, poorer, less enchanted, more exposed.
- Millhaven is the first true alarm-bell village: crossroads, mill, tavern, a few dozen lives, doors open and people gone.

## Andorhal
- Agricultural nerve center, not a glamorous city.
- Built around the granaries, loading yards, manifests, wagons, and clerks that feed half the kingdom.
- Key feel: grain dust everywhere, warm dry sweetness, constant labor noise, practical low walls.
- Cult insertion point: contamination added after harvest inside the distribution system, which keeps farms innocent and the downstream damage enormous.
- Jaina should feel the wrongness in the grain itself; Sylvanas should read the logistics and embedded human network.

## Hearthglen
- Fortified town, military logic, walls and gates that make sense to soldiers.
- Important mostly as offstage proof that the plague can turn defenders from inside the defense.
- More disciplined and martial than the early villages or Andorhal.

## Stratholme
- Grand religious city and civic showpiece. If Capital City governs, Stratholme believes.
- Before the fall: bakeries, bells, crowds, flower baskets, festival dressing, confidence.
- The contaminated bread reaches the city through ordinary trade. Nothing is smuggled in. The city buys its own destruction.

### Districts that matter
- King's Square: civic center, magistrates, notices, the city's public face.
- Market Row: grain merchants, bakeries, daily commerce, the real plague artery.
- Crusaders' Square and Alonsus Chapel: Silver Hand ground, faith-center turned staging area for the break.
- Residential rings: inner merchants, middle trades, outer working districts and warehouses.

### Gates and flow
- Main southern gate: formal military approach.
- Northern service gate: goods, staff, bulk movement, later the cleaner way in for S&J.
- Segmented internal defenses help a city under siege and also help purge teams move district by district.

### In this story
- S&J do not witness the opening choice. They arrive too late and watch the last part from above after slipping in through the service side.
- The city should feel both vast and legible from the wall.
- During and after: controlled fires, cleanup-silence, burning grain, city-scale contamination, Arthas emerging with the wrong stillness in him.

## Southshore
- Working coastal town and evidence waypoint.
- Useful for manifests, harbor records, and merchant traffic on the grain trail.
- Important negative note: this is not the Northrend departure point.

## Seastone Harbor
- Small northern fishing harbor tucked under coastal cliffs.
- Exists for fish, not glory. Too minor to have been stripped the way the major ports were.
- The last Farstrider relay point. After this, the network ends.
- The *Wolfheart* and Dagny matter because they are the viable way north.
- Key feel: switchback descent, tar, kelp, salt, wet stone, nets, barnacles, the last human scale before the sea takes over.

## Capital City
- Grand, pale-stone, unworried capital still half-living in parade mood when S&J return.
- Wide boulevards, cathedral bells, return banners, commemorative junk in the markets.
- Palace on the highest ground, visibly built to project permanence.

### Palace spaces that matter
- Imperial Hall: formal audience space and political waiting room.
- Royal quarters and gardens: humanize the palace without making it soft.
- Armory and crypts: remind the reader this is both dynasty and fortress.
- Evidence vault below the tribunal wing: Frostmourne goes here and starts freezing it almost immediately.

### Service passages
- Narrow, dim, logistical guts of the palace.
- Not secret, just beneath notice.
- Crucial because they bypass the ceremonial routes and let S&J reach the throne room from behind the visual story everyone else will later believe.

## Imperial Chamber
- Ceremony room first, nightmare battlefield second.
- Long white marble floor, gold seal inlaid at center, raised throne platform, octagonal window, six columns to a side, galleries too far away to matter.
- Service doors flank the throne end behind curtains.
- Bronze main doors at the far end create the fatal tableau when the guards arrive.
- Acoustics matter: every step, strike, crown-fall, and blade-ring announces itself.
- Aftermath image should be clean and damning: blood on white stone, frost racing the seams, two dead royals, two foreign women, one active cursed sword.

## Northrend
- Not a destination but a condition.
- The Scourge is ambient here: in the wind, in the silence, in the pressure of the cold.
- Grey light, early dark, almost no ordinary life-sound. The place itself feels like the Lich King's body.

## Daggercap Bay
- Northrend landing point and the last exit point that still exists.
- Arthas's temporary harbor, abandoned camp, and burned ships are the first hard proof that he already values the objective over the army.
- The *Wolfheart* waiting here matters morally as much as practically: S&J keep a retreat Arthas destroyed.
- Key feel: charred hull ribs frozen into ice, fuel-smell ghosting in the cold, empty military footprint.

## Dragonblight
- White waste, dragon bones, corpse trail, relentless wind.
- The bones should make the human scale feel tiny and recent.
- Arthas's march reads in abandoned gear, thinning supplies, and soldiers left where they fell.
- This is where the pursuit turns into reading his collapse one mile at a time.

## Muradin's camp
- Dwarven ice fort held together by skill, stubbornness, and forge heat.
- Archaeology and siege coexist in the same cramped space: notes, rubbings, weapons, bandages, hot metal, bad ale.
- Muradin and Baelgun make this the only friendly foothold on the continent.
- It matters because it gives S&J intelligence, warning, and one brief place to breathe before the cave.

## Frostmourne cavern
- Glacier entrance marked by one terrible image: Arthas's tracks going in and Light's Vengeance left in the snow outside.
- Descending ice-over-stone passages into a chamber built for warning and temptation at once.
- The altar inscription is explicit about the price. It warns, but it does not stop anyone.
- Frostmourne's field should read as pressure, cold, and wrongness. Jaina recognizes it as kin to the plague.
- Arthas's selected men already look half-lost by the time S&J reach the chamber.
- This is the point of no return: the cave takes a prince and gives back a death knight.
