# The Kirin Tor Thread

- Mirror to the Silvermoon thread: Antonidas and Lor'themar are both right, both late, and both trapped inside institutions built to slow crises down.
- Use this as the Antonidas/Council state tracker, not as a second story.

## Arc I — Investigation
- Ch 2: first stone call. Antonidas believes Jaina, asks her home, and shields her absence as field work. The Council has not grasped the crisis.
- Ch 4-6: offstage, plague reports move through Dalaran at committee speed. Antonidas starts quiet consultations while the Council treats the whole thing as an academic problem.
- Ch 7: Antonidas finally delivers useful archive intelligence on Cult methods. The institution is slow, not worthless.
- Ch 8: Jaina names Kel'Thuzad. Antonidas loses his qualifiers. The plague now carries Kirin Tor fingerprints as well as Scourge ones.

## Arc II — Scholomance
- Offstage: the Council shocks, reviews, drafts, and delays.
- Ch 10: inside Scholomance, Jaina keeps finding Kirin Tor method turned inside out: notation, books, structure, handwriting, Jandice's citation habits.
- Ch 12: hardest call in the book. Jaina reports the Lich King, the Legion, and the real scale. Antonidas hears the full cost of the institution's failure.
- Ch 13-14: emergency posture still means "within the week." The field is always chapters ahead of the Council.

## Arc III — Arthas
- Ch 15-17: Antonidas wins monitoring and consultation, not intervention. Corroboration piles up while the Council keeps debating scope.
- Ch 16: Stratholme damages the cautious faction but does not make them fast.
- Ch 18: Antonidas checks in after Stratholme. He can offer extraction on paper, not a world Jaina can safely return to.

## Arc IV — Endgame
- Ch 19: last real call. Jaina tells Antonidas she is going north and not coming back. This is the emotional severance.
- Ch 21-23: Antonidas wins only a tiny advisory presence in Capital City. It is real help and nowhere near enough.
- Ch 24: he appears in person, asks for time, asks for expert review, and still cannot stop the verdict. This is his wall, same as Lor'themar's.
- Ch 25: he remains in the city as guilty institutional pressure, not rescue. Book 2 starts with him diminished by public impotence.

## Tone line
- Start: mentor's calm.
- Middle: stripped-down urgency.
- End: presence without power.
