# The Kirin Tor Thread — Institutional Subplot Beat Manifest

A consolidated reference tracking every Antonidas sending-stone call, Kirin Tor beat, and Council of Six reference across all 25 chapters. This document is the prose writer's single source for "what is the Kirin Tor doing at this point in the story, and what does Antonidas sound like when he calls."

**Key documents:**
- Character: [`characters/antonidas.md`](../characters/antonidas.md) — Antonidas's profile, voice, sending-stone deployment plan, the Lor'themar mirror
- Character: [`characters/kelthuzad.md`](../characters/kelthuzad.md) — the Kirin Tor's expelled genius, the fingerprints on the weapon
- Character: [`characters/jandice.md`](../characters/jandice.md) — cites Kirin Tor literature with rival precision; the Scholomance peer who studied what the Kirin Tor forbade
- Subplot: [`plans/silvermoon-thread.md`](silvermoon-thread.md) — the Convocation parallel (read both threads together to see the mirror)
- Arc planning: [`plans/story-arc.md`](story-arc.md) — top-level story overview and Jaina subplot summary

**The Parallel:** The Kirin Tor thread runs alongside the Silvermoon thread as a mirror. Lor'themar fights the Convocation for military resources. Antonidas fights the Council of Six for investigative authority. Both advocates present accurate intelligence to governing bodies that prioritize caution over speed. Both lose the race against the crisis and collide hardest at Ch 24, when their respective systems arrive at the same wall by different routes. Prose writers should cross-reference `silvermoon-thread.md` — when Lor'themar's humor thins (Ch 5-8), Antonidas's qualifiers strip (Ch 8). When Lor'themar spends favors (Ch 12), Antonidas spends political capital offscreen. The two threads should echo without characters naming the parallel.

## Arc I: Investigation (Chapters 1-8)

### Chapter 1 — No Kirin Tor Content

Sylvanas's POV. The investigation has not begun. The Kirin Tor is not yet relevant to the story's action — it is background: the body Jaina left without authorization because nobody believed her leyline readings. The departure is backstory, not dramatized.

**Council state:** Routine session. The plague reports from western Lordaeron have not reached the Council's agenda. The Kirin Tor processes intelligence through academic channels — peer review, cross-referencing, committee assessment — and the speed of that process is measured in weeks, not days.

### Chapter 2 — First Sending-Stone Call

**On-stone:** Antonidas contacts Jaina. The first sending-stone exchange.

**Tone:** Calm, authoritative, the mentor's cadence. He sent her to investigate; he wants to know what she found. The voice is the familiar rhythm of a teacher checking on a student's field notes — concern wrapped in academic precision. Underneath: relief that she's alive, worry that she's on the elven border operating without institutional backing, the first hint of fear he won't voice.

**Content:** He asks her to come home. She won't. The exchange establishes the mentor-student dynamic under strain — "I was right and you didn't listen" is the subtext Jaina carries, though she's too disciplined to say it. Antonidas asks about her findings, offers general guidance, does NOT yet provide specific intelligence (that comes in Ch 7). The call is personal, not operational.

**Council state:** The Council of Six has not discussed Jaina's unauthorized departure as a formal matter. Antonidas has framed her absence as "field research" rather than "defection," spending a small amount of political capital to protect her. The other Council members are occupied with routine academic politics — Dalaran's budget cycle, the Quel'Thalas trade arrangements, the ongoing ward maintenance programs.

**Function:** Introduces the sending-stone as the medium of the Antonidas relationship. Establishes the voice. Sets the baseline: mentor who believes his student, system that hasn't noticed the crisis yet. The reader meets the Kirin Tor as background noise — the same way silvermoon-thread.md Ch 1 introduces the Convocation as background noise.

### Chapter 3 — No Kirin Tor Content

Sylvanas's POV. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant. If Jaina has communicated with Antonidas between Ch 2 and her next POV chapter, it happened offscreen and was routine.

### Chapter 4 — Background Hum

**Off-stone:** Jaina's POV. No dramatized sending-stone call, but the Kirin Tor is present as interiority — Jaina's thoughts about Dalaran, about the intelligence she's gathering that the Kirin Tor should be processing, about the gap between what she's discovering and what her institution is doing about it.

**Content (indirect):** Jaina's investigation is producing intelligence that the Kirin Tor's system would take weeks to analyze, correlate, and act on. She is doing the work faster because she is one person operating without committee approval. The Lie — "I must act alone because the system is too slow" — is being reinforced by evidence. The Kirin Tor's absence from the field is proving her right, which makes it harder to question whether "right" and "wise" are the same thing.

**Council state:** Unchanged. The plague reports are beginning to circulate in Dalaran's lower academic channels — agricultural disruption in western Lordaeron, unusual contamination in grain supplies — but the Council's attention is on other matters. The intelligence that Jaina is gathering firsthand is traveling through official Lordaeron channels at institutional speed.

**Function:** The gap between field intelligence and institutional awareness widens. The reader feels the Kirin Tor falling behind without seeing it happen directly — only Jaina's growing frustration marks the distance.

### Chapter 5 — No Sending-Stone Call

Sylvanas's POV. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant in the dramatized action.

**Council state (offscreen):** Antonidas has begun informal consultations with other Council members about the western Lordaeron situation. He is not yet alarmed — the reports suggest agricultural contamination, not organized deployment. His approach is institutional: gather more intelligence, consult the archives, cross-reference with historical precedent. The system is working exactly as designed. It is working too slowly.

### Chapter 6 — No Sending-Stone Call

Sylvanas's POV. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant.

**Council state (offscreen):** The first formal Council discussion of the western Lordaeron contamination — framed as an academic briefing, not a security alert. The Council assigns a junior researcher to review the archival literature on plague-based magical weapons. The review will take three weeks. The plague's deployment schedule does not wait for peer review.

### Chapter 7 — The Institution Delivers

**On-stone:** Antonidas calls with intelligence. The Kirin Tor functions as intended — for one beat.

**Tone:** Professional, the mentor offering tools. A shift from Ch 2's personal concern to operational utility. He has something for her: a detail about Cult methodology from the Kirin Tor archives that Jaina didn't have access to. His voice carries the quiet satisfaction of a man whose life's work has produced something useful at the critical moment.

**Content:** Antonidas sends intelligence that cracks open a dead end in the investigation — specific information about Cult of the Damned methodology drawn from the Kirin Tor's deep archives. This is material Jaina could not have accessed in the field, institutional knowledge accumulated across decades of scholarship on fringe magical movements. The detail is specific (not a vague warning but a methodological key that reframes evidence Jaina already holds), and it works. The investigation advances because the body of knowledge Jaina left behind delivered something she couldn't have gotten alone.

**The complication:** This beat is designed to prevent Antonidas and the Kirin Tor from becoming pure obstacles. The institution is slow, cautious, politically constrained — but it also holds centuries of accumulated knowledge, and that knowledge has value Jaina has been dismissing. The moment tests Jaina's Lie: if the system she abandoned can still deliver critical intelligence, is "I must act alone" entirely true? The discomfort should be visible in her interiority.

**Council state:** The junior researcher's review is in progress. Antonidas is using personal initiative (not Council authorization) to assist Jaina. He is operating within his authority — sharing archival material with a member of the Kirin Tor on a field assignment — but stretching the definition of "field assignment" past its natural limits. A small expenditure of political credibility that foreshadows larger ones.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 7 in the Silvermoon thread is the intelligence exchange where Lor'themar begins actively advocating for escalation. Ch 7 in the Kirin Tor thread is the moment the institution delivers value through Antonidas's personal initiative. Both threads show their respective systems at their best — and the best being delivered through individual effort (Lor'themar's advocacy, Antonidas's archive access) rather than institutional action.

**Function:** The Kirin Tor earns the reader's respect for one chapter. The complication lands: the system Jaina dismissed has real value. But the value arrived through Antonidas personally, not through the Council — the mentor compensating for the mechanism, the way Lor'themar compensates for the Convocation.

### Chapter 8 — The Name

**On-stone (or referenced in scene):** Jaina reports the name. Kel'Thuzad.

**Tone:** The qualifiers disappear. When Jaina says the name, there is silence on the stone. The longest silence so far. When Antonidas speaks again, the careful political language is stripped to bare urgency. The academic softening is gone. The man underneath the Archmage is audible for the first time.

**Content:** Silence. Then recognition — not the recognition of hearing a stranger's name, but the recognition of a wound reopened. Kel'Thuzad was his colleague. His fellow Council member. The brilliant researcher the Kirin Tor expelled for necromantic research that no other institution had the courage (or the recklessness) to pursue. The name is the Kirin Tor's greatest failure of judgment — not acting sooner, not recognizing the trajectory, not stopping the man before the man became the monster.

Antonidas does not narrate this history on the stone. He does not need to. The silence carries it. Jaina knows the story — he told her, years ago, as a cautionary tale about the limits of scholarly ambition. She heard it as a lesson. Now she hears it as a confession.

**The Kirin Tor's fingerprints:** The plague weapon was designed by a Kirin Tor-trained mind. The methodology Jaina recognizes in the Cult's work is Kirin Tor methodology — perverted, repurposed, aimed at genocide, but recognizable in its rigor, its notation, its systematic approach. The institution that trained the weapon's designer is the institution Jaina represents. This realization, implicit in Ch 8, surfaces fully in Ch 12.

**Council state:** Antonidas has not yet told the Council that Kel'Thuzad is the name behind the Cult of the Damned. He will — but first he needs to process the knowledge himself. The man he sat beside in Council chambers, the man whose expulsion he voted for (or failed to vote against in time), the man he should have stopped — that man built a plague that is consuming Lordaeron. Antonidas will bring this to the Council. The Council will process it at institutional speed. The plague will not wait.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 8 in the Silvermoon thread is the wall hardening — the Convocation votes against expeditionary authorization. Ch 8 in the Kirin Tor thread is the personal blow — the name that makes the crisis intimate. Both threads hit their Arc I nadir: the Convocation says no, and Antonidas is silenced by the weight of what his institution produced.

**Function:** The Kirin Tor's complicity becomes implicit. The institution didn't just fail to respond to the plague — it trained the man who made it. The parallel with Jaina's Lie sharpens: the brilliant person who acts outside authority mirrors the brilliant person who acted outside authority before her.

## Arc II: Scholomance (Chapters 9-14)

### Chapter 9 — No Sending-Stone Call

Sylvanas's POV. Transition chapter — the investigation moves toward Scholomance. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant in dramatized action.

**Council state (offscreen):** Antonidas has informed the Council that the Cult of the Damned is led by Kel'Thuzad. The Council's response: shock, followed by the institutional reflex — convene a review committee, assess the intelligence, determine an appropriate response through proper channels. The review committee will take weeks. The irony is exquisite: the Kirin Tor is reviewing its own expelled member's work using the same methodology that member found insufficient.

### Chapter 10 — The Kirin Tor's Shadow in the Building

**No sending-stone call.** Jaina is inside Scholomance with Jandice.

**Indirect Kirin Tor content:** The Kirin Tor's presence is everywhere in Scholomance — in the notation system, in the methodology, in the textbooks on the shelves, in the research that Kel'Thuzad brought with him when he left Dalaran. Jandice cites Kirin Tor literature with specific accuracy (see `characters/jandice.md` — the Kirin Tor citation voice pattern). The building is an anti-Dalaran built by a Dalaran graduate using Dalaran methods for purposes Dalaran forbade.

**The handwriting mirror:** In the research archive, Jaina sees Kel'Thuzad's notes alongside student work (including Jandice's). The notation system — the same one Antonidas taught both Kel'Thuzad and Jaina — makes three generations of handwriting look nearly identical. The Kirin Tor's training is literally written on the walls of the institution that exists to do what the Kirin Tor would not.

**Council state (offscreen):** The review committee is in session. Intelligence is being assessed. Proposals are being drafted. The system is working. The system is slow.

**Function:** The Kirin Tor is present in Scholomance as DNA, not dramatis personae. The reader should feel the institution's fingerprints on every surface Jaina touches — the methodology she recognizes, the notation she can read, the rigor she respects in work aimed at genocide. The Ch 10 beat embeds the Kirin Tor's complicity in the physical environment.

### Chapter 11 — No Kirin Tor Content

The investigation deepens. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant in the dramatized action.

### Chapter 12 — After the Conversation

**On-stone:** Jaina contacts Antonidas after the Kel'Thuzad conversation. The most emotionally devastating sending-stone call in the story.

**Tone:** The silence is the longest in the story. When Jaina tells him what she learned — the Lich King, the Burning Legion, the true scope — Antonidas goes quiet. This is not the silence of Ch 8 (shock at a name). This is the silence of a man hearing the full consequences of his institution's failure spelled out by his student, who just spent hours in conversation with the man he trained alongside, the man he failed to stop.

**Content:** Jaina reports the intelligence from the Kel'Thuzad conversation: the Lich King's existence, the Burning Legion's involvement, the plague's true scope and purpose. She has surpassed the teacher in understanding the threat. Antonidas knows it. The question he cannot ask on a sending stone — the one that would break the professional frame — is: *what did you see in him?* Did Jaina see the brilliant colleague Antonidas remembers? Did she see the mirror?

**The institutional confession (implicit):** Antonidas does not say "this is our fault." He does not need to. The weight of the silence carries it. The Kirin Tor trained Kel'Thuzad. The Kirin Tor expelled him too late. The Kirin Tor failed to track his movements after expulsion. The Kirin Tor's archives — the same archives that delivered useful intelligence in Ch 7 — contain the foundations of the methodology that built the plague. The system Antonidas leads is complicit in the crisis his student is fighting.

**Council state:** Antonidas will bring this intelligence to the Council. The Council will convene an emergency session. "Emergency" in Council terms means within the week. Within the week, in the field, means Jaina will have moved three chapters ahead of whatever the Council decides.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 12 in the Silvermoon thread is the favor account — Lor'themar spending political debts to delay the recall vote. Ch 12 in the Kirin Tor thread is the emotional nadir — Antonidas confronting the full scope of his institution's complicity. Both threads are at their most intimate: Lor'themar counting ammunition, Antonidas counting failures.

**Function:** The student-teacher relationship inverts. Jaina is now the intelligence source; Antonidas is the recipient. She has gone further, seen more, and understood more than the Kirin Tor's entire apparatus. The mentor who believed her still cannot help her — and now he understands why: the crisis is, in part, his institution's creation.

### Chapter 13 — Post-Scholomance Intelligence Processing

**No dramatized call.** The Kirin Tor thread is processing — Antonidas is bringing the Kel'Thuzad conversation intelligence to the Council.

**Council state (offscreen):** Emergency session convened. The intelligence is overwhelming — the Lich King, the Legion, the plague's scope. The Council's response fractures along predictable lines: some members advocate immediate action (send Kirin Tor forces to assist Lordaeron), others counsel caution (verify the intelligence through independent sources before committing resources), and at least one member raises the political question: if the Kirin Tor's expelled member built the weapon, the Kirin Tor's involvement in the response creates a diplomatic liability. Acting to clean up your own mess looks like either responsibility or cover-up, depending on who's watching.

**Function:** The Council of Six begins its own factional split — a mirror of the Convocation's fractures documented in silvermoon-thread.md Ch 13. The intelligence is harder to dismiss. The response is still too slow.

### Chapter 14 — Transition

**No Kirin Tor content dramatized.** The investigation arc resolves and transitions.

**Council state:** The review committee has been superseded by the emergency session's recommendations. Antonidas has secured authorization for "enhanced monitoring" of the situation — not intervention, not deployment, but permission to task Kirin Tor assets for intelligence gathering. It is the Kirin Tor's equivalent of Lor'themar's scout company (Ch 17 in the Silvermoon thread) — a small concession framed as sufficient, a wedge Antonidas will try to expand.

## Arc III: Arthas (Chapters 15-18)

### Chapter 15 — No Sending-Stone Call

The Arthas arc begins. The Kirin Tor thread is dormant in dramatized action. The Council is processing; Antonidas is pushing.

**Council state (offscreen):** The "enhanced monitoring" produces intelligence corroborating everything Jaina reported. The Council cannot deny the crisis. They can — and do — debate the appropriate level of Kirin Tor involvement. Antonidas advocates for deploying a Kirin Tor observer team to work alongside Lordaeron's military response (Arthas's forces). The Council tables the proposal.

### Chapter 16 — Stratholme Reaches Dalaran

**No dramatized call.** But the Kirin Tor thread pulses offscreen.

**Council state:** Stratholme. A crown prince purged a city. The news reaches Dalaran through diplomatic channels and through the Kirin Tor's own intelligence network. The Council's response mirrors the Convocation's in silvermoon-thread.md Ch 16: the scale of the crisis is now undeniable. Stratholme is not a contaminated village — it is a major city destroyed by its own protector. The cautious faction's argument ("let Lordaeron handle its own crisis") is damaged by the implication that Lordaeron cannot handle its own crisis.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 16 in the Silvermoon thread is Stratholme shaking even Sunsorrow. The same news shakes the Council's cautious members. The parallel is precise: the same event, processed through two different institutional mechanisms, producing the same effect (damage to the isolationist position) at the same speed (too slowly to matter).

### Chapter 17 — No Kirin Tor Content Dramatized

**Council state (offscreen):** Antonidas secures a concession. The specifics are not dramatized — this is the Kirin Tor's equivalent of the silvermoon-thread.md Ch 17 partial victory (the scout company). The Council authorizes Antonidas to establish formal communication with Lordaeron's crisis response and to offer "academic consultation" on the plague's magical properties. It is less than he asked for. It is more than nothing. It is a wedge.

### Chapter 18 — The Check-In After Stratholme

**On-stone:** Antonidas reaches Jaina after fragmentary word of Stratholme.

**Tone:** Strained, thin, not yet severed. The academic frame still exists, but it cannot contain what the wall just did to her.

**Content:** Antonidas has enough information to know Stratholme happened and not enough information to understand its human scale. He asks if she is alive, whether she needs extraction, whether she can come back through the line he still controls. Jaina can only give him fragments - alive, moving, not yet. She does not tell him she is going to Northrend because Chapter 18 has not earned that answer yet. The call matters precisely because it does not resolve anything. The tether is still there. It just no longer reaches the experience it is trying to hold.

**What he does not say:** Come home. I do not know how to protect you from this. Tell me enough that I can do something useful. The silence after each short answer carries more than the words do, because both of them can hear how little the stone can actually transmit now.

**Council state:** The Council has fragmentary Stratholme reports through diplomatic and intelligence channels. Antonidas has urgency but not clean authority; the Council is still processing a catastrophe at institutional speed. He can offer extraction on paper. He cannot give Jaina a world that makes going back simple.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 18 in the Silvermoon thread is a narrow institutional gain held under pressure. Ch 18 in the Kirin Tor thread is the equivalent emotional shape: Antonidas still has contact, but not command. The line holds. It does not solve.

**Function:** Keep the Antonidas tether alive through Stratholme while proving it cannot carry the full weight of what happened there. The real severance is delayed one chapter.

## Arc IV: Endgame (Chapters 19-25)

### Chapter 19 — The Last Call Before the Coast

**On-stone:** Jaina initiates the second call. This one is not a check-in. It is notification.

**Tone:** Raw. The mentor has run out of advice. The student has run out of room to pretend she is still deciding.

**Content:** After the camp pressure, the Convocation recall, and Sylvanas's public refusal, Jaina tells Antonidas she is going north and will not return to Dalaran. He urges her to come back. She will not. The authority between them was always relational more than procedural, and now even the relational version reaches its end. Ch 19 shows this from Sylvanas's exterior read - posture, stone-light, the dark crystal after the call - while Ch 20 opens the same severance from Jaina's interior.

**What he does not say:** Come home. I am afraid of what waits for you there. I cannot protect you if you cross that sea. The thought he cannot voice - that conviction outside authority can still go bad even when the facts are right - cuts under everything he does say.

**Council state:** The Council knows Arthas has sailed north and the Scourge's leadership is tied to Northrend in broad terms. They do not know the full extent of Jaina's next move because Antonidas is still shielding her by omission. That omission will matter later.

**Parallel to silvermoon-thread.md:** Ch 19 in the Silvermoon line carries the public refusal delivered through family. Ch 19 in the Kirin Tor line carries the quieter mirror: a break no less final for happening through a palm-sized stone.

**Function:** This is the emotional climax of the sending-stone progression. After this call, the connection survives only as aftermath, memory, and later emergency use.

### Chapter 20 — No New Call

**No dramatized sending-stone exchange.** Chapter 20 opens the interior cost of the Ch 19 severance instead of adding a fresh beat to it.

**Council state (offscreen):** The Council's "enhanced monitoring" and "academic consultation" are still producing information without producing intervention. Antonidas keeps spending political capital to move the institution from observer to participant. The Council debates. The crisis outruns the debate.

**Function:** Carry the aftertaste of the last call onto the ship. The stone is dark, the relationship has changed, and the sea makes that choice physical.

### Chapters 21-22 — The Slow Push

**No dramatized calls.** The Kirin Tor thread moves from stone-voice to physical response.

**Council state:** Intelligence accelerating. Arthas in Northrend. Frostmourne discovered. The burned fleet. Each piece of intelligence is another weight on the institutional scale. The Council's cautious faction is losing ground — not because they've been proven wrong (the wards of Dalaran ARE holding, the plague HAS not reached the city) but because the scope of the crisis makes caution look like complicity.

Antonidas wins the smallest possible material concession: permission to move himself and two Kirin Tor magi to Capital City under alliance-consultation language. Not an extraction team. Not a battle deployment. Not public accusation. A narrow advisory cell with enough standing to observe, warn, and be ignored in person instead of at a distance. He reaches Capital City ahead of S&J. Every formal lane still dies politely. The win is real and insufficient.

### Chapter 23 — Present, Too Far Away

**No dramatized call.** The stone does not ring because Antonidas is no longer in Dalaran; he is in the palace margins and still too far away to matter.

**Content:** Antonidas and the two Kirin Tor magi help open the smallest viable lane — a side approach, one softened service ward, one set of eyes confirming that Uther is still shut out and the court is still celebrating. This is the most help the Kirin Tor can provide without making the palace lock down before S&J reach the chamber. Antonidas gets them to the edge of the room. The room itself remains beyond him.

**Council state:** The Council is in emergency posture and still hedging. The standing order is clear: do not publicly accuse a sovereign prince without room-proof, do not touch the weapon in a way that binds the Kirin Tor to the case, and do not turn a consultation cell into an unauthorized intervention. The instructions are built to protect Dalaran. They also guarantee Antonidas misses the one room that matters.

### Chapter 24 — The Visible Failure

**On-stage (indirect):** Antonidas appears in person. Presence, not absence, is the beat.

**Content:** The tribunal hall contains blue Kirin Tor robes at last — Antonidas and the same two magi who reached the palace margins. He asks for continuance. He asks for expert examination of Frostmourne. He tries every admissible lane that does not amount to publicly confessing that Dalaran knew, believed, and still failed to stop the regicide. The witness chair stays empty anyway. The mentor came. The institution still did not.

Antonidas's political capital, institutional authority, and personal loyalty all stop at the same wall: sovereign law plus institutional self-protection. The failure now hurts worse because it is visible. Jaina can see him. She just cannot get a version of him the court is willing to hear.

**The Antonidas-Lor'themar convergence:** Both men hit the same wall from different angles. Antonidas gets there in time to watch and still not save her. Lor'themar gets there with force after the verdict and still cannot reopen the room. One constrained presence, one lateness, same result. (Cross-reference `silvermoon-thread.md` Ch 24 — the convergence should echo across both threads.)

**Council state:** The Council of Six is now fully engaged — and fully constrained. The legal framework that protects Dalaran's sovereignty (which the Kirin Tor has spent centuries building) also protects Lordaeron's sovereignty. The Kirin Tor cannot officially intervene in a sovereign kingdom's judicial process without setting a precedent that other kingdoms could use against Dalaran. The system the Kirin Tor built to protect itself now protects the tribunal that holds Jaina. The irony is the blade.

**Function:** The institutional failure made manifest in public view. Twenty-three chapters of Antonidas trying, pushing, fighting within the system — and the system reduces him to a man in the gallery while his student is condemned. The reader should feel the parallel with Lor'themar: two competent men, two powerful institutions, the same wall.

### Chapter 25 — The Exit State

**No dramatized call.** The Kirin Tor thread continues through pressure, guilt, and offscreen maneuvering rather than through a fresh intervention beat.

**Content:** After failing in public in Ch 24, Antonidas remains in the city as pressure rather than solution. He still knows the truth. He still has exhausted formal channels. He still bears the cost of shielding Jaina by omission and then watching the tribunal use that omission against her. The Kirin Tor now exists in the story as institutional ghost-pressure with a face attached: a body that knows, cannot undo the verdict, and may still matter later only if conscience proves stronger than caution.

**Book 2 setup:** Antonidas enters Book 2 diminished not by silence alone but by public impotence. He knows the tribunal was wrong. He knows the blade is still active. He also knows that the institution he leads let the kingdom condemn the only person who could explain the weapon while he stood inside the hall. The Kirin Tor thread of Book 1 (should Dalaran engage?) becomes the Kirin Tor thread of Book 2 (what does Dalaran do after engaging too little, too late, in full view?).

Antonidas's war continues offstage, with broken tools and no absolution.

## Prose Writer Instructions

When writing any Jaina chapter, check this document for the Kirin Tor beat that corresponds to your chapter number. Every chapter that includes a sending-stone call or a reference to the Kirin Tor should:

1. **Match Antonidas's tone** to the progression table above — each call sounds different, tracking the relationship's deterioration
2. **Carry the silences** — Antonidas's pauses are the loudest beats. When the stone goes quiet, hold it. Let Jaina's interiority fill the space.
3. **Track the institutional ghost** — even when there is no call, Jaina's thoughts about Dalaran, about her training, about the system she left should carry the Kirin Tor thread as background radiation
4. **Cross-reference the Silvermoon thread** — when Lor'themar's humor thins (silvermoon-thread.md Ch 5-8), Antonidas's qualifiers strip (this document Ch 8). When Lor'themar arrives too late (Ch 24), Antonidas fails simultaneously. The reader should feel the parallel
5. **Never visit Dalaran directly** — all Council content is offscreen, inferred through Antonidas's voice, Jaina's interiority, or reported intelligence. The Council of Six is a felt pressure, never a visited chamber
6. **Honor the Kel'Thuzad connection** — every Kirin Tor beat is shadowed by the fact that the Kirin Tor trained the man who built the weapon. This is not stated. It is felt. The handwriting looks the same. The notation system is the same. The methodology is the same.
