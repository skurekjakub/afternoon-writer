## How an Unheld Blade Hunts

Frostmourne was never meant to be wielded by one person forever. The Nathrezim built a cycle into the blade: claim a wielder, consume them, find the next. Arthas was not the first. The blade's seeking behavior is not a malfunction — it is the weapon working as designed.

Arthas's death breaks the stable bearer-circuit, not the deeper Northrend tether. The blade remains under Ner'zhul's pressure through its own binding, but with no hand on the hilt that pressure radiates outward instead of traveling cleanly through one servant.

The mechanism is proximity influence. The blade's damaged bearer-circuit and still-live tether make it broadcast outward. The consuming phase reaches into nearby minds, reading desire the way a predator reads movement. The radiating phase projects the blade's offer: purpose, clarity, the certainty that you were meant for more than this. The whispers are the cycle's output, translated through human perception into language.

Ner'zhul does not point at one guard by name like a chess piece. The blade reads the emotional landscape around it and presses into the mind most likely to reach back. Grief. Ambition. Duty unfulfilled. Conviction without an outlet. These are the cracks the blade exploits.

## The Seeking Progression

### Hours 1-6: Physical Dominance

The vault freezes. Frost crawls the walls in patterns that follow the stone's natural fractures, spreading outward from the lead-lined chest. The cold is sharp and wrong — not winter cold but absence, the heat pulled from the air rather than the air being cold. Palace heating enchantments fail within a ten-pace radius. Torches gutter. Breath clouds thick enough to obscure vision.

The guards stationed at the vault door feel it first: pressure behind the eyes, a wrongness in the chest, the instinct to leave. No whispers yet. Just the animal awareness that something in this room is paying attention to them.

### Hours 6-24: The Whispers Begin

Faint. Constant. In a language none of the guards speak — Eredun, the tongue of the Burning Legion, stripped of meaning and reduced to cadence. The guards cannot understand the words. They understand the rhythm. The rhythm says: *you are seen. You matter. There is purpose waiting.*

Guards stationed for a full eight-hour watch report the whispers growing louder toward the end of their shift. The volume is not changing. The listener's resistance is lowering. Each hour of proximity peels back another layer of the mind's natural defenses.

### Days 1-3: The Dreams

Guards who complete a full watch cycle dream. The dreams are specific — not generic nightmares but tailored provocations. Each dreamer encounters the same core offer, dressed in the fabric of their own desires:

- The guard who worries about feeding his family dreams of a table laden with food, and a voice that says the table is his if he is willing to *serve.*
- The guard who lost a brother to the orc wars dreams of the brother alive and whole, and a voice that says the dead can return if the right hand holds the right blade.
- The guard who secretly resents his commanding officer dreams of standing above the man, sword in hand, and the weight of the weapon feeling like *justice.*

The dreams are precise because the blade has been reading these minds for hours. Proximity is intimacy. By the end of a watch cycle, the blade knows what each guard wants more than anything, and the dream offers exactly that.

Guards request transfer. New guards are assigned. The cycle restarts with fresh minds. The blade is patient. It has done this on other worlds, in other ages. It can afford to cycle through a thousand guards if necessary. Sooner or later, one stays.

### Days 3-7: The Influence Spreads

The cold bleeds past the vault. The corridor outside drops ten degrees. Then twenty. Servants who use the passage detour. The kitchen staff, who once carried trays through this route, now take the long way around through the east wing. The passage becomes a dead zone — avoided, whispered about, officially attributed to "enchantment leakage from the evidence."

The whispers carry further. A palace mage examining the wards from ten paces away reports hearing a voice "like someone speaking through water." She steps back. The voice stops. She does not examine the wards again. She writes in her report: *containment holds. Recommend continued observation.*

The dreams extend to staff who work near the vault but were never inside it. A servant who cleans the corridor outside dreams of drowning in blue light. She tells her supervisor she is unwell and requests reassignment. A guard posted at the end of the corridor — thirty paces from the vault door — dreams of a crown on his head and the weight of it feeling right.

### Days 7-14: The Candidates Emerge

The blade has cycled through dozens of guards by now. Most resist. Most transfer. But a pattern emerges: certain individuals return. They volunteer for extra vault shifts. They linger in the corridor. They cannot articulate why. Something about the post feels *important.* Like the work they were meant to do is down here, in the cold, near the thing in the lead-lined chest.

Three candidates crystallize. The blade does not need all three. It needs one.

## The Vulnerable

These are not villains. They are ordinary people with ordinary cracks — grief, ambition, devotion — and the blade finds those cracks the way water finds stone.

### The Ambitious Knight — Ser Edric Valecrest

A young officer of the palace guard. Second son of House Valecrest — minor Lordaeron nobility, landed gentry in the Eastweald foothills, the kind of family that has a name and a crest and not enough wealth to support the lifestyle the name implies. He volunteered for palace duty because proximity to the crown is proximity to advancement. He is competent, loyal, and desperate to be noticed. (Full profile: [`characters/supporting.md`](../characters/supporting.md) — "Book 2 Setup Characters" section.)

The blade offers what ambition craves: certainty. The dreams show him standing before the court with the blade in his hand, and the court *seeing* him — not as a minor noble's second son but as someone who matters. The dreams do not promise power. They promise recognition. The distinction is important: ambition's deepest hunger is not for control but for the world to confirm that you were always meant to be more.

He volunteers for vault duty three times in the first week. By the second week, he requests permanent assignment.

### The Grieving Father

A palace steward. Elderly. His son was among the soldiers who marched north with Arthas and did not return. (Not every soldier who followed Arthas became undead. Some simply died — of cold, of combat, of the Scourge's advance guard.) The steward has no body to bury, no grave to visit, no answers about how or where his boy fell.

The blade offers what grief craves: reunion. The dreams show the son, alive, whole, smiling, reaching out his hand from a place of blue light. The voice in the dream says: *he is here. He is waiting. You can reach him if you are willing to reach.*

The steward does not volunteer for vault duty. He has no reason to be near the vault. But he begins walking the cold corridor during his rounds, lingering at the edge of the dead zone, listening. He tells himself he is checking on the heating enchantments. He is not checking on the heating enchantments.

### The Devout Initiate

A Silver Hand initiate — one of the two who moved the blade into the lead-lined chest on the day of the arrest. He lifted Frostmourne through blessed gauntlets and felt the blade's field press against the Light's protection. The gauntlets held. The Light held. But in the moment of contact, before the chest closed, the initiate felt something that was not entirely revulsion.

The blade spoke to his faith. Not against it — *through* it. The same certainty the Light carries, the same conviction that your purpose is larger than yourself, the same voice that says *you were chosen.* The blade mimicked the Light's cadence so precisely that for one heartbeat the initiate could not tell the difference.

He told his superior he was disgusted by the experience. He prays about it every night. He dreams of the vault. In the dream, the Light and the blade's call are the same voice, and the voice says: *the strongest servant is the one who can carry both.*

He is the most dangerous candidate because he believes his resistance is a sign of strength. The blade agrees.

## The Ward Degradation

The palace mages' wards are designed for cursed objects — haunted heirlooms, hexed weapons, artifacts recovered from old battlefields. Frostmourne is not a cursed object. It is a channel.

The distinction matters. A cursed object radiates a fixed amount of dark energy. Wards contain it. The energy pushes against the wards; the wards push back. Equilibrium.

Frostmourne's resonance loop does not push against the wards. It *vibrates through them.* The blade's consuming-radiating cycle operates on harmonics that find the wards' natural frequency and match it. The wards do not fail under force. They fail under resonance — the same principle that lets a voice shatter crystal.

**Day 1-3:** Wards hold. The mages report the containment as stable. Monitoring continues.
**Day 4-7:** First signs of degradation. The ward-stones' glow dims. Hairline fractures appear in the containment glyphs. The cold leaks through the lead lining. The mages reinforce with additional ward-layers.
**Day 7-14:** The reinforcements degrade faster than the originals. Diminishing returns: each new layer lasts shorter than the one before, because the blade has already found the harmonic frequency of the palace mages' enchantment style. They are all trained in the same school. Their wards all resonate at similar frequencies. The blade learns one, it has learned them all.
**Day 14-21:** Containment is nominal. The wards are technically intact but functionally compromised — they contain the cold and the whispers within a radius, but the radius has expanded from ten paces to thirty. The vault is no longer usable for any other purpose. The corridor is abandoned.
**Day 21+:** The mages send a formal request to the tribunal for Kirin Tor assistance. The tribunal denies it. Allowing Dalaran to examine evidence in the regicide trial of a Kirin Tor mage would constitute evidence tampering. The palace mages are on their own, outmatched, and losing ground.

## The Lor'themar Factor

Lor'themar's expeditionary force arrives after the verdict. Among his rangers and officers are elves raised within generations of the Sunwell — their sensitivity to magical disturbance is not learned but bred.

A human mage senses the vault's wrongness as cold and whispers. An elf of Quel'Thalas senses it as *violation* — the Sunwell's light in their blood recoiling from the blade's resonance the way a nerve recoils from fire. Where the human mages measure the vault's emanations in increments, the elves feel it as a scream.

This creates a detection path that Lordaeron's human court lacks. If Lor'themar's mages examine the evidence vault, they can corroborate what Jaina has been saying from her cell: the blade is not an artifact. It is an active weapon. It is hunting. And the containment is failing.

The political obstacle: Lor'themar arrived too late to testify. His force has no standing in Lordaeron's tribunal. The elves can sense the danger but have no authority to act on it. Book 2 urgency: converting elven perception into political action before the blade finds its next hand.

## Jaina's Sensitivity

From her cell, two floors below the vault, Jaina hears enough to be dangerous.

Not the whispers the guards describe — she hears the resonance itself. The consuming-radiating cycle she first encountered in the Frostmourne Cavern (Ch 21), the harmonic she exploited in the throne room (Ch 23), the field her own magic punched through. Her perception is unusually raw to the blade's broadcast now. This is not a bond or a conversation. It is the ugly aftereffect of having neutralized the thing at point-blank range.

She hears the blade test the wards. She hears the harmonics shift as the blade learns the palace mages' enchantment patterns. She hears the seeking — the outward pulse of the consuming phase reaching into the minds above her. She cannot identify the targets by name, but she can feel the blade's attention focus, narrow, intensify when a vulnerable mind enters its radius.

At rare moments, when she listens too hard, the pulse skews for one beat as if it has noticed her listening. Nothing follows. The recognition itself is the horror.

She tells the walls. She tells the guard who brings her food. She tells the magistrate who questions her. The sword is hunting. The containment is failing. Someone will pick it up.

No one listens. Her testimony about the blade is, to the tribunal, further evidence of her involvement with dark magic. How does she know the blade's behavior if she was not its accomplice?

The cruelty: the mage who can stop the blade is in a cell. The blade that needs stopping is two floors above her. The people who could free her are the people the blade's new wielder will destroy.

## The Ticking Clock

If nothing changes — if S&J remain imprisoned, if the Kirin Tor is denied access, if the palace mages continue their failing ward reinforcements, if Lor'themar's elves are denied authority to act — then within three to four weeks of the arrest, the blade finds a hand.

The most likely candidate is Ser Edric Valecrest — the ambitious knight. His proximity is consistent, his visits increasingly frequent, his resistance eroding with each dream. The blade has had two weeks to learn his desires, his fears, the specific shape of the emptiness it needs to fill.

The moment of claiming will not be dramatic. It will be quiet. A night shift. The vault empty except for one guard who requested this post because it felt important. The lead-lined chest, its wards visibly cracked, the cold seeping through the seams. The guard stands in the cold for hours, listening to whispers that have become familiar, almost comforting. His hand moves to the chest's latch. He does not remember deciding to open it.

The sword is warm in his hand. That is the lie that seals it: after weeks of cold, the blade radiates warmth — the consuming field, reversed for one heartbeat, drawing the guard in the way a fire draws a freezing traveler. By the time the warmth becomes cold again, it is too late. The guard is gone. The wielder has arrived.

Lordaeron's second death knight rises in the heart of the capital, in the evidence vault of the tribunal that condemned the only people who could have prevented it.

**This is the Book 2 clock.** Every day S&J spend in prison is a day closer to the blade's next victim.

## Prose-Writer Language Guide

**Constraint from brief:** Magic descriptions should read as approachable fantasy, not technical physics. This planning document uses mechanism language for precision — the prose must translate to sensory, visceral language. (See also `throne-room-mechanism.md` line 15 and the equivalent guide in `frostmourne-lure.md`.)

### Translation Table

| Planning Term | Prose Equivalent(s) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| The seeking / the blade seeks | The cold reaching; the hunger spreading; the whispers finding new ears | "Seeking" itself is borderline acceptable in prose — it implies will, which is correct. Avoid "scanning" or "targeting." |
| Vulnerable / vulnerability profile | The cracks it finds; the hollow places; the want it can smell | The blade reads desire and loss, not magical signatures — render as emotional predation, not technical assessment |
| Ward degradation / containment failure | The cold seeping through; the cracks in the lead; the seals going thin; the wards growing tired | Wards are described as things that wear out, not things that fail technically — they age, they thin, they tire |
| Resonance / resonant | The hum; the song; the wrongness; the pull | Same vocabulary as frostmourne-lure.md — consistency across all Frostmourne documents |
| The consuming field | The cold that reaches; the hunger; the emptiness | Same as frostmourne-lure.md |
| The ambitious knight / candidate | The guard who dreams; the one who keeps coming back; the man who asked for this post | Always rendered through behavior, not designation — the reader sees the symptoms before the diagnosis |
| Sympathetic contamination | The traces left in living tissue; the stain that won't wash; the cold that stays in the bones | The contamination is described as physical residue — something left behind in the body, not a magical status effect |

### The Vault Scenes (Book 2 Setup)

The evidence vault where Frostmourne is stored should read as:
- **Temperature:** Cold that has weight. Not winter cold but absence-of-warmth cold — as if the blade is drinking the heat from the room. Guards notice their breath. The stone walls sweat.
- **The whispers:** Not words. Not coherent speech. Sounds that almost form words — a voice heard through a wall, a conversation in the next room that never quite resolves. The guards who spend the most time near the vault begin finishing the almost-sentences in their heads. They don't notice when the completions start matching their own desires.
- **The dreams:** The guard who dreams of the blade dreams of warmth. The blade appears as the answer to whatever he lacks — purpose, strength, belonging, revenge. The dreams are not nightmares. That is the trap: they are the best dreams he has ever had.
- **The moment of claiming:** Quiet. Not dramatic. A hand on a latch. The lead chest opening. The blade warm in the grip. The warmth is the final lie — after weeks of cold, one heartbeat of warmth that seals the bond before the cold returns forever.

### General Principles

- **The blade is patient, not dramatic.** Frostmourne does not monologue, does not taunt, does not make dramatic reveals. It waits. It listens. It offers. The horror is in the patience — a predator that can wait months for one hand to reach.
- **Render the mechanism as behavior.** "The wards degraded by 40%" becomes "the ice on the vault door was thicker this morning." "The candidate's resistance is eroding" becomes "he asked for the night shift again." Show the effect. Trust the reader.
- **The ticking clock is felt, not counted.** Do not give the reader a countdown. Give them symptoms: the cold spreading, the dreams becoming nightly, the guard's visits becoming longer. The urgency should build through accumulation, not deadline.
