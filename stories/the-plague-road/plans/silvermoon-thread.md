# The Silvermoon Thread

- Use this as the Lor'themar/Convocation pressure line.
- Mirror to Antonidas: one advocate, one slow institution, one crisis moving faster than debate.

## Arc I — Investigation
- Ch 1: baseline Lor'themar. Patrol budgets and normal Convocation nonsense.
- Ch 5: first real field report gets the official answer: investigate within current mandate, no new resources.
- Ch 6: Sylvanas's authority is questioned. Dawnstar starts abstaining instead of joining the certainty faction.
- Ch 7: Lor'themar shifts from defense to active advocacy. Humor thins.
- Ch 8: full expedition vote fails. Recall threat begins.

## Arc II — Scholomance
- Ch 9: Lor'themar appears in person looking worn down. He takes the Scholomance proof because it might finally move votes.
- Ch 10: Jaina becomes the new political weapon. The partnership with a human mage is easier to attack than the plague facts.
- Ch 12: Lor'themar spends a major favor to delay the recall three weeks. He starts counting ammunition instead of making jokes.
- Ch 13: Scholomance evidence cracks the certainty faction but does not beat it yet.

## Arc III — Arthas
- Ch 15: Arthas's mobilization becomes Lor'themar's new argument and Vandellor's new counterargument.
- Ch 16: Stratholme finally shakes even Sunsorrow. The buffer-state logic starts breaking.
- Ch 17: Lor'themar wins the scout company, 4-3, by spending his last real leverage. Small military effect, huge political precedent.
- Ch 18: the wedge holds. The question becomes "send more?" instead of "send anyone?"

## Arc IV — Endgame
- Ch 19: Vereesa reports Lor'themar gutted by the effort. The Convocation recalls Sylvanas anyway by shifting from threat assessment to mandate.
- Ch 20-23: scout reports do the slow work politics would not do on their own. The room grinds toward mobilization.
- Ch 23: full expedition finally passes. Too late to matter for the throne room, right on time to matter for aftermath.
- Ch 24: Lor'themar reaches Capital City with the force one beat late. Same wall as Antonidas, different path.
- Ch 25: the force becomes leverage for Book 2: custody pressure, vault scrutiny, and proof that Lordaeron is not handling its own disaster.

## Tone line
- Ch 1-4: wry.
- Ch 5-8: sharpened.
- Ch 9-16: stripped down.
- Ch 17-25: exhausted and efficient.
