# The Plague Mechanism

## Purpose

This document defines how the plague actually works beneath the investigation arc, what Jaina can and cannot infer before Chapter 12, and why Kel'Thuzad's private ambition is related to the village plague without being identical to it.

Use this as the source of truth when patching or writing Chapters 1-12.

**Relevant chapters:** 2-12, with special payoff pressure on 4, 6, 7, 8, 10, and 12.

**Constraint from brief:** Jaina is too brilliant to be blind to the dark-arts shape of the problem. She should identify necromantic signatures, deliberate magical engineering, and host-pattern behavior early. What she lacks is not intelligence. What she lacks is formal necromantic education, applied practice, and the conceptual vocabulary Dalaran's ban kept outside her training. The midpoint conversation with Kel'Thuzad is where the partial reads become a complete theorem.

---

## Part 1: The Core Thesis

The plague is one magical system operating at two different fidelities.

1. **Field deployment / low-fidelity preservation**
   - Delivered through contaminated grain
   - Scalable, cheap, and meant for mass conversion
   - Produces ghouls, shambling dead, crude coordinated movement, and necromantic answerability
   - Preserves function, appetite, and response more than personhood

2. **Kel'Thuzad's private ambition / high-fidelity preservation**
   - Built from the same underlying magical logic
   - Adds the missing layer needed to preserve memory, intellect, and continuity of self
   - Explains why he is not afraid of death
   - Explains why the villagers become mindless dead while he seeks something closer to selfhood after death

The same theory governs both. The difference is **pattern fidelity**.

That term is for planning documents. Do not make early-chapter Jaina say it on-page before she has earned the concept.

## Part 2: How the Field Plague Works

### Step 1: The Grain Carries the Outer Shell

The grain is the carrier, not the full mechanism.

It is washed or treated with a necromantic preparation that:
- survives transport, storage, weather, and baking better than an ordinary poison would
- leaves a strong magical signature in the grain stores
- produces misleading field reads because the strongest detectable layer is not the deepest operative layer

This is the **outer shell** of the plague. It is why Jaina's early reads keep finding the grain and still failing to explain the whole chain.

### Step 2: The Body Becomes the Answering Chamber

Repeated ingestion matters more than one exposure.

The treated grain does not merely poison. It conditions living tissue. The contamination settles in the body, especially through the gut first, then into wider system response. The body is not only a victim. It becomes the place where the second half of the mechanism is assembled.

This is why:
- villages can look merely sick before they collapse
- heavier bread-eaters become worse cases faster
- one contaminated site is not enough for Jaina to solve the whole mechanism
- the plague can feel partly environmental and partly bodily at the same time

### Step 3: False Rings Mislead Investigation

The plague is built with detectable but incomplete layers.

Jaina can truthfully identify:
- necromantic contamination
- deliberate magical design
- physical ingress through ingestion
- tissue conditioning instead of simple poisoning

But her field reads keep catching **false rings**:
- strong carrier signature in the grain
- broad ambient saturation in contaminated settlements
- visible reanimation effects in dying animals or advanced victims

Those layers are real. They are simply not the whole mechanism.

### Step 4: Death Completes the Lock

The conditioned body reaches a point where death is not the end of the process but the activating threshold.

The plague teaches the body a repeatable death-pattern. When the host dies in that conditioned state, the stored necromantic logic answers through the body and wakes a low-fidelity undead result.

The important point for planning:
- the dead are not being animated from entirely outside
- the living body has already been prepared to answer

### Step 5: The Result Is a Choir, Not a Person

What rises from the field plague is not full selfhood preserved through death.

It is enough retained pattern for:
- motion
- appetite
- crude task or directional response
- rough synchronization with similarly conditioned dead
- easy answerability to outside command once a handler establishes the line

That is why villages can produce northbound mustering and mass movement without producing articulate, intact persons. Left alone, that result still retains crude self-organization — herd logic, shared pull, rough directional convergence. The Cult of the Damned can then seize and harden that behavior: low-rank acolytes and "necromancers" taught command signals can force direct compliance from converted peasants and other infected without actually understanding the full theory that made them.

The result is a **choir** of similarly taught bodies, not a community of minds. The dead do not become intelligent soldiers. They can self-muster crudely on their own and become far more useful once a conductor imposes a stricter line.

## Part 3: Kel'Thuzad's Added Layer

Kel'Thuzad is not trying to become one more ghoul.

He is pursuing the same theorem at a higher resolution. The field plague proves that pattern can be carried through the body, conditioned toward death, and reawakened after death. His private work adds the missing preserving layer for selfhood.

For planning purposes, treat that added layer as a **mnemonic scaffold**:
- an individualized preserving structure
- built to retain memory, intellect, and identity continuity
- too expensive, complex, or precise for mass village deployment

This is the "extra ingredient" the villagers do not get.

So:
- **villagers** get low-fidelity preserved behavior and necromantic answerability
- **Kel'Thuzad** seeks high-fidelity preserved selfhood

This is why his calm about death should feel earned rather than delusional. He believes he has moved beyond mere reanimation.

Kel'Thuzad is still the mortal architect of the field system, but he is not its only designer. The plague was refined through patron knowledge running down from Ner'zhul and the chain above him. That means Kel'Thuzad understands the work more deeply than any living scholar in Lordaeron while still not necessarily grasping every side effect, downstream behavior, or hidden purpose embedded upstream by the larger Scourge / Legion design.

## Part 4: Why Dalaran Banned It

The Kirin Tor ban is not proof that the theory is incoherent.

The ban exists because once necromancy can preserve pattern through death, the discipline stops being "moving dead tissue" and starts becoming a direct assault on:
- personhood
- consent
- civic trust
- the boundary between mortal death and continued agency

Jaina should be able to understand this once Kel'Thuzad explains the continuity between sanctioned magical principles and forbidden necromantic application.

Before that point, she can know the work is necromantic and brilliant without having the full banned framework that makes the deeper implications legible.

## Part 5: Jaina's Learning Ladder

### Chapters 2-3
- She correctly identifies the contamination as necromantic / death-aligned rather than ordinary arcane corruption.
- She understands she is dealing with designed magic, not marsh fever, coincidence, or natural blight.
- She can see saturation, ingress pressure, and reanimation effects.
- She cannot yet separate carrier, host response, and waking threshold.

### Chapters 4-6
- She performs targeted analysis rather than generalized alarm.
- She isolates ingestion as the route.
- She sees gut-first spread and concludes the body is participating in the mechanism.
- She recognizes repeated exposure and deployment scale.
- She can say the system is elegant, necromantic, and built by a trained magical mind.
- She still cannot explain the exact step where living contamination becomes undead continuity.

### Chapters 7-8
- She sees that the grain treatment has layered design rather than a simple poison profile.
- She understands that the operation includes monitoring, cleanup, and tactical silencing.
- She can start to infer that tighter undead coordination is being imposed by live Cult handlers rather than arising from fully preserved peasant intelligence.
- She should also be allowed to notice that the dead are not blank puppets; they show crude self-mustering that Cult handlers can seize, sharpen, and direct.
- She gets closer to the truth that the strongest field signature is not the whole mechanism.
- She still lacks the banned conceptual frame that would let her finish the explanation.

### Chapters 9-12
- Her research begins to show that the plague is not just delivered through the body but completed through it.
- Kel'Thuzad provides the missing theoretical bridge:
  - the body as answering chamber
  - preserved pattern rather than mere corruption
  - low-fidelity vs high-fidelity survival through death

Chapter 12 is where the previous observations stop being fragments and become one coherent model.

## Part 6: Outline and Prose Rules

### Rule 1: Jaina is never clueless

Do not write the early investigation as if Jaina is staring into blank mystery with no necromantic literacy at all.

She should make smart, specific, escalating reads:
- identify necromantic signature
- isolate probable ingress
- distinguish living-tissue conditioning from simple residue
- notice repeated-exposure thresholds
- recognize deliberate magical craftsmanship

### Rule 2: Jaina is still incomplete

Do not let her solve the full theorem early.

Before Chapter 12 she should not fully and confidently explain:
- the outer shell versus the deeper operative layer
- the body as the answering chamber in finalized terms
- the exact reason the dead can move directionally after death
- the distinction between low-fidelity village reanimation and high-fidelity self-preservation

### Rule 3: The midpoint conversation must feel like completion, not contradiction

Kel'Thuzad should not reveal a brand-new idea from nowhere.

He should give names, hierarchy, and philosophical completion to things Jaina has already half-seen:
- why her field reads kept stalling
- why repeated ingestion mattered
- why the body never behaved like a passive victim
- why the villagers rose as ghouls while he seeks something more exact

### Rule 4: Keep the diction fantasy-facing

Even in planning files, prefer:
- contamination
- ingress
- host response
- preserved pattern
- answering chamber
- necromantic signature
- low-fidelity / high-fidelity preservation

Avoid modern medical or laboratory diction that would drag the mechanism out of setting.
