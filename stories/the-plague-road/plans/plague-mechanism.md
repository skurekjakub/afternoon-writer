# The Plague Mechanism

- One system, two resolutions:
  - field plague = cheap mass deployment, low-fidelity undead
  - Kel'Thuzad's private work = expensive high-fidelity preservation of self
- Same logic underneath both. The difference is fidelity, not category.

## Field plague, in plain terms
1. Grain carries the outer shell.
   - Strong magical signature.
   - Survives storage, baking, and travel.
   - Misleads early investigation because it is loud but not complete.
2. Repeated ingestion conditions the body.
   - Gut first, then wider system response.
   - The body is not just poisoned; it becomes part of the mechanism.
3. False rings waste time.
   - Grain, ambient saturation, and visible reanimation are all real clues.
   - None of them alone explain the whole chain.
4. Death completes the lock.
   - The conditioned body answers when the host dies.
   - The dead are not being driven only from outside; the host has been prepared to respond.
5. What rises is a choir, not a person.
   - Motion, appetite, crude convergence, rough answerability.
   - Cult handlers sharpen that behavior into usable obedience.

## Kel'Thuzad's added layer
- He wants more than a ghoul.
- His private work adds the preserving scaffold that holds memory, intellect, and identity together after death.
- Villagers get conditioned behavior. Kel'Thuzad wants continuity of self.
- He understands the system more deeply than anyone alive in Lordaeron, but even he is still downstream of Ner'zhul and the larger design.

## Why Dalaran banned the field
- Once necromancy starts preserving pattern instead of just moving corpses, it attacks personhood, consent, civic trust, and the boundary of death itself.
- Jaina can recognize brilliance and necromantic design early.
- She lacks the banned conceptual frame until Kel'Thuzad gives it names.

## Jaina's ladder
- Ch 2-3: sees necromancy, design, saturation, ingress, and reanimation. Cannot yet split carrier, host change, and death trigger.
- Ch 4-6: isolates ingestion, repeated exposure, and body participation. Still missing the final step.
- Ch 7-8: sees layered design, live monitoring, handler-directed obedience, and the fact that the loudest signature is not the whole mechanism.
- Ch 9-12: research plus Kel'Thuzad finally turn fragments into one model: body as answering chamber, low fidelity vs high fidelity preservation.

## Writer rules
- Jaina is never clueless.
- Jaina is still incomplete before Ch 12.
- Kel'Thuzad completes the model; he does not replace it with a brand-new one.
- Keep diction fantasy-facing: contamination, ingress, answering chamber, preserved pattern, choir.
