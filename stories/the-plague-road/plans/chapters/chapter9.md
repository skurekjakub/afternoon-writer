# Chapter 9: The Trap

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Opens after the Andorhal victory has widened into several weeks of Eastweald patrols, tracking the sickness, and hunting cult hideouts.
**Where:** Cult-held Eastweald north of Andorhal, on the approach toward Barov-held country.
**Travel:** Mounted travel on Thalassian horses into the Eastweald; on foot through orchards, outposts, and the outskirts.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Goal:** use fresh clues from cult hideouts before signatures fade, uncover the next layer of the Cult, and turn the Andorhal victory into a path to their leaders.

## What we know

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- Grain is the poison, Andorhal was the heart of the plague, and treated grain is already moving through northern Lordaeron.
- Kel'Thuzad has been named as the mastermind operating somewhere north of Andorhal.
- The Cult of the Damned is a hidden organization with living members, mage support, and disciplined ranks.
- Barov holdings recur in the records, but the Barov name is still paper trail rather than confirmed guilt.
- Jaina's sensitivity keeps the party ahead of the crown's awareness, but still one step behind the spread itself.
- Millhaven remains the first village lost to the curse; the eastward spread since then has mostly hidden itself inside ordinary-looking sickness or quietly occupied ground.
- The old seller / newer grain-man / repeated farewell puzzle is still thin, weak, and unresolved.
- The four women now move as one flawless hunting pack.

**What Sylvanas doesn't know yet**
- Kel'Thuzad's exact lair.
- Whether the Barovs are patrons, hosts, or a corrupted noble house.
- Whether Farstrider and Kirin Tor leadership will back the hunt once more evidence vanishes.
- Whether the repeated farewell belongs to one being, one cult cabal, or coincidence.
- Which of the Eastweald trails will actually reach the inner circle of the Cult.

**What NOT to do**
- Do not imply any Mal'Ganis identification.
- Do not confirm that the Barov name equals total guilt.
- Do not assume that Arthas will automatically help once reached.
- Do not flatten the old seller / newer grain-man clue into a solved answer.

**What they learn by the end**
- Lyriel is dead and their pack is permanently shattered.
- Jaina leaves with the Barov paper trail and fractured spell fragments.
- Sylvanas and Cyndia leave with the Arthas / Hearthglen thread under collapsing political backing.
- The repeated farewell sharpens again, but remains weak and unresolved.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- At the start of the chapter, Sylvanas believes that hard relentless hunting and fast decisions are all they need to break the next layer of the Cult wide open.
- She trusts her own excellence, tempo, and force, assuming those traits will carry her team through territory that is actually specifically prepared to punish exactly those strengths.
- This test breaks her confidence. A fading trace of necromancy invites a hard push; Sylvanas makes a split-second physical call that lands completely wrong, and Lyriel dies because Sylvanas read the terrain using only half the necessary methods.
- She is forced to accept that treating Jaina's magical sweeps as just "support" to real combat is a fatal mistake. Jaina's method now defines whether a physical charge is safe at all.
- By chapter close, Sylvanas knows the small scouting pack cannot keep carrying this burden alone. She intimately knows the heavy price of underweighting Jaina's expertise. Chapters 10 and 11 will open under the weight of this forced split, deep grief, and the bitter new habit of pricing time in lives instead of clean maneuvers.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Sylvanas initially sees Jaina as their ritual expert, magical warning line, and the partner whose timing determines whether a path is real or bait.
- Sylvanas trusts Jaina deeply now, but subconsciously still feels the actual, final the hardest choices belong on Sylvanas's side of the line.
- After Lyriel's death, this assumption is destroyed. Sylvanas cannot pretend the magic read is just support work anymore; it is explicitly essential for survival itself.
- Their shorthand is practiced and immediate. After the loss of Lyriel, every exchange between them gets heavier, shorter, and far more careful.

### Partnership
- The four women move almost entirely on reflex at the start.
- The cost of one bad call proves that every blind spot belongs to both Jaina and Sylvanas. By the end, they realize the split is not just a dire necessity to chase two leads—it actively hurts the work itself.
- The deep rhythm is locked in. They no longer explain the basics to each other.
- The grief remains hard and practical. The proof of their deepened bond is shown in their deliberately changed tempo and the agonizing decision to split, not in tearful speeches.

## Scene 1: The Approach

**What happens:** The rangers move at peak precision until a fading magical scar invites the first fatal mistake.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Where we start:** Eastweald hunt is now about finding the cult's inner circle.

### Beat 1
- The party advances perfectly through Cult-held Eastweald toward a marked cult hideout. The landscape isn't just sick; it has been physically shaped. Cleared roads, trimmed sight lines, and dead orchards prove the territory is run like an actively occupied district.
- Sylvanas correctly reads the region as a district under heavy cult supply wagons, not a random chain of ruins. The four-woman pack has become seamless after weeks in the field. They don't know which settlement ahead matters enough to run the cult to ground, or how much the enemy already knows about their hunting pattern.
- Dead orchards with bark peeling in grey strips; pale, sickly fungus in fields that should still be producing food; a road so pristine and empty it feels deliberately hollowed out.
- The perfect rhythm only lasts until Jaina finds the exact thing worth stopping for.

### Beat 2
- Jaina detects a rapidly fading magical scar inside a ruined farmhouse. They must decide whether to pass it and keep their momentum, or stop to let Jaina read the residue and potentially uncover the plague's full spell to wake the dead.
- Sylvanas decides to halt the pack and search. The signature is recent, not ancient residue, and reads as mass-necromancy rather than lingering sickness. The farmhouse is a recent Cult hideout. They don't know how much time they have before the signature evaporates entirely, or if the site was fully abandoned or deliberately left warm as bait.
- Jaina stopping solidly mid-stride with the specific look that means her magic found something huge; a thick heat-haze shimmer distorting the cold air over a half-collapsed building; the rest of the pack instantly holding their ground because Jaina says the place is still loud in the wrong register.

## Scene 2: The Scholar's Blunder

**What happens:** Jaina's best instinct—to stop and understand—creates an exposed target that gets Sylvanas wounded.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Cult ambushers.
**Where we start:** The farmhouse may contain the plague's missing dark spellwork, but the residue is fading exceptionally fast.

### Beat 3
- Jaina needs fifteen minutes in a hot enemy site to read the dark spellwork. Sylvanas knows a standing still in active territory is exactly how skilled teams get killed, but she grants the time because the arcane knowledge is undeniable.
- The site contains ritual scoring, reagent remnants, and a partially intact matrix tied directly to mass-necromancy. Jaina believes this read could finally yield necromantic bindings, how the soul binds to the rotting flesh, and the beginnings of a cure. Sylvanas yields because Jaina's arcane calls have been right too often to ignore. They don't know if Jaina will get the full read before contact is made, or if the site is actively watched.
- A flagstone floor cut with dark geometric lines that still carry faint, pulsing light; one remaining jar of amber reagent moving far too slowly when tilted; Jaina dropping instantly into deep magical trance while Sylvanas starts counting the seconds.


### Beat 4
- Eight minutes in, living Cult ambushers aggressively hit the farmhouse. Jaina is still emerging from deep magical trance, forcing Sylvanas to cover the withdrawal from a highly compromised doorway.
- Sylvanas manages a fighting retreat, but the full ritual read is lost and Sylvanas takes a serious wound pulling Jaina out. The enemy clearly anticipated that a strong magical signature would draw curious investigators into a static, vulnerable position. Jaina only salvages broken spell fragments: usable necromantic bindings and proof that the living body is part of the answer, but no complete spell to wake the dead. They don't know who set the ambush timing or if the broken spell fragments will be enough later to matter.
- The sharp, physical crack of a crossbow from the tree line instead of mindless undead noise; Sylvanas's ranger barks cutting violently through the room as Jaina abruptly tears herself free of the matrix; hot blood already visibly soaking through Sylvanas's leathers by the time they clear the doorway.


### Beat 5
- In the wounded silence that follows, Sylvanas and Jaina have to decide whether to voice their mutual accusation or reprice their lost time and keep moving with what they salvaged.
- They dress the wound, mentally file the broken spell fragments, and stubbornly continue the mission without naming the blame they both perfectly understand. Sylvanas's wound is survivable but very practical: her draw strength, motion, and patience all take a hit. Jaina's partial read is now permanently fixed at "incomplete but critical." Sylvanas quietly notices that Jaina is now treating time as a physical cost, something she wouldn't have done a few chapters ago. They don't know if the broken spell fragments will ever become a full countermeasure, or how much the wound will distort Sylvanas's next call.
- Jaina packing the bloody cut with practiced, steady hands she completely lacked chapters ago; a stark white bandage already spotting bright red at the edges; Sylvanas answering the unspoken time question instead of addressing the emotional one.

## Scene 3: The Soldier's Blunder

**What happens:** Sylvanas's greatest pride—reading a perimeter and acting on it—becomes the exact thing that gets Lyriel killed.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Where we start:** The team is wounded, moving with fractured spell fragments, and trying to salvage the terrible day by reaching a larger enemy compound.

### Beat 6
- Sylvanas reads a perfectly concealed physical seam in the patrol pattern outside the compound. She aggressively moves the pack through the gap before Jaina finishes the arcane sweep that would classify the seam as bait.
- Sylvanas commits the rangers into the opening, utterly blind to the one layer of terrain she naturally cannot read. Her fresh wound and the lost eight minutes compress Sylvanas's patience, but don't make her stupid; the perimeter is just sophisticated enough to manufacture extremely attractive dead ground. Jaina is still running passive magical vigilance even after the ambush. They don't know if Jaina's warning will land before the trap completes, or how many layers of magical defense the compound actually carries.
- Low ground lying perfectly under scrub oak, forming a physical corridor just too clean to distrust at a glance; Sylvanas aggressively reading sight lines, timing, and cover while habitually ignoring the invisible arcane layer; Jaina opening her mouth to speak as the physical line is already fatally moving.


### Beat 7
- To survive the deadly trap, Jaina tries to throw a barrier, but the ward is intricately calibrated to Farstrider spacing. It detonates directly on Lyriel's position before any ranger reflexes can possibly matter.
- Lyriel dies instantly. The corridor reveals itself as a horrific sequence of terrible, high-arcanum magical traps built specifically to eat a disciplined scouting pack. The enemy understands ranger tactics well enough to weaponize their exact spacing against them. Jaina would have detected the ward almost immediately if Sylvanas had given her the pause. The traps are lethal magical wards, not mere plague residue. They don't know who designed this layer of the defense or how deep the leadership is beyond a team capable of this level of trapwork.
- Lyriel standing exactly where she should be in formation, and then horribly not being there at all; Jaina's barrier catching the second blast of death magic in a blinding burst of cold light; ozone and sweet-rot wrongness hitting all at once in the narrow, ruined corridor.


### Beat 8
- They are left in a state of shocked absence. They have to decide whether to stop under the crushing weight of what just happened, or keep moving and let their tactics forcefully rewrite themselves around the missing right flank.
- They continue the mission, but with devastating, permanent habits born of the agonizing gap. Cyndia starts checking the right flank twice and will compulsively keep doing it. Sylvanas begins waiting for Jaina's magical sweep at chokepoints without ever admitting out loud why she is suddenly pausing. Jaina's once-abstract question of "how much time" is now the literal blood-price tag on every sustained magical trance. They don't know if the hunt can absorb one more mistake like this, or if they are still enough to carry the two massive threads alone.

## Scene 4: The Split

**What happens:** Bring the surviving clues together, close political walls on both leads, and forcefully split the Barov thread and the Arthas thread apart.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lor'themar's runner, Antonidas (via sending stone).
**Where we start:** The team has lost Lyriel, holds fractured spell fragments, and still has two live directions pointing furiously outward from the Eastweald.

### Beat 9
- In the exhausted ridge regrouping, the day's losses and fragments finally produce two urgent directions at once: Barov paper trail recovered from records, and a Hearthglen/Arthas lead born from Cyndia sighting a retreating figure matching a military column.
- The proof is very real on both fronts, but it can no longer be safely pursued by a single, intact pack. The recovered records conclusively tie the grain system back to Barov holdings. Cyndia saw a retreating figure mention Arthas at Hearthglen. The exact same farewell phrase returns again to Sylvanas's mind, strengthening the old seller / newer grain-man file without definitively resolving it. They don't know whether the repeated farewell is one being, one cult habit, or pure coincidence, or whether the Barov trail proves guilt, corruption, or just proximity.
- Wind cutting coldly across exposed stone while Cyndia delivers scouting reports in a completely stripped-flat voice; Sylvanas pausing only a tiny fraction of a second when the farewell phrase hits the old file in her head; papers and fresh memory both offering massive answers that feel too incomplete to trust.


### Beat 10
- They face exhausted pressure from every direction at once. They must decide whether to keep the investigation contained inside collapsing Farstrider and Kirin Tor credibility, or to find a larger outside power before both Dalaran and Silvermoon forcibly shut their hunt down forever.
- They completely look to Arthas as a necessary outside power and accept that the Barov thread must be run quickly through Jaina's remaining status as an Archmage's apprentice before that shuts too. Lor'themar's inspectors found cleaned sites, and the Convocation is actively beginning to question Sylvanas's entire mission. Antonidas's inquisition into her methods is the Kirin Tor formally building the paper case against Jaina. Arthas's royal authority matters immensely now because his royal backing could overrule two rigid powers that are currently choking on their own court rules. They don't know whether Arthas will actually read the plague as a magical plague or only as a target, or whether Jaina's remaining Kirin Tor credibility is enough to get her through Barov doors.
- Lor'themar's handwriting tight enough to visibly read the immense political pressure in it; the sending stone carrying Antonidas's measured voice like cold, formal distance made completely audible; the shared, grim understanding that both women are actively losing their political backing at the exact same time.


### Beat 11

- Both threads get the hunter perfectly suited to them, but the pack splinters at the exact moment both women have learned not to ever trust their solo blind spots again. Jaina takes the Barov thread entirely alone, carrying the paper trail, Cyndia's field notes on the estate approach and layout, and the incredibly precious fractured spell fragments from the ruined farmhouse. Sylvanas takes the Hearthglen/Arthas thread with Cyndia, using her title as Ranger-General and the argument that they desperately need a full army. Lyriel's death is the heavy, unspoken weight inside the decision even when the choice is clear. They don't know which thread will successfully reach command first, or whether separation is the move that saves the hunt or breaks it forever.
