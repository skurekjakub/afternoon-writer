# Chapter 23: The Throne Room

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 22. Opens at the palace service entrance and closes in custody after the Imperial Chamber engagement.
**Where:** Capital City palace service passages.
**Travel:** Stealth movement on foot through service corridors -> live combat across the Imperial Chamber -> escorted arrest on foot into custody.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers.
**Goal:** Reach Frostmourne, buy three to five seconds, and break the blade's hold before Arthas and the room around him finish the kingdom.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- The anti-blade framework is real: frost for rhythm, arcane for breach, fire for transformation.
- Sylvanas is the combat partner who will buy the seconds the cast needs.
- Antonidas and two Kirin Tor magi got the pair to a side service lane and cannot come any farther without collapsing stealth.
- Arthas is already in the palace and Uther cannot reach the king by formal means.
- Jaina's reserves are critically low, but her sensitivity is sharp enough to track the blade directly through walls.
- Cyndia and the rangers must hold the exit lane because the room itself is all the plan can currently afford.

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- Whether Terenas is still alive when they reach the chamber.
- Whether Uther or Antonidas intersects them again before the room closes in a way that matters.
- Whether the seam stays usable once the spell is under live pressure rather than cave observation.
- What the political shape of survival looks like even if the spell works.

**What NOT to do:**
- No clean or total solution; the best possible outcome here is still imperfect.
- No guarantee that any outsider witnesses the murder itself.
- No promise that breaking Arthas's hold on the blade frees the trapped souls inside it or cuts the deeper tether north.
- No narrator access to Sylvanas's or Arthas's interiority.

**What they learn by the end**
- Terenas dies before S&J can stop the murder.
- Jaina's three-school sequence ruptures Arthas's bearer-bond and scars Frostmourne's field from the inside without cutting the deeper tether to Ner'zhul.
- Sylvanas's second arrow kills Arthas through the seam Jaina forces open permanently.
- The blade remains active, unheld, and full of trapped souls after the victory.
- Antonidas and the Kirin Tor cell never reach the chamber in time to become useful witnesses.
- The room's evidence profile is catastrophic for anyone trying to prove innocence later.
- The palace guard arrests the women who just saved the kingdom because the room reads the wrong way to every late witness.

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- The framework works on paper and in her head; now it has to survive a real room, a real king, and a blade close enough to hurt simply by being near it.
- Reach casting range, hold the sequence steady, and convert the seam from theory into victory before Sylvanas's bought seconds run out.
- Near-empty reserves, the blade's proximity, Arthas and Terenas in the same room, and the knowledge that Uther may be close enough to help and still too far away to matter.
- Jaina still wants the spell to produce a clean answer if she executes it perfectly, even though the room has already made cleanliness impossible.
- The chapter asks whether she can accept an imperfect save - king dead, souls trapped, blade still active, deeper tether uncut - if imperfection is the only form survival can take.
- Cast now without witness or reinforcement, or wait and let the blade keep feeding.
- Jaina goes from theorist and tracker to the person who scars the blade from the inside and survives just long enough to see the cost.
- Chapter 24 opens in cells and chains with the spell successful, the evidence damning, and the blade still whispering overhead.

- Combat partner, terrain-maker in a room with almost no terrain, and the body holding the death knight's attention while Jaina works.

- Prince-shaped wielder of the blade rather than a reachable version of the man Jaina knew.

- Peak - every learned tell and shared rhythm compressed into one room and one engagement.
- The partnership proves itself capable of doing the impossible and also proves that impossible victories still come with ruin attached.

## Scene 1: The Infiltration

**What happens:** Get the pair into the palace, prove the interior is already compromised, and spend Uther's credibility just outside the room that needs it most.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Uther
**Where we start:** The plan exists, the palace is open, and the room ahead matters more than any outer defense.

### Beat 1
- Enter the palace unseen and place Cyndia's team on the extraction route.
- Every second spent moving carefully is another second the blade can use; every careless sound risks collapsing the room before the plan starts. The service door gets them inside cleanly and the split works, but the moment Jaina crosses into the passages the blade's proximity starts exacting a bodily price. The service lanes are unguarded because peacetime habit still rules the palace margins. Cyndia takes the exit route with two rangers while one ranger stays nearer the pair. Jaina's channel gets louder enough indoors that the bread-taste becomes almost unmanageable. They do not know whether the service split is enough if the room implodes or how many undead nodes actually stand between them and the chamber.
- Kitchen grease and lye in narrow stone corridors; Service door wood worn smooth by years of trays and carts; Cyndia's last sweep before she disappears around the corner.
### Beat 2
- Remove the undead chamber guards without alerting Arthas.
- The sentries look living at a distance, and if either gets a signal off the entire plan dies outside the room. Sylvanas and Jaina clear the guards cleanly and silently, but their presence proves the palace is already seeded with the dead while the court keeps pretending nothing is wrong. Jaina can distinguish blade-echoes from living bodies through resonance alone. Sylvanas can read the same wrongness from motion and stillness even without magic. Arthas has placed undead sentries inside the king's own house while the living staff continue routine work. They do not know whether the dead sentries are isolated nodes or part of a responsive chain or whether the service door concealment still holds once the chamber opens.
- Halberds held too still in torchlight; Armor and meat hitting stone without any human cry; Bread-taste roaring louder with the throne-room door only paces away.
### Beat 3
- Use Uther as validation and lose the room's timing, or let him go get the Order and accept that his absence from the murder will later destroy his usefulness in court.
- S&J go on. Uther runs for the Silver Hand. Uther confirms the same wrongness in the palace with paladin senses. He is already a discarded voice in a building that should have listened to him days ago. The pair need seconds in the chamber now, not thirty more minutes of moral correctness. They do not know whether Uther returns in time to see anything that matters or whether the Silver Hand can affect the room after the blade feeds again.
- Plate armor humming lightly through the corridor stones; Uther's voice rough from repeated failed audience attempts; the bread-taste standing in for a clock no one else can hear.

## Scene 2: The Murder

**What happens:** Reveal the room, let the murder happen, and prove immediately that conventional force is not enough.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Arthas, Terenas, two undead throne guards, living guards at distance
**Where we start:** The pair are at the service entrance with no credible witness in the room.

### Beat 4
- Enter the chamber unseen long enough to read positions and locate the seam at close range.
- The blade's immediate proximity opens Jaina's channel all the way and starts damaging her before the fight even begins. The pair get concealment behind curtain and column, and Jaina confirms the seam is real and reachable at close range, but the nosebleed starts the moment the room opens to her senses. Terenas is still alive when they reach the chamber. Frostmourne's seam is exactly where Jaina found it in the cave. The room is every bit as tactically hostile as Sylvanas expected - open, echoing, ceremony architecture, no forgiving cover. They do not know whether the pair can move before Arthas kills the king or whether any cast is possible once the blade starts fully broadcasting.
- White sunlight pouring through the octagonal dome window; Golden seal of Lordaeron on polished marble; Warm blood from Jaina's first nosebleed under the blade's full signal.
### Beat 5
- Prevent the murder if there is still a heartbeat left to prevent.
- They are one beat too late, and the first perfect arrow proves the blade can simply erase ordinary killing logic. Terenas dies, and the first arrow that would end any living target only proves that Arthas now lives inside a different ruleset. Arthas kills Terenas with no hesitation and no visible human struggle. Frostmourne feeds visibly through the murder. Sylvanas's perfect throat shot fails and reveals the pair's position at once. They do not know whether any conventional damage matters now except as distraction or how much stronger the blade becomes after consuming a king.
- Crown ringing on marble and rolling in the sun; Dark blood in Arthas's throat wound closing instead of acting like blood should; One short line from Arthas flattening fatherhood into procedure.

## Scene 3: The Fight

**What happens:** Let Sylvanas buy the seconds, Jaina force the seam open, and the partnership win in the only way this room still permits.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Arthas
**Where we start:** The king is dead, concealment is gone, and the only remaining answer is the plan itself.

### Beat 6
- Put Arthas fully on Sylvanas and create Jaina's approach lane behind him.
- Frostmourne's consuming field starts eating Sylvanas the moment she closes, and the chamber offers almost no forgiving terrain. Sylvanas gets Arthas moving and facing forward, but every second of success visibly drains the body Jaina is depending on. Bow work now exists only to steer Arthas's attention, not to kill him. The columns matter because the open floor offers Sylvanas nothing else. The consuming field drains warmth and speed in real time instead of as abstract threat. They do not know whether the bought window lasts long enough for all three schools or what happens if Sylvanas's speed margin collapses before Jaina reaches the seam.
- Column stone cracking under Frostmourne's impact; Cold thickening across the floor in a visible radius; Blood on Sylvanas's forearm staying cold as it runs.
### Beat 7
- Hold the frost synchronization long enough for the seam to stay trackable under live combat.
- Jaina has to maintain exact rhythm while Sylvanas takes hits and the room keeps trying to break concentration apart. The frost lock holds and the seam stays visible, but Sylvanas takes a real wound and the survival margin narrows sharply. Jaina can tune herself to the blade's cycle under pressure, not just in post-fight analysis. Sylvanas taking a hit is almost enough to break the cast before it starts. The field's weakest lane is behind Arthas, exactly where the plan needed it to be. They do not know whether Jaina has enough left to complete the full sequence or whether Sylvanas can keep Arthas interested for the remaining seconds.
- Jaina's cold overlay distinct from Frostmourne's cold; Sylvanas hitting marble with one knee and rising anyway; the seam pulsing like a tiny gap between hammer blows.
### Beat 8
- Complete the sequence - arcane through the seam, fire through the breached bearer-channel, and break Arthas's clean hold on the blade without detonating its deeper binding.
- The cast demands all remaining reserves and offers no clean way out if it works. The spell succeeds and ruptures Arthas's bearer-channel, but the price is Jaina dropping to the marble almost emptied out of usable strength while the deeper tether still hums beneath what she just damaged. The binding stack has layers; the local bearer-channel can be burnt out without shattering the deeper tether. Frostmourne's pull north remains, but its clean local conduit is scarred and unstable. The seam becomes a permanent flaw once the bearer-channel burns through. They do not know whether a still-tethered blade leaves a lethal wielder standing once the local conduit fails or what a wounded, unheld Frostmourne means for the souls already trapped inside the weapon.
- Arcane force crossing the seam as a colorless punch through the gap; Fire turning the blade's inner channels amber from within; Jaina's face hitting cold marble when the cast finishes.
### Beat 9
- Kill Arthas through the permanent flaw in the blade's defenses.
- Sylvanas is drained, wounded, and down to an emergency recurve and one remaining arrow. **yes** - the arrow passes the seam, takes the eye, and drops Arthas where the first shot could not. The permanent flaw matters immediately, not just in theory. Sylvanas's reserve weapon and final arrow are enough once the blade can no longer fully close around them. Arthas dies like a puppet cut loose rather than like a prince receiving a noble ending. They do not know whether the room is safer now that the wielder is dead or whether the blade remains merely dangerous or actively hungry without a hand.
- Small recurve bow instead of longbow elegance; Arrow slipping through a gap the field can no longer seal; Blade and body hitting marble as two separate final sounds.

## Scene 4: The Aftermath

**What happens:** Reveal the imperfect nature of the victory and flip the heroes into criminals in a single room-reading.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Frostmourne, palace guards, later Uther and Silver Hand off-beat
**Where we start:** Arthas is down and the blade no longer rides cleanly through him.

### Beat 10
- There is no decision yet - only the room learning what an unheld Frostmourne sounds like.
- None. Survival takes the form of enduring the burst. The wounded blade erupts in frost and resonance the moment it hits the floor. Trapped soul-echoes flash visibly, including Terenas's freshest imprint. The transformation killed Arthas's clean conduit but did not free the dead or cut the deeper tether. They do not know whether the room can contain the weapon's new state or how anyone outside this chamber will ever understand what just happened.
- Frost racing through floor grooves like veins; Faces above the blade for two heartbeats and no longer; the room temperature dropping past ordinary winter into something with no season.
### Beat 11
- Fight exhausted living guards while the blade still hums, or surrender and let the room's evidence lock the pair into a story they did not write.
- Surrender. Not because the accusation is fair, but because there is nothing left to spend on explaining the room to people who arrived late. Guards see two foreigners, two dead royals, and a cursed sword on the floor, and that is enough to decide the shape of events. No one in the first wave dares touch Frostmourne. Uther and the Silver Hand arrive too late to witness the murder and early enough only to find the aftermath. They do not know whether any legal process in the palace can hold the blade safely or whether any witness can now stop the tribunal logic from forming.
- Bronze doors opening on the chamber's freeze; Spearpoints lowering toward two women too exhausted to explain anything well; Frost on Jaina's cheek as hands bind her where she fell.
