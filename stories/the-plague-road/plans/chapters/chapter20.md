# Chapter 20: The Voyage

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 19. Opens at the northern coast after the ride out of Lordaeron's interior and closes with landfall at Daggercap Bay in Northrend.
**Where:** Northern Lordaeron harbor road and quay.
**Travel:** Horseback arrival -> harbor work on foot -> schooner crossing aboard the *Wolfheart* -> dockside landfall.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Captain Dagny Ironhull and the *Wolfheart* crew.
**Goal:** Secure passage north and keep the pursuit alive across open water after both institutions have fallen away.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- North is no longer a theory lane; Sylvanas chose it over recall and Jaina chose it over Dalaran.
- Arthas already has a sea lead and a hand-picked force that followed him through Stratholme.
- The resonance thread grows stronger toward the coast, which means the source really does lie across the water.
- The relay network dies at the harbor; once they sail, no institution is carrying them.
- The pursuit now depends on money, a willing ship, and whatever Jaina can still hear.

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- Whether any captain will accept a Northrend run at all.
- How directly the source can answer her probing once she is on open water.
- What the sea crossing will cost her body if she has to force it.
- What Arthas is chasing in Northrend besides the source itself.

**What NOT to do:**
- No Muradin briefing or Frostmourne specifics yet.
- No full explanation of the source as a named cosmic hierarchy.
- No promise that arrival means they can still catch Arthas before he acts.
- No assumption that Jaina can push her Frostmourne sensitivity wider and then simply settle it again.

**What they learn by the end**
- Captain Dagny's *Wolfheart* can get them to Northrend if Jaina pays the crossing cost in power.
- The source opposes them directly through supernatural headwinds once the ship is committed.
- The forced stillness of the voyage opens a new honesty lane between Jaina and Sylvanas.
- Jaina can push through the opposition, but doing so sharpens her sensitivity and makes the bread-taste part of her baseline.
- Sylvanas will stop Jaina when Jaina will not stop herself.
- Daggercap Bay proves they arrived, but it also proves Arthas is still weeks ahead in practical terms.

## Scene 1: The Harbor

**What happens:** Turn the north choice into literal departure before the coast can reclaim either woman for an institution that already failed them.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Dagny, harbor hands
**Where we start:** North is chosen, the relay network ends here, and the chase dies if they cannot buy passage.

### Beat 1
- Secure a captain and a vessel willing to sail for Northrend.
- Northrend is a bad run under the best conditions, and this passage offers no institutional backing - only urgency, money, and risk. Dagny agrees to carry them on the *Wolfheart*, but she agrees as a captain pricing danger, not as an ally stepping into their cause. The *Wolfheart* is a hard-water schooner already working northern routes. Dagny accepts that the job is dangerous and raises the price instead of refusing the run. The coast is the last place where Farstrider or Dalaran infrastructure can still touch this pursuit at all. They do not know whether the sea itself will let them through or whether the captain's weather clause becomes more than a bargaining point.
- Salt wind and gull noise over tarred rope; Horses steaming at the quay after the hard northbound ride; Dagny looking at the weather and at Jaina as if pricing both together.
### Beat 2
- Board, settle the formation into shipboard roles, and convert "north" into a vessel, a deck, and a tide window.
- The practical work is easy; the emotional weight is not. Jaina has to cross the last edge of land carrying a sending-stone that is already dead. The formation boards cleanly and the ship is ready on the tide, but the transition makes the institutional severance feel heavier rather than lighter. The horses and land gear stay behind; from here forward the chase belongs to the ship. Jaina still keeps the cold sending-stone instead of discarding it. Sylvanas and Cyndia fall into shipboard discipline as fast as they fall into road discipline. They do not know whether Jaina can keep the resonance thread useful without making herself visible to the source or what the crew will do when the crossing turns unnatural.
- Wet planks under boots; Saddles and road dust left on the quay instead of taken aboard; the dead weight of the stone in Jaina's coat pocket.

## Scene 2: The Departure

**What happens:** Kill the last easy routes home and let the coastline become absence.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Where we start:** Passage is secured, but every message or support lane that matters still belongs to land.

### Beat 3
- Hand off the last shore-side instructions and accept that everything beyond this harbor runs on competence and guesswork.
- The relay network can carry route facts, not rescue, and Jaina gets nothing from the stone except the proof that she already cut herself loose. Cyndia leaves a clean final relay trail and the formation departs with its route known, but the real help line is gone before the ship clears the breakwater. Cyndia leaves precise harbor and destination notes with the last usable Farstrider contact. Jaina tests the stone only enough to prove it stays cold and mute. No escort, no portal, and no institutional correction arrives before departure. They do not know whether anything sent from shore can matter before Arthas acts or whether Jaina's next contact with the source will be observation or retaliation.
- Brass relay cylinder warm from a runner's hand; the stone staying dark even under Jaina's touch; Harbor bells marking the tide while nobody calls them back.
### Beat 4
- Clear the harbor and commit the pursuit to open water.
- The ship moves exactly as it should, which makes the emotional cost land harder; leaving is technically smooth and personally absolute. The *Wolfheart* gets cleanly to sea, but the disappearing coast makes the loss of ordinary world, rank, and safety newly concrete. The Eastern Kingdoms vanish in stages rather than all at once. Shipboard life immediately reduces the formation to watch rotations, bunks, and deck lanes. Jaina feels the north thread more clearly once nothing landbound is crowding it. They do not know whether arrival is still possible on usable time or whether the sea is empty or actively hostile.
- Coastline becoming first a cliff line, then a grey strip, then nothing; Rigging strain replacing road noise; Sea-cold taking over where harbor smells used to be.

## Scene 3: The Crossing

**What happens:** Put Jaina's gift and the source in direct contest, then turn forced stillness into the partnership beat the road kept deferring.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Where we start:** They are at sea, northbound, and fully beyond land support.

### Beat 5
- Identify the new resistance and decide whether the voyage can continue through it.
- The headwinds are not weather. They carry the same frequency family as the plague, which means the source has noticed them and is pushing back. The ship's ordinary progress collapses, and Jaina realizes her own northward probing is what made them legible to the thing opposing them. The headwinds behave like intention rather than weather - constant, directional, and timed. Jaina's resonance sensitivity is bidirectional; every probe north announced her. Dagny does not break the contract when she hears the truth; she recalculates what Jaina can buy with her body. They do not know whether Jaina can force them through without ruining herself or whether the source's opposition has a limit.
- A gale that feels like a door slammed shut; Sailcloth snapping against a wind that never offers a natural gap; Jaina reaching into the wind and feeling something reach back.
### Beat 6
- How do duty and certainty work once the institution defining them becomes an obstacle instead of a frame?
- Neither woman backs away from the question, even when the question starts sounding too much like Arthas and Kel'Thuzad from another angle. Sylvanas stopped treating the Convocation as the relevant variable long before she openly defied it. Jaina recognizes that her Dalaran severance followed the same shape - action first, spoken break second. The first outline of the certainty parallel surfaces: they are chasing a man destroyed by conviction while sailing north on conviction of their own. They do not know whether their certainty is different in kind or only in consequence or what the partnership becomes once there is no institution left around it.
- Mainmast cold against Jaina's back; Ship creak and contested water under a conversation with no witnesses; Sylvanas settling across from her without asking permission because that part is already solved.
### Beat 7
- Recover the voyage timeline before provisions and distance make the crossing impossible.
- Jaina can match the headwinds only by sustained channeling that turns her body into the second engine of the ship. The *Wolfheart* regains lost distance and crosses the halfway point, but the recovery cost is Jaina's reserves, her blood, and a channel she may not be able to narrow again. The crossing becomes a raw endurance contest between Jaina's sustained output and the source's resistance. The bread-taste stops being an intermittent symptom and becomes a constant companion. The channel widens far enough that Jaina can feel not just a point in the north but a cold structure answering back. They do not know whether recovery after arrival is possible before the next fight or whether the widened channel is damage, asset, or both.
- Blood drying on the back of Jaina's hand while her hands never lower; the hull shuddering between two opposed winds; Dagny watching her like a captain watching an engine burn too hot.
### Beat 8
- If the ship can still move, who gets to decide when the cost is too high - the person pushing, or the person watching what the push is doing to her?
- Sylvanas decides for her. One word. No debate. Jaina will not stop herself once arrival becomes mathematically possible. Sylvanas's authority still reaches the part of Jaina that obeys competence. The care lands through command, water, warmth, and shared silence rather than through explanation. They do not know whether the widening channel will narrow again once the push ends or what the source has done to Jaina by holding that line open for so long.
- One word crossing a noisy deck and landing harder than any shouted order; Leather waterskin warm from Sylvanas's cloak; the natural wind reappearing under the killed channel as if there had always been weather beneath the contest.

## Scene 4: The Approach

**What happens:** Turn shipboard cost into philosophical pressure, then into ice, coast, and hard proof that the chase is still late.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Where we start:** The crossing will succeed, but Jaina paid for that success visibly.

### Beat 9
- If Kel'Thuzad, Arthas, and Jaina all broke from their institutions because they were certain they were right, what actually separates Jaina from the men whose certainty destroyed everything around them?
- No answer yet. The question is planted and carried forward unresolved. Jaina can now frame the parallel cleanly enough to frighten herself with it. Being right is not the differentiator; Kel'Thuzad and Arthas were right about parts of their problems too. The most dangerous thing happening to Jaina is not failure but repeated validation. They do not know whether friction from another person is the missing difference or only a partial answer or what that doubt will demand when she has to act fast again.
- Narrow bunk rocking under a body too tired to sleep well; Bread-taste and sea-sway sharing the same night; Cold stars over a ship still moving north.
### Beat 10
- Survive the last approach through Northrend waters and translate source-pressure into a place.
- The headwinds ease only because the ship has entered a colder, harsher problem - ice, ambient resonance, and a coast that does not care whether they survive it. They enter Northrend waters and keep advancing, but the resonance stops being something Jaina reaches for and becomes something that reaches for her. Northrend's cold is a place-pressure, not just a weather pressure. The source's signature is ambient this close; Jaina no longer has to extend to hear it. The contamination echo in Jaina aligns harder under proximity rather than fading. They do not know how much of Jaina's altered perception will remain once they leave the source's immediate radius or whether the coast offers a usable lead or only proof of lateness.
- Iron-tipped poles ringing off ice floes; Frost webbing the rigging at dawn; the cold changing from wind-bite to something that presses behind the eyes.
### Beat 11
- Make landfall at Daggercap Bay and read Arthas's lead in the traces he left behind.
- Arrival is real, but arrival is not rescue. The harbor already shows weeks of military use and departure. They reach Northrend alive, but the camp traces, docks, and abandoned signs say Arthas is still well ahead and fully inland now. Daggercap Bay carries fresh signs of Arthas's expedition - expanded docks, caches, bootprints, and empty camp traces. The source stops opposing the crossing once they enter its domain. The pursuit now changes from sea arithmetic to inland tracking. They do not know how much farther north the real answer sits beyond the harbor.
- Who, if anyone, is still alive inland who can tell them what Arthas found; Frozen mud under boots after four days of deck motion; New timber on dwarven trade docks; the resonance everywhere at once instead of only at the edge of thought.
