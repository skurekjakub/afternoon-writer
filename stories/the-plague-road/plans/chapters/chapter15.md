# Chapter 15: The Prince

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 14 on the road south. Opens on the approach to Hearthglen and closes with Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia riding ahead of Arthas's main column toward Stratholme to try to kill the plague at its source before the city fully turns.
**Where:** South road into Hearthglen's blighted outskirts.
**Travel:** Horseback approach from the Eastweald; on foot through Hearthglen and Arthas's camp; back to horseback for the ride toward Stratholme.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia.
**Goal:** Reach Arthas, hand over the full plague picture, and decide whether there is still any move left before Stratholme goes under.

## What we know

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- Jaina gave her the Lich King chain, the Burning Legion threat behind it, the resonance mechanism, the ward-failure problem, and Kel'Thuzad's expected lich return.
- Jaina still withheld the room, the method, and the inner turn inside Scholomance, so the trust scar is active.
- Sylvanas pointed Arthas at this crisis in Chapter 11 because she needed a prince and an army aimed at the plague.
- Hearthglen has already been hit, Uther is on the field, and Arthas is moving fast enough that the road south may already be too late.
- The repeated farewell has already surfaced three times across the investigation.

**What Sylvanas doesn't know yet**
- Whether Arthas can still hear intelligence without turning it straight into speed.
- The full history between Arthas and Jaina.
- Whether Jaina's new plague theory can actually be used in the field or only explained after the fact.
- What Uther has already concluded and how long he has known it.
- What the farewell pattern means if it surfaces again.

**What NOT to do:**
- No explicit Mal'Ganis identification.
- No on-page Culling yet.
- No interior access to Arthas, Jaina, or Uther.
- No clean repair of the partnership crack.

**What they learn by the end**
- Hearthglen shows the plague has moved into full-force attack, with the dead still rising and being driven forward even after Kel'Thuzad's death.
- Arthas has the full plague picture and is marching his main force on Stratholme at once.
- Jaina has a narrow source plan: get ahead of the army, hit the granaries and bakeries, and try to stop the city from turning before it finishes seeding itself.
- Sylvanas rides with that plan, which gives her the first external proof that Jaina means to spend the Scholomance knowledge to save people, not sit with it.
- Uther is still with the column, but fear is already visible in him.
- The farewell pattern reaches a fourth sighting in the camp's departure rush.

- Prince, commander, and hard center of the camp's pace.
- Sylvanas wants to read him as ally first and danger second.

## Scene 1: The Held City

**What happens:** Show that Hearthglen survived only by brute force against a spreading, self-feeding outbreak, then let Uther confirm that the dead are now coming in numbers that no single kill can end.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, refugees, local soldiers, Uther
**Where we start:** Arthas is ahead, Hearthglen has already taken the blow, and the partnership crack is still riding with them.

### Beat 1
- The plague had time to settle here in a way the earlier chapters only warned about
- Refugee movement is slow, exhausted, and too large for any local response to absorb cleanly. Jaina is already straining under the ambient necromantic pressure before Hearthglen comes into view. They do not know what shape Arthas is in after fighting through Hearthglen or whether the city is merely scarred or still actively losing ground.
- A child's shoe half sunk in road mud; Blackened fruit still hanging in an orchard because no one stayed to pick it; Wagon wheels dragging under too much family weight and too little horse strength.
### Beat 2
- Hearthglen's grain had already activated inside a living town before anyone understood the full mechanism
- Newly dead townsfolk and soldiers are still rejoining the attack if the field misses them for even minutes. The self-mustering choir effect is now strong enough that the dead keep coming without Kel'Thuzad standing over them personally. The Cult's orders are still being carried out in the field despite Scholomance's loss. They do not know how much of this is blind choir behavior and how much is guided by living handlers or whether Stratholme is already farther gone than Hearthglen was.
- Holy-fire scoring on stone near the gate; Men hauling a body toward the pyres before it can sit up; Bells ringing again before the smoke from the last alarm is gone.
### Beat 3
- Uther is still a field healer first, not only a title
- The city was saved by speed, holy fire, and constant reclearing, not by breaking the plague cycle. Uther notices the three-rider formation and its scar before formal introductions do any work. He already fears what Arthas will do in a city larger than this one. They do not know whether Uther thinks the prince can still be checked or whether Arthas will hear anything except urgency once the next city is named.
- Golden warmth rising off Uther's scarred hands; a wounded soldier breathing easier under the Light before Uther even steps back; the older hammer at Uther's side, heavier and plainer than the prince's blessed weapon.

## Scene 2: The Tent

**What happens:** Put the plague picture on Arthas's table, then let Jaina turn that picture into a narrow source plan before the room can collapse into fatalism.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, staff officers
**Where we start:** Hearthglen proves the outbreak is accelerating and Stratholme is the next obvious city.

### Beat 4
- Arthas has stopped spending time on anything that does not move him toward the next decision
- The title "Lady Proudmoore" is a loaded choice, not neutral etiquette. Uther stands behind the prince carrying concern in his grip rather than in his voice. They do not know whether Arthas can still hear a plan that buys time rather than spends bodies.
- The full shape of the Jaina-Arthas history gap; a cracked shoulder guard splitting the Menethil crest; Dried blood in polished grooves that were never cleaned out; Jaina's posture tightening for a fraction before she answers him professionally.
### Beat 5
- Arthas now has the full plague picture: Cult routes, resonance, ward failure, Lich King chain, and Kel'Thuzad's death
- Uther recognizes how cleanly Sylvanas and Jaina still work under pressure. Arthas interrupts only for timing, roads, and where the next city will fail first. They do not know whether the room contains any answer other than immediate movement or whether the theory can do more than explain why cities are dying.
- Daggers pinning the map corners instead of proper weights; Sylvanas's road marks crossing Jaina's finer arcane notes; Arthas's eyes settling on Stratholme and not leaving it.
### Beat 6
- Jaina's countermeasure can be used against source nodes if she gets to them early enough
- The target is not "the city" in the abstract but specific grain stores, bakehouses, and flow points. Jaina is trying to spend Kel'Thuzad's teaching against the plague at once instead of hoarding it. Sylvanas sees that attempt and cannot fold it cleanly into her old sanctum read. They do not know whether Stratholme is already too far seeded for the source plan to matter or whether Arthas hears "head start" as opportunity or as delay.
- Jaina's hand flattening on one quarter of the city map instead of the whole thing; Chalk marking grain nodes rather than theory circles; Uther looking at the plan like a man hearing one last door open.
### Beat 7
- Arthas's answer to uncertainty is still motion
- Uther stays with the main column because the break has not happened yet. The source plan and the army march now run in parallel against the same clock. They do not know whether the source team can buy enough time to matter or what Arthas will do if Jaina says "too late" at the city itself.
- Arthas's gauntlet striking the map beside Stratholme; Staff officers already moving before the last words in the room are done; Falric turning toward the tent flap on the first clear order.
### Beat 8
- The camp has enough discipline to throw a fast rider team out ahead of the army within minutes
- Cyndia hears the fourth farewell from an ordinary aide handling departure work. The same exact phrase now sits in Sylvanas's awareness as a pattern rather than a curiosity. They do not know whether the pattern matters to the road they are about to take or whether the head start is already too small.
- Fresh reins still stiff from the tack chest; a route packet slapped onto a crate and pinned under a knife; "May the stars keep your road" landing in the middle of shouted departure orders.

## Scene 3: The Head Start

**What happens:** Put Jaina's source plan on the road and let Sylvanas recognize, without any speech about it, that Jaina is trying to use the terrible knowledge the right way.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia
**Where we start:** The main army is marching behind them and the city clock is already running.

### Beat 9
- Jaina is already breaking the city into real nodes she can reach, not abstract theory she can admire
- Sylvanas is willing to put her own speed and authority behind that attempt even while the crack remains. The partnership now has two clocks on it at once: the city's and the one between them. They do not know whether the source plan can beat the city's spread or whether success or failure will change the sanctum read enough to matter.
- Three horses stretching into a faster line than the road wants to give them; Jaina shouting granary names and district order over the wind instead of speaking in theorem language; Cyndia riding the left shoulder of the road while the main column's dust starts rising behind them.
