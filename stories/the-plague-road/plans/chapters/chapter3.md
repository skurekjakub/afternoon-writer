# Chapter 3: The Dead Village

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 2's final approach to Millhaven.
**Where:** Millhaven, just south of the Quel'Thalas borderlands.
**Travel:** Mounted approach on Thalassian horses; on foot during the village sweep.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, one unnamed escort ranger.
**Goal:** Sweep Millhaven, find out what happened to its people, and determine if the danger is moving toward Quel'Thalas.

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- The contamination is real, physical, and big enough to matter beyond one village.
- Jaina's magical readings are useful even when her sense of the physical road is poor.
- Millhaven is the first place on the south road where the wrongness knots tight.

**What Sylvanas doesn't know yet**
- How the sickness first got into the village.
- Whether the dead are organized.
- Whether any living witnesses remain.
- How large the threat is beyond this village.

**What NOT to do**
- Do not confirm that grain is the carrier.
- Do not prove there is an organized cult.
- Do not treat the old trader as anything but a helpful local source.

**What they learn by the end**
- Millhaven is the first fully claimed village on this road.
- The survivor girl's testimony and the physical tracks confirm the dead marched north toward Quel'Thalas.
- Neral has ridden north with the survivor and the warning, leaving only Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel to ride south.
- The mention of a "newer grain seller" is noted as a weak, unresolved clue.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- Sylvanas starts the chapter treating this as border defense—a problem to read, report, and manage, not a human catastrophe she has to personally own.
- She wants to read Millhaven cleanly and send a warning home, but the missing dead and the silent village force her to weigh tracks, corpses, and magic all at once.
- She treats Jaina as a specialist she can consult rather than a full partner in reading the field. None of the evidence resolves cleanly if she ignores Jaina's half.
- She has to decide whether to keep Jaina on a short leash as a useful prisoner or let her magical readings shape their next move.
- By the end of the chapter, Sylvanas still doesn't trust Jaina, but she stops treating the mage's input as optional. It becomes a permanent part of her map.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Sylvanas initially sees Jaina as a useful human mage who might read the magic clearly but struggle with the actual ground under their boots.
- However, Jaina doesn't just name the wrongness. She separates what is loud from what actually matters, keeping the field investigation from collapsing into blind horror.
- Sylvanas still holds back any warmth, but Jaina earns the right to speak as an equal on the investigation.

### Partnership
- They start as a custody escort under pressure: Jaina is a useful prisoner and an armed guard, with zero trust between them.
- The horror of the village makes side-by-side work unavoidable. By the end, they are building a single picture out of both physical and magical evidence.
- There is no easy softening between them. When Sylvanas uses Jaina's name internally, it's because the mage is doing real work, not because their mood has warmed.

## Scene 1: Millhaven

**What happens:** Show the first village the plague has truly destroyed, and make the missing dead a tangible, practical problem.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, escort.
**Where we start:** The contamination is real and physical, but how it arrived is completely unclear.

### Beat 1
- They enter Millhaven carefully to see if it is occupied or trapped, but the village is completely, acoustically dead. Normal village sounds are missing, and that absence is worse than visible damage.
- Millhaven wasn't burned or sacked. The wrongness is recent enough that ordinary life seems interrupted rather than erased. They don't know where the people went or if anything is still alive inside.
- Cold stoves; a swinging door; the eerie lack of livestock noise; horses reacting nervously to the silence; dust settling on places that should have been touched today.
- Unwilling to leave the whole village to Jaina's magic, Sylvanas orders a physical sweep.

### Beat 2
- The rangers sweep the buildings looking for bodies or a kill site, but every house just looks like people left in the middle of living.
- They find evidence of a staggered collapse over days, not a sudden event. Domestic life just froze in place before emptying out. They still don't know if the population fled, was taken, or rose.
- Food gone stale on tables; a child's toy left exactly where it fell; a cat wandering as the only casual living presence in the village.
- With no bodies to study, Jaina tests the contamination inside the most heavily saturated structures.

### Beat 3
- Jaina examines the well, root cellar, granary, and soil. The entire village is soaked in the sickness, but she cannot point to what first brought it in.
- The granary gives the strongest single reading, but Jaina refuses to assume the loudest magic is the whole answer. Sylvanas begins treating Jaina as exactly the kind of reader she needs but doesn't have.
- Jaina kneeling in the grain while Sylvanas clears the room behind her; normal-looking winter stores hiding an invisible wrongness; stale dust and closed-space heat.
- Knowing magic isn't enough, Sylvanas takes the search outside to check the ground herself.

### Beat 4
- Sylvanas checks the ground behind the village for tracks. The dirt is churned with drag marks and broken crusts, but there isn't a clean enough line to justify sending a border warning yet.
- The ground confirms many of the dead left Millhaven on their feet. They didn't just collapse and stay there. Sylvanas refuses to call the direction until she has a clean track or a living witness. They still don't know who is directing the dead.
- Broken dirt where too many dragging feet ruined a lane; Sylvanas crouched over muddy tracks she refuses to overcall; Cyndia frustrated by trying to count bodies without a clean line.
- Realizing the ground isn't enough, they desperately need a living witness.

## Scene 2: The Survivor and the Proof

**What happens:** Put a living child in the center of the disaster and use her testimony to turn guesswork into a military warning.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, survivor girl.
**Where we start:** The contamination is real and bodies are missing, but the ground hasn't proved where the dead went.

### Beat 5
- They find a traumatized, dehydrated girl hidden in a cellar. She is terrified and her memory of the sequence of events is completely shattered.
- She is the only known survivor. Neral is the only ranger the girl doesn't recoil from, making her the obvious choice to escort the child out. Her broken testimony confirms that people died, rose, and moved out at night.
- The damp smell of the cellar; the child's hoarse whisper; Jaina lowering herself into the dark while Sylvanas secures the space above; Neral crouching to the girl's height instead of looming over her.
- They need to extract useful information from the girl without pretending she is a reliable witness.

### Beat 6
- The girl remembers the horror in scattered images: sweetness on people's breath, sick neighbors, bodies rising, the dead shuffling toward the north road at night, and a passing complaint about a newer grain man on the route.
- Sylvanas gets confirmation that the dead rose and moved with purpose. She also notes the grain seller as an oddity. Jaina proves her worth by gently pulling these useful pieces out of the girl's broken story. They still need physical tracks to back up the child's memory.
- The girl's fingers locked tightly around a blanket scrap; Jaina hearing the tremor in every detail; Neral keeping her steady with quiet, practical questions instead of orders.
- The testimony points north, but Sylvanas needs the ground to agree before she spends riders on a warning.

### Beat 7
- Sylvanas and Cyndia check the road cut north of the village. The tracks are damaged, but they finally piece together enough physical evidence to confirm the girl's story.
- Tracks confirms a mass movement north-northeast toward Quel'Thalas involving at least sixty reanimated bodies marching in rough formation. Jaina's magic strips the ordinary road damage out of the tracks, helping Sylvanas read them. The emergency is now a direct threat to the Elven border.
- Parallel grooves finally standing out cleanly at the north road cut; Sylvanas reading the ground with her fingertips; Cyndia's count turning from professional doubt into raw alarm.
- With the proof solid enough to justify a warning, they must send the survivor and a dispatch rider north immediately.

## Scene 3: The Grain Seller on the Road

**What happens:** Turn the proof into action, send the warning north, and cross paths with the old trader as a helpful local before pressing south.
**Cast:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, old grain seller, survivor girl.
**Where we start:** The dead are confirmed moving north, and the child must be evacuated with the warning dispatch.

### Beat 8
- Sylvanas has to decide whether to keep Neral for the dangerous ride south or send her away so Quel'Thalas gets the warning and the child gets to safety. 
- She chooses the border. Neral takes the warning dispatch, the unnamed fourth rider, and the survivor girl, heading north. Neral's unexpected skill with frightened children makes the handoff easier. Sylvanas's southbound party is now reduced to the four named leads.
- A written message sealed in absolute haste; the dispatch tube strapped tightly to Neral's saddle; Neral calming the girl with promises of safe stables and the horned white "unicorns" that elven children dream about.
- Before the party splits, they encounter a traveler on the road who might sharpen their picture of the country ahead.

### Beat 9
- An old grain seller with an empty wagon approaches on the road north. Sylvanas questions him for route intelligence, and his visible shock at hearing Millhaven is gone makes him seem like a completely genuine, unforced local.
- He confirms that settlements ahead are still trading and only lightly touched by sickness. Millhaven is the first village to fall completely. Sylvanas accepts his read of the road, completely unaware that he is linked to the sickness itself.
- Grain dust visible in the slats of his empty wagon; genuine surprise breaking through his otherwise forgettable face; the survivor girl settling in front of Neral as the horse sidesteps.
- The exchange clarifies the road ahead just as the two groups finally separate.

### Beat 10
- They quickly finish the conversation and part ways. The trader's farewell lands just strangely enough to earn a brief second glance from Sylvanas, but the moment doesn't linger.
- Neral and the girl ride north, leaving the chapter completely. Sylvanas turns her attention away from the trader and back to the disaster ahead.
- Sylvanas's brief, narrowed glance back; the wagon wheels starting north again after a startled pause; the girl sitting silently against Neral's saddle as the horse steps out.
- The road south now belongs only to Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel.

### Beat 11
- With the border warned, Sylvanas has to decide whether to fall back or commit to a multi-day push deeper into Lordaeron following the escalating magical readings.
- They push south toward the next still-living village. They are committing to the pursuit, even though they still don't know that grain is the carrier or who is organizing the plague.
- The road opening into broader human farmland; the magical readings thickening ahead; the physical absence of one escort horse in the party after Neral's departure.
- The chapter hands off, skipping two days of travel before opening Chapter 4 at a village that still misunderstands its own sickness.
