# Chapter 14: The Revelation

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 13. Opens on the road south after the Caer Darrow escape and closes with the party still moving toward Hearthglen and Arthas after both women refuse their institutions.
**Where:** Eastweald road south of the Barov shore, late afternoon sliding toward dusk.
**Travel:** Back on horseback after the lake crossing; southbound road ride with relay and sending-stone pauses.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Goal:** Deliver the Scholomance intelligence, decide what not to say about the room that produced it, and get south before the Arthas thread outruns them.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- Kel'Thuzad gave her the full resonance model, the animation trigger, the Lich King chain, the Burning Legion threat behind it, and the warning that his death is only a transition
- Sylvanas saw the sanctum's externals, not the argument that happened inside Jaina before Sylvanas opened the door
- Sylvanas has already filed a harder read of Jaina and is now checking where she used to trust
- Cyndia has the Farstrider relay line, and Lyriel's absence is still visible in the three-rider formation
- Hearthglen is already in crisis and Arthas is moving fast enough that the road south cannot wait for emotional cleanup

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- Whether Sylvanas will ever ask what really happened in the sanctum
- How much uglier Sylvanas's route to the sanctum was than the guided path Jandice gave Jaina
- Whether Antonidas or the Convocation can be made to move at the speed this threat requires
- Whether Arthas can still be reached before urgency hardens into something worse
- Whether Kel'Thuzad's return can be prevented rather than merely anticipated

**What NOT to do:**
- No clean repair of the sanctum crack
- No explicit Mal'Ganis identification
- No field-ready resonance weapon
- No Jandice payoff

**What they learn by the end**
- Sylvanas now has the full strategic picture: Lich King, Burning Legion, resonance mechanism, ward failure, and Kel'Thuzad's expected lich return
- Jaina shared the intelligence product but still withheld the room, the method, and the moment of recognition
- Jaina now knows Sylvanas came through the hidden work rooms and saw the peasant stock, failed remnants, and body-part experiments her own route never touched
- Both the Convocation and Antonidas have effectively been refused in favor of the mission
- Hearthglen and Arthas are now the immediate southbound priority, and the damaged three-rider formation is still moving

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- If she gives Sylvanas clean, useful intelligence and keeps the room out of it, the mission can keep moving.
- Deliver what Scholomance taught her without reopening the wound Scholomance left behind.
- Sylvanas's half-step, the way Sylvanas now listens for gaps, and two institutions about to demand control of what Jaina now knows.
- Jaina keeps treating correct data as sufficient payment. If the briefing is right, the process can stay buried.
- The road asks whether Jaina can choose honest partnership over clean analysis once the analysis becomes personally compromising.
- Explain the room and risk breaking the formation in the field, or give only the usable intelligence and carry the rest alone.
- Jaina chooses the partial truth on purpose, not by accident.
- Chapter 15 opens with the intelligence shared, the omission intact, and the partnership still functioning with trust that now comes with a check attached.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- Commander, listener, and a woman measuring the source even while she needs it.
- Jaina reads the half-step as oversight and distrust. She cannot yet price what continued commitment is still hiding inside that same posture.
- Sylvanas keeps listening, keeps moving with Jaina, and keeps treating the information as too important to ignore even while reclassifying the source.
- Keep Sylvanas short, controlled, and mostly external. Her listening is the pressure.

### Partnership / team
- Damaged but functional three-rider formation, still moving south under time pressure.
- The pair shifts from collaborative back-and-forth into formal briefing and hard listening, then chooses to keep going anyway.
- No melodrama. Let the damage show through spacing, pauses, and what each woman does not ask.
- This chapter is persistence, not healing.

## Scene 1: The Road

**What happens:** Show the crack from inside Jaina's saddle, then force her to decide that the briefing will cover the intelligence product and not the room that produced it.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Where we start:** Kel'Thuzad is dead, the intelligence is in Jaina's head, and the road south cannot wait.

### Beat 1
- Jaina is already dividing Scholomance into what can be reported and what cannot
- The world outside Caer Darrow feels too ordinary after hours underground. Jaina notices the changed formation before anyone says a word. They do not know whether compartmentalizing will hold once Sylvanas starts listening.
- Reins damp in Jaina's hand from the return ride off the lake; Late light lying warm over fields she knows are not clean; Memory of chalk pressure still alive in her fingers.
### Beat 2
- Sylvanas has shifted from easy working trust to quiet verification
- Cyndia notices the silence and leaves it alone. The old riding rhythm between Jaina and Sylvanas is gone for now. They do not know whether Sylvanas will ask directly about the sanctum or whether the formation can carry a full briefing without cracking wider.
- Three horses holding one line but not one rhythm; Cyndia's automatic glance toward the missing right flank; Sylvanas's mount staying just ahead without any visible effort.
### Beat 3
- Jaina knows the omission is useful and dishonest at the same time
- She frames the choice as road necessity, even while knowing cowardice is mixed into it. The gap between what happened and what will be said is now deliberate. They do not know whether Sylvanas will let the clean version stand.
- Jaina's hand closing on her pack strap where the missing chalk should be; Saddle leather creaking in the long silence; Hooves thudding south on packed Eastweald dirt.

## Scene 2: The Briefing

**What happens:** Deliver the Scholomance intelligence cleanly, then let Sylvanas force the missing human cost back into Jaina's clean account.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Where we start:** Jaina has chosen to report the product, not the process.

### Beat 4
- The plague is part of a larger campaign directed by the Lich King from Northrend
- The Burning Legion sits above that chain instead of the plague being a purely local Lordaeron disaster. Lordaeron is being treated as a conversion project and staging ground, not just a target. They do not know what either woman can do with that scale before the next city falls or how much of Sylvanas's silence is trust in the facts and how much is scrutiny of the source.
- A brief arcane map hanging over Jaina's gloved hand; Horse breath smoking in cooling air; Sylvanas watching the map instead of Jaina's face.
### Beat 5
- The plague works by resonance through living tissue, not by brute magical impact
- Force-blocking ward architecture can miss or only partly blunt that kind of attack. Sylvanas came through rooms full of peasant stock and failed work that Jandice never showed Jaina. Reanimated body parts were being tested separately from whole corpses. The right answer is disruption during the buildup window, but Jaina does not yet have a deployable method. They do not know how to apply the countermeasure at city or kingdom scale or whether Jaina can defend the theory without sounding closer to Kel'Thuzad than she wants to.
- Jaina's small arcane sketch stuttering when Sylvanas cuts across; Horse ears twitching at the sharpness in Sylvanas's voice; Reins biting deeper into Jaina's palm when "hands" and "peasants" land out loud.
### Beat 6
- Kel'Thuzad considered bodily death a transition rather than an ending
- Crude undead continuity and high-fidelity preserved selfhood are different products of the same broader system. The same institution treating peasants as stock reserved a higher tier of preservation for its chosen master. Kel'Thuzad's dying calm now reads as expectation, not courage. They do not know how a lich transition can be interrupted or whether Sylvanas will ever speak aloud about the reporting gap she just noticed.
- A fractional pause long enough for tack leather to creak; Sylvanas's hand flexing once on the bow grip; Cyndia cutting her eyes across and then forward again.
### Beat 7
- Jaina knows Sylvanas's read is built on real external evidence, not fantasy
- Jaina's first instinct was to answer horror with theory, and she knows Sylvanas heard that instinct clearly. The part Jaina cannot explain cleanly is recognition, not coercion. Silence now protects the mission and injures the partnership at the same time. They do not know whether this silence will remain workable once Arthas enters the field.
- Road dust lifting behind the horses; Jaina keeping her eyes on the road instead of Sylvanas; Pack strap under her fingers where chalk should be.

## Scene 3: The Vise

**What happens:** Force both women to choose the mission over their institutions, then shove the chapter hard toward Hearthglen and Arthas.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lor'themar by relay, Antonidas by sending stone
**Where we start:** The strategic briefing is done and the omission is still in place.

### Beat 8
- The Convocation has escalated from disapproval to formal recall
- Lor'themar is delivering the order under visible political strain. Cyndia witnesses Sylvanas's refusal as silence and continued motion rather than speech. They do not know what the long political cost of ignoring the order will be.
- Pale green relay crystal warming in Cyndia's hand; Lor'themar's voice clipped flat through the stone; Sylvanas not checking her horse when the message ends.
### Beat 9
- Antonidas now knows the Lich King chain and the Burning Legion threat behind it
- Jaina still does not give him the full resonance formalization or the truth of the sanctum exchange. "Come home" confirms Dalaran is still thinking in terms of safety and recall instead of field speed. They do not know what Dalaran can do with the partial theory Jaina has given them or whether Antonidas will move faster now that he knows what Kel'Thuzad became part of.
- Violet sending stone dimming and brightening against Jaina's glove; Antonidas's voice sounding thin with distance; Jaina's thumb pressing the engraved edge after the call ends.
### Beat 10
- Hearthglen barely held against a major plague activation
- Uther is on the field and the Silver Hand is already committed. Arthas is moving toward Stratholme instead of consolidating where he is. The party is no longer following a cold trail; they are chasing a live fuse. They do not know whether they can reach Arthas before urgency turns into atrocity or how many other grain-fed sites are already too far gone.
- New relay cadence coming fast enough that Cyndia barely has time to pocket the stone; a dark smear of smoke or fire low on the southern horizon; Horses pressed into a faster road pace without discussion.

**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Where we start:** The full picture is shared, the institutions have been refused, and the southbound threat has overtaken every private question.

The three-rider formation still works as the shape that gets them where they need to go even though trust now comes with a check attached. They do not know what Arthas will look like when they catch him or whether the next arc hardens the crack or starts closing it.
