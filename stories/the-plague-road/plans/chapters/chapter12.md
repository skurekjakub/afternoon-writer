**When:** Runs parallel to Chapter 11. Opens after Jaina's descent at the end of Chapter 10 and ends at the instant Sylvanas's approach reaches the deep levels of Scholomance.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- She reached the lower levels alone by using Jandice's scholarly vanity as the pathway
- The Barov estate and Scholomance are live cult strongholds, not rumor or abandoned ruins
- Her field read yielded binding words but not the awakening spell; that missing magic is why she descended.
- She already knows the treated grain is only the carrier layer and that the body participates in the rest, but not how the body rises after death
- Jandice's theory named resonance as the missing link.
- The Kirin Tor is too slow to help at the pace this crisis is moving
- Sylvanas and Cyndia took the track heading toward Arthas in Chapter 9, so Jaina is operating without combat backup

**What they don't know**
- The true awakening spell
- Whether Kel'Thuzad wants to recruit her, test her, or simply teach her
- Who Kel'Thuzad answers to
- Whether Sylvanas is still far south or already turning north
- How she leaves with the dark secrets if the conversation goes bad

**What NOT to do:**
- No Jaina agreement or moral conversion
- No theatrical villain register from Kel'Thuzad
- No rescue already inside the room
- No resolution of the old seller / repeated-farewell clue

- Jaina leaves the conversation with the complete awakening spell, including the difference between mindless ghouls and thinking dead, plus the realization that necromancy flows directly from Kirin Tor teachings
- She now knows, from Kel'Thuzad's own disclosures, that the plague serves the Lich King and that something called the Burning Legion sits above that chain
- Kel'Thuzad remains alive in the sanctum while Jandice remains above it
- Sylvanas is descending toward the room.

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- If Jaina can finally get the full answer, she can hold the room on intellect alone.
- Get the complete process and the real scale of the threat from the one man who can explain it cleanly.
- Kel'Thuzad is not a ranting monster. He is lucid, rigorous, and speaking in the language Jaina was trained to love.
- Jaina keeps leaning toward the explanation itself, as if a clear theorem can stay morally separate from what it is for.
- The answer arrives through a scholar whose mind feels legible to her, and the intellectual fit keeps pulling her forward faster than judgment does.
- Keep treating the process as pure answer, or confront what the answer serves and what line it crossed to become this elegant.
- Jaina gets the full process, but loses the safety of believing intellect is its own moral guardrail.
- Chapter 13 opens on aftermath Sylvanas did not witness, with Jaina intellectually and morally scorched by what she learned.

### Kel'Thuzad through Jaina's POV
- Living archmage, plague mastermind, and the scholar who can finally speak to Jaina at full speed.
- The danger is not overt monstrosity, madness, or theatrical villainy.
- The danger is that Kel'Thuzad is lucid, rigorous, intellectually legible to her, and working from extensions of the same magical schooling she received.
- Keep him calm, human, and scholarly. The threat is the clarity.

### Partnership
- Jaina alone in the room, with no Sylvanas friction on the thinking.
- The missing partnership becomes the chapter's engine. Every terrible revelation goes unchallenged because no one is there to price the room, cut the lecture short, or force the question of what the theory does to actual people.
- Keep Sylvanas fully off-page until the final beat. Her absence should land through missing checks, not recap.
- This is exposure and threshold, not conversion or agreement.

## Scene 1: The Sanctum

**What happens:** The deepest room in Scholomance is a scholar's study, where Kel'Thuzad delivers the awakening spell using Kirin Tor language.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad, Jandice briefly
**Where we start:** Jaina descended because the dark magic's source was here.

### Beat 1
- Jaina reaches the room that contains the answer, but the room is built to feel like her own Dalaran education turned inside out.
- Kel'Thuzad's sanctum is a working study, not a theatrical throne room. Jandice's authority has a ceiling, and that ceiling is Kel'Thuzad. The scholarship in the room is recognizably Kirin Tor-shaped even where the subject matter is necromantic. They do not know whether the conversation is trap, test, or genuine exchange.
- Why Kel'Thuzad is willing to receive her at all; Cold, dry air and preservative compounds; Green luminescent fungi throwing a submerged light across books and chalkboards; Research logs arranged with the same margin habits Antonidas taught serious students.
### Beat 2
- The plague's animation model is resonance-based rather than force-based
- The treated grain carries only the outer shell of the plague, not the full waking process. Conditioned tissue retains patterned potential that can be amplified instead of externally commanded. The body is the answering chamber that completes what the carrier started. Most village dead preserve only low-fidelity pattern enough for movement and answerability; preserving full selfhood requires an additional deliberate layer. The Cult's field "necromancers" are mostly acolyte conductors taught command signals; they can seize and harden the crude choir into direct compliance without creating that underlying self-mustering from nothing. Jaina's fractured spell notes now has a complete arcane home. Kel'Thuzad frames necromancy as an extension of accepted arcane work on persistence, binding, and sympathetic carryover rather than as a separate profane system. Jaina has nobody in the room who will interrupt the beauty of the model with what it costs outside the room. They do not know what Kel'Thuzad thinks the completed theory justifies or what he wants from Jaina beyond simple comprehension.
- Chalk rasp on slate; Elegant notation that looks like Antonidas's tradition pushed into forbidden territory; Jaina recognizing that the equations are sound before she decides what they mean.

## Scene 2: The Duel

**What happens:** Jaina forces the conversation from elegant theory back to the mass death above.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Where we start:** The process is sound enough that Jaina cannot dismiss it as sloppy fraud

### Beat 3
- He takes moral challenge seriously enough to answer, not theatrically enough to rant
- The Kirin Tor's arcane instruments are good at proving facts and weak at proving value. Dalaran banned necromancy because its practical use collapses consent, personhood, and civic trust, not because the underlying magical logic is unsound. The horror is the gradient itself: once low-fidelity preserved function is possible, the step toward preserving the person becomes a question of degree rather than category. They do not know whether Jaina can find a framework that reaches him or whether any moral argument using shared Kirin Tor standards can actually defeat his case.
- Specimen jars turning from background into accusation; Kel'Thuzad's hands remaining still while Jaina's keep tracing the argument in the air; the room staying clinically ordinary while the content turns unbearable.
### Beat 4
- Kel'Thuzad acknowledges a strong point when he hears one, which makes the exchange worse rather than easier
- Jaina notices how easily her own body has adopted scholar habits in his room. Jaina realizes her strongest objection is ethical because the magical framework itself remains horribly coherent. They do not know whether the foothold changes anything beyond the local exchange or how much of Jaina's resistance depends on frameworks Kel'Thuzad can simply route around.
- Chalk left in Jaina's hand before she realizes she picked it up; Wards humming under the floor like a second conversation; Silence used pedagogically rather than mercifully.
### Beat 5
- Jaina and Kel'Thuzad were both produced by the same institution and broke with it for opposite ends
- The difference between them is direction and cost, not basic method. Kel'Thuzad is not trying to corrupt her so much as document her as living proof of his theories. They do not know how motive can be defended in a framework that distrusts anything unverifiable or whether Jaina can keep the mirror from opening wider once it has been named.
- Kirin Tor texts on the shelves reading less like trophies than a still-active library; Kel'Thuzad's gentleness making the claim land harder; Jaina recognizing the pattern before she can reject it.

## Scene 3: The Mirror

**What happens:** Jaina sees the structural beauty in Kel'Thuzad's reasoning and cannot unknow it.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Where we start:** The mirror is named, and Jaina cannot dismiss it without lying to herself

### Beat 6
- Jaina does not agree with him, and she still follows the logic far enough to see its terrible beauty for one heartbeat
- The resonance model implies continuity as gradient, not clean wall. Jaina's mind can see magical beauty in a structure built for atrocity. Kel'Thuzad notices the precise instant she crosses and recoils. They do not know what she can do with the fact that her mind followed the path at all or whether this moment changes anything practical before Chapter 13 breaks the room open.
- Jaina's hands flattening on the table and going still; a ward ripple moving through the sanctum like an involuntary magical gasp; Silence dense enough to count as an event of its own.
### Beat 7
- Necromancy is not magically alien to her education; it is a continuous extension of familiar arcane logic, which is exactly why the ban had to be moral rather than mathematical
- They do not know how she keeps hunting without letting the same lens run unchecked again or how Dalaran would distinguish understanding from agreement if she reported this exactly.
- Breath discipline used for composure instead of spellwork; the room's banality becoming worse precisely because it does not react; Research notes lying on the desk as if none of this were personal.
### Beat 8
- Antonidas trained both Jaina and Kel'Thuzad in the same intellectual tradition
- Institutional caution helped leave the kingdom grossly unprepared for necromantic war. Jaina's anger at Dalaran and fear of herself can coexist without cancelling each other. Antonidas and the Council banned practical necromancy because once the theory touches real bodies, the line between scholarship and violation collapses. They do not know what she can safely tell Antonidas later without being treated as contaminated by the exchange or whether Antonidas's caution was failure, mercy, or both.
- Warmth entering Kel'Thuzad's voice only when he says Antonidas's name; Antonidas's notation system visible on every surface in the room; Jaina feeling the teacher present in both the monster's handwriting and her own habits.

## Scene 4: The Revelation

**What happens:** The threat expands from the spell to cosmic supply lines, showing that Kel'Thuzad has already priced his own death.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Where we start:** Jaina understands the awakening spell and the personal mirror.

### Beat 9
- Kel'Thuzad sees the relationship as patron-scholar, not master-servant
- The poisoned grain is a supply line, not a mere experiment. Kel'Thuzad did not derive the whole design in isolation; Ner'zhul supplied theory, revisions, and patron knowledge beyond anything Dalaran could have given him. They do not know what created the Lich King or whether anything above Kel'Thuzad and the Lich King is directing the preparation.
- Older, colder ward signatures in the walls that do not feel arcane in any Kirin Tor sense; Kel'Thuzad sounding grateful rather than afraid when he describes the voice; Jaina feeling the room as instrument rather than shelter.
### Beat 10
- The Scourge is preparation for invasion, not the final threat
- Jaina gets the name and large-scale frame, not a full cosmology lecture. Some plague behaviors belong to the Burning Legion rather than Kel'Thuzad. They do not know which current institutions can respond to a threat at this scale or whether the countermeasure she can now design will matter in time.
- Jaina's fingers whitening where they lock together in her lap; Specimen jars shrinking into insignificance beside what they now imply; Kel'Thuzad's voice flattening into briefing register rather than debate register.
### Beat 11
- His attachment is to scholarship and continuation of method, not to mortal life as such
- He treats the village plague's crude choir as proof-of-concept, not as the preservation standard he wants for himself. Jaina's real separation from him is relational context and value, not mere magical brilliance. Whether she can get out with the dark secrets before the room changes from seminar back into battlefield. Whether Sylvanas's arrival will heal the absence or wound it further. Kel'Thuzad discussing his own death with no more heat than he used for harmonic notation. Sanctum light dimming slightly as the wards cycle colder. Jaina thinking of Sylvanas not as theory but as the person who would come.

## Scene 5: The Silence and the Sound

**What happens:** the chapter settle the weight of what Jaina learned, then break the sanctum's stillness with the first sign of Sylvanas's approach.
**Characters:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
Jaina has everything she came for and one wound she cannot file away

### Beat 12
- Stay in the silence long enough for the weight to settle rather than pretending it has not changed her
- Jaina now has the complete process, the cosmic frame, and the knowledge of her own dangerous susceptibility to elegant logic. She now understands necromancy as continuous with accepted arcane knowledge rather than as a magically disconnected profanation. Kel'Thuzad is satisfied because he was understood, not because he won a theatrical contest. The room remains offensively ordinary despite what has happened inside it. How any outsider will interpret this scene when they arrive. Whether Jaina can separate what she learned from what she felt about the learning. Silence deep enough to feel physical. The research log from Chapter 10 sitting on the desk as if it belonged there all along. Jaina sitting back not in relief but in receded shock.
### Beat 13
- The rescue is coming, but it will arrive too late to prevent the moment and early enough to misread its aftermath
- Someone is moving through Scholomance with combat, physical precision rather than magical blundering. The ward cascades describe a descent from the upper levels toward the sanctum. Jaina recognizes the approach as Sylvanas before the name is spoken. Kel'Thuzad reads the change on Jaina's face but does not understand the partnership behind it. How Sylvanas will interpret the tableau when she arrives. Whether Chapter 13 opens on rescue, confrontation, or both at once. Defensive wards activating in layered ripples through the building. No visible spellcraft attached to the approach, only relentless room-clearing motion. Kel'Thuzad's attention redirecting upward with amused curiosity.
