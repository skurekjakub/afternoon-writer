# Chapter 12: The Necromancer

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Runs parallel to Chapter 11. Opens after Jaina's descent at the end of Chapter 10 and ends at the instant Sylvanas's approach reaches the deep levels of Scholomance.
**Open location:** Kel'Thuzad's inner sanctum beneath Scholomance, deep below Caer Darrow.
**Transport:** On foot through the lower crypt levels before open; seated inside the sanctum at chapter start.
**Active cast at open:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad, Jandice at the threshold only.
**Immediate objective:** Extract the missing animation-trigger mechanism, understand the architecture behind the plague, and survive the conversation without mistaking comprehension for surrender.

## Arc position

### Jaina
- **Current stance at open:** If Jaina can finally get the full answer, she can hold the room on intellect alone.
- **Surface objective:** Get the complete mechanism and the real scale of the threat from the one man who can explain it cleanly.
- **Pressure source:** Kel'Thuzad is not a ranting monster. He is lucid, rigorous, and speaking in the language Jaina was trained to love.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Jaina keeps leaning toward the explanation itself, as if a clear theorem can stay morally separate from what it is for.
- **Chapter test:** The answer arrives through a scholar whose mind feels legible to her, and the intellectual fit keeps pulling her forward faster than judgment does.
- **Forced choice:** Keep treating the mechanism as pure answer, or confront what the answer serves and what line it crossed to become this elegant.
- **End-state shift:** Jaina gets the full mechanism, but loses the safety of believing intellect is its own moral guardrail.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 13 opens on aftermath Sylvanas did not witness, with Jaina intellectually and morally scorched by what she learned.

### Kel'Thuzad through Jaina's POV
- **Visible function:** Living academic endpoint, plague architect, and the scholar who can finally speak to Jaina at full speed.
- **POV misread at open:** The danger should look like overt monstrosity, madness, or theatrical villainy.
- **Correction earned here:** The danger is that Kel'Thuzad is lucid, rigorous, intellectually legible to her, and working from extensions of the same magical schooling she received.
- **Interaction rule:** Keep him calm, human, and scholarly. The threat is the clarity.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Jaina alone in the room, with no Sylvanas friction on the thinking.
- **Operational change this chapter:** The missing partnership becomes the chapter's engine. Every escalation survives because no one is there to price the room, cut the lecture short, or force the question of what the theory does to actual people.
- **Naming/attitude rule:** Keep Sylvanas fully off-page until the final beat. Her absence should land through missing checks, not recap.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** This is exposure and threshold, not conversion or agreement.

## Scene 1: The Sanctum

**Scene function:** Establish that the deepest room in Scholomance is an office, not a lair, and let Kel'Thuzad complete the missing mechanism in theorem-and-hypothesis language open enough that Jaina's mind engages before her guard can close.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad, Jandice briefly
**Knowledge at scene start:** Jaina descended because the answer was here; she has not yet learned what kind of room gives it to her

### Beat 1 - Scene: The office
- **Goal:** Orient Jaina in the sanctum and determine what kind of encounter this is
- **Conflict:** Everything in the room says scholar rather than monster: organized desk, notation boards, specimen jars, Antonidas's notation system everywhere, and Jandice being dismissed like a graduate student rather than a co-conspirator
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina reaches the room that contains the answer, but the room is built to feel like her own education turned inside out
- **Value shift:** descent into enemy ground -> intimate intellectual danger
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad's sanctum is a working study, not a theatrical throne room
  - Jandice's authority has a ceiling, and that ceiling is Kel'Thuzad
  - The scholarship in the room is recognizably Kirin Tor-shaped even where the subject matter is necromantic
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Why Kel'Thuzad is willing to receive her at all
  - Whether the conversation is trap, test, or genuine exchange
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Cold, dry air and preservative compounds
  - Green luminescent fungi throwing a submerged light across books and chalkboards
  - Research logs arranged with the same margin habits Antonidas taught serious students
- **Transition intent:** Once the room is legible, Kel'Thuzad can give her exactly what she came for and make the danger worse

### Beat 2 - Scene: The resonance
- **Goal:** Obtain the missing animation-trigger mechanism from the incomplete Chapter 9 field data
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad does not force the answer from her; he teaches it, and the teaching is accurate enough that Jaina's analytical mind engages before she can reject the source
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina gets the missing mechanism, but getting it means meeting Kel'Thuzad on shared intellectual ground
- **Value shift:** intimate intellectual danger -> rigorous engagement with the mechanism
- **New on-page information:**
  - The plague's animation model is resonance-based rather than force-based
  - The treated grain carries only the outer shell of the plague, not the full waking mechanism
  - Conditioned tissue retains patterned potential that can be amplified instead of externally commanded
  - The body is the answering chamber that completes what the carrier started
  - Most village dead preserve only low-fidelity pattern enough for movement and answerability; preserving full selfhood requires an additional deliberate layer
  - The Cult's field "necromancers" are mostly acolyte conductors taught command signals; they can seize and harden the crude choir into direct compliance without creating that underlying self-mustering from nothing
  - Jaina's incomplete Chapter 9 data now has a complete theoretical home
  - Kel'Thuzad frames necromancy as an extension of accepted arcane work on persistence, binding, and sympathetic carryover rather than as a separate profane system
  - Jaina has nobody in the room who will interrupt the beauty of the model with what it costs outside the room
- **Dialogue guidance:** Let this sound like a seminar between elite Kirin Tor minds: named theorems, competing hypotheses, chalkboard revisions, and half-finished chains Jaina completes a beat before she regrets keeping up.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What Kel'Thuzad thinks the completed theory justifies
  - What he wants from Jaina beyond simple comprehension
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Chalk rasp on slate
  - Elegant notation that looks like Antonidas's tradition pushed into forbidden territory
  - Jaina recognizing that the equations are sound before she decides what they mean
- **Transition intent:** Once the mechanism works, Jaina has to force the conversation back to what the work has done in the world above

## Scene 2: The Duel

**Scene function:** Shift from mechanism to moral and epistemological combat, use Dalaran's ban to expose the gap between theoretical coherence and ethical acceptability, then let Kel'Thuzad name the structural parallel between his path and Jaina's.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Knowledge at scene start:** The mechanism is sound enough that Jaina cannot dismiss it as sloppy fraud

### Beat 3 - Scene: The ethics
- **Goal:** Force the conversation from elegant theory back to plague victims, contaminated grain, and mass death
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad does not dodge the moral challenge; he answers it by separating theoretical validity from civic prohibition, arguing that Dalaran banned necromancy because of what it does to persons and institutions, not because the arcane chain fails
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Jaina cannot simply win by pointing at the dead, and the terms of the argument shift under her feet
- **Value shift:** rigorous engagement with the mechanism -> epistemological vertigo
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad sees the campaign and the research as one continuous act
  - He takes moral challenge seriously enough to answer, not theatrically enough to rant
  - The Kirin Tor's analytical tools are good at proving facts and weak at proving value
  - Dalaran banned necromancy because its practical use collapses consent, personhood, and civic trust, not because the underlying magical logic is unsound
  - The horror is the gradient itself: once low-fidelity preserved function is possible, the step toward preserving the person becomes a question of degree rather than category
- **Dialogue guidance:** Jaina should invoke the ban as proof of corruption; Kel'Thuzad should answer like a Council dissenter citing policy, precedent, and sound theorems the Council never disproved.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Jaina can find a framework that reaches him
  - Whether any moral argument using shared Kirin Tor standards can actually defeat his case
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Specimen jars turning from background into accusation
  - Kel'Thuzad's hands remaining still while Jaina's keep tracing the argument in the air
  - The room staying clinically ordinary while the content turns unbearable
- **Transition intent:** Jaina has to shift ground if she wants any purchase at all

### Beat 4 - Sequel: The epistemology
- **Emotion:** The ground under the argument is moving
- **Dilemma:** Keep fighting inside Kirin Tor proof-rules that favor him, or shift to consent, personhood, and the human ground those rules were never designed to measure
- **Decision:** Move to consent and coercion - if the mechanism preserves pattern without permission, the violation begins before any elegant theory can save it
- **Value shift:** epistemological vertigo -> precarious foothold
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina's best answer is about persons, not purely about models
  - Kel'Thuzad acknowledges a strong point when he hears one, which makes the exchange worse rather than easier
  - Jaina notices how easily her own body has adopted scholar habits in his room
  - Jaina realizes her strongest objection is ethical because the magical framework itself remains horribly coherent
- **Dialogue guidance:** This is the turn where Jaina stops trying to beat him on theorem alone and starts arguing from consent, selfhood, and the point at which a valid method becomes an atrocity. It is also the turn where she reaches for the human ground Sylvanas would have forced her to price much earlier.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the foothold changes anything beyond the local exchange
  - How much of Jaina's resistance depends on frameworks Kel'Thuzad can simply route around
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Chalk left in Jaina's hand before she realizes she picked it up
  - Wards humming under the floor like a second conversation
  - Silence used pedagogically rather than mercifully
- **Transition intent:** With the framework war exposed, Kel'Thuzad can finally name the personal pattern running beneath it

### Beat 5 - Scene: The parallel
- **Goal:** Refute or contain Kel'Thuzad's claim that Jaina followed the same impulse he did
- **Conflict:** The facts support him too well: unauthorized border crossing, contempt for institutional delay, trust in one's own analysis over sanctioned procedure
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the parallel cannot be dismissed cleanly, and it reframes Jaina's entire investigation as evidence for the same danger she is trying to deny
- **Value shift:** precarious foothold -> the mirror is named
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina and Kel'Thuzad were both produced by the same institution and broke with it for opposite ends
  - The difference between them is direction and cost, not basic method
  - Kel'Thuzad is not trying to corrupt her so much as document her as confirming data
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How motive can be defended in a framework that distrusts anything unverifiable
  - Whether Jaina can keep the mirror from opening wider once it has been named
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Kirin Tor texts on the shelves reading less like trophies than a still-active library
  - Kel'Thuzad's gentleness making the claim land harder
  - Jaina recognizing the pattern before she can reject it
- **Transition intent:** Once the mirror is named, the chapter can reach the one heartbeat where Jaina follows the logic too far

## Scene 3: The Mirror

**Scene function:** Deliver the chapter's core wound - the heartbeat where Jaina sees the structural beauty in Kel'Thuzad's reasoning and cannot unknow that she saw it.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Knowledge at scene start:** The mirror is named, and Jaina cannot dismiss it without lying to herself

### Beat 6 - Scene: The moment
- **Goal:** Resist following Kel'Thuzad's logic past the boundary where understanding becomes dangerous
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad moves from mechanics to implication: preserved neural pattern, continuity of learned structure, and the theoretical gradient between function and self
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Jaina does not agree with him, and she still follows the logic far enough to see its terrible beauty for one heartbeat
- **Value shift:** the mirror is named -> comprehension shock
- **New on-page information:**
  - The resonance model implies continuity as gradient, not clean wall
  - Jaina's mind can see theoretical beauty in a structure built for atrocity
  - Kel'Thuzad notices the precise instant she crosses and recoils
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What she can do with the fact that her mind followed the path at all
  - Whether this moment changes anything practical before Chapter 13 breaks the room open
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina's hands flattening on the table and going still
  - A ward ripple moving through the sanctum like an involuntary magical gasp
  - Silence dense enough to count as an event of its own
- **Transition intent:** The chapter now has to price that moment rather than rush past it

### Beat 7 - Sequel: The cost of understanding
- **Emotion:** Controlled intellectual horror
- **Dilemma:** Deny what happened and call it contamination, or admit that her analytical mind is morally neutral and would have kept going without friction from outside herself
- **Decision:** Sit with the fact honestly and recognize that Sylvanas's absent perspective was part of what kept this from happening earlier
- **Value shift:** comprehension shock -> analytical self-distrust
- **New on-page information:**
  - Understanding is not surrender, but it can still wound
  - Jaina now sees the partnership as epistemic friction, not only physical protection
  - The same habits that make her good at this work also make this kind of danger possible
  - Necromancy is not magically alien to her education; it is a continuous extension of familiar arcane logic, which is exactly why the ban had to be moral rather than mathematical
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How she keeps investigating without letting the same lens run unchecked again
  - How any institution would distinguish understanding from agreement if she reported this exactly
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Breath discipline used for composure instead of spellwork
  - The room's banality becoming worse precisely because it does not react
  - Research notes lying on the desk as if none of this were personal
- **Transition intent:** Kel'Thuzad now turns the wound toward Antonidas and makes the mirror intimate rather than abstract

### Beat 8 - Scene: The Antonidas thread
- **Goal:** Test the shared-teacher wound and expose how institutional caution shaped both Jaina and Kel'Thuzad
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad's assessment of Antonidas is accurate enough to hurt: generous teacher, limited thinker, unable to follow where his own best students went
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina gains a sharper language for the institutional failure, but the language also implicates the mentor she still loves
- **Value shift:** analytical self-distrust -> personal wound
- **New on-page information:**
  - Antonidas trained both Jaina and Kel'Thuzad in the same intellectual tradition
  - Institutional caution helped leave the kingdom analytically unprepared for necromantic war
  - Jaina's anger at Dalaran and fear of herself can coexist without cancelling each other
  - Antonidas and the Council banned practical necromancy because once the theory touches real bodies, the line between scholarship and violation collapses
- **Dialogue guidance:** Let Kel'Thuzad frame the ban as Dalaran losing its nerve at the point where sound theory touched real bodies; Jaina should hear how seductive that argument is even while rejecting its conclusion.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What she can safely tell Antonidas later without being treated as contaminated by the exchange
  - Whether Antonidas's caution was failure, mercy, or both
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Warmth entering Kel'Thuzad's voice only when he says Antonidas's name
  - Antonidas's notation system visible on every surface in the room
  - Jaina feeling the teacher present in both the monster's handwriting and her own habits
- **Transition intent:** Once the personal wound is open, Kel'Thuzad can widen the frame from one institution's failure to the real scale of the war

## Scene 4: The Revelation

**Scene function:** Expand the threat from plague mechanism to cosmic logistics, then show that Kel'Thuzad has already priced his own death into the system.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Kel'Thuzad
**Knowledge at scene start:** Jaina now understands both the mechanism and the personal mirror; the room is ready for scale

### Beat 9 - Scene: The Lich King
- **Goal:** Learn who or what sits above Kel'Thuzad and why the plague exists at all
- **Conflict:** The answer is bigger than rogue scholarship: a patron-intelligence in Northrend that found Kel'Thuzad before his expulsion and accelerated work the Kirin Tor would never sanction
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina gets the first classified layer of the real architecture, but the scale instantly outruns the frame she came in with
- **Value shift:** personal wound -> scope expands
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad names the Lich King as a bound intelligence in Northrend rather than a simple necromancer
  - Kel'Thuzad sees the relationship as patron-scholar, not master-servant
  - The plague is logistics, not merely experiment
  - Kel'Thuzad did not derive the whole design in isolation; Ner'zhul supplied theory, revisions, and patron knowledge beyond anything Dalaran could have given him
- **Disclosure provenance:** Kel'Thuzad is the first and only on-page source for the Lich King here; Jaina does not already have the term from Dalaran-side knowledge.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What created the Lich King
  - Whether anything above Kel'Thuzad and the Lich King is directing the preparation
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Older, colder ward signatures in the walls that do not feel arcane in any Kirin Tor sense
  - Kel'Thuzad sounding grateful rather than afraid when he describes the voice
  - Jaina feeling the room as instrument rather than shelter
- **Transition intent:** The patron now points to its own creator, and the frame widens one more time

### Beat 10 - Scene: The Burning Legion
- **Goal:** Understand the customer for the infrastructure Kel'Thuzad built
- **Conflict:** Kel'Thuzad widens the frame by naming the force above the Lich King, but he does it as strategic briefing rather than exhaustive cosmology, leaving Jaina with scale before she gets detail
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina gets the full strategic picture, but the picture is so large that it reveals how structurally insufficient her current tools are
- **Value shift:** scope expands -> world-scale threat
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad names the Burning Legion as the force above the Lich King
  - The Scourge is preparation for invasion, not the final threat
  - Jaina gets the name and strategic frame, not a full cosmology lecture
  - Some plague side effects and downstream behaviors may belong to the design stack above Kel'Thuzad rather than to anything he fully modeled end-to-end himself
- **Disclosure provenance:** Kel'Thuzad is also the first on-page source for the Burning Legion; it enters as a brief top-of-chain namedrop, not as ambient Dalaran knowledge or a full lore dump.
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Which current institutions, if any, can respond to a threat at this scale
  - Whether the countermeasure she can now design will matter in time
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina's fingers whitening where they lock together in her lap
  - Specimen jars shrinking into insignificance beside what they now imply
  - Kel'Thuzad's voice flattening into briefing register rather than debate register
- **Transition intent:** Once the scale is fully visible, the chapter only needs the final mirror - what Kel'Thuzad thinks his own death means inside it

### Beat 11 - Sequel: The expendability
- **Emotion:** The calm of a concluded proof
- **Dilemma:** Argue with a man who has already priced his own death into the continuity of his work, or finally stop debating and understand what actually separates her from him
- **Decision:** Stop arguing and register the real difference: Kel'Thuzad has a patron who will preserve his usefulness; Jaina has a partner who would come for her
- **Value shift:** world-scale threat -> the mirror completes
- **New on-page information:**
  - Kel'Thuzad expects resurrection as a lich and treats it as solved continuity rather than sacrifice
  - His attachment is to scholarship and continuation of method, not to mortal life as such
  - He treats the village plague's crude choir as proof-of-concept, not as the preservation standard he wants for himself
  - Jaina's real separation from him is relational context and value, not mere intelligence
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether she can get out with the data before the room changes from seminar back into battlefield
  - Whether Sylvanas's arrival will heal the absence or wound it further
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Kel'Thuzad discussing his own death with no more heat than he used for harmonic notation
  - Sanctum light dimming slightly as the wards cycle colder
  - Jaina thinking of Sylvanas not as theory but as the person who would come
- **Transition intent:** The conversation is functionally over; what remains is the silence it leaves behind and the disturbance coming through it

- **Emotion:** Heavy stillness
- **Dilemma:** Move immediately on incomplete internal processing, or sit one beat longer and understand what the conversation actually did to her before the next action begins
- **Decision:** Stay in the silence long enough for the weight to settle rather than pretending it has not changed her
- **Value shift:** the mirror completes -> the weight settles
- **New on-page information:**

- **New on-page information:**

  - Jaina recognizes the approach as Sylvanas before the name is spoken

- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How Sylvanas will interpret the tableau when she arrives
  - Whether Chapter 13 opens on rescue, confrontation, or both at once
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Defensive wards activating in layered ripples through the building
  - No visible spellcraft attached to the approach, only relentless room-clearing motion
  - Kel'Thuzad's attention redirecting upward with amused curiosity
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 13 begins at entry - Sylvanas in the sanctum, seeing the aftermath without the context
