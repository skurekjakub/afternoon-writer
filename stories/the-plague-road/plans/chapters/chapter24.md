# Chapter 24: The Condemned

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Opens hours after Chapter 23's arrest and closes after the verdict with Lor'themar outside the cell.
**Where:** Capital City palace dungeons.
**Travel:** Static imprisonment -> escorted movement in chains to the tribunal hall -> return to cells -> corridor-side contact through bars.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina in adjacent cell, palace guards, tribunal officials.
**Goal:** Survive the legal machinery long enough to keep the blade intelligence alive while the kingdom misreads the room above them.

## What we know

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- The pair saved the kingdom in the chamber and still left the room with the worst possible evidence profile.
- Jaina is alive but deeply depleted in the adjacent cell.
- Frostmourne sits in custody above them, no longer riding through Arthas but still tethered north and still active.
- Antonidas reached Capital City before the murder and still missed the room by the same brutal minutes that beat Uther.
- Uther arrived too late to witness the murder itself.
- Every warning the investigation ever sent can now be reframed as premeditation.

**What Sylvanas doesn't know yet**
- Whether Antonidas defies Dalaran publicly or obeys the line it draws around the tribunal.
- Whether Silvermoon stands behind her or cuts her loose completely.
- How quickly Frostmourne is seeking another mind through the palace.
- Whether Lor'themar or any outside ally reaches the city in time to affect anything.

**What NOT to do:**
- No cosmic visitor yet; that belongs to the next chapter's night.
- No promise of prison break, acquittal, or even competent containment.
- No certainty about who in the palace is already vulnerable to the blade's call until Jaina starts counting the rhythm through stone.
- No softening of the legal mechanism into simple corruption; it has to work cleanly and still produce the wrong answer.

**What they learn by the end**
- The tribunal convicts both women on every major count and commutes death to indefinite imprisonment.
- Antonidas comes with two Kirin Tor magi, but no sanctioned Kirin Tor witness takes the stand.
- Uther testifies clearly and is dismissed as a wounded former commander.
- Vereesa delivers the Convocation's disavowal in person and stays in the city afterward.
- Jaina clocks the blade's acceleration as days rather than weeks.
- Lor'themar arrives with three hundred Farstriders and mages who can independently feel the blade's wrongness.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- The battlefield is gone, but the need to read what is happening has not gone with it.
- Keep Jaina reachable through stone, keep the truth in circulation as long as possible, and find any remaining tactical surface inside a system built to deny one.
- The cold from the vault, Jaina's condition through the wall, the tribunal's elegance, and the certainty that Sylvanas can see each move coming without being able to stop it.
- Some part of Sylvanas still expects clean reading to generate leverage, even in a courtroom designed to leave the accused none.
- The chapter asks whether she can endure perfect comprehension without action - the form of helplessness most poisonous to her.
- Keep speaking the truth even as it arms the prosecution, or let silence cede the record entirely.
- Sylvanas moves from total abandonment to the first outside reinforcement, but only after the verdict has already locked.
- Chapter 25 opens with the pair still imprisoned, the blade seeking faster, and the larger board about to walk through the wall.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Adjacent voice, resonance instrument, and the only person in the palace who can hear how close the blade is to finding another hand.
- **POV pressure:** Sylvanas reads Jaina through breath, smell, tapping, and the body language of a woman forced to sit near the weapon she already scarred.
- No interiority. Jaina's pain and calculation stay external.

### Partnership / team
- Post-peak and reduced to what survives stone.
- The partnership stops being combat choreography and becomes shared endurance plus information transfer.
- Let the wall, the silence, and the chain of outside failures prove the bond. Do not overwrite the institutional brutality with sentiment.

## Scene 1: The Cells

**What happens:** Reframe the triumph as helpless imprisonment and establish Frostmourne as the second, more urgent prison above them.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina through the wall, guards, interrogators
**Where we start:** The room above them proved innocence only to people who were not there to see it.

### Beat 1
- Sylvanas can map the cell and listen to Jaina through the wall, but neither action opens the door or reaches the blade.
- She listens anyway because inventory is the only useful discipline left. The cell is small enough to map in paces and still useless once mapped. The cold rising through the floor is wrong in direction and intensity, which means the blade is already affecting the building. Jaina's breath and bread-smell through stone become the only functioning proof that the partnership still exists inside the system. They do not know how quickly the blade's pressure worsens or whether the tribunal reads the room as murder, conspiracy, or something even simpler.
- Thin torch bars on the wall through the grate; Frost smell replacing old dungeon damp; Jaina's uneven breathing carrying through masonry to elven ears.
### Beat 2
- Put the investigation's full truth into the official record.
- The truth is too large for the officers taking it down and will read as invention more cleanly than it reads as reality. Sylvanas tells the entire truth and the listeners visibly stop believing well before she finishes, which means the transcript itself becomes ammunition. The prosecution's questions were written before the interrogator entered the cell. Sylvanas can watch belief fail in real time as the scale of the truth grows. The entire investigation, stated plainly, sounds like the defense a guilty person would invent if she needed something too large to rebut. They do not know whether any judge or clerk reads past the implausibility of the content or whether Jaina hearing the whole account through the wall helps or only hurts.
- Clerk's pen scratching quickly across a board balanced on knees; Guard-captain posture shifting from attention to endurance; Iron lock turning again after the truth is filed away as fantasy.
### Beat 3
- The blade is active and looking, but the only people who can interpret what that means are in separate stone boxes.
- Sylvanas speaks through the wall and starts treating Jaina's attunement as the only live intelligence source left. The dungeon is getting colder because the blade is pulling heat through the foundations. Jaina can hear the weapon actively looking for a next mind. The partnership's wall-language now includes live threat reporting, not just endurance. They do not know which people in the palace are most vulnerable first or whether anyone outside the cells can be made to understand the danger before it chooses a new hand.
- Breath-clouds thickening in a cell that should not be this cold; Jaina's voice through stone saying one terrible verb: looking; Wool blanket on the cot smelling of frost instead of damp.

## Scene 2: The Trial

**What happens:** Show the system working well enough to condemn the wrong people and let the blade become evidence against its only readers.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Thornton, Ashcombe, palace mages, Uther, guards, gallery
**Where we start:** The blade is active, the accused are right, and the room above them is built to convert their competence into guilt.

### Beat 4
- Establish the prosecution's narrative and its control of the room.
- The narrative is elegant because it is built from real facts and only reverses the direction of causation. Ashcombe builds a clean conspiracy from the investigation's own paperwork, and Sylvanas can see every move while having no way to break the sequence. The prosecution treats months of correct investigation as months of deliberate preparation. Scholomance knowledge, Northrend travel, relay work, and warnings all become "proof" of design instead of response. Thornton's competence matters because the courtroom is not lawless; it is precise. They do not know whether any witness can puncture the prosecution's causation chain or whether the tribunal sees the pair as traitors, spies, or something worse.
- Light columns catching courtroom dust; Lighter courtroom chains that still say the system already won; Two-second look between Jaina and Sylvanas across a floor too wide to cross.
### Beat 5
- Present Frostmourne and hear how the court interprets its presence.
- The blade is genuinely horrifying, but the horror is being used to prove the accused knew too much to be innocent. The room feels the blade's wrongness, and the prosecution turns that wrongness directly into an argument against the only two people who understand it. Even lead, wards, and oak only muffle the blade; they do not quiet it. Palace mages can describe the weapon's effects without understanding its will or its history. Jaina is actively harmed by the exhibit while the court mistakes her knowledge for complicity. They do not know how much faster the vault wards degrade for every minute the blade spends in open hearing space or whether anyone in the room notices Jaina's reaction for what it really is.
- Lead-lined chest on a center table drawing warmth out of the room; Fogging breath above the clerk's notes; Jaina gripping chair arms hard enough for the knuckles to whiten.
### Beat 6
- Let Uther give the cleanest truth available to the room.
- The prosecution does not have to prove Uther wrong; it only has to prove him wounded and therefore interpretable. Uther's testimony lands with full factual force and is then neutralized by cross-examination built around his humiliation at Stratholme and his lateness to the actual murder. Uther can describe Hearthglen, Stratholme, and Arthas's deterioration in a way that should dismantle the case. The court only needs the fact that he was stripped of command and arrived after the act. Twenty minutes of architectural bad timing become the difference between witness and hearsay. They do not know whether any later witness is stronger than Uther (none are) or how Jaina is physically holding up under the blade's continued presence.
- Uther's hands closing into fists while the prosecutor reframes him; Formal paladin surcoat where armor should have been; Tribunal room going quieter because everyone feels the witness should matter more than the court lets him matter.

## Scene 3: The Betrayals

**What happens:** Let every outside system fail in its own style before the law says the word guilty.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Antonidas, two Kirin Tor magi, Vereesa, Thornton, Ashcombe, gallery
**Where we start:** The strongest witness in the kingdom just failed to change the frame.

### Beat 7
- Antonidas is in the hall with two Kirin Tor magi, but any sworn testimony or direct blade examination would publicly expose Dalaran's prior warning and failure.
- Sylvanas reads the line for what it is and does not waste breath pretending presence is the same thing as help. Antonidas came in person and is allowed to observe, object, and ask for continuance, but not to turn himself into official Kirin Tor evidence. The tribunal treats unsanctioned Kirin Tor examination of Frostmourne as tampering rather than rescue. The witness chair still stays empty, and that emptiness now lands harder because blue Kirin Tor robes are visible in the room. They do not know whether Antonidas's conscience breaks later into open defiance or whether the tribunal reads his constrained presence as caution, guilt, or political theater.
- Antonidas standing in the gallery instead of the witness box; One Kirin Tor mage going pale when the lead-lined chest is opened; Jaina not looking toward her mentor until the objection is denied.
### Beat 8
- Hear Silvermoon's formal position on Sylvanas in the aftermath of Stratholme and the palace killings.
- The Convocation's words are political, but the voice reading them belongs to Sylvanas's sister. The diplomatic shield dissolves completely, and the family bond is made into the instrument that delivers the cut. The Convocation claims Sylvanas acted without authorization and with no sovereign protection behind her. Vereesa was chosen as courier precisely because the words would hurt more in her voice. Vereesa stays in the hall afterward, which means the clean narrative is already cracking around the edges. They do not know whether Vereesa is still acting wholly as the Convocation's instrument by the chapter's end or whether any of Silvermoon's political cost can be turned back toward leverage later.
- Windrunner crest pinned to the cloak of the woman reading the disavowal; Gold wax seal broken in a room full of strangers; One brief look between sisters carrying more than the formal text does.
### Beat 9
- Hear the tribunal's official answer to everything the investigation did.
- The system is competent, the evidence is real, and the truth exceeds the legal frame offered to hold it. guilty on every major count, death commuted to indefinite imprisonment because politics prefers living prisoners to executed heroines. Thornton's ruling follows the prosecution's causation chain all the way to its intended end. Commutation is political management, not mercy. The kingdom now has an official history in which its saviors murdered its royals. They do not know whether the blade claims another mind before any appeal fiction matters or whether any outside force now has enough leverage to reopen what the court just closed.
- Thornton's pen steady in the same hand that handled every earlier procedure; Jaina's tremor stopping briefly in shock rather than recovery; Guards tightening chains only after the word guilty lands.

## Scene 4: The Aftermath

**What happens:** Make the verdict feel instantly secondary to Frostmourne's acceleration and bring the first outside reinforcement to the door too late.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina through wall, Lor'themar, guards
**Where we start:** The verdict is final on paper and useless against the blade above them.

### Beat 10
- They now know the blade's timetable is collapsing, but the only people who know it are still behind locked doors.
- Sylvanas starts treating elven sensitivity as the first corroboration path that might exist outside the cells. Jaina's tapping through the wall is how she counts the blade's accelerating cycle. The timeline has shrunk to days, not weeks. Elven mages near the vault might independently feel enough wrongness to corroborate what Jaina hears precisely. They do not know whether any corroboration can move fast enough to beat the blade or which palace minds are already nearest the call.
- Fingertip taps through stone counting the blade's heartbeat; Air in the cell getting colder after the verdict instead of warmer; Sylvanas holding a possible plan warm in her head because that is all she can physically hold.
### Beat 11
- Bring Lor'themar to the cell and establish what outside force is actually in play now.
- He arrived after the verdict, which means his presence changes the landscape but cannot undo what just happened. Lor'themar brings three hundred Farstriders and mages who already felt the blade in the palace foundations, but the cell remains shut and the blade's timeline remains brutally short. Lor'themar won just enough in Silvermoon to move a real force into Capital City. The elven mages can independently feel the vault's wrongness from outside the cells. Sylvanas is no longer alone politically, only still trapped physically. They do not know whether Lor'themar can turn perception into leverage before the blade chooses or whether Vereesa's continued presence helps or hinders that move.
- Familiar Farstrider cadence in dungeon footsteps before the face appears; Road-dust on Lor'themar where court polish should be; Two hands meeting across cold iron bars for the only contact the chapter allows.
