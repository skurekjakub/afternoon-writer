# Chapter 4: The Sick Village

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Two days after Chapter 3. The party has ridden south through ordinary-looking country marked by scattered sickness and stronger magical readings, not open collapse.
**Where:** A still-functioning village south of Millhaven where sickness is spreading but has not yet been named for what it is.
**Travel:** Southbound on Thalassian horses; on foot inside the village.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Goal:** Assess an ordinary-looking outbreak site before it quietly becomes another Millhaven.

**What Jaina knows at open**
- The survivor girl's testimony plus the follow-up patrol confirmed Millhaven's dead marched north toward Quel'Thalas.
- Millhaven appears to be the first village on this road fully claimed; the country beyond still looks ordinary enough to hide milder outbreaks.
- Her magical sensitivity lets the party notice trouble earlier than anyone else would, but not early enough to save a village once the sickness is already planted.
- The contamination is physical, pervasive, and lethal.
- The magical signature is necromantic in the broad Kirin Tor sense, even if the banned theories behind it sit outside her formal education.
- The survivor girl was found in Millhaven and sent north under Neral's escort on horseback.
- Neral has already departed north and is no longer with the riding party.
- The old seller and the girl's testimony together produced one weak clue about a newer grain seller, but nothing conclusive.

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- How the sickness first gets into people.
- Whether the old grain seller from the road matters.
- Whether this current village can still be saved.
- Whether someone is steering the outbreak or it is spreading on its own.
- The exact point where sickness turns a living person into the walking dead.

**What NOT to do**
- Do not confirm the grain connection before Jaina figures it out from patient tracking.
- Do not prove there's a cult before the granary scene.
- Keep the old seller a vague memory, not a solved mystery.
- Do not suggest Jaina is ignorant of necromancy in general; her gap is strictly in banned practice, not in knowing the subject exists.

**What they learn by the end**
- Grain carries the plague into people, and the road and storehouses are how it spreads.
- A live watcher existed inside the village and died by poison before he could be questioned.
- Andorhal is the next destination on the route.
- The old seller and the newer grain man remain loose, unproven scraps of information.

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- At the start, Jaina believes that if she can just figure out how the sickness enters the body, she can stop this village from becoming another Millhaven.
- She keeps reaching for a clean magical answer and treats Sylvanas's observations of the locals as background noise instead of part of the solution.
- As the chapter progresses, every magical lead forces her back to mundane details: food, labor, timing, and village habits.
- She has to choose whether to keep forcing a purely arcane explanation or to let Sylvanas's understanding of human fear, routine, and supply lines guide her investigation.
- By the end, Jaina has her answer but realizes she has less control than she thought. She drops the assumption that Sylvanas is just escort muscle and starts bringing the messy human details directly to the ranger.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- Jaina starts out seeing Sylvanas as a sharp soldier, but someone working below the level of the "real" magical problem.
- However, Sylvanas consistently spots the practical pressure points Jaina misses. This forces Jaina to admit Sylvanas isn't less intelligent, she just cuts through the same problem from a different angle.
- Jaina finds it easier to think of Sylvanas by name now, though the tone between them stays tense. By the end of the chapter, she stops lecturing downward.

### Partnership
- They walk into the village as two specialists sharing a road because neither can finish the job alone.
- The investigation proves their work only succeeds when Jaina reads the arcane layer and Sylvanas reads the human layer at the same time.
- The constant irritation remains, but Jaina's sense of superiority weakens. This isn't sudden trust or softness; it's just the first time Jaina fully accepts the other half of their method.

## Scene 1: The Village That Is Not Dead Yet

**What happens:** Show the plague while the village still thinks it's ordinary sickness, splitting the investigation between Jaina's sickroom magic and Sylvanas's read of the people.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel, militia captain, villagers.
**Where we start:** The contamination is real, but how it gets into people is still unknown.

### Beat 1
- The party enters a living settlement, but Jaina immediately senses the magical wrongness buried inside everyday life. The danger is hard to pin down because the locals only see a bad run of seasonal sickness, not a plague.
- Jaina realizes the contamination is observable before open collapse. The villagers don't know they are dealing with a spreading weapon. They still don't know how the sickness is getting in, or if intervening now will do more than just delay death.
- Bread smoke and market noise threaded with coughing; a flushed child kept on her feet because no one knows she should be in bed; a sick-sweetness hiding under ordinary village smells.
- The local militia captain blocks their path, forcing Sylvanas to negotiate their presence.

### Beat 2
- The militia captain has no reason to trust armed elves during a local outbreak. Sylvanas has to secure access without starting a fight over territory, bargaining with his exhausted authority instead of bulldozing it.
- Sylvanas gets them inside by confirming Millhaven's fate, forcing the captain to take the threat seriously. Jaina is surprised to see Sylvanas solve the room through diplomacy and pressure rather than force. They still don't know if what they find here will apply to other towns.
- Work-rough hands on the militia captain; Sylvanas lowering her voice instead of raising it; the heavy weight of "no one left in Millhaven" finally landing on the man.
- Jaina heads to the infirmary while Sylvanas works her way through the village's doors, stores, and frightened people.

### Beat 3
- Jaina begins mapping the contamination in living bodies. She is looking at the same necromantic pattern from Millhaven, but now it’s alive and spreading through tissue in real time.
- Early cases cluster the contamination in the gut. Only the sickest patients show the pattern spreading outward from the stomach, proving the body is taking it in and carrying it further. The village medic has been treating it as a fever because he doesn't know better. Jaina still doesn't know if the entry route is food, water, or contact.
- Sickly sweetness on infected breath; the glow of Jaina's magic examining living tissue; borrowed cots crammed into a room never meant to be an infirmary.
- Jaina doubles down on medical pattern recognition while Sylvanas gathers the village's recent history outward.

## Scene 2: Complementary Minds

**What happens:** Let each woman solve half the mystery until their answers finally meet in the middle.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel, medic, granary keeper, village civilians.
**Where we start:** The contamination is getting into people somehow, and the village's mundane routines hold the key.

### Beat 4
- Jaina uses stabilization wards to slow the contamination in some patients, but she can't treat the root cause while the village keeps eating from the same stores.
- She proves that partial magical intervention is possible, but it only buys time, not a cure. Most patients are still early enough to help if the entry route is found quickly, but she still doesn't know exactly what they are all ingesting.
- Blue stabilization light washing over the amber-wrong contamination patterns; a farmer's wife's hands going still; a child watching Jaina work from the corner.
- Jaina starts cross-referencing patient conditions against their daily diets and routines.

### Beat 5
- Jaina sorts through the ordinary food habits of the sick to isolate the exact entry route, locking onto the gut concentration as her primary clue.
- She proves the contamination enters through ingestion, and heavy bread-eaters are the worst cases. Those eating home-grown oats or separate provisions have lighter symptoms. The sickness is cumulative—repeated meals matter, not just one bad swallow. The grain is the weapon. She still doesn't know where the tainted grain enters the local supply.
- The village bakery visible from the infirmary door; grain dust lingering on wood and clothes; Jaina gripping the doorframe when the terrifying pattern finally clicks.
- Jaina now knows what is making people sick, but not who put it there.

### Beat 6
- Meanwhile, Sylvanas tracks what changed in the village just before the outbreak started. The locals only remember practical things—prices, deliveries, and routines—so she builds a model out of those mundane details.
- Sylvanas finds the village got a new grain supplier three months back. The supplier matches the "pleasant, forgettable, better prices" description from before. Sylvanas has completely mapped the people-and-road pattern, though she doesn't yet know mathematically why it matters. Jaina watches through the window, realizing how effectively Sylvanas is working.
- The grain-dusted hands of the storekeeper; Sylvanas asking very few questions but getting highly specific answers; Jaina watching through the glass, still half-caught in medical triage.
- The two women realize they need to piece their half-pictures together.

### Beat 7
- Jaina and Sylvanas share their findings immediately. Both realize their partial explanations fail alone but fit together perfectly.
- Jaina's medical answer and Sylvanas's practical answer form a complete picture: tainted grain is being moved through the trade routes on purpose. The granary isn't just a contaminated building; it's a crime scene. Jaina catches herself waiting for Sylvanas's half of the puzzle before fully trusting her own answer, a new and slightly disorienting feeling. They still don't know if this is a lone saboteur or a massive organization.
- Grain dust on Sylvanas's gloves; the residual ward-light fading from Jaina's hands; the heavy silence when both realize they reached the exact same horrifying conclusion from different directions.
- They drop everything and head straight for the granary to secure the grain.

## Scene 3: The Granary

**What happens:** Turn the medical investigation into an active hunt for the saboteurs.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel, cult initiate, militia captain.
**Where we start:** Grain is carrying the plague; the road and storehouses are spreading it.

### Beat 8
- They move to secure the grain stores, only to find a young initiate inside actively monitoring the operation. When he realizes he's caught, he reaches for a lamp to burn the evidence.
- Sylvanas stops the fire and Jaina restrains the cultist. The presence of a live watcher proves this is an organized poisoning operation. The contamination exists in a concentrated liquid form before it gets mixed into the flour. They still don't know the chain of command or the scale of the organization.
- A clay vessel shattering on the floorboards; the cloying, wrong smell of the concentrated agent; the thud of Sylvanas's arrow pinning his sleeve instead of killing him.
- Capturing and interrogating the watcher becomes their urgent priority.

### Beat 9
- The initiate is terrified and clumsy, making him look like an amateur, but Jaina has to triage the spilled evidence fast. They physically hold him down while Jaina works.
- Jaina realizes the concentrated liquid is master-level alchemical design, not something a local hedge-witch threw together. The plague sits on top of banned necromantic theories she only knows from whispered warnings in Dalaran. The boy is just a low-level cog, but they can't get any usable answers out of him yet.
- Shaking torchlight on the prisoner's terrified face; the shimmer of a magical barrier thrown over the broken liquid; Lyriel and Cyndia hauling the prisoner up under armed control.
- They move him to a holding cell, but the interrogation falls apart before it begins.

### Beat 10
- By the time they lock him in the cell, the initiate has already collapsed. He swallowed poison he was carrying specifically for this moment.
- The witness dies before he can talk. His suicide proves the plague operation is large and cold enough to train village-level watchers for capture and treat them as disposable members. They still don't know the exact name of the organization or the network's center point.
- A warm, freshly dead body on the cold cell floor; a bare trace of foam at the corners of his mouth; the quiet horror in the room as they realize how quickly they were outplayed.
- With their only lead dead, their only viable move is to trace the supply chain backward.

### Beat 11
- Exhausted but resolute, Jaina and Sylvanas have to decide whether to stay and help a village that thinks it just has a fever, or leave the partial relief behind to hunt the source before more towns fall.
- They decide to follow the grain network southwest. The source is no longer just an idea; it has a road and a destination: Andorhal. Jaina and Sylvanas are now fully solving the same puzzle from opposite ends. They still don't know who sits at the center of the network.
- Nighttime coughing echoing through otherwise quiet, ordinary houses; the fading hum of magic from Jaina's tired hands; the grim imagining of grain wagons already rolling somewhere to the southwest.
- Chapter 5 opens with the smaller riding party tracking ledgers, storehouses, and wagon routes toward Andorhal.
