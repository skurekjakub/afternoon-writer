# Chapter 6: The Pattern

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 5's route choice. The party is already on the southern feeder road that runs near Strahnbrad before the final Andorhal approach.
**Where:** Southern feeder road into Andorhal's network; Strahnbrad military zone ahead.
**Travel:** Mounted travel on Thalassian horses.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Goal:** Confirm the scale of the grain dark cult's plot and force someone in Lordaeron's command structure to take the warning seriously.

- Her sensitivity has put them ahead of what most of Lordaeron understands, but they are still chasing a poison already spread rather than preventing it

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- At the start, Jaina still believes clear evidence will force the prince to act, and wants to make Arthas understand the threat before Andorhal locks down.
- She keeps pushing facts while ignoring that Arthas and his officers care more about rank and visible skirmishes than an invisible plague.
- When Arthas brushes off her warning to deal with a visible enemy, Jaina is forced to accept Sylvanas's harsher view and act without official permission.
- By the end, she realizes that being right means nothing if Lordaeron's captains refuses to listen, entering the next chapter looking to the city for answers instead of the army.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- Sylvanas reads the soldiers, the rank, and the refusal much faster than Jaina does.
- Though Jaina hopes clear evidence will prove Sylvanas wrong, she quickly sees that Sylvanas accurately diagnosed Arthas's blindness before Jaina even finished talking.
- Jaina is still sharp with Sylvanas, but they argue as equals now rather than a mage talking down to a soldier.

### Partnership
- They are working together under strain, relying on collected proof rather than politics.
- They finally stop arguing about whether Lordaeron will listen and aim their shared frustration at Arthas instead.
- Disagreements stay grounded in the work without defaulting back to old prejudices.
- They still aren't comfortable with each other, but they now hate the military's blindness more than each other.

## Scene 1: Conspiracy Crystallizes

Make the plague look like deliberate, dark sorcery, not just a string of unfortunate sick villages.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel
**Where we start:** Grain road, organized cult, Barov connection, chosen road

### Beat 1
- Put the pieces together into one dark truth before they reach the city.
- Jaina has to face how elegantly designed the threat is—dark alchemy, necromancy, and daily bread trade perfectly combined.
- The dreadful truth solidifies, making the enemy look terrifyingly capable rather than just chaotic.

  - The plague is too perfectly tuned to be an accident or a lone wizard's stunt.
  - The living body is an active part of the design, not just a target.
  - Whoever built this had serious, formal magical training.
**What they don't know:**
  - The specific name of the mastermind.
  - How deep the cult's ladder goes before hitting the top.

  - Jaina mapping the route from horseback.
  - Ambient corruption causing a climbing headache.
  - The horses nervously cutting through damp road mist as the theory clicks.
- Jaina is privately repulsed by the sheer quality of the magical work as they approach the military camp.

### Beat 2
- Jaina wrestles with her own academic recognition of the enemy's brilliance.
- She can either suppress how flawless the dark magic is or admit that it borders on mastery.
- She admits the horrific quality of the work and forces herself to keep riding.

  - Jaina sees magical techniques she'd respect if they weren't being used for a genocide.
  - The plague rubs right up against forbidden Kirin Tor theories about keeping bodies useful after death.
  - Her disgust is sharper because part of her admires the craftsmanship.
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether she's looking at the work of someone she went to school with.

  - The magic buzzing harder in her temples.
  - A tight, white-knuckled grip on the reins.
  - The nausea of understanding the mechanics too well.
- They ride straight into the visible Lordaeron military encampment, bringing an invisible threat to a very visible war.

## Scene 2: Strahnbrad — The Prince

**What happens:** Jaina delivers a true warning to a prince who is too distracted to care.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel, Arthas, Lordaeron troops, Silver Hand presence
**Where we start:** The protagonists understand the plague's design; Lordaeron's leadership doesn't.

### Beat 3
- Find a Lordaeron captain with the authority to act on their warning.
- The camp at Strahnbrad is entirely consumed by the Blackrock orc raids, which are loud and politically easy to understand.
- They locate Arthas right where he was supposed to be, but he is already fighting a different war.

  - Their chosen route led them straight to the prince.
  - Lordaeron is pouring all its energy into a threat it can already see with its own eyes.
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether her past with Arthas will help make him listen or just get in the way.

  - Mud, shouting officers, and the heavy press of infantry banners.
  - A military chaos that feels completely different from the quiet Farstrider scouting.
  - Jaina realizing the army is looking the wrong way before Arthas even speaks.
- Jaina finally gets an audience with Arthas, but under the worst possible conditions.

### Beat 4
- Convince Arthas to hunt the poisoned grain.
- Arthas is perfectly polite but entirely focused on his skirmish. He hears her plague warning but treats it as a peasant's rumor.
- Jaina gives him exactly the right warning, and Arthas effectively brushes it off anyway.

  - Arthas listens to the whole warning but prioritizes his visible orc problem.
  - Their history makes the dismissal sting more, but it isn't the only reason he ignores her.
  - Jaina notices Sylvanas reading the prince's blind spots faster than Jaina can finish her argument.
**What they don't know:**
  - How many people will die because Arthas delayed.

  - Arthas looking polished, armored, and entirely too certain.
  - Jaina holding out the maps and notes like useless useless parchment.
  - Arthas using good manners like a shield to end the conversation.
- Arthas walks away, leaving Jaina to swallow the failure of the warning and the personal sting at the same time.

### Beat 5
- Process the sting of the audience and figure out what it means for the hunt.
- Jaina has to figure out if Arthas didn't listen because of their history, or if he just failed as a commander.
- She decides it's both, buries her anger, and accepts she has to bypass him.

  - Jaina realizes that being right about his stubbornness years ago and being right about the plague today yields the exact same empty result.
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether any human authority figure will believe them before the cities start dying.

  - The lingering space in the camp where he just stood.
  - A sour, metallic taste of swallowed anger.
  - The surrounding army still rushing past them, completely unaware.
- Sylvanas sizes Arthas up as a combat obstacle instead of just Jaina's ex.

### Beat 6
- Regroup and figure out the next step.
- Jaina's boiling anger and Sylvanas's cold read of Arthas need to match up so they can keep moving.
- They align their frustration outward. Lordaeron's captains are now just another wall to climb over.

  - Sylvanas diagnoses Arthas's exact military flaws without needing Jaina to explain their shared past.
  - They finally bond over the shared realization that the men in charge are blind.
  - Jaina realizes she is already looking to Sylvanas for the combat reality of the failure, not just sympathy.
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether Andorhal will give them evidence so loud nobody can ignore it.

  - Arthas's absence leaving a sharp clarity between the two women.
  - Sylvanas cutting right to the practical truth of his refusal.
  - The party wheeling their horses back toward the road.
- They leave the chaotic camp behind and ride into the heavily contaminated zone toward Andorhal.

## Scene 3: The Approach to Andorhal

**What happens:** Move past the frustration of the Arthas meeting, drop the creepy seller clue a second time, and finally put the plague city on the horizon.
**Cast:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Lyriel, second helpful trader (hidden cultist), Andorhal approach traffic.
**Where we start:** Arthas is officially useless to them; searching the city itself is their only remaining play.

### Beat 7
- They ask a seemingly normal grain trader for final directions. He helps them, but parts with the exact same eerie farewell phrase as the old man in Millhaven. "May the stars keep your road"
- The farewell wasn't a local quirk; it's a deliberate pattern. Sylvanas instantly locks onto it. They still don't know *why* it's happening, or that it's the exact same being wearing a different face.
- The trader's disarming, practiced helpfulness; the sudden chill of hearing that specific phrase land a second time; Jaina entirely failing to detect any magical signature on the man.
- They ride onward, the road narrowing as the ambient magical corruption turns physically sickening.

### Beat 8
- The magical rot radiating from the unseen city is now bad enough to physically hurt Jaina. She is tempted to stop and recover but forces herself to keep riding through the pain.
- Jaina isn't just reading the magic intellectually anymore; her body is reacting to the sheer, crushing volume of it. They still don't know if Andorhal is already a dead city.
- A heavy buzzing pressure directly behind Jaina's eyes; the air feeling sluggish and thick; the horses growing skittish and restless long before any buildings appear.
- They push through the pain until the city finally breaks the horizon line.

### Beat 9
- They finally lay eyes on the dark heart of the poisoned grain supply. Instead of a smoldering ruin, Andorhal looks completely normal and fully practical.
- The city is happily trading infected grain without knowing it's already dead. They don't yet know who inside the walls is managing the spread of the poison.
- The sprawling, active silhouette of the city; granaries visibly working on the skyline; a deep, invisible wrongness churning underneath the completely normal sounds of commerce.
- The chapter cuts off right there, handing the active approach to Chapter 7.
