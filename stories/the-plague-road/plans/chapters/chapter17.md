# Chapter 17: The Gate

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 16 at Stratholme's gates. Opens on the moment after Jaina answers Arthas honestly and closes after the Culling, with Arthas emerging from the gate beneath the wall — clean hands, wrong eyes.
**Where:** Stratholme's main gate and inner approach.
**Travel:** On foot at the gates and through the service lane; short climb to the inner wall; static witness position once the Culling begins.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers.
**Goal:** Survive Arthas's answer to "too late," get a workable read on the city once the source plan fails, and keep Jaina moving inside a decision neither of them controls.

## What we know

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- Jaina's source plan was real, practical, and too late at city scale.
- At least one granary could be broken, which proves the method. The problem is not theory now. The problem is time and mouths.
- The city is both seeded and partly steered: the crude choir can self-muster, but living Cult handlers are still hardening and directing pieces of the dead.
- Arthas arrived at the gate needing a yes-or-no answer and received no.
- Uther heard the same answer.

**What Sylvanas doesn't know yet**
- How far Arthas will carry "too late."
- Whether Uther will oppose him in the moment or only afterward.
- How long Jaina can stay upright once the prince turns the answer into policy.
- What the fifth farewell will attach itself to if it appears again.

**What NOT to do:**
- No clean emotional reconciliation.
- No explicit Mal'Ganis reveal.
- No interiority for Arthas, Jaina, or Uther.
- No framing that makes the Culling tactically easy just because it is tactically legible.

**What they learn by the end**
- Arthas answers Jaina's "too late" with the Culling.
- Uther breaks with him at the gate.
- Jaina refuses the order and stands outside the command chain with Sylvanas.
- The fifth farewell surfaces in the service lane from a fleeing grain carter who also provides useful route information to the wall.
- From the wall, Sylvanas reads the Culling as deliberate, methodical, and effective at preventing a full city-wide turn while still remaining an atrocity.
- The crack from Scholomance is functionally welded by shared witness before either woman says anything about it.
- Arthas emerges from the gate afterward with clean hands and the look of someone who has already moved on to the next necessity.

## Character Arcs

### Sylvanas
- Arthas may still be a usable ally if his answer stays inside hard containment rather than crossing into something worse.
- Keep a clear read on the city, keep Jaina on her feet, and decide where to stand once the prince chooses.
- Jaina's failed source plan, Arthas's speed, Uther's looming response, and the fact that Sylvanas lit this prince's road back in Chapter 11.
- Sylvanas still wants to believe there may be a version of this decision that remains brutal but contained.
- The chapter asks whether she can watch a tactically coherent atrocity and refuse to let coherence excuse it.
- Stay close enough to read and survive, or step away far enough to keep her own hands clean and risk losing the truth of what happened here.
- Arthas stops reading as a hard ally and starts reading as a man who has crossed a line fast enough that only the aftermath remains to track.
- Chapter 18 opens with Jaina on the wall after the screaming stops and Arthas walks away.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- Witness, failed rescuer, and the person whose honest answer made the next order possible without causing it.
- **POV correction:** The source attempt gives Sylvanas the field proof she needed. Whatever Jaina did in Kel'Thuzad's room, she spent the knowledge here trying to save strangers.
- No interiority. Show Jaina through silence, posture, spell residue, and where she fixes her eyes once the gate order lands.

### Partnership / team
- Damaged but synchronized.
- Shared witness finishes what the source attempt started. The crack is not talked through. It is welded by where they stand and what they keep seeing.
- No spoken absolution. No emotional summary language. Let the weld live in placement, timing, and who stays beside whom when leaving would be easier.

## Scene 1: The Gate

**What happens:** Turn Jaina's honest answer into Arthas's order and force the moral split into the open.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, gate soldiers
**Where we start:** The source plan failed because it was late, not because it was wrong.

### Beat 1
- Arthas converts "too late" directly into the Culling
- He is now moving faster than anyone else in the gate group can morally follow. Falric and the command edge are already orienting around execution rather than debate. They do not know whether Uther will refuse in words only or in action or where Sylvanas and Jaina can stand that keeps them clear enough to witness and alive enough to leave.
- Arthas's voice flattening instead of rising; Gate guards stiffening before the last words land; Jaina's flour-gray hands still dirty from the failed stores while the prince gives a clean order.
### Beat 2
- Uther's break is immediate and public
- Jaina stands outside Arthas's order, which means the old bond between them is functionally over in the only way that matters now. Sylvanas is no longer watching a hard ally make a hard call; she is watching a man step past his own checks. They do not know whether the city can still be read from anywhere but inside the purge line or what the prince will look like once the city is done.
- Uther's hammer held low but final; the scrape of armored men shifting when loyalties become physical; Arthas turning away from both mentor and former lover without losing pace.

## Scene 2: The Service Lane

**What happens:** Move the trio off the main gate line, surface the farewell a fifth time, and let the city become legible as a military operation unfolding under horror.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, fleeing civilians, grain carter, scattered soldiers
**Where we start:** The Culling is underway. Witness now matters as much as survival.

### Beat 3
- Sylvanas actively chooses witness instead of futile participation
- Jaina keeps moving only because motion is simpler than standing at the main gate and listening. The service route offers access to the inner wall without putting them under Arthas's immediate command. They do not know how much of the city they can still see from the wall or whether the wall will make the Culling feel cleaner or worse.
- A side lane clogged with handcarts and dropped sacks; Cyndia using her horse as moving cover long enough to clear the turn; Jaina stumbling once and correcting without speaking.
### Beat 4
- The city's service routes make the wall reachable without reentering the main gate crush
- An apparently ordinary grain carter has precise, useful knowledge at exactly the right moment. "May the stars keep your road" surfaces a fifth time under pressure and cannot be dismissed as coincidence any longer. They do not know whether the pattern ties to the plague, the route, or only to these women being watched or what the city will look like once viewed from above.
- Empty wagon bed dusted with flour; a man talking too fast because fear is finally catching him; the farewell spoken like habit, not performance.
### Beat 5
- Arthas's purge is being run district by district, not as blind slaughter
- That method is what will keep the dead from claiming the city in one mass turn. Tactical coherence does not make the thing below them any less monstrous. They do not know how long Arthas can keep the pattern controlled or what Jaina is doing with herself beside Sylvanas while the city starts dying under a correct pattern.
- Torch lines cutting across a street grid; Smoke rising in separate columns instead of one general burn; the sound of doors being broken in rhythm rather than panic.

## Scene 3: The Wall

**What happens:** Deliver the operational witness to the Culling and end on the clean-hands image that hands the inner reckoning to Chapter 18.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas's forces below
**Where we start:** The source attempt failed, the Culling is underway, and the city is now a process they cannot stop.

### Beat 6
- Arthas is tactically preventing a full city-wide turn by killing the city in controlled sections
- The same method that makes the operation effective is what makes it unforgivable. Jaina stays on the wall beside Sylvanas instead of breaking away, which matters more than either woman saying anything. They do not know what this will leave in Arthas when it is over or whether Sylvanas can ever reduce what she is seeing back into report language.
- District fires stepping outward in sequence; Soldiers rotating lines with the discipline of a siege instead of a panic; Jaina so still beside the parapet that Sylvanas starts tracking her the way she tracks her own bow hand.
### Beat 7
- Arthas had time, or made time, to wash before stepping back into view
- He knows the wall holds witnesses and prepares for that fact. Sylvanas now has an image she can carry south that is cleaner and more damning than battlefield gore. They do not know what Jaina is making of the same image from inside her own history with him or what direction the pursuit takes once the city is behind them.
- Pale hands catching firelight with no blood on them; Arthas's face emptied out instead of inflamed; the city below gone quiet except for burning timber.
