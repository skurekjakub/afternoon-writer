# Chapter 22: The Return

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore in Scenes 1-3, Sylvanas Windrunner in Scenes 4-5 (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Opens hours after Chapter 21's retreat from the Frostmourne cavern and closes at the palace approach in Capital City.
**Open location:** The road south toward Muradin's camp in Dragonblight.
**Transport:** Casualty carry on foot -> camp handoff -> return march to Daggercap Bay -> southbound crossing aboard the *Wolfheart* -> immediate landfall at Southshore -> overland race on the King's Road -> final approach on foot toward the palace.
**Active cast at open:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, unconscious Muradin, Baelgun shortly after, Dagny later.
**Immediate objective:** Get the cave intelligence off Northrend, turn it into a workable anti-blade plan, and reach Capital City before the kingdom's lie hardens completely around Arthas.

## Knowledge ledger

**What the formation knows at open**
- Arthas claimed Frostmourne and the prince they chased is gone in the only useful sense.
- Muradin is alive enough to carry but badly wounded.
- Frostmourne's field contains a seam - an oscillation point between consuming and radiating phases.
- The *Wolfheart* is still the only usable route back south.
- No warning is going to matter unless it is fast, brutal, and simple.

**What the formation does NOT know at open**
- Whether Muradin survives the handoff to his camp.
- How much of Arthas's return can still be interrupted politically.
- Whether any warning sent from the bay reaches someone in time to matter.
- Whether Jaina can turn the oscillation point into something usable under live combat pressure.
- How deeply Arthas has already settled his people inside the palace by the time they reach Lordaeron again.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No fully complete throne-room choreography before the road-planning beats earn it.
- No clean promise that Antonidas's arrival, Uther's access, or any institution can still stop Arthas from outside the room.
- No claim that Jaina can narrow the widened sensitivity once this chapter finishes using it.
- No tribunal logic yet; that belongs to the aftermath chapter.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Muradin is handed to Baelgun's camp alive enough to keep.
- The warnings go out by every available lane and fail in practice.
- Arthas has been in Capital City for four days before the formation reaches the walls.
- Jaina's return push is faster than the voyage north and more damaging.
- The oscillation finding becomes a three-school framework and then a physical choreography.
- Sylvanas is the named combat partner who must buy the spell its seconds.
- Antonidas reaches Capital City with two Kirin Tor magi, but their help stops at the palace margins.
- Uther is in the palace but still cannot reach the king in time by formal means.
- Cyndia's protective sweep now covers Jaina as well as her older lane.
- The formation reaches the palace with no public alarm and no useful witness guaranteed.

## Arc position

### Jaina (Scenes 1-3)
- **Current stance at open:** The cave loss only matters if she can turn what she heard there into a different kind of weapon.
- **Surface objective:** Extract the formation, get the warning south, and turn the seam in Frostmourne's field into a framework someone else can actually help execute.
- **Pressure source:** Muradin's weight in the snow, Arthas's lead, the louder bread-taste, the widened channel, and the fact that every hour spent being thorough is an hour the prince gets closer to the throne room.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Jaina still wants the finding itself to be the answer, as if seeing the seam clearly enough could bridge the distance between understanding and delivery.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can stop treating intelligence as self-sufficient and admit it only matters if it can survive in another person's hands and body.
- **Forced choice:** Spend herself to get home fast and keep thinking, or conserve herself and arrive with more reserves but no kingdom left to save.
- **End-state shift:** Jaina moves from holding a flaw in the blade to holding a framework that only matters if someone else buys her time to use it.

### Sylvanas (Scenes 4-5)
- **Current stance at entry:** Political rescue is over. If there is still a way to matter, it lives inside the room where Arthas stands, not in any court or council around it.
- **Surface objective:** Deliver Jaina to the throne room in castable condition and turn the anti-blade framework into a live engagement plan before they reach the palace doors.
- **Pressure source:** Jaina's depletion, the kingdom's festive ignorance, the lack of allies who can act, and the knowledge that the final plan spends Sylvanas's own body as part of its machinery.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas still wants the problem to become tactically neat if she plans hard enough, even though politics has already made it dirty beyond repair.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can stop leaving "someone" abstract and become the combat partner in practical terms rather than in implication.
- **Forced choice:** Keep the role language vague, or let the planning itself expose the fact that she is the one who must stand in Frostmourne's reach.
- **End-state shift:** Sylvanas moves from co-investigator to the person who will physically hold the death knight in place while the mage works.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Post-recognition under retreat strain - warning, analysis, motion, no spare language.
- **Change this chapter:** The partnership moves from "we know the problem" to "we have a real answer only if both bodies do their part."
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** Let warning failure, permitted overreach, pronoun shift, and final threshold carry the change. Do not narrate romance or sacrifice ahead of page proof.

## Scene 1: The Handoff

**Scene function:** Strip the cave down to its immediate cost and force the return south to begin before grief, warmth, or argument can slow it.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Muradin, Baelgun
**Knowledge at scene start:** Arthas has Frostmourne, Muradin is down, and the formation got out clean only by retreating fast.
**Arc pressure:** Keep Muradin's body, dwarven camp labor, and Jaina's shaking hands visible so the chapter starts in aftermath rather than in theory.

### Beat 1 - Scene: Baelgun
- **Goal:** Get Muradin to people who can keep him alive and learn whether the formation can stay without being swallowed by the same danger that wrecked the cave fight.
- **Conflict:** The camp can treat Muradin; it cannot solve Frostmourne or keep S&J safe if they linger.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Baelgun takes Muradin and promises the camp will hold him, but the handoff comes with the blunt truth that S&J need to leave now and carry the warning themselves.
- **Value shift:** clean retreat -> responsibility narrowed to the pair who understand it
- **New on-page information:**
  - Muradin is alive enough for treatment, even if nothing more can be promised yet
  - Baelgun reads the danger as something that can come looking for them if they stay put
  - The camp keeps Muradin; the investigation keeps the burden of warning the south
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Muradin wakes in time to matter later
  - Whether any warning can beat Arthas home
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Dwarven hands taking Muradin's weight without ceremony
  - Blood freezing dark in beard braids and on fur trim
  - Baelgun sounding angrier than shocked because anger is the only useful shape left
- **Transition intent:** Once Muradin is handed off, the chapter can let Jaina sit with what the cave actually gave her.

### Beat 2 - Scene: Departure
- **Goal:** Leave the camp before comfort, grief, or debate can slow the return.
- **Conflict:** The camp offers a pocket of warmth, but warmth here is just delay dressed kindly.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the formation turns back toward the coast immediately, but they leave behind the only shelter they have seen in days and carry no guarantee that the race south is still winnable.
- **Value shift:** responsibility narrowed -> the return begins
- **New on-page information:**
  - No one wastes time pretending a fuller recovery window exists
  - Light's Vengeance and the cave both remain behind them, unresolved
  - Jaina leaves still half inside the fight because the field's rhythm has not left her head
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the return crossing can be made faster than the voyage north
  - Whether Jaina's head is carrying a weapon or only the ghost of one
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Camp heat falling away after a few strides
  - Muradin gone from view behind palisade timber
  - Jaina's fingers already tracing the same invisible pattern as they walk
- **Transition intent:** The chapter now has room to stay inside the march long enough for the oscillation point to become more than panic memory.

- **Decision:** She keeps working the rhythm in her head instead of turning the retreat into blank grief.
- **Value shift:** retreat begins -> the blade acquires a flaw

- **New on-page information:**

  - Whether she can carry this line of thought without letting it hollow her out
- **Sensory anchors:**

  - The remembered pitch-change in Frostmourne's cycle repeating under her skull
  - Sylvanas beside her as the silent proof that the thinking is not happening in isolation
- **Transition intent:** After the echo beat, the body has to remind Jaina what this thinking is costing.

### Beat 5 - Sequel: The Resonance Hangover
- **Emotion:** The body's revolt against everything the last two chapters demanded of it.
- **Dilemma:** Jaina can keep using the channel that is making her useful, but every use makes it more likely the channel stops being optional.
- **Decision:** She keeps using it because the return requires intelligence more than comfort.
- **Value shift:** moral pressure -> the cost becomes bodily and permanent-feeling
- **New on-page information:**

  - Jaina's tremor is now steady baseline, not merely post-casting aftershock

  - Whether the price of hearing the blade matters first in the fight or only in the aftermath
- **Sensory anchors:**

  - Fingers that never fully stop shaking

- **Transition intent:** Once the body has stated the cost, the chapter can get them back to the coast and test whether warnings matter at all.

## Scene 3: Daggercap and the Crossing

**Scene function:** Spend every warning lane, prove how late they are, and turn the sea crossing into the price of a usable framework.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Knowledge at scene start:** The pair have a flaw in the blade's cycle, but no delivery method and no political cover.
**Arc pressure:** Keep docks, pigeons, quartz stone, sail strain, food, and nosebleeds concrete so warning failure and breakthrough both feel paid for.

### Beat 6 - Scene: Daggercap Bay
- **Goal:** Reach the bay and get the ship turned around immediately.
- **Conflict:** Every hour spent in harbor is an hour Arthas uses better than they can, and nobody on the quay can solve that.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Dagny gets the *Wolfheart* ready on speed terms rather than comfort terms, but the harbor itself offers no answer beyond "leave now."
- **Value shift:** body cost -> urgency becomes logistical
- **New on-page information:**
  - Dagny understands from one look that the southbound run must be immediate
  - The bay still bears the scar of Arthas's passage and offers no new hope
  - The return voyage will be faster only if Jaina is willing to pay harder than she paid northbound
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the price of that speed leaves anything for the throne room
  - Whether a warning launched from here can reach anyone meaningful in time
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Same harbor, now uglier because they know what left it
  - Crew moving turnabout work at a captain's clipped command pace
  - Jaina looking at the water like distance has become a personal insult
- **Transition intent:** Once the ship can sail, the chapter has to spend every message lane that still exists.

### Beat 7 - Scene: The Warnings
- **Goal:** Send every warning available before the ship clears the bay.
- **Conflict:** Every channel is structurally weak for a truth this large - birds can miss, stones can drain, institutions can believe and still fail to act.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the warnings are sent, and the most important return intelligence says Arthas is already in Capital City.
- **Value shift:** urgency becomes logistical -> urgency becomes hopelessly late
- **New on-page information:**
  - Sylvanas launches the warning through military shorthand and Farstrider signaling while Jaina reopens the Antonidas line one last time
  - Antonidas confirms Arthas arrived home days ago and the court is celebrating rather than bracing
  - The world believes the prince ended the threat instead of carrying it home
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any single bird or reader acts against the momentum of celebration
  - Whether arriving before the parade matters less than arriving before the blade feeds too deeply
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Carrier pigeons flinging themselves south over frozen water
  - Amber light in the sending-stone dying in Jaina's hand before the conversation is finished
  - A flare or relay signal blooming uselessly beautiful against a sky too wide to answer
- **Transition intent:** Once the warnings fail in practice, the only thing left is speed.

### Beat 8 - Scene: The Return Crossing
- **Goal:** Get back to Lordaeron faster than the northbound voyage took.
- **Conflict:** The southbound sea offers no supernatural wall, which means Jaina can push harder and damage herself more cleanly.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the crossing compresses brutally, but the speed comes straight out of Jaina's reserves and leaves almost nothing unspent by landfall.
- **Value shift:** hopelessly late -> progress purchased at a harsher exchange rate
- **New on-page information:**
  - The return run is faster because the source no longer cares enough to oppose them directly
  - Dagny can tune the ship around Jaina's output with unnerving efficiency
  - Cyndia's protective attention to Jaina becomes visible even without explanation once the bread-smell strengthens
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Jaina arrives with enough left for the real fight
  - Whether anyone ashore has used the warning intelligently
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Almost constant nosebleed by the end of the run
  - Food forced into Jaina's hands because logistics outrank appetite
  - The *Wolfheart* running fast under a wind that now comes from one exhausted mage instead of from weather
- **Transition intent:** Once the ship is moving south fast enough, the chapter can let the flaw become something closer to a weapon.

### Beat 9 - Sequel: The Breakthrough
- **Emotion:** Exhaustion sharp enough to strip away ornament.
- **Dilemma:** The oscillation point is real, but a flaw is useless unless Jaina can imagine what actually passes through it and changes the blade from inside.
- **Decision:** Jaina builds the first complete framework: frost to match the rhythm, arcane to penetrate the seam, fire to alter the binding from inside. It still needs delivery.
- **Value shift:** progress purchased at a harsher exchange rate -> the flaw becomes a working framework
- **New on-page information:**
  - The weakness is not a break point; it is a transition point
  - The answer requires three schools in sequence rather than one overwhelming spell
  - The framework only matters if someone can buy Jaina three to five seconds within arm's reach of the blade
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who actually gives her those seconds
  - Whether Jaina's depleted body can cast the framework even once under combat conditions
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Night stars over a ship still carrying the day's push in its timbers
  - Sylvanas sitting beside Jaina because the finding now needs to become work
  - Jaina laying the sequence out in plain field language instead of Scholomance terms
- **Transition intent:** Once the framework exists, the chapter can land them in Lordaeron and turn "someone" into a road south problem.

### Beat 10 - Sequel: Landfall
- **Emotion:** Arrival without relief.
- **Dilemma:** Home soil should mean support; instead it confirms the warning died in the distance and the final leg has to be walked anyway.
- **Decision:** South. Immediately. No pause longer than dockwork requires.
- **Value shift:** the flaw becomes a working framework -> the final movement begins
- **New on-page information:**
  - Southshore is ordinary, calm, and fatally unwarned
  - Jaina's collapse on stopping the push proves how little she has left under the skin
  - The three-school framework survives the voyage intact enough to carry into planning
- **Still unknown after beat:**

  - Lordaeron earth feeling soft and almost warm after Northrend ice
  - Jaina's knees giving under her the moment she stops feeding the sails
  - Sylvanas's hand near enough to catch without actually taking Jaina's weight
- **Transition intent:** The second half opens on the King's Road with garlands, a road-dirty kingdom, and a plan that still needs a body to carry it.
