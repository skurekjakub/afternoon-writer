# Chapter 7: Andorhal

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 6. The party reaches Andorhal after Arthas's dismissal and enters before the city understands it is being investigated.
**Where:** Andorhal's gates and market district, with inspection offices, granaries, and the mage quarter beyond.
**Travel:** Mounted arrival on Thalassian horses; on foot once inside the city.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel.
**Goal:** Enter Andorhal under cover, identify how the plague is being planted at the city's heart, and map the cult movements carrying it.

## Scene 1: The Infiltration

****What happens:**** Get the party inside Andorhal under plausible cover and add the two local allies who let the investigation operate in layers instead of brute force.
****Cast:**** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, city guards, clerks, Corin, Captain Havel
****Where we start:**** Grain is the poison; Andorhal is the heart; the city still functions outwardly

### Beat 1
- Enter Andorhal without advertising that the investigation has reached the city itself
- The city is functioning well enough that officials still think in terms of paperwork, borders, and routine inspections rather than an active plague conspiracy
- the cover holds and gets them through the gate, but the ordinary city life makes the scale feel worse rather than safer


  - Andorhal is still operating as a market city rather than a ruin
  - Jaina can navigate guard checkpoints and court rules well enough to make the cover believable
  - Sylvanas immediately starts reading guards, exits, choke points, and granary traffic
**What they don't know:**
  - How the grain is being treated inside the city
  - Which officials are merely bureaucrats and which belong to the cult

  - Hooves on stone at the gate
  - Grain wagons and market chatter moving past people who think their city is normal
  - Jaina's false paperwork beside Sylvanas's real threat assessment
 Once inside, they need someone local who has been seeing the pattern without being believed

### Beat 2
- Find a magical contact who can get Jaina into the right rooms and translate what Andorhal's mages have been missing
- Corin is competent but junior, frightened, and already trained by experience to expect his correct reports to vanish into Dalaran's endless bureaucracy
- Corin becomes an ally and opens institutional doors, but his history proves the scribes have already been absorbing and discarding warning signs


  - Corin has been tracking tainted shipments for weeks
  - His reports disappeared into the same kind of bureaucracy Jaina has always trusted
  - The magical layer of the city has been producing warnings long before the field party arrived
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether Corin has seen the ritual itself or only the symptoms around it
  - Whether any part of the Kirin Tor's silence is incompetence rather than compromise

  - Ink-stained hands and sleepless eyes
  - Stacks of ignored notices and copied route marks
  - Corin trying not to sound relieved when somebody finally takes him seriously
 Corin can open the mage-side doors, but Sylvanas still needs someone who reads the city from the street up

### Beat 3
- Add a ground-level local read on Andorhal's granary district and human routines
- Havel is suspicious of outsiders and has only pattern, not proof: sick workers, new grain routes, and a district full of people who behave like civilians until they don't
- Havel gives them the physical city they need, but his evidence is still instinct and repetition rather than a case anyone above him would honor


  - Havel has been seeing sickness cluster around the grain system for months
  - A new supplier pattern entered the city roughly three months ago
  - Certain workers in the granary district keep terrifying discipline under peasant clothes
**What they don't know:**
  - Who directs those workers
  - Whether the city knows it is hosting cult movements or merely living atop it

  - Militia leather carrying street dust instead of parade polish
  - Havel's short, exact answers once he decides to answer at all
  - Names and routes spoken like ranger barks rather than wild guesses
 With Corin and Havel in place, the investigation can split along the exact seam Sylvanas and Jaina have been building toward

### Beat 4
- Professional recognition stripped of ceremony
- Keep the team together for safety, or split Jaina and Sylvanas so each can work at full competency in the layer only she can read
- Jaina takes Corin and the mage-side access to study the contamination; Sylvanas takes Havel and the granary district to map the human structure


  - Jaina can function unsupervised in magical halls Sylvanas would only slow down
  - Sylvanas can read hidden cultists and supply movements in ways Jaina cannot
  - Cyndia and Lyriel anchor the physical security layer while the leads split
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether the city is already reacting to them
  - Which half of the investigation will reach the real danger first

  - Havel unrolling district notes while Corin fumbles for keys and access seals
  - The four-woman pack redistributing itself without discussion
  - Sylvanas realizing the split feels efficient, not reckless
 The city now yields two separate halves of the same answer

## Scene 2: The Mechanism Revealed

****What happens:**** Let Jaina solve the how and Sylvanas solve the who until the halves lock together into one picture of the cult.
****Cast:**** Jaina, Corin, Sylvanas, Havel, Cyndia, Lyriel, granary workers, dark alchemists
****Where we start:**** Andorhal has both magical and corrupted guards; the city is hiding a system, not an accident

### Beat 5
- Determine exactly how contaminated grain is being treated inside a working city mills
- The dark ritual is buried inside routine packaging work, meaning Jaina has to read active arcane traces without alerting everyone standing around them
- she isolates the dark ritual cleanly, but the sophistication tells her the enemy is closer to dark scholarship than hedge magic


  - The grain is treated during packaging with a necromantic carrier layer rather than fouled at harvest
  - Milling, moisture, and baking carry that outer shell deeper into ordinary use instead of cleansing it away
  - The strongest signature in the grain still looks like only the carrier half of the plague, implying the host body finishes the work later
  - The work shows high-arcanum alchemy and disciplined arcane mastery
  - Jaina's answer lands cleanly even without Sylvanas standing beside her
**What they don't know:**
  - Who designed the process
  - Whether Andorhal is the top of the system or merely a mid-chain hub
  - How the cursed victim becomes undead after death

  - Dusty grain bags carrying a magical wrongness too fine for normal senses
  - Corin holding his breath while Jaina works through the poison's shape
  - Profane taint hidden in places any ordinary inspector would miss
 Jaina now has the dark ritual but needs the human map Sylvanas is building elsewhere in the city

### Beat 6
- Identify how many living hidden cultists operate in the granary district and how they are structured
- The city is full of workers, porters, guards, and clerks; the enemy survives by looking like one more layer of ordinary labor
- Sylvanas maps the local structure and hidden supply lines, but the mastermind stays out of reach


  - At least eight hidden cultists operate in the district
  - The cell has a two-tier chain of command rather than random zealots
  - Off-ledger caches and movement patterns prove the conspiracy has been operating here for months
**What they don't know:**
  - Who sits above the local cult leaders
  - Whether the city's militia captains are corrupted or merely blind

  - Workers whose stillness reads like trained waiting, not fatigue
  - Havel noticing the same people never drink with the rest after shift
  - Hidden route marks and storage habits that make no sense on clean ledgers
 The two paths of the hunt now need to be put on the same table before the city moves

### Beat 7
-
-
-


  - Andorhal is a functioning mill city cursed from the inside
  - High-arcanum poison and disciplined cult movements are inseparable parts of one system
  - Jaina has the poison's source and Sylvanas the cult's shape, but the final waking curse still has to be earned elsewhere
  - Corin and Havel can support the hunt, but neither can replace one of the two leads
  - Sylvanas cannot honestly describe Jaina's work as support anymore; without that half, there is no case against the city at all
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether the city already suspects them
  - Whether the cult has already completed its wider spread

  - Jaina's notes and Sylvanas's route marks overlaying each other
  - Corin and Havel watching two different forms of expertise arrive at one answer
  - The room going quiet when the scale stops being a wild guess
 Once the true conspiracy is visible, the next move becomes a choice between tracing material and taking a live witness

## Scene 3: The Load-Bearing Partnership

****What happens:**** Turn the Andorhal breakthrough into an hunting plan and make Sylvanas admit, privately, that the partnership is no longer optional.
****Cast:**** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Corin, Havel
****Where we start:**** The dark ritual and the local cult cell are both in hand, but the inner circle remains unnamed

### Beat 8
- Decide the next move before the enemy realizes Andorhal has been breached
- Jaina wants the arcane trail back to its source; Sylvanas wants a captured zealot who can name the cult masters; either move alone leaves half the gap open
- the disagreement resolves into a joint plan instead of a split plan, but that only works because each woman trusts the other to handle the half she cannot


  - A live capture is possible tonight because the cult still feels secure in its own city
  - Jaina's arcane tracking can validate or break whatever a prisoner says
  - Cyndia and Lyriel are strong enough on perimeter to let the leads commit fully
**What they don't know:**
  - Whether the city has already marked Corin or Havel as leaks
  - How fast the cult can scatter once it senses contact

  - Candlelit maps weighted by knives, keys, and chalk
  - Havel's fallback routes and Corin's access notes spreading across the same table
  - Jaina and Sylvanas disagreeing without losing alignment
 Once the plan exists, the chapter only needs the private recognition of what that plan depends on

### Beat 9

-

  - The mission's real structure is now a true bond of shared steel and magic

**What they don't know:**
  - Who will break first once the capture starts

 Chapter 8 opens with the ambush and the moment the hunters discover the city has been hunting back
