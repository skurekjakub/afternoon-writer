# Chapter 25: The Stinger

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore in Scene 1, Sylvanas Windrunner in Scene 2 (both limited third, absolute)
**When:** Opens the night after Chapter 24's verdict, with S&J still in adjacent dungeon cells beneath a palace holding an unheld Frostmourne still tethered north.
**Where:** Capital City palace dungeons - adjacent cells divided by two feet of stone.
**Travel:** Static imprisonment only. Motion belongs to the blade's pulse, voices through the wall, and the visitor who ignores walls entirely.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas.
**Goal:** Survive the cell-night, move the blade intelligence through stone, and hold onto the partnership long enough for it to remain useful.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- Frostmourne is in the evidence vault above them and its cycle is accelerating.
- The verdict changed nothing about the blade's appetite.
- Sylvanas is alive on the other side of the wall and still capable of carrying intelligence forward if a surface appears.
- Lor'themar is in the city, but the door between knowledge and action is still shut.
- Jaina already knows the anti-blade counter; the practical problem is delivery, not theory.

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- How fast the blade will actually choose a next mind if nothing interrupts it.
- Whether any ally outside the cells can move fast enough to matter.
- What exactly comes through the dark in Scene 2.
- How many earlier hands or worlds Frostmourne has already consumed.

**What NOT to do:**
- No clean taxonomy for the visitor inside immediate POV; the women understand scale before labels.
- No prison break or release route yet.
- No assumption that knowing the larger board makes the smaller problem any easier.
- No claim that Jaina knows the full five-face pattern before Sylvanas's memory cascade earns it.

**What they learn by the end**
- Jaina finally names the truth of her arc: being right alone was never enough.
- The larger board exists and the enemy behind Arthas is older, broader, and more amused than the women knew.
- Frostmourne will seek another hand because that is what it was built to do.
- The being visiting the cell has worn five earlier faces across the investigation.
- Sylvanas now has a linguistic tell tied to that being's disguises.
- Book 2 begins with the pair still imprisoned, the blade seeking, and the new enemy still in the city.

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- She has the answer to Frostmourne and nowhere to put it but through a wall.
- Track the blade's cycle, get the important parts through to Sylvanas, and refuse to let the cell reduce the truth to private despair.
- The accelerating heartbeat overhead, the guilt of victory that did not save the dead, the closed door, and the realization that understanding without delivery is just another dead end.
- The last shreds of Jaina's old Lie still whisper that if she can think clearly enough, the rest should follow.
- The chapter asks whether she can say out loud that the partnership, not solitary intelligence, was the only mechanism that ever worked.
- Spend the night sinking inward, or turn even the wall into one more working channel.
- Jaina moves from trapped intelligence to named truth - right together, or not enough.
- Book 2 opens with Jaina still imprisoned but no longer misunderstanding what actually solved Book 1.

### Sylvanas (Scene 2)
- **Current stance at entry:** She is still assessing even in a stone box, armed only with memory, pattern, and the voice through the wall.
- Survive the visitation, hold on to whatever intelligence it spills, and turn impossible recognition into the next hunt's first tool.
- No weapon, no door that matters, Jaina's fear through stone, the blade above, and the sense that the scale of the problem is about to widen without giving her any new freedoms.
- Sylvanas still wants any new threat to be something a better read can contain tactically.
- The chapter asks whether she can absorb proof that the board is older and larger without surrendering the need to hunt anyway.

### Partnership / team
- Reduced to voices, breath, and counting through masonry.
- The partnership survives full institutional collapse and becomes the thematic answer of the book rather than only its best method.
- Let the wall-talk, the visitor's scale, and the final memory cascade carry the chapter. Do not write explanatory speeches where the stone and the silence can do the work.

## Scene 1: Through the Wall

**What happens:** Let Jaina name both the blade's urgency and her own hard-won truth before the larger enemy arrives.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas through the wall
**Where we start:** Verdict delivered, cells closed, blade active.

### Beat 1
- Jaina can hear exactly what the blade is doing and still cannot put a hand on the door that would let her stop it.
- She keeps counting instead of letting the pulse become one more form of helpless noise. The cycle is shortening from pulse to pulse. The blade is not broadcasting blindly; it is narrowing toward individual minds. Jaina can now distinguish the difference between ambient hunger and targeted interest. They do not know which mind the blade lands on first or whether the palace wards fail before a person does.
- Wrong heartbeat through stone above her; Guard-mind brushing the blade's radius and catching on it; Bread-taste and pulse becoming the same rhythm in her body.
### Beat 2
- Speak the truth of what stopped Arthas.
- The truth humiliates Jaina's old self-concept because it says seeing clearly was never enough by itself. Jaina says it aloud and Sylvanas hears it, but the truth arrives in a cell where the pair can barely act on it beyond continuing to think together. Jaina can now name border work, grain work, Scholomance, Northrend, and the throne room as partnership achievements rather than solitary insights. Kel'Thuzad becomes the warning version of solitary brilliance rather than merely the enemy teacher. Sylvanas answers from the wall not by soothing Jaina but by sharpening the statement into something usable. They do not know how this truth changes the next investigation once the door actually opens or whether the world outside the cells ever learns the same lesson.
- Consonants blunted by two feet of stone; Long pauses that still feel full because the other voice is listening; One sentence crossing the wall harder than the verdict did.
### Beat 3
- Jaina knows how to hurt the blade and still cannot touch it.
- She starts packaging the intelligence for Sylvanas and for anyone outside who might yet become a delivery surface. Jaina can already describe the next likely vulnerable targets in practical terms. The counter remains valid even outside the throne room, but only if delivered in time. The partnership's wall-language is now sufficient to carry technical threat reporting. They do not know whether outside allies can receive and act on the intelligence before the blade chooses or whether the next wielder is in the palace already or only being shaped toward it.
- Fingertip tapping marking cycle length between spoken lines; Stone making every sentence cost extra effort; the blade spiking harder every time Jaina says something true about it.

## Scene 2: The Visitor

**What happens:** Reframe the scale of the story, confirm that the blade is still in play, and resolve the five-face thread into a sequel-hunt.
**Characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina through the wall, the visitor
**Where we start:** The partnership still works through stone and the blade is seeking above them.

### Beat 4
- She has no weapon and no useful classification, only the choice to face what is coming or cower from it.
- She stands and faces it because anything else would make no tactical sense even if tactics are now failing. The blade's cold is not the only cold in the prison. Jaina's fear through the wall is qualitatively different from everything before it. The darkness itself changes before the visitor fully appears. They do not know what the visitor is or whether the blade and the visitor are allied, parallel, or nested.
- Torch flame shrinking inward; Air gaining weight before shape; Jaina's sharp inhale through the wall.
### Beat 5
- Understand what the visitor thinks the women actually accomplished.
- The visitor does not deny their victory; it simply places that victory on a board so large it almost erases the satisfaction of it. The pair learn the board is vastly larger and that Arthas was only one piece, and the visitor casually confirms the blade is designed to seek again. Arthas's death is a setback to the larger enemy, not a decisive blow. The plague, Cult, and Scourge apparatus already accomplished more than the pair understood while they were stopping the prince. "The blade will find another hand" is spoken with maker-level familiarity rather than rumor-level fear. They do not know who the next hand might be.
- Exactly where this being sits relative to the Lich King and the plague apparatus; Voice too calm for what it is saying; Scale conveyed not by explanation but by boredom; Jaina's steadying breath through the wall as she hears the blade line land.
### Beat 6
- Survive the visitor's departure and catch the one impossible phrase that turns memory into pattern.
- The visitor ignores stone, warding, and prison architecture as if those things only ever applied to smaller beings. The visitor leaves untouched and unopposed, and its final farewell in ancient Kaldorei provides the missing key Sylvanas needed to place every earlier encounter. The visitor never treated the cell as confinement in the first place. The original farewell is older than any living polity in the current world. The repeated Common phrase from earlier chapters was always an echo of something much older. They do not know whether the visitor intended the clue as mockery, sloppiness, or deliberate bait or how much of the investigation it steered from behind those other faces.
- Body passing through solid stone without noise or strain; One impossible phrase hanging in the cell after the shape is gone; Torch relighting only after the room belongs to ordinary dark again.
### Beat 7
- Sylvanas cannot act from this cell, but she also cannot unknow that the same being has been beside the investigation from almost the beginning.
- Hold the pattern, the phrase, and the linguistic tell as the first usable counterintelligence tool of the sequel. The grain trader, the Andorhal contact, the retreating figure, the military aide, and the Stratholme-road merchant all collapse into one operative line. The repeated star-road farewell is no longer a curiosity; it is a tell. The new investigation lane is not just the blade but the disguised manipulator moving through the kingdom behind it. They do not know how many other faces the same being wore that the pair never caught or whether the tell remains usable now that the being knows it has been noticed.
- Five remembered farewells striking in sequence behind the ancient original; One dead language making five old scenes re-light at once; Frostmourne pulsing above them while the pattern locks into place below.
