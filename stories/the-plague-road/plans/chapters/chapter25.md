# Chapter 25: The Stinger

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore in Scene 1, Sylvanas Windrunner in Scene 2 (both limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Opens the night after Chapter 24's verdict, with S&J still in adjacent dungeon cells beneath a palace holding an unheld Frostmourne still tethered north.
**Open location:** Capital City palace dungeons - adjacent cells divided by two feet of stone.
**Transport:** Static imprisonment only. Motion belongs to the blade's pulse, voices through the wall, and the visitor who ignores walls entirely.
**Active cast at open:** Jaina, Sylvanas.
**Immediate objective:** Survive the cell-night, move the blade intelligence through stone, and hold onto the partnership long enough for it to remain useful.

## Knowledge ledger

**What Jaina knows at open**
- Frostmourne is in the evidence vault above them and its cycle is accelerating.
- The verdict changed nothing about the blade's appetite.
- Sylvanas is alive on the other side of the wall and still capable of carrying intelligence forward if a surface appears.
- Lor'themar is in the city, but the door between knowledge and action is still shut.
- Jaina already knows the anti-blade counter; the practical problem is delivery, not theory.

**What Jaina does NOT know at open**
- How fast the blade will actually choose a next mind if nothing interrupts it.
- Whether any ally outside the cells can move fast enough to matter.
- What exactly comes through the dark in Scene 2.
- How many earlier hands or worlds Frostmourne has already consumed.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No clean taxonomy for the visitor inside immediate POV; the women understand scale before labels.
- No prison break or release route yet.
- No assumption that knowing the larger board makes the smaller problem any easier.
- No claim that Jaina knows the full five-face pattern before Sylvanas's memory cascade earns it.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Jaina finally names the truth of her arc: being right alone was never enough.
- The larger board exists and the enemy behind Arthas is older, broader, and more amused than the women knew.
- Frostmourne will seek another hand because that is what it was built to do.
- The being visiting the cell has worn five earlier faces across the investigation.
- Sylvanas now has a linguistic tell tied to that being's disguises.
- Book 2 begins with the pair still imprisoned, the blade seeking, and the new enemy still in the city.

## Arc position

### Jaina
- **Current stance at open:** She has the answer to Frostmourne and nowhere to put it but through a wall.
- **Surface objective:** Track the blade's cycle, get the important parts through to Sylvanas, and refuse to let the cell reduce the truth to private despair.
- **Pressure source:** The accelerating heartbeat overhead, the guilt of victory that did not save the dead, the closed door, and the realization that understanding without delivery is just another dead end.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** The last shreds of Jaina's old Lie still whisper that if she can think clearly enough, the rest should follow.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can say out loud that the partnership, not solitary intelligence, was the only mechanism that ever worked.
- **Forced choice:** Spend the night sinking inward, or turn even the wall into one more working channel.
- **End-state shift:** Jaina moves from trapped intelligence to named truth - right together, or not enough.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Book 2 opens with Jaina still imprisoned but no longer misunderstanding what actually solved Book 1.

### Sylvanas (Scene 2)
- **Current stance at entry:** She is still assessing even in a stone box, armed only with memory, pattern, and the voice through the wall.
- **Surface objective:** Survive the visitation, hold on to whatever intelligence it spills, and turn impossible recognition into the next hunt's first tool.
- **Pressure source:** No weapon, no door that matters, Jaina's fear through stone, the blade above, and the sense that the scale of the problem is about to widen without giving her any new freedoms.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas still wants any new threat to be something a better read can contain tactically.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can absorb proof that the board is older and larger without surrendering the need to hunt anyway.
- **Forced choice:** Treat the visitor's gloat as intimidation only, or listen hard enough to steal something useful from it.
- **End-state shift:** Fragmented memory becomes pattern - five faces, one farewell, one operative enemy.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Book 2 opens with a concrete tell Sylvanas can listen for even while the cell still stands between her and the city.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Reduced to voices, breath, and counting through masonry.
- **Change this chapter:** The partnership survives full institutional collapse and becomes the thematic answer of the book rather than only its best method.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** Let the wall-talk, the visitor's scale, and the final memory cascade carry the chapter. Do not write explanatory speeches where the stone and the silence can do the work.

## Cast and handoff rules

- **The blade:** Present through pulse, cold, and predation. Never reduce it to background scenery.
- **Lor'themar and outside allies:** Off-page pressure only. Their presence matters because the door is still closed.
- **The visitor:** Immediate POV does not need the name first. The horror is scale, ease, and oldness.
- **Ancient language beat:** The phrase must land as impossible before it lands as pattern-key.

**Chapter handoff target:** Book 2 opens from this prison state - Frostmourne seeking, the Nathrezim thread active, and the partnership still the only mechanism that actually works.

## Scene 1: Through the Wall

**Scene function:** Let Jaina name both the blade's urgency and her own hard-won truth before the larger enemy arrives.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas through the wall
**Knowledge at scene start:** Verdict delivered, cells closed, blade active.
**Arc pressure:** Keep darkness, stone, pulse-counting, and voice through masonry concrete so the scene stays intimate and material.

### Beat 1 - Sequel: The Blade's Heartbeat
- **Emotion:** Knowledge trapped in stone.
- **Dilemma:** Jaina can hear exactly what the blade is doing and still cannot put a hand on the door that would let her stop it.
- **Decision:** She keeps counting instead of letting the pulse become one more form of helpless noise.
- **Value shift:** condemned -> the blade becomes the real night
- **New on-page information:**
  - The cycle is shortening from pulse to pulse
  - The blade is not broadcasting blindly; it is narrowing toward individual minds
  - Jaina can now distinguish the difference between ambient hunger and targeted interest
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Which mind the blade lands on first
  - Whether the palace wards fail before a person does
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Wrong heartbeat through stone above her
  - Guard-mind brushing the blade's radius and catching on it
  - Bread-taste and pulse becoming the same rhythm in her body
- **Transition intent:** Once the blade is understood as active threat, the chapter can force Jaina to say what Book 1 actually taught her.

### Beat 2 - Scene: Through the Wall
- **Goal:** Speak the truth of what stopped Arthas.
- **Conflict:** The truth humiliates Jaina's old self-concept because it says seeing clearly was never enough by itself.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Jaina says it aloud and Sylvanas hears it, but the truth arrives in a cell where the pair can barely act on it beyond continuing to think together.
- **Value shift:** the blade becomes the real night -> the book's truth is named
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina can now name border work, grain work, Scholomance, Northrend, and the throne room as partnership achievements rather than solitary insights
  - Kel'Thuzad becomes the warning version of solitary brilliance rather than merely the enemy teacher
  - Sylvanas answers from the wall not by soothing Jaina but by sharpening the statement into something usable
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How this truth changes the next investigation once the door actually opens
  - Whether the world outside the cells ever learns the same lesson
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Consonants blunted by two feet of stone
  - Long pauses that still feel full because the other voice is listening
  - One sentence crossing the wall harder than the verdict did
- **Transition intent:** Once the truth is spoken, Jaina can turn immediately back to the practical problem - getting the anti-blade counter out anyway.

### Beat 3 - Sequel: The Counter She Cannot Deploy
- **Emotion:** Frustration tightened into purpose.
- **Dilemma:** Jaina knows how to hurt the blade and still cannot touch it.
- **Decision:** She starts packaging the intelligence for Sylvanas and for anyone outside who might yet become a delivery surface.
- **Value shift:** the book's truth is named -> partnership becomes working channel again
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina can already describe the next likely vulnerable targets in practical terms
  - The counter remains valid even outside the throne room, but only if delivered in time
  - The partnership's wall-language is now sufficient to carry technical threat reporting
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether outside allies can receive and act on the intelligence before the blade chooses
  - Whether the next wielder is in the palace already or only being shaped toward it
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Fingertip tapping marking cycle length between spoken lines
  - Stone making every sentence cost extra effort
  - The blade spiking harder every time Jaina says something true about it
- **Transition intent:** Once the counter is moving through the wall, the chapter can widen the board by sending something older through the dark.

## Scene 2: The Visitor

**Scene function:** Reframe the scale of the story, confirm that the blade is still in play, and resolve the five-face thread into a sequel-hunt.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina through the wall, the visitor
**Knowledge at scene start:** The partnership still works through stone and the blade is seeking above them.
**Arc pressure:** Keep torch behavior, cold shift, stone-wall violation, and the ancient phrase concrete so the scene lands through sensory impossibility.

### Beat 4 - Sequel: Something in the Dark
- **Emotion:** Vigilance hitting the edge of a category Sylvanas does not yet possess.
- **Dilemma:** She has no weapon and no useful classification, only the choice to face what is coming or cower from it.
- **Decision:** She stands and faces it because anything else would make no tactical sense even if tactics are now failing.
- **Value shift:** partnership as working channel -> the room stops belonging to known threats
- **New on-page information:**
  - The blade's cold is not the only cold in the prison
  - Jaina's fear through the wall is qualitatively different from everything before it
  - The darkness itself changes before the visitor fully appears
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - What the visitor is
  - Whether the blade and the visitor are allied, parallel, or nested
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Torch flame shrinking inward
  - Air gaining weight before shape
  - Jaina's sharp inhale through the wall
- **Transition intent:** Once the room belongs to the unknown, the chapter can let the visitor speak and show what scale it operates on.

### Beat 5 - Scene: The Gloat
- **Goal:** Understand what the visitor thinks the women actually accomplished.
- **Conflict:** The visitor does not deny their victory; it simply places that victory on a board so large it almost erases the satisfaction of it.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the pair learn the board is vastly larger and that Arthas was only one piece, and the visitor casually confirms the blade is designed to seek again.
- **Value shift:** unknown threat -> scale redefined
- **New on-page information:**
  - Arthas's death is a setback to the larger enemy, not a decisive blow
  - The plague, Cult, and Scourge apparatus already accomplished more than the pair understood while they were stopping the prince
  - "The blade will find another hand" is spoken with maker-level familiarity rather than rumor-level fear
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Exactly where this being sits relative to the Lich King and the plague apparatus
  - Who the next hand might be
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Voice too calm for what it is saying
  - Scale conveyed not by explanation but by boredom
  - Jaina's steadying breath through the wall as she hears the blade line land
- **Transition intent:** Once the blade's future is confirmed, the chapter can make the visitor's departure more frightening than its arrival.

### Beat 6 - Scene: Kalar'shorel'anor
- **Goal:** Survive the visitor's departure and catch the one impossible phrase that turns memory into pattern.
- **Conflict:** The visitor ignores stone, warding, and prison architecture as if those things only ever applied to smaller beings.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the visitor leaves untouched and unopposed, and its final farewell in ancient Kaldorei provides the missing key Sylvanas needed to place every earlier encounter.
- **Value shift:** scale redefined -> impossible recognition
- **New on-page information:**
  - The visitor never treated the cell as confinement in the first place
  - The original farewell is older than any living polity in the current world
  - The repeated Common phrase from earlier chapters was always an echo of something much older
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the visitor intended the clue as mockery, sloppiness, or deliberate bait
  - How much of the investigation it steered from behind those other faces
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Body passing through solid stone without noise or strain
  - One impossible phrase hanging in the cell after the shape is gone
  - Torch relighting only after the room belongs to ordinary dark again
- **Transition intent:** Once the original phrase lands, the chapter can let Sylvanas's memory do the rest.

### Beat 7 - Sequel: Five Faces
- **Emotion:** Pattern-recognition shock - not confusion, but instant alignment.
- **Dilemma:** Sylvanas cannot act from this cell, but she also cannot unknow that the same being has been beside the investigation from almost the beginning.
- **Decision:** Hold the pattern, the phrase, and the linguistic tell as the first usable counterintelligence tool of the sequel.
- **Value shift:** impossible recognition -> huntable pattern
- **New on-page information:**
  - The grain trader, the Andorhal contact, the retreating figure, the military aide, and the Stratholme-road merchant all collapse into one operative line
  - The repeated star-road farewell is no longer a curiosity; it is a tell
  - The new investigation lane is not just the blade but the disguised manipulator moving through the kingdom behind it
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How many other faces the same being wore that the pair never caught
  - Whether the tell remains usable now that the being knows it has been noticed
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Five remembered farewells striking in sequence behind the ancient original
  - One dead language making five old scenes re-light at once
  - Frostmourne pulsing above them while the pattern locks into place below
- **Transition intent:** Book 2 opens from this locked state - prison, blade, demon, tell, and a partnership still doing the work through stone.
