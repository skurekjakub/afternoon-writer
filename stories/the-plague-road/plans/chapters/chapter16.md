# Chapter 16: Before the Gates

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 15. Opens on the hard ride ahead of Arthas's main column and closes at Stratholme's gates when Jaina has to admit the source plan cannot save the city cleanly anymore.
**Open location:** North road into Stratholme's outer farming belt.
**Transport:** Hard horseback approach, then rapid on-foot work through storehouses and market routes, then return to the gates as the main army comes up.
**Active cast at open:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Immediate objective:** Reach Stratholme before the army, hit the plague's feeding points, and prove there is still a way to stop the city from turning.

## Knowledge ledger

**What Jaina knows at open**
- Hearthglen proved the outbreak can tip from bad grain and sick households into a city actively feeding itself to the dead.
- Kel'Thuzad's explanation gave her something usable, not only explanatory: the plague can be broken at concentrated feeding points if she reaches them before too much of the city has eaten.
- Arthas is marching behind them and will not wait for proof longer than the road makes him wait.
- Sylvanas chose to ride with the source plan instead of staying with the prince's main force.
- The crack from Scholomance is still there even though the team is working cleanly.

**What Jaina does NOT know at open**
- Whether Stratholme is still early enough for the source plan to matter.
- How many grain nodes in the city are already hot.
- Whether any part of Arthas still wants an answer other than steel.
- Whether she can tell Sylvanas "too late" out loud if that is what the city says back.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No on-page Culling yet.
- No clean forgiveness between Jaina and Sylvanas.
- No claim that the source plan was doomed from the start.
- No interior access to Sylvanas or Arthas.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Jaina's source plan was real and works at the node level: at least one granary can be spoiled and its pull broken.
- The city is already too far gone for that narrow success to scale. Too many stores are hot, too much bread is already in houses, and too much plague is now sitting inside living bodies.
- Cult handlers are still present in the city and can harden or direct the dead when the choir alone would only clump and feed.
- Sylvanas has direct field proof that Jaina used Kel'Thuzad's teaching to try to save people immediately.
- Arthas reaches the gates and forces Jaina to answer the only question that matters: can Stratholme still be saved cleanly?
- Jaina's answer is no.

## Arc position

### Jaina
- **Current stance at open:** If she can get to the right places fast enough, being right may finally save somebody instead of only explaining why they died.
- **Surface objective:** Break the plague's feeding points before the city finishes passing them through itself.
- **Pressure source:** The clock behind her, the city ahead, Arthas's speed, and Sylvanas riding close enough to make every choice visible.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Jaina still wants correct theory plus speed to be enough.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can take a true field shot, watch it fail at scale, and still speak the truth without softening it.
- **Forced choice:** Keep reaching for one more source node, or stop the attempt in time to tell Arthas the city cannot be saved the way she hoped.
- **End-state shift:** Jaina stops asking whether the source plan is elegant enough and starts counting how many mouths already outran it.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 17 opens with Sylvanas holding the gate line after Jaina's answer while Arthas decides what "too late" means.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- **Visible function:** Rider, cover, witness, and the partner who makes this attempt field-real instead of theoretical.
- **POV pressure:** Jaina can feel that Sylvanas is watching not just whether the plan works, but what Jaina does with knowledge that once looked dangerous in her hands.
- **Interaction rule:** Do not enter Sylvanas's interior. Use proximity, timing, questions, and where Sylvanas puts her body when Jaina has to work.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Fast, efficient, and not healed.
- **Change this chapter:** The repair comes through labor. Jaina names targets, Sylvanas makes room for the work, and neither wastes breath on what cannot be solved on horseback.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** The chapter must never call this proof or trust in the abstract. Let the proof live in the attempt itself.

## Cast and handoff rules

- **Sylvanas:** Tactical cover, hard questions, fast read on streets and lines of retreat.
- **Cyndia:** Relay, horse control, flank watch, and second set of eyes on crowd shift and route closure.
- **Arthas:** Arrives late in the chapter, already moving. Keep him exterior and immediate.

**Chapter handoff target:** Chapter 17 opens at the gates after Jaina has answered Arthas honestly and before the city has been formally reduced to his next decision.

## Scene 1: The Ride

**Scene function:** Turn the source plan into a physical race and make clear, through the way Jaina works, that this knowledge is being used to save lives rather than admired for its own sake.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Knowledge at scene start:** The plan is narrow, the army is behind them, and the city clock is running.
**Arc pressure:** If Jaina talks like a scholar here, she loses the chapter. The plan has to live in roads, districts, ovens, and arrival order.

### Beat 1 - Scene: Fast Horses, Short Window
- **Goal:** Reach Stratholme before Arthas's main force closes the distance.
- **Conflict:** The road will not give them all the speed they want. Wagons have torn the ruts deep, the horses are already hot from the last stretch, and every slow bend in the road feels like bread cooling in another oven they have not reached yet.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they keep the head start, but the road itself keeps reminding Jaina how small that lead is.
- **Value shift:** possible rescue -> rescue under a shrinking clock
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina's mind is no longer on the whole plague chain; it is on delivery order and how fast bread leaves heat
  - Sylvanas and Cyndia are treating the plan as a field task, not humoring it
  - The main army's dust is still visible far enough back to matter
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the lead is enough
  - Which district of Stratholme took the first seeded grain
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Horses blowing hard and white at the mouth
  - Cart ruts forcing them to pick the firmer shoulder of the road
  - Dust from Arthas's column hanging low behind them
- **Transition intent:** Once the ride is established, the plan needs to be spoken in names a soldier can use, not only in mage terms.

### Beat 2 - Scene: The Plan in the Saddle
- **Goal:** Give Sylvanas the exact order of targets and what Jaina needs at each one.
- **Conflict:** Jaina has to talk while riding and strip the plan down to what matters: main grain stores first, then bakehouses already working from them, then any district carts not yet unloaded. She can foul a store, break its resonance, and burn what must be burned. She cannot unfeed people who already ate.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the team locks into a shared working picture, but the plan's weakness is named in the same breath as its strength.
- **Value shift:** rescue under a shrinking clock -> practical plan with a visible limit
- **New on-page information:**
  - The source plan depends on concentration and order, not a city-wide wave of magic
  - Jaina is openly using Kel'Thuzad's method against the plague, which Sylvanas hears in terms she can test
  - Cyndia takes the route and relay role without argument, showing the trio is working as a unit
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the first store they hit will still be intact enough to matter
  - Whether the city has already spread too far beyond stores and ovens
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina shouting district names into the wind
  - Sylvanas riding close enough to hear without making Jaina repeat herself
  - Cyndia cutting ahead at a fork to keep a side lane clear
- **Transition intent:** The next move has to prove the plan can do something real before the chapter asks it to fail.

## Scene 2: The First Stores

**Scene function:** Give the source plan one real success, then show why that success cannot scale fast enough to save Stratholme.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, local guards, teamsters, bakers, civilians, one Cult handler
**Knowledge at scene start:** The plan is physically workable if the target is concentrated and early enough.
**Arc pressure:** One small win is necessary. One small win is also not enough.

### Beat 3 - Scene: East Granary
- **Goal:** Reach the nearest large grain store before the city moves too much more of it.
- **Conflict:** The granary is still standing and still guarded, which gives Jaina one clean shot, but nobody at the door understands why a mage is screaming about spoiled grain while the city is still trying to open for trade.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they force entry and secure the site, but the delay costs them the calm they needed.
- **Value shift:** practical plan with a visible limit -> first target still reachable
- **New on-page information:**
  - Stratholme is not yet openly dead at the edges; parts of the city are still functioning on habit
  - Local guards are confused rather than informed
  - The outbreak is early enough here that a concentrated source might still be hit before it fully spreads
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How many other stores in the city are already moving the same grain
  - Whether this granary is primary or only one branch
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Sack dust hanging in the air under high beams
  - Teamsters arguing over deliveries while Sylvanas forces the door
  - The first sickly sweet necromantic note rising stronger at the grain itself
- **Transition intent:** The plan has to work here, visibly, or nothing later carries weight.

### Beat 4 - Scene: One Real Win
- **Goal:** Break the plague's hold on the granary before more of it leaves the building.
- **Conflict:** Jaina has only minutes and no proper circle room, only sacks, beams, flour dust, and a floor rough with spilled grain. Sylvanas and Cyndia hold the space while she forces the store to answer wrong: spoil, clot, collapse, go dead.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the node breaks. The grain slumps uselessly, the pull in the room dies, and the first real success lands. But carts have already left.
- **Value shift:** first target still reachable -> proof the plan works in one place
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina can foul a concentrated plague source in the field
  - Sylvanas directly enables that work instead of doubting or slowing it
  - The method is ugly, fast, and practical rather than elegant
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether they can repeat this enough times before the city outruns them
  - How far the carts that already left have gone
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Grain souring in a rush instead of rotting slowly
  - Flour turning wet and gray where the spell bites through it
  - Jaina's hands shaking once the pull in the room breaks
- **Transition intent:** The win has to immediately show its limit.

### Beat 5 - Scene: Bread Already in Houses
- **Goal:** Chase the next node before the first success turns into false hope.
- **Conflict:** The street outside kills the illusion quickly. Bakers are already carrying morning loaves. Carts have already unloaded. Women are already tearing bread for children in doorways. Jaina cannot unbake what has already been eaten.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the first win stands, but the city has already outrun it and turned that success into proof of how late they are.
- **Value shift:** proof the plan works in one place -> the city is already ahead of the plan
- **New on-page information:**
  - Distribution speed, not only contamination, is now the problem
  - The plague has already crossed from stores into kitchens and bodies
  - Jaina now has to count people, not only nodes
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether a central store remains that matters enough to justify another push
  - How many households are already carrying dormant activation
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Warm bread steam in a street Jaina no longer trusts
  - A mother breaking a loaf with flour still on her wrist
  - Sylvanas looking at ordinary market traffic like a kill zone delayed by hours
- **Transition intent:** The chapter must test whether there is still any point in striking more nodes after this.

### Beat 6 - Scene: The Second Storehouse
- **Goal:** Hit one more major store and find out whether the city can still be bent back at all.
- **Conflict:** The second site is worse: already half stripped, already carrying sickness among the workers, and already watched by a living handler using taught command signals to stiffen the nearest dead when panic starts. The choir is real. So is deliberate direction.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - they break the second store, but the city answers by showing both spread and command. This is not one bad warehouse problem anymore.
- **Value shift:** the city is already ahead of the plan -> the city is both seeded and steered
- **New on-page information:**
  - Cult handlers are still present and can directly shape undead compliance in the city
  - The plague has enough blind self-organization to keep pressure up even without a handler on every street
  - Jaina's method can destroy supply, but not erase what already moved into flesh
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any third strike is worth the time
  - What answer Arthas will demand if he reaches the gates before they finish counting the damage
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - A human voice whistling a command pattern while the nearest dead jerk toward it
  - Sick workers trying to stand straight long after they should be lying down
  - Sylvanas's arrow pinning the handler before Jaina even finishes turning toward the sound
- **Transition intent:** The chapter now owes the cleanest and ugliest thing Jaina can say: what this means at city scale.

## Scene 3: The Count

**Scene function:** Force Jaina to stop chasing one more fix and say the city-scale truth before Arthas turns uncertainty into policy.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, main-column soldiers
**Knowledge at scene start:** The method works locally, but the city has already distributed too much plague into households and bodies.
**Arc pressure:** If Jaina cannot say "too late," Arthas will fill the silence with his own answer anyway.

### Beat 7 - Scene: Counting Mouths
- **Goal:** Decide whether the team should keep striking stores or break off for the gates.
- **Conflict:** Jaina can still think of more places to hit. That is the problem. Each one might help one district, one morning, one street. None of them pulls the plague back out of stomachs, ovens, and blood. The plan is no longer failing because it is wrong. It is failing because it arrived after the city had already eaten.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Jaina abandons the fantasy of one more useful stop, but doing so means she has to carry a worse answer back to the gates.
- **Value shift:** the city is both seeded and steered -> the truth is now human, not technical
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina's break point is not magical exhaustion but the count itself
  - She understands the exact way in which the plan lost: not bad theory, bad timing
  - The line between source contamination and population contamination is now irretrievably crossed
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Arthas will hear the difference between "cannot be saved cleanly" and "nothing can be done"
  - Whether Uther will stand with Jaina's answer or against Arthas's response
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina staring at a row of open shutters and thinking about breakfasts instead of spellwork
  - The sour necromantic drag now mixed with kitchen smoke and morning air
  - Hoofbeats from the main column finally close enough to stop being imagined
- **Transition intent:** The army reaches the gates, and Jaina no longer has the right to stay inside the problem instead of naming it.

### Beat 8 - Scene: The Gate Answer
- **Goal:** Give Arthas the true answer before he makes the city answer for her silence.
- **Conflict:** Arthas arrives already expecting the world to be narrowed into a usable yes or no. Jaina would like one more hour and knows an hour would change nothing. Uther is present. Sylvanas is present. The city is behind them. Arthas asks if Stratholme can still be saved.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Jaina answers honestly: not cleanly, not now, not before the plague finishes what it has already started inside the living.
- **Value shift:** the truth is now human, not technical -> the city has no clean rescue left
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina will not lie to buy herself comfort or buy Arthas false hope
  - Arthas now has the one answer he was never going to hear as anything but a hard edge
  - Uther hears the same words and understands exactly what they are about to cost
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - The exact form Arthas's next order will take
  - Where Sylvanas and Jaina will stand once that order is spoken
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Arthas's horse still lathered from the ride up
  - Gate guards flattening themselves against stone to make room for the command group
  - Jaina tasting flour dust and ash when she says no
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 17 opens on Sylvanas holding this answer at the gate while Arthas turns it into action.
