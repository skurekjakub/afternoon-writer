# Chapter 16: Before the Gates

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**When:** Directly follows Chapter 15. Opens on the hard ride ahead of Arthas's main column and closes at Stratholme's gates when Jaina has to admit the source plan cannot save the city cleanly anymore.
**Where:** North road into Stratholme's outer farming belt.
**Travel:** Hard horseback approach, then rapid on-foot work through storehouses and market routes, then return to the gates as the main army comes up.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia.
**Goal:** Reach Stratholme before the army, hit the plague's feeding points, and prove there is still a way to stop the city from turning.

## What we know

**What Jaina knows at open**
- Hearthglen proved the outbreak can tip from bad grain and sick households into a city actively feeding itself to the dead.
- Kel'Thuzad's explanation gave her something usable, not only explanatory: the plague can be broken at concentrated feeding points if she reaches them before too much of the city has eaten.
- Arthas is marching behind them and will not wait for proof longer than the road makes him wait.
- Sylvanas chose to ride with the source plan instead of staying with the prince's main force.
- The crack from Scholomance is still there even though the team is working cleanly.

**What Jaina doesn't know yet**
- Whether Stratholme is still early enough for the source plan to matter.
- How many grain nodes in the city are already hot.
- Whether any part of Arthas still wants an answer other than steel.
- Whether she can tell Sylvanas "too late" out loud if that is what the city says back.

**What NOT to do:**
- No on-page Culling yet.
- No clean forgiveness between Jaina and Sylvanas.
- No claim that the source plan was doomed from the start.
- No interior access to Sylvanas or Arthas.

**What they learn by the end**
- Jaina's source plan was real and works at the node level: at least one granary can be spoiled and its pull broken.
- The city is already too far gone for that narrow success to scale. Too many stores are hot, too much bread is already in houses, and too much plague is now sitting inside living bodies.
- Cult handlers are still present in the city and can harden or direct the dead when the choir alone would only clump and feed.
- Sylvanas has direct field proof that Jaina used Kel'Thuzad's teaching to try to save people immediately.
- Arthas reaches the gates and forces Jaina to answer the only question that matters: can Stratholme still be saved cleanly?
- Jaina's answer is no.

## Character Arcs

### Jaina
- If she can get to the right places fast enough, being right may finally save somebody instead of only explaining why they died.
- Break the plague's feeding points before the city finishes passing them through itself.
- The clock behind her, the city ahead, Arthas's speed, and Sylvanas riding close enough to make every choice visible.
- Jaina still wants correct theory plus speed to be enough.
- The chapter asks whether she can take a true field shot, watch it fail at scale, and still speak the truth without softening it.
- Keep reaching for one more source node, or stop the attempt in time to tell Arthas the city cannot be saved the way she hoped.
- Jaina stops asking whether the source plan is elegant enough and starts counting how many mouths already outran it.
- Chapter 17 opens with Sylvanas holding the gate line after Jaina's answer while Arthas decides what "too late" means.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- Rider, cover, witness, and the partner who makes this attempt field-real instead of theoretical.
- **POV pressure:** Jaina can feel that Sylvanas is watching not just whether the plan works, but what Jaina does with knowledge that once looked dangerous in her hands.
- Do not enter Sylvanas's interior. Use proximity, timing, questions, and where Sylvanas puts her body when Jaina has to work.

### Partnership / team
- Fast, efficient, and not healed.
- The repair comes through labor. Jaina names targets, Sylvanas makes room for the work, and neither wastes breath on what cannot be solved on horseback.
- The chapter must never call this proof or trust in the abstract. Let the proof live in the attempt itself.

## Scene 1: The Ride

**What happens:** Turn the source plan into a physical race and make clear, through the way Jaina works, that this knowledge is being used to save lives rather than admired for its own sake.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia
**Where we start:** The plan is narrow, the army is behind them, and the city clock is running.

### Beat 1
- They keep the head start, but the road itself keeps reminding Jaina how small that lead is.
- Jaina's mind is no longer on the whole plague chain; it is on delivery order and how fast bread leaves heat. Sylvanas and Cyndia are treating the plan as a field task, not humoring it, and the main army's dust is still visible far enough back to matter. They do not know whether the lead is enough or which district of Stratholme took the first seeded grain.
- Horses blowing hard and white at the mouth; cart ruts forcing them to pick the firmer shoulder of the road; dust from Arthas's column hanging low behind them.
### Beat 2
- The team locks into a shared working picture, but the plan's weakness is named in the same breath as its strength.
- The source plan depends on concentration and order, not a city-wide wave of magic. Jaina is openly using Kel'Thuzad's method against the plague, which Sylvanas hears in terms she can test, and Cyndia takes the route and relay role without argument, showing the trio is working as a unit. They do not know whether the first store they hit will still be intact enough to matter or whether the city has already spread too far beyond stores and ovens.
- Jaina shouting district names into the wind; Sylvanas riding close enough to hear without making Jaina repeat herself; Cyndia cutting ahead at a fork to keep a side lane clear.

## Scene 2: The First Stores

**What happens:** Give the source plan one real success, then show why that success cannot scale fast enough to save Stratholme.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, local guards, teamsters, bakers, civilians, one Cult handler
**Where we start:** The plan is physically workable if the target is concentrated and early enough.

### Beat 3
- They force entry and secure the site, but the delay costs them the calm they needed.
- Stratholme is not yet openly dead at the edges; parts of the city are still functioning on habit, and local guards are confused rather than informed. The outbreak is early enough here that a concentrated source might still be hit before it fully spreads. They do not know how many other stores in the city are already moving the same grain or whether this granary is primary or only one branch.
- Sack dust hanging in the air under high beams; teamsters arguing over deliveries while Sylvanas forces the door; the first sickly sweet necromantic note rising stronger at the grain itself.
### Beat 4
- The node breaks. The grain slumps uselessly, the pull in the room dies, and the first real success lands. But carts have already left.
- Jaina can foul a concentrated plague source in the field, and Sylvanas directly enables that work instead of doubting or slowing it. The method is ugly, fast, and practical rather than elegant. They do not know whether they can repeat this enough times before the city outruns them or how far the carts that already left have gone.
- Grain souring in a rush instead of rotting slowly; flour turning wet and gray where the spell bites through it; Jaina's hands shaking once the pull in the room breaks.
### Beat 5
- The first win stands, but the city has already outrun it and turned that success into proof of how late they are.
- Distribution speed, not only contamination, is now the problem. The plague has already crossed from stores into kitchens and bodies, and Jaina now has to count people, not only nodes. They do not know whether a central store remains that matters enough to justify another push or how many households are already carrying dormant activation.
- Warm bread steam in a street Jaina no longer trusts; a mother breaking a loaf with flour still on her wrist; Sylvanas looking at ordinary market traffic like a kill zone delayed by hours.
### Beat 6
- They break the second store, but the city answers by showing both spread and command. This is not one bad warehouse problem anymore.
- Cult handlers are still present and can directly shape undead compliance in the city. The plague has enough blind self-organization to keep pressure up even without a handler on every street, and Jaina's method can destroy supply but not erase what already moved into flesh. They do not know whether any third strike is worth the time or what answer Arthas will demand if he reaches the gates before they finish counting the damage.
- A human voice whistling a command pattern while the nearest dead jerk toward it; sick workers trying to stand straight long after they should be lying down; Sylvanas's arrow pinning the handler before Jaina even finishes turning toward the sound.

## Scene 3: The Count

**What happens:** Force Jaina to stop chasing one more fix and say the city-scale truth before Arthas turns uncertainty into policy.
**Characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Arthas, Uther, Falric, main-column soldiers
**Where we start:** The method works locally, but the city has already distributed too much plague into households and bodies.

### Beat 7
- Jaina abandons the fantasy of one more useful stop, but doing so means she has to carry a worse answer back to the gates.
- Jaina's break point is not magical exhaustion but the count itself. She understands the exact way in which the plan lost: not bad theory, bad timing, and the line between source contamination and population contamination is now irretrievably crossed. They do not know whether Arthas will hear the difference between "cannot be saved cleanly" and "nothing can be done" or whether Uther will stand with Jaina's answer or against Arthas's response.
- Jaina staring at a row of open shutters and thinking about breakfasts instead of spellwork; the sour necromantic drag now mixed with kitchen smoke and morning air; hoofbeats from the main column finally close enough to stop being imagined.
### Beat 8
- Jaina answers honestly: not cleanly, not now, not before the plague finishes what it has already started inside the living.
- Jaina will not lie to buy herself comfort or buy Arthas false hope. Arthas now has the one answer he was never going to hear as anything but a hard edge, and Uther hears the same words and understands exactly what they are about to cost. They do not know the exact form Arthas's next order will take or where Sylvanas and Jaina will stand once that order is spoken.
- Arthas's horse still lathered from the ride up; gate guards flattening themselves against stone to make room for the command group; Jaina tasting flour dust and ash when she says no.
