# Subplot: Jaina and Arthas

- Use this as backstory pressure, not as a live romance.
- Core wound: Jaina left because she saw trajectory. Arthas heard only rejection.

## Before the story
- They meet in Dalaran. Arthas loves her intensity. Jaina loves his certainty.
- For a while they sharpen each other: she slows him down, he gets her moving.
- The mismatch is not affection. It is judgment speed. He values clean decisions more than diagnostic pause.
- Diagnostic incident: a border-land dispute where Arthas makes the legally correct call fast and ruins real lives in practice. Jaina sees the future in miniature.
- She ends it cleanly because the trajectory is wrong, not because the present moment is bad.

## What Arthas carries
- Courtesy gone rigid. He uses formality where warmth used to be.
- He distrusts analysis because analysis is what, in his mind, made Jaina leave.
- By Stratholme he treats hesitation as betrayal, not caution.

## What Jaina carries
- She was right about the shape of him and still could not change the outcome.
- Every later scene with Arthas feels like the old diagnostic incident at larger scale.
- The guilt is not leaving. It is failing to make him hear what she saw.

## What Sylvanas reads
- She knows there is history.
- After the Scholomance crack, that history becomes another reason to watch Jaina carefully around dangerous men.
- Sylvanas reads the surface accurately and the interior badly.
