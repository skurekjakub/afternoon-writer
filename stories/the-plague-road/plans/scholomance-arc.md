# The Scholomance Arc — Chapters 9–14

- Mid-book pivot: split the pair, let each flaw run without the other's counterweight, then bring them back together damaged.
- Partnership track: tests of faith -> crack -> damaged reformation.
- Guardrail: Ch 10 = entry and choice, Ch 11 = external interrupt, Ch 12 = duel, Ch 13 = damage event, Ch 14 = road out.

## Ch 9 — The Trap
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Jaina burns time on a ritual trace. The Cult ambush hits before she is done.
- Sylvanas answers by forcing a route through a baited gap and gets Lyriel killed by a necromantic trap.
- New habits are born here: Sylvanas waits for sweeps; Jaina asks how long.
- Split routes emerge: Jaina takes Barov and Scholomance. Sylvanas takes Arthas after the breadcrumb and repeated farewell.

## Ch 10 — The Estate
- POV: Jaina.
- Jaina uses Kirin Tor authority to get into Barov lands and through Scholomance's front door.
- The place reads like Dalaran method turned rotten: libraries, wards, lecture rooms, research discipline, necromancy in the bones.
- She maps what they are hiding and chooses to go deeper alone instead of reporting out.

## Ch 11 — The Fuse
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Operating without Jaina feels tactically wrong from the first step.
- Sylvanas briefs Arthas fully and lights the rest of his arc by aiming state power at the plague.
- He believes her too fast.
- Once Sylvanas learns Jaina went inside Caer Darrow, she pivots toward Scholomance.

## Ch 12 — The Necromancer
- POV: Jaina.
- Kel'Thuzad does not threaten first; he teaches.
- The duel is intellectual before it is magical.
- Jaina follows the logic far enough to see why it could tempt someone like her. That is the real danger beat.
- He gives her the final theory: Lich King, Legion, plague architecture, fidelity of preservation.

## Ch 13 — The Arrival
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Sylvanas infiltrates the fortress alone and reads it like hostile terrain.
- She finds Jaina apparently engaged with Kel'Thuzad and arrives after the internal fight has already happened.
- She reads the surface exactly and the truth incompletely. That is the crack.
- They still kill Kel'Thuzad together in seconds, which makes the damage worse, not better.

## Ch 14 — The Revelation
- POV: Jaina.
- Jaina briefs the new intelligence clinically: Lich King, Legion, scale, grain already out.
- No one says what the room cost.
- Sylvanas's trust now carries a fractional delay. Jaina notices.
- Road pressure returns immediately: Hearthglen is falling and Arthas is moving toward Stratholme.

## Key notes
- Mal'Ganis is steering both routes.
- Jaina alone with Kel'Thuzad is the three-way mirror at full strength.
- Sylvanas's Arthas briefing is correct and catastrophic.
- The arc must feel sharp, not smeared: entry, duel, damage, exit.
