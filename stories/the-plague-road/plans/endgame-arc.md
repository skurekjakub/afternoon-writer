# The Endgame Arc — Chapters 19–25

- Tone: stripped thriller. No playful register left.
- Partnership track: competence recognition -> peak competence -> survives condemnation.

## Ch 19 — The Decision
- Arthas sails north. Sylvanas refuses recall. Jaina refuses Antonidas.
- Vereesa shows what the political war has done to Lor'themar.
- Both women choose the mission over institution.

## Ch 20 — The Voyage
- The ship forces the quiet they have been dodging.
- Talk duty, obedience, and what happens when the protector becomes the threat.
- Jaina drives the ship with wind magic. Sylvanas stops her from burning herself out.

## Ch 21 — Frostmourne
- Northrend: burned fleet, Muradin, cave too late.
- The fight proves their sync even in defeat.
- They retreat clean with Muradin because they read the same instant to disengage.

## Ch 22 — The Return
- Dual POV.
- Jaina finally gets a workable read on how to hit the blade.
- Every warning route fails. Arthas is already home and already being celebrated.
- They plan the room: Jaina needs a few seconds; Sylvanas will buy them.

## Ch 23 — The Throne Room
- Peak sync through the service passages.
- They arrive as Arthas kills Terenas.
- First arrow fails because the blade has carried him past ordinary death.
- Jaina improvises the three-school seam strike. Sylvanas lands the second arrow and kills him.
- Frostmourne remains active. Guards enter to the worst possible picture.

## Ch 24 — The Condemned
- Evidence looks perfect for guilt and useless for truth.
- Antonidas, Uther, and Lor'themar all hit their own versions of the same wall.
- The court refuses the scale of the truth and picks the cleaner lie.
- Verdict: condemned.

## Ch 25 — The Stinger
- In cells, the partnership reduces to two voices and holds.
- Jaina finally names the real lesson: right alone is nothing.
- Mal'Ganis appears, confirms the larger game is intact, and ties himself to the repeated farewell.
- Exit state: Arthas dead, blade live, real enemy still moving.
