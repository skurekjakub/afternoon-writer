# The Arthas Arc — Chapters 15–18

**Tone:** Humor recedes permanently by Ch 16. Stratholme (Ch 17-18) kills the Dramione register entirely.
**Partnership phase transition:** SAVING EACH OTHER (damaged but functional) → bonded through shared witness to atrocity

*NOTE: This arc follows the Scholomance Arc (Ch 9-14). Arthas now has intelligence from Sylvanas (given in Ch 11), not from Kel'Thuzad's death alone. He's been investigating since she told him. The partnership carries the crack from Ch 13 — Sylvanas saw Jaina listening to Kel'Thuzad. Stratholme welds that crack shut through shared witness. Additionally, Sylvanas watching Stratholme knows SHE sent Arthas here — the fuse she lit in Ch 11 is now detonating.*

---

## Chapter 15: The Prince
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** The aftermath of Scholomance → Hearthglen → Arthas's military camp
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Arthas Menethil, Uther the Lightbringer, Captain Falric
**Stakes:** Arthas in full crisis mode. The fuse Sylvanas lit in Ch 11 is burning. He has conviction but acts without the analytical foundation S&J built over weeks.
**Partnership phase:** SAVING EACH OTHER — the partnership reveals itself to outsiders. The crack from Ch 13 is present but functional.

Arthas has been mobilizing since Sylvanas told him about the plague (Ch 11). He moved on Hearthglen. The city was already compromised — contaminated grain in the food supply. The dead rose. Arthas fought his way through, Silver Hand paladins at his side, Uther commanding the defensive lines. The city held, barely.

Now he's in a war camp, assembling intelligence, and the two women who have been tracking this plague for weeks arrive with the full picture: Kel'Thuzad is dead, the Cult's command structure is decapitated, but the plague is already deployed. The grain is in the food supply across northern Lordaeron. Killing the architect changed nothing.

The Jaina/Arthas dynamic has shifted since Strahnbrad (Ch 6). Then, he was dismissive but polite — too busy with orcs. Now he's furious. He should have moved sooner. He WAS moving — Sylvanas told him — but the orc distraction (Mal'Ganis's work) cost him weeks. And the people who have the intelligence he needs are his ex-lover and the elven ranger who told him the truth when he wasn't ready to hear all of it.

**Three-way friction:** Arthas wants to burn (military decisiveness, escalating to impatience — playing catch-up and overcompensating). Jaina wants to understand (the investigation isn't finished, they need to know the full scope — the Lich King, the Burning Legion). Sylvanas wants intelligence (tactical, not emotional — she reads Arthas and sees the same prince from Ch 6, but harder, the certainty curdling into something dangerous). She also reads the room differently now: she PUT him on this path. If he breaks, part of the breaking is on her.

**Partnership reveal:** When Sylvanas and Jaina deliver their combined intelligence, they finish each other's briefing without coordinating — Sylvanas provides tactical terrain, Jaina provides arcane analysis, the seams are invisible. Arthas doesn't notice because he's not listening. Uther notices.

## Chapter 16: Before the Gates
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** The hard ride ahead of Arthas's main column → Stratholme's grain stores and market routes → the main gate
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Arthas (arriving late), Uther (arriving late)
**Stakes:** Jaina gets one real shot to save the city before Arthas's answer becomes the only answer left.
**Partnership phase:** SAVING EACH OTHER — field-unit proof under pressure

Arthas does not wait. That matters, but it is not the whole chapter. Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia ride ahead of the army and try to beat the city clock at the source — granaries, bakehouses, cart routes, whatever still holds the plague in concentrated form before it finishes moving through homes and bodies.

This is the first hard proof Sylvanas gets about what Jaina means to do with the knowledge she took from Kel'Thuzad. She uses it at once, brutally and practically, to try to save strangers. The source plan works at the node level. A granary can be spoiled. A store can be killed. A route can be interrupted. The city is simply farther gone than the plan can reach.

That distinction matters. Jaina does not fail because she was seduced by theory. She fails because the theory arrived after breakfast. Arthas's shadow sits over every beat of the chapter because the answer waiting at the gate depends on whether Jaina will lie about that difference. She does not.

## Chapter 17: The Gate
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** Stratholme's main gate → service lane → inner wall
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Uther, Falric, a grain carter, Arthas
**Stakes:** The fuse detonates. The prince she aimed at the plague turns on a city. The intelligence picture becomes a moral reckoning. The fifth farewell crosses the threshold from anomaly to signal.
**Partnership phase:** SAVING EACH OTHER — the shared witness on the wall cements the bond

Jaina says the city is too late. Arthas answers with the Culling. Uther breaks at the gate, publicly and at once. What matters for Sylvanas is not a debate after that. It is the fact that the prince no longer needs anyone's approval once the answer stops being yes.

The fifth sighting arrives in the service lane. A grain carter with an empty wagon gives exact route information to the inner wall and finishes with the same archaic farewell. The phrase is no longer a curiosity. It is a pattern attached to the road behind them.

From the wall, Sylvanas gets the full military read. Arthas is cutting the city into controlled sections and preventing one mass turn by reaching the living first. The method is real. So is the atrocity. Tactical coherence does not excuse it. It only makes the thing below more legible and therefore harder to deny.

**Sylvanas's private weight:** The causal chain from Ch 11 arrives at its terminus. She lit the fuse. She told Arthas the truth because she needed institutional power aimed at the plague. The institutional power aimed itself at a city. She does not speak this aloud. She stands on the wall beside Jaina in silence — the silence of shared witness, different from every earlier silence.

The chapter ends with Arthas emerging from the main gate. Clean hands. Wrong eyes. He looks up at the wall, sees the two women who chose a different road. Says nothing. Turns away. The prince who walked in is not the prince who walks out.

## Chapter 18: The Wall
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** Stratholme's inner walls -> the aftermath -> the road out south of the city
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Arthas (departed, his absence as present as his presence)
**Stakes:** Shared witness to atrocity. The crack welds shut. The partnership shifts from professional to personal.
**Partnership phase:** The bonding point — the partnership shifts through shared witness, not conversation

Two women on a wall. The Culling has ended. The fires still burn but the screaming has stopped — the specific silence of a city where nothing living remains to scream. This chapter is Ch 17's mirror: the same wall, the same aftermath, now from Jaina's interiority. Where Sylvanas read the operational signature (four flatlined metrics, military methodology, the prince's trajectory), Jaina reads the personal dimension — the man she once loved, the trajectory she diagnosed three years ago at the Alterac border, the countermeasure she built that failed at the scale where it mattered most.

**Jaina's paralysis, Sylvanas's anchor:** In the aftermath, Jaina cannot move. Standing, eyes open, seeing nothing - the paralysis not of fear but of comprehension. The countermeasure's failure (Ch 16) left no alternative in Arthas's mind, and the alternative he chose is burning below her. The resonance disruption she developed in Kel'Thuzad's sanctum, refined on the ride from Scholomance into the war camp, carried into the city as a weapon - it works, and it is not enough, and the gap between "works" and "enough" was filled by soldiers. Sylvanas does not comfort her. She puts a hand on Jaina's shoulder and gives her the only thing that works in that moment: a direction off the wall. South is the road out, not the answer. The real next question turns north only after they are moving again.

**The crack welds shut.** Shared witness does what no conversation could. The micro-calibrations from Ch 14 — Sylvanas's timing shifts, the extra beat before committing to Jaina's evaluations — vanish. Not because Sylvanas forgot what she saw in Scholomance, but because Stratholme makes it irrelevant. Whatever Jaina's intellectual vulnerability to Kel'Thuzad's arguments, the woman on the wall beside her — mute, shattered, watching the aftermath of a city's end — is the same woman who has been her partner for weeks. The Scholomance room was a moment. The wall is everything.

**Narration shift:** After Stratholme, Sylvanas's internal references to Jaina change tone. Not warmer — more precise. She stops evaluating Jaina and starts integrating her. Jaina's position, stamina, emotional state, magical reserves become as automatic in Sylvanas's tactical awareness as her own arrow count.

**The Arthas reading from inside:** Jaina has the romantic history to read what Sylvanas could not. The Alterac border dispute: Arthas at nineteen, making a decision that was tactically correct and strategically catastrophic, and Jaina seeing the pattern that no one else saw. Three years later, the pattern has expressed itself fully. The man she loved became this. She diagnosed the trajectory before anyone else and the diagnosis helped nothing.
