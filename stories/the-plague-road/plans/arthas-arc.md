# The Arthas Arc — Chapters 15–18

- Tone: humor dies by Ch 16. Stratholme kills whatever is left of the lighter register.
- Partnership track: damaged trust -> mutual rescue -> shared witness -> welded bond.
- Carry-in: Sylvanas set Arthas moving in Ch 11. The Scholomance crack is still live until Stratholme burns it shut.

## Ch 15 — The Prince
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Hearthglen aftermath. Arthas is already running hot.
- Sylvanas gives ground truth; Jaina gives mechanism. They brief as one unit.
- Uther clocks the partnership at once.
- Arthas hears the actionable part and overcorrects toward speed.
- Sylvanas realizes the certainty she aimed in Ch 11 is becoming dangerous.

## Ch 16 — Before the Gates
- POV: Jaina.
- Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia ride ahead to spoil the source before the city tips.
- Jaina spends Kel'Thuzad's knowledge on strangers at once: granaries, bakehouses, routes, stores.
- The tactic works locally and fails strategically. Stratholme is already too far gone.
- Jaina tells Arthas that truth cleanly. She does not lie to buy time.

## Ch 17 — The Gate
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Arthas answers with the Culling. Uther breaks with him on the spot.
- Sylvanas can see both truths at once: the move is militarily coherent and still atrocity.
- This is where the chain she started by recruiting Arthas becomes visible in full.
- The road-farewell appears again and is now clearly a pattern, not coincidence.
- End beat: Arthas leaves changed and sees S&J watching.

## Ch 18 — The Wall
- POV: Jaina.
- Jaina freezes from comprehension, not fear.
- Sylvanas gets her moving with direction, not comfort.
- Stratholme welds shut the Scholomance crack. After this Sylvanas stops assessing Jaina and starts counting on her.
- Jaina sees the old relationship with Arthas reach its final form: he became exactly what she once feared, and being right still saved no one.
