# The Partnership Arc — Sylvanas x Jaina

- Not romance. Tactical intimacy, earned trust, and the habit of survival.
- Arc line: threat -> asset -> partner -> necessary -> best possible team.
- Track it through changed behavior, naming, timing, and who covers whose blind spots.

## Phase 1 — Threat / Contained Asset (Ch 1-4)
### Sylvanas on Jaina
- Ch 1: security breach in violet robes.
- Ch 2: brilliant, condescending, poor road instincts, still useful.
- Ch 3-4: academic recklessness remains true, but Jaina turns magical chaos into usable intelligence faster than anyone else on the board.
- Marker: Sylvanas thinks "the mage," never "Jaina."

### Jaina on Sylvanas
- Hard soldier. Cold custody. Little patience for theory.
- She keeps translating herself downward and misses how much Sylvanas already sees.
- Ch 2 road fight is the first time she sees the shape Sylvanas gives a fight instead of brute force.
- Ch 3-4 teaches her that Sylvanas reaches some of the same conclusions through a discipline Jaina never took seriously.

### First cracks
- Sylvanas catches the register shift and says nothing.
- Deer attack makes Jaina necessary in the field.
- Millhaven makes Sylvanas's tracking and Jaina's magical read feel like halves of the same job.
- Sick-village logistics force their first real collaborative solve.

## Phase 2 — Grudging Respect (Ch 5-8)
- The plague only makes sense when their methods are combined.
- Sylvanas quietly starts thinking "Jaina."
- Jaina stops simplifying and starts waiting for Sylvanas's tactical answer because the analysis feels unfinished without it.
- Andorhal is the first phase where they divide work by aptitude without arguing about control.
- Kel'Thuzad's name turns respect into dependence: each woman has only half the picture alone.

## Phase 3 — Tests of Faith (Ch 9-14)
### The mistakes
- Jaina stays with a ritual trace too long. The Cult hits while she is still studying it. Sylvanas bleeds covering the exit.
- Sylvanas pushes through a baited gap without waiting for the arcane sweep. Lyriel dies in the trap.
- Neither apologizes. Both change behavior.

### The split
- Jaina follows the Barov trail to Scholomance.
- Sylvanas follows the Arthas trail after the Mal'Ganis breadcrumb.
- Separation proves the partner is not helpful extra weight. The partner is structural.

### The crack
- Kel'Thuzad is Jaina's dark mirror: same brilliance, same rigor, no ethical anchor.
- Sylvanas arrives after the worst internal moment and only sees Jaina apparently listening.
- From that point on, trust is still present but no longer effortless. Timing and spacing shift.

## Phase 4 — Saving Each Other (Ch 15-18)
- The saves stop being speeches and become embedded habits.
- Ch 15-16: Sylvanas sees Jaina spend dangerous knowledge to save strangers immediately, which matters more than anything argued in Scholomance.
- Ch 17-18: Stratholme welds the crack shut. Shared witness replaces unfinished explanations.
- After the wall, Sylvanas stops evaluating Jaina and starts tracking her automatically as part of the combat picture.

## Phase 5 — Competence Recognition (Ch 19-22)
- Northrend strips away almost all friction.
- They communicate in shorthand, split work without discussion, and move like people who have been operating together for years.
- Muradin notices it before either of them names it.
- The Frostmourne retreat proves the team was right even when the fight was unwinnable.

## Phase 6 — Peak Competence (Ch 23)
- Sylvanas takes the direct fight and loses it honestly.
- Jaina invents the answer under pressure and holds the seam open.
- Sylvanas rises and makes the kill shot.
- Final note: the qualities Sylvanas first filed under "dangerous mage" are exactly what save her life.

## Tracking cues
- Naming change: "the mage" -> "Jaina."
- Register change: Jaina stops explaining downward.
- Habit change: Sylvanas waits for the sweep; Jaina asks how long.
- Emotional truth: trust is built in actions, not in conversations about trust.
