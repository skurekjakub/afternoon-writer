# The Investigation Arc — Chapters 1–8

- Tone: competence thriller first, dread by Andorhal.
- Partnership track: trespasser -> custody -> grudging respect -> dependence.
- Use: trace the plague from a border anomaly to an organized conspiracy.

## Ch 1 — The Border
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Open on routine troll work and easy banter with Lor'themar. This is the last normal beat in the book.
- Cyndia's runner reports a human mage inside the first ward boundary, testing ward-stones.
- Sylvanas arrives angry, recognizes Jaina Proudmoore, and treats the whole thing as a sovereign breach.
- End on the interrogation opening line.

## Ch 2 — The Mage
- POV: Jaina.
- Jaina insists this is leyline corruption, not espionage.
- Sylvanas keeps her in custody and says, in effect, "show me."
- The ride south proves the contamination is growing stronger.
- Plague-maddened deer gives the first physical proof: this is present, contagious, and wrong in the body.

## Ch 3 — Millhaven
- POV: Sylvanas.
- First dead village: open doors, food left out, bodies already gone north.
- Jaina reads huge necromantic saturation but cannot isolate the carrier from one site.
- Sylvanas reads drag marks and organized movement.
- Survivors mention a new grain supplier, but it does not mean anything yet.
- Mal'Ganis appears as an old grain trader with an empty wagon and the odd road-farewell.

## Ch 4 — The Dying Village
- POV: Jaina.
- Living victims this time: fever, coughing, people still salvageable or almost.
- Jaina sees the plague inside living bodies and can only slow it.
- Sylvanas works the village logistics and hears the same supply answer again.
- Grain becomes the likely weapon once sickness lines up with diet.
- A cult watcher at the granary tries to burn evidence, gets caught, and dies by poison before he can talk. This proves organization, discipline, and kill-switch training.

## Ch 5 — The Grain Road
- POV: Sylvanas.
- Now the question is scale.
- Records, manifests, and depots reveal a distribution network, not a local outbreak.
- Barov operations sit in the middle of the compromised routes.
- The old trader and empty wagon from Ch 3 snag retroactively.
- Sylvanas starts thinking "Jaina" instead of "the mage."

## Ch 6 — The Pattern
- POV: Jaina.
- The plague is clearly engineered and continent-minded.
- Arthas appears still chasing the wrong war. He is polite, dismissive, and already dangerous in the way certainty can be dangerous.
- Mal'Ganis appears again in disguise and repeats the road-farewell.
- End on Andorhal: functioning city on the surface, contamination underneath.

## Ch 7 — Andorhal
- POV: Sylvanas.
- They enter as supply inspectors.
- Corin gives mage-guild access. Havel gives militia traction.
- Labor splits naturally: Jaina handles magical method; Sylvanas handles people, logistics, and threat layout.
- Jaina finds how the grain is being treated at the hub.
- Sylvanas finds the embedded network, hierarchy, and scale.
- Together they finally have the full picture.

## Ch 8 — The Hunters Hunted
- POV: Jaina.
- Live-capture operation shows the partnership at full efficiency.
- The prisoner confirms design, live handlers, and a human architect high in the chain.
- He is killed mid-sentence by necromancy from inside the city.
- The Cult knows they are here and has standard response procedures.
- Final turn: the grain is already shipped, Andorhal was only the hub, and the name Kel'Thuzad sends them toward Scholomance.

## Hard rules
- Before Ch 4, neither woman knows grain is the carrier.
- Jaina should be smart early, not magically omniscient.
- Mal'Ganis is only retroactively obvious.
- Humor peaks in Ch 1-6 and starts dying in Ch 7-8.
