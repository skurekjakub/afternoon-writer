# The Investigation Arc — Chapters 1–8

**Tone:** Dramione-register competence thriller. Dry wit, mutual assessment, procedural investigation. Humor peaks in Ch 1-6, recedes through Ch 7-8 as the conspiracy's scope emerges.
**Partnership phase transition:** PRE-CONTACT → THREAT ASSESSMENT → GRUDGING RESPECT → DEPENDENCE

---

## Chapter 1: The Border
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** Southeastern Eversong Woods (Amani frontier), the forest trail south, Farstrider border post at Thalassian Pass
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Lor'themar Theron (first 3 beats), Ranger Cyndia, Jaina (final 3 beats — seen, barely heard)
**Stakes:** Sylvanas's world before the story happens to her — the inciting incident arrives as a prisoner at the border
**Partnership phase:** PRE-CONTACT → first sight → confrontation

Sylvanas is in the field — not investigating anomalies, but doing routine work she loves. The tail end of an Amani troll patrol, southeast Eversong. Seven trolls, clean kills. Post-fight banter with Lor'themar, avoiding the Convocation's budget review. The only moment of genuine ease in the entire book. This is who Sylvanas is before the story takes it from her.

A Farstrider runner from Cyndia's border post: human mage intercepted inside the first ward boundary. Running arcane diagnostics on the ward-stones. Female. No credentials. Lor'themar pivots north to manage the Convocation; Sylvanas rides south to the border, furious that her reprieve has become an international incident.

At the border post, Cyndia briefs her. Then the recognition: Jaina Proudmoore. Antonidas's protégée. Monitored threat classification. Inside the ward network. The situation escalates from trespasser to security breach. The chapter ends with Sylvanas opening the interrogation — one cutting line, Jaina firing back about leylines, CUT.

## Chapter 2: The Mage
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** Farstrider border post (interrogation), the road south into Lordaeron, the forest before Millhaven
**Key characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Ranger Cyndia, Farstrider escort
**Stakes:** Hostile interrogation becomes field investigation. The leyline corruption is confirmed. A plague-maddened animal proves the threat is physical, present, and moving.
**Partnership phase:** THREAT ASSESSMENT → custody → first crack (competence recognition, unacknowledged)
**Detailed outline:** chapter2.md (3 scenes, ~13 beats)

The interrogation. Sylvanas methodical, Jaina impatient. Jaina's defense: leyline corruption, not espionage. She sensed necromantic wrongness on a courier route, followed it north, and crossed the border when the Thalassian ward-stones began carrying the same damage. Nobody believed her readings. She came alone. Sylvanas's calculation: the mage's story, if true, describes a threat her entire network can't cover. Decision: custody, not partnership. "Show me."

They ride south. Jaina reads the leylines; Sylvanas reads the terrain. Professional friction, not collaboration. The road itself becomes evidence — contamination strengthening with every mile south.

The wildlife attack: plague-maddened deer crash from the treeline. Filmed eyes, lurching gait. DYING, not undead — necromantic energy animating failing tissue. The deer keeps moving after arrows pin it. Triple duty: proves contamination is physical/present, gives Sylvanas a threat she can SEE, bridges the tonal shift from interrogation drama to field horror.

## Chapter 3: Millhaven
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** Millhaven (the first dead village), survivor farm outside the contamination radius, the road south
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel (Farstrider escort pair), survivor family, Mal'Ganis (disguised as old grain trader)
**Stakes:** First dead village. The scope of the contamination is revealed. Mal'Ganis's first appearance — unrecognized.
**Partnership phase:** CONTAINED ASSET → first fracture (competence recognition, grudging)
**Detailed outline:** chapter3.md (3 scenes, 10 beats)
**GRAIN-AWARENESS CONSTRAINT:** Neither Sylvanas nor Jaina knows the grain is the plague vector. Jaina detects massive ambient necromantic contamination but can't isolate the delivery mechanism — everything is saturated (grain, water, soil, organic matter). She needs multiple data points. The granary has the highest concentration, but grain naturally absorbs ambient moisture, so it COULD be concentrating waterborne contamination. One data point isn't enough.

Millhaven: doors open, food on tables, no bodies. Jaina analyzes the contamination — pervasive necromantic energy saturating everything, strongest in the granary but present in water, soil, building materials. She can't isolate the vector. Sylvanas reads the ground — sixty-plus drag marks heading north in rough formation, not military precision but directed movement. Something organized the dead.

Survivors: a family outside the contamination radius. The wife was traveling when it happened. She describes the supply chain — new grain supplier about three months ago — but this is noted by Sylvanas as one detail among many, not connected to a vector they haven't identified.

Mal'Ganis: an old, stooped trader on the road south. Empty grain wagon heading north — delivered his load. Helpful route intelligence about road conditions ahead. "May the stars keep your road" — an archaic farewell. Sylvanas notes the phrasing as odd but doesn't chase it. They don't know to look at grain yet, so the empty wagon is meaningless. The encounter is designed to SNAG retroactively when the grain vector is confirmed in Ch 5.

## Chapter 4: The Dying Village
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** A village two days south of Millhaven — still alive, but sick. Makeshift infirmary. The granary. Militia barracks.
**Key characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider escort, militia captain, village medic, Cult initiate (dies)
**Stakes:** THE GRAIN VECTOR CONFIRMED. First cult encounter. The investigation upgrades from "trace contamination" to "hunt a conspiracy." First genuine partnership collaboration.
**Partnership phase:** CONTAINED ASSET → first cracks toward grudging respect — the partnership becomes functional
**Detailed outline:** chapter4.md (3 scenes, 11 beats)

A dying village: not aftermath (Millhaven) but present-tense horror. People coughing. Children with fevers. A medic treating marsh fever that isn't marsh fever. Jaina works the infirmary — first time seeing the contamination in LIVING tissue. She can slow the progression (stabilization wards, improvised) but not stop it. Three patients too far gone. The compassion beat: the clinical arrogance from Ch 2's interrogation room is still there, but it's being used to ease suffering she can't cure.

Sylvanas's parallel thread: logistics interrogation of militia captain, general store owner, granary keeper. Same answer as Millhaven — new grain supplier, three months ago, pleasant and forgettable. Second data point. Supply chain warfare.

The ingestion discovery: contamination concentration correlates with DIET. Heaviest bread-eaters are sickest. Lightest cases: a nursing mother on broth, a traveler eating his own provisions, a boy who eats only home-grown oats. The grain is the weapon.

The intelligence exchange: Jaina has the mechanism (ingestion vector), Sylvanas has the logistics (same supplier). Both halves on the same table. The first real collaboration — neither acknowledges the shift.

THE GRANARY: They go to secure the grain stores and find a cult initiate — young, low-level, monitoring contamination levels. He tries to burn the granary. Sylvanas pins him with an arrow. He flees, caught by rangers. Put in militia barracks. Suicides with a concealed poison capsule while Jaina and Sylvanas secure the granary evidence. Trained. Conditioned. Kill-switch protocol. This is a military operation with expendable personnel and operational security. The conspiracy is real, organized, and conditions its people to die rather than talk.

## Chapter 5: The Grain Road
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** Lordaeron's trade road network — merchant routes, grain depots, a roadside inn, the supply chain infrastructure
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, merchants, grain traders, a depot overseer, Cyndia, Lyriel
**Stakes:** DISTRIBUTION SCALE CONFIRMED. The Barov connection surfaces. The name shift. Andorhal convergence.
**Partnership phase:** GRUDGING RESPECT forming — "Jaina" replaces "the mage" in internal narration
**Detailed outline:** chapter5.md (3 scenes, 8 beats)

The grain vector is known (Ch 4). Now: HOW BIG? They track the supply chain backward through depot records, delivery manifests, merchant contracts. The scope: not one village, not two — DOZENS. The contaminated grain radiates from a central distribution network. Someone weaponized Lordaeron's agricultural infrastructure at scale.

The Barov connection: the supply chain leads to Barov family grain operations — one of Lordaeron's oldest noble houses, massive agricultural holdings. Not the Barovs themselves necessarily, but their distribution network has been compromised or co-opted. The investigation gains a target that isn't just "somewhere southwest."

The retroactive snag: the old trader's empty grain wagon from Ch 3. Sylvanas remembers it now — not with alarm, but with the quiet irritation of a detail she should have flagged. The forgettable merchant, the empty wagon, the same supply route. She files it with more weight.

The name shift: Sylvanas stops calling her "the mage" internally. First use of "Jaina" is unremarked — it happens mid-thought during a tactical assessment. No fanfare.

Jaina's register shift: she stops simplifying explanations. Delivers conclusions at full complexity and waits for Sylvanas's tactical angle. The pause that was pedagogical becomes collaborative.

All roads converge on Andorhal — Lordaeron's agricultural distribution hub.

## Chapter 6: The Pattern
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** Approaching Andorhal — Strahnbrad (military camp), the outer trade network, smaller depots
**Key characters:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Arthas Menethil (first encounter), Cyndia, Lyriel, Cult scouts (disguised), Mal'Ganis (disguise #2)
**Stakes:** The transition from "epidemic" to "conspiracy." First encounter with Arthas — chasing the wrong threat. Mal'Ganis's second appearance — the farewell REPEATS.
**Partnership phase:** GRUDGING RESPECT solidifying — operational dependence forming
**Detailed outline:** chapter6.md (3 scenes, 9 beats)

The evidence crystallizes: this is not a natural plague. The contamination pattern is too precise, too systematic. Someone is poisoning the grain supply at scale using Lordaeron's own agricultural infrastructure.

**The Strahnbrad encounter:** Arthas, golden and certain, chasing Blackrock orc raids — a Mal'Ganis distraction. Jaina tries to warn him about the grain. He's polite, dismissive. The personal history makes the dismissal cut. She was right about him when she ended it, she's right now, and being right changes nothing.

**Mal'Ganis disguise #2:** A grain trader near Andorhal. Helpful information about the distribution hub. "May the stars keep your road." Sylvanas notes the farewell — déjà vu, files with more weight, doesn't resolve the thread.

The chapter ends on the outskirts of Andorhal. A functioning city on the surface. The wrongness is underneath. Jaina's head aches from the leyline contamination.

## Chapter 7: Andorhal
**POV:** Sylvanas
**Setting:** Andorhal — gates, market district, inspection offices, granary district, alchemical quarter, safe house
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Apprentice Corin (Kirin Tor contact), Captain Havel (militia), Cult operatives (disguised)
**Stakes:** Infiltrating the source. The Cult's operation is inside the city's infrastructure.
**Partnership phase:** RESPECT → DEPENDENCE — the partnership becomes load-bearing
**Detailed outline:** chapter7.md (3 scenes, 9 beats)

Infiltration under cover as supply inspectors. Jaina handles institutional navigation; Sylvanas provides threat assessment. New allies: Corin (junior Kirin Tor, nervous, competent, his reports ignored by Dalaran) and Havel (militia captain, right instincts, no framework).

They divide labor by aptitude: Jaina takes the mage-guild quarter and grain analysis; Sylvanas takes the granary district and logistics. First time Sylvanas operates without line-of-sight on Jaina. The word "asset" dies.

The mechanism revealed: Jaina finds the HOW (the grain is treated during hub packaging with a necromantic carrier layer, built to survive ordinary use while the host body finishes the deeper work later). Sylvanas finds the WHO (eight-plus operatives, two-tier hierarchy, supply caches off the city ledgers, embedded infrastructure operating for months). Together: the full city-level picture. Alone, each had half, and the host-side trigger remains unresolved.

## Chapter 8: The Hunters Hunted
**POV:** Jaina
**Setting:** Andorhal at night — granary district, city streets, safe houses
**Key characters:** Sylvanas, Jaina, captured Cult operative, Cult enforcers, Cyndia, Lyriel, Corin, Havel
**Stakes:** The Cult knows they're here. The investigation flips from proactive to reactive. Kel'Thuzad named.
**Partnership phase:** DEPENDENCE confirmed — they can't operate alone anymore
**Detailed outline:** chapter8.md (3 scenes, 9 beats)

The capture operation: Sylvanas runs the ambush, Jaina provides arcane support. Partnership at peak efficiency. Operative taken alive — mid-tier, terrified, trained. Partial disclosure: single mortal architect at the human level, contamination agent DESIGNED not discovered, field acolytes taught command signals for forcing or sharpening obedience in converted dead, and a name they can't speak.

The operative DIES mid-sentence. Necromantic strike from inside the city walls — precise, surgical. The Cult has mages. The Cult knows they're here. The investigation FLIPS.

Evasion through hostile streets. Jaina scans the magical layer; Sylvanas reads the physical layer. The partnership as survival mechanism. The Cult's response is too fast, too coordinated — established security protocol, not improvisation.

The devastating revelation: contaminated grain is ALREADY SHIPPED. Andorhal isn't the target — it's the distribution center. The plague is deployed. They arrived too late.

A name surfaces: Kel'Thuzad. Former Kirin Tor. Expelled by Antonidas. Jaina goes still. She knows the name — her mentor's greatest failure of institutional judgment. *He also thought he was right.* Bridge to the Scholomance arc.
