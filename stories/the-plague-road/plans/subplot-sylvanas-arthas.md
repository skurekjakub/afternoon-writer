# The Causal Chain — Sylvanas and Arthas

- Use this as the note that Sylvanas recruits the weapon that later destroys a city.
- This is not guilt. It is the weight of causation: the right briefing still helped light the worst possible fire.

## How Sylvanas reads him
- She reads commanders, not lovers.
- What she tracks: decision tempo, use of expert input, situational awareness, and sustainability.
- His pause between assessment and action shrinks every time she sees him.
- Her own pause widens over the same span because Jaina taught her to wait for what she cannot see alone.

## The chain
- Ch 6: Arthas is a useful but mis-aimed military asset.
- Ch 11: Sylvanas briefs him fully because she needs institutional power on the plague.
- Ch 15: she sees what that speed has become at Hearthglen and knows the tempo is now wrong.
- Ch 16: Jaina spending plague knowledge to save real people starts repairing the Scholomance crack.
- Ch 17: Stratholme is the detonation. Arthas no longer needs anyone else's judgment once he has the answer he wants.
- Ch 23: Sylvanas closes the same chain with the arrow.

## Thematic note
- Crossing the border is still the right decision.
- The cost is that engagement means causing outcomes, not just witnessing them.
- Sylvanas is right at every step and still helps produce catastrophe.
