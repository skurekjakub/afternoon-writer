# Chapter 2: The Ring (Cambridge Laboratory / Auror Office)

**POV:** Draco
**Scene categories:** competence-collision, forced-proximity, personal-assessment, vulnerability-mapping, involuntary-cataloguing, family-artefact, first-kindness, verbal-sparring
**Continuity:** Picks up mid-scene from ch1's cliffhanger (Lumos cast at Granger's chest). The lab assessment shifts from physical security to personal vulnerability. Draco's tracking of Granger deepens — from catalogue-the-adversary into something more granular and physical than the assignment requires.
**Setting:** Granger's laboratory + office (Cambridge, King's Hall) — same location as ch1's penetration test, now continuing into personal security. Later: Auror Office (private schedule-study). Then back to Cambridge office for the ring delivery.
**Notes:** THE INVOLUNTARY CATALOGUE. This chapter is where Draco starts noticing Granger's body — her calves, her collarbones, her tiredness — framed as professional assessment but lingering longer than professional assessment requires. He notices the shape of her knee through tights and calls it "Muggle fashion foibles." He clocks that she's overworked and undernourished, and the concern is genuine but unexamined. Meanwhile: "tall, arrogant ponce with good hair" (via Legilimency) is the first time we see Draco through Granger's eyes, and the "good hair" is the crack in her hostility she doesn't know is there. The chapter ends on the first kindness either has offered the other — and both immediately retreat from it.

### Beats

**SCENE 1: Continuation — The Personal Assessment (Laboratory, Monday evening)**

1. Resolution of ch1 cliffhanger: the Lumos dissipates harmlessly into Granger's robes. Shock, hand at her breast. "That was unnecessary." Draco saunters. "I promise you other spells wouldn't be so friendly." The demonstration IS the argument — her research is safe, but she isn't.

2. Quick-draw test. Draco turns with wand raised. She's readier this time — spits out a Protego. "Better." He's training her reflexes and she hasn't agreed to be trained.

3. Imperius Curse provocation. Granger grows still: "If you cast that on me in my own laboratory, I shall drown you in Sanitatem and enjoy the irony." Every vial of healing potion levitates off the benches and hovers over his head. Nonverbal magic — impressive. In a real fight he'd Vanish the lot and blast her through two walls, but: noted.

4. Legilimency. "How's your Occlumency?" — accompanied by a "friendly bit" of mind-reading. Draco gets a clear view of Granger's perception of him: tall, arrogant ponce with good hair. Then he's mentally slapped out. Finger pressed to his forehead — his brain stings. She looks like she wants to follow up with a physical slap. School days callback.

5. "You're the vulnerability." The argument crystallized — all her research lives in her head and can be read or tortured out. Her staff knows fifteen percent scattered through thirteen minds. She's the single point of failure. She looks predictably offended.

6. Travel patterns. Floo, Apparition — both trackable. Broom? "I detest flying." Draco makes a valiant effort not to curl his lip. What a terrible position to take. What a dreadful thing to hate. Granger falls in his esteem quite irredeemably.

**SCENE 2: The Office Search**

7. Office search. More incomprehensible Muggle jargon. Conference invitations, grant application results, student notes. Useless tat. Another computer on the desk — Draco stares at it intimidatingly, waiting for it to confess its sins. It offers him wobbly lines.

8. **The Involuntary Catalogue.** Granger pulls off her Healer robes and drops into the chair. Sigh of unadulterated fatigue. Draco glances at her — Muggle clothing underneath. Opaque black tights that barely merit the name "trousers." He can see the precise outline of her calf and the exact shape of her knee. "Was this decent public attire by Muggle standards? Shocking." — the notice is involuntary, the framing is displacement.

9. **The Concern.** He shifts from noticing her body to noticing her condition. How thin she is, collarbones shadowing, neck too dainty for the mass of hair pinned on her head. Pale, peaky, overdrawn. The assessment is no longer professional — he doesn't need her health metrics for a security report.

10. The schedule. "What's your schedule like, Granger?" — asked as though continuing about travel patterns, but really wanting to know what this woman does with herself. She produces a colour-coded schedule planned to the hour (of course). He circles moments of exposure using his wand as a quill.

11. Granger is everywhere, does everything. Lab hours, clinic hours, teaching, volunteering for Good Causes, tutoring, mentoring, Healing at St. Mungo's AND a Muggle surgery, one (1) pub night every fortnight with Potter and Friends, college dinners, "yoga" at unholy hours, "Crooks Vet" every three months, and scattered days marked only with asterisks.

12. The asterisk mystery. "Holidays," she says. "Your Occlumency might be passable, but your lying isn't." She grows snippy — personal life is off-limits. Draco drops it. But: he puzzles over the asterisks privately later — too scattered for her period, not lunar, not matching Potter or Weasley's time off. "Was he looking at Granger's sex schedule?" He checks the leave book at the Auror Office. No coincidence. The mystery endures.

13. The wall of achievements. Certificates, diplomas, awards — a "nice mosaic." She doesn't laugh. Not quite twelve doctorates but approaching it: Biomedical Sciences, Microbiology and Immunology, joint M.D.-PhD in Oncology, Genetics certificate, Healer's Seal (Cambridge), Master's in Transfiguration (Edinburgh), Specialised Healing in Blood Magicks (Sorbonne). Her record-breaking O.W.L.s and N.E.W.T.s are in a dusty box on a low shelf. An Order of Merlin, First Class, doesn't merit wall space. Potter has his on his cubicle wall.

14. Tea and espionage. She excuses herself to make tea, asks if he'd like a cup — the civility visibly challenging to produce. He says no. She looks relieved. While she's gone: Draco casts tracking spells on her trainers, hairpins, and a half-finished mug of tea. Pragmatic. Sneakish.

15. Computers and magic. Small ping from the computer. Draco's realization — how are these computers working in a magical building? Granger: "I found ways to circumvent the issue." Three metallic pucks around each computer cluster — an Anti-Magical Forcefield. "I'm calling it an Anti-Magical Forcefield, for lack of a better term." Blocking magic is the stuff of legend, theoretical discussion, lost artefacts. And she built it from a café wifi hotspot analogy.

16. "I'm not entirely certain that those are legal." "Better report me to Shacklebolt." Her eyes meet his: unfriendly, unafraid. Draco decides Granger has balls, possibly rivalling Tonks' enormous pair.

**SCENE 3: The Quicksand Exit**

17. Wrapping up. Draco scribbles notes for his plan. Copy of her schedule via Duplicatus + Protean Charm. Report with recommendations. He'll convince Shacklebolt she doesn't need a daily minder. "A relief for all parties."

18. "Stop giving my owl treacle tart, it makes him unruly." — the first hint of a recurring soft detail. She looks only slightly abashed.

19. Exit. Granger sits down to work some more — she is a normal, well-adjusted individual. Draco has ceased to exist. He shows himself out. "Mind the tile just in front of the door — Quicksand Curse. It was to catch the baddies on the way out." "Saw it, Granger." "Of course you did."

**SCENE 4: The Plan (Auror Office, several days)**

20. Exchanges with Shacklebolt. Draco outlines his approach — convinces the Minister it's the only viable option because the Principal is too uncooperative for alternatives.

21. Schedule study and the asterisk obsession. Private moments, Draco puzzling. Rules out period, lunar cycle. Romantic entanglement? "Would she really take entire days off? Draco felt that he ought to shake the hand of the man responsible." Checks Potter and Weasley's off-days. Nothing lines up.

22. Tinkering with "ancient magicks best left untouched." Days of work, off-page. The reveal will be the ring.

**SCENE 5: The Ring (Cambridge Office)**

23. Draco slaps a roll of recommendations onto Granger's desk. It reaches the floor. She blinks slowly. "Item fifty-six." She finds it: "The Principal must agree to wear the Ring at all times, until completion of the Project."

24. The ring presented. Small silver band, tossed casually. He doesn't want to train her on Imperius resistance, Veritaserum defense, physical self-defence ("gods forbid; you look like your punches might concuss a gnat, at best"), or stand sentry at her door. The ring lets him be alerted if anything happens and Apparate to her instantly.

25. The Katie Bell Incident. Granger pokes the ring with her wand instead of putting it on. "I saw the aftermath of the last piece of jewellery that Draco Malfoy handed out." If he had feelings, they would've been a little hurt by this display of mistrust stemming from an idiot boy being manipulated by the Darkest wizard of the century. But he doesn't, so the point is moot.

26. Granger's diagnostic. Revelation spells set the ring glowing — Locator Charm, protective runes, distress beacon, heart rate monitoring. "You've invented a Wizarding FitBit." Draco is mortally affronted that his exceptional creation is a knock-off of a Muggle thing.

27. The unfinished Portkey. An incomplete knot of Arithmantic calculations — days of frustrating work. "What's this unfinished mess here?" Nostrils pinch. Meant to be an on-demand Portkey for when Apparition fails. "Portus is a pain in the arse of an enchantment." Granger looks mildly impressed.

28. The Artefact reveal. Granger deduces: this isn't a trinket. It's an Artefact. A family heirloom. She taps away the concealment charm — an ornate silver ouroboros, ever eating its own tail. Inside: Sanctimonia Vincet Semper. Purity will always conquer.

29. The temperature drop. "You're certain this ring won't immediately attempt to amputate my finger? I'm not Pure, after all." The office goes cold. "Did you see a sign of Dark magic?" — too quickly, defensive, blast. "If there was Dark magic, it's gone now." She taps the ring back to the plain silver band. The family motto hanging between them, a grenade with the pin out and then carefully replaced.

30. Granger asks to keep the ring, analyze it further, have Bill Weasley look. "When you've quite settled any doubts, owl me, and we can get on with our lives." She perks up at "get on with her life without a Draco-shaped barnacle."

31. The first kindness. Students knock — excited about a new development. Granger dons her white coat to join them. Conflicted look. Draco raises an eyebrow, offering nothing. She pushes through: "I suppose I want to say thank you. For working through this as you are. I haven't exactly been pulling my weight." Draco: "I think you're more than pulling your weight elsewhere." — the first genuine kindness either has given the other. Brief. Unannotated. He leaves.
