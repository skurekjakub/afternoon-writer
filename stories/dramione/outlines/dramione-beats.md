# Chapter 1: The Mortifying Ordeal (Auror Office / Cambridge)

**POV:** Draco
**Continuity:** Fifteen years post-war. Draco is a top Auror. Granger is a researcher at Cambridge. They haven't spoken since school. No existing relationship — all history is hostility and war baggage.
**Setting:** Auror Office (London, greying cubicles, Monday morning); Trinity College Cambridge (magical concealment, King's Hall, sixteenth-century Gothic, over-carved wood, candlelight); Granger's laboratory (Muggle-sterile, white coats, glowing computers, contrast with the Gothic); Minister's office (brief, fire-lit).
**Notes:** NO ROMANCE. All professional friction, competence display, and mutual assessment. The attraction is invisible to both characters — buried under fifteen years of antagonism. It surfaces only through the narrator's involuntary cataloguing: Draco notices her hair, her mouth, her knees, her smirk. He doesn't register that he's noticing. The reader does. The chapter's engine is two hyper-competent people forced into a room together, neither willing to cede ground. Ends on a cliffhanger that shifts the power dynamic.

---

### Beats

**SCENE 1: The Assignment (Auror Office, Monday morning)**

1. Establishing Draco — background narrated in his own voice. Post-war acquittal, Auror career chosen strategically ("just enough heroics for positive coverage... just enough Ministry-sanctioned murders to keep him interested"). The narrator IS the character — every aside belongs to his register.

2. Tonks distributing monthly assignments. The office parade: hag coven murders, necromancy, Yorkshire vampires, Transmogrifian Torture. Each case dropped on a desk. Draco watching from his cubicle. The competence-gets-punished setup: Tonks reaches him last because he handled the Lanark Lunatic.

3. Two choices: inappropriate acts with trolls, or a Ministerial protection assignment. Draco opens the troll folder. A strangled sound of disgust. Folder closed. "I'll take the protection assignment." Fernsby inherits the horror.

4. Draco reads the protection file. Shacklebolt wants everything — security audit, warding, escorting, surveillance. The works. Draco is preemptively irritated: this sounds like effort. He flips to the Principal's page.

5. Hermione. Bloody. Granger. The photograph blinks at him once, then leaves the frame.

6. Draco attempts refusal. Tonks' office — "We have a rather unhappy history." Tonks offers the swap: Fernsby and the troll-buggerer. The Foe Glass silhouettes lean in to eavesdrop. Tonks' eyes turn hawkish yellow. "I won't have your complex about Granger domino its way through the entirety of it." Door closes, squeezing him out.

7. The capitulation. Draco strides back. Fernsby's cubicle: a gurgle of horror emanating from within. Swap abandoned. "Fine. He'd do the Granger thing. It was, at any rate, not troll pornography."

**SCENE 2: The Correspondence**

8. Cold note exchange. Draco: professionally pleasant, requests a meeting. Granger: equally cold, the Minister is overreacting, disregard the request. Draco enjoys an afternoon off instead of telling Tonks.

9. Granger writes again — the Minister hasn't budged. Would Draco be available Thursday? Nine o'clock. The Granger Laboratory. Trinity College, Cambridge. Draco tosses the letter into the fire. "Cambridge, of course. How could we expect anything less from Hermione Granger?"

**SCENE 3: Cambridge (Thursday morning)**

10. Arrival at Trinity College. The porter doesn't glance twice at robes — academics wear gowns. But he bristles at "Granger." Correction #1: "Doctor Granger." Draco waved in. The quad that isn't a quad ("we call them courts at Cambridge").

11. Entering the wizarding concealment — the plaque for King's Hall, the wand-tap, the medieval building materializing. Draco gives Granger 2/10 on his security assessment. At least Muggles can't find her.

12. King's Hall interior. Scholarly witches and wizards, scholarly bustle, the sixteenth-century ambience — dark, over-carved wood, candlelight. Draco navigating five staircases, lost. He asks a spotty youth for directions. Correction #2: "Professor Granger."

13. Third-floor corridor. Offices, lounges, a small zoo. The door: "GRANGER. Ring for attention." No overzealous titles. He rings.

14. A Being answers — white coat, translucent face-coverings, goggles. "Healer Granger doesn't take walk-ins." Correction #3. Security assessment drops to 1/10.

15. The Awkward Moment. Draco enters Granger's office. They stare. Fifteen years of war, school, antagonism compressed into the distance between a desk and a chair. "Malfoy." "Granger." Equal eloquence.

16. Mutual assessment. She catalogs him: hair, face, Auror insignia, robes, boots. He catalogs her: the curling pile of hair coiled at her crown, the thinner face, the white lab coat, black jeans (so Muggle), casual trainers. Neither is subtle about it.

17. "This is an absolute waste of Auror resources." No preamble, no pleasantries. Typey for Granger. Draco settles into the chair. "Give me a bit more to go on and I can make a case to Shacklebolt to withdraw." He'd rather not be here either.

18. Granger's partial disclosure. A research project — not under Ministry purview, not Ministry-funded. She shared good news with Shacklebolt as a friend; he overreacted. The project has "implications for a certain segment of the population." She won't say which segment or what implications.

19. Draco probes: isn't it possible Shacklebolt is right? The deflection — "If I told you I'd solved world famine, would you pause to worry about a few naysayers?" Draco: "One naysayer would be enough to dispatch a do-gooding researcher, especially one who keeps her laboratory secured with a third-rate locking charm and some chicken wire." Her knee bounces. Cat-tail twitch.

20. The deal. Draco proposes a penetration test. If she satisfies it, they can convince Shacklebolt she's safe. The challenge is issued with a minimum of arrogance. Granger's eyes harden: challenge recognized. "When will this penetration test take place?" "I'm not giving you a warning." "I do love surprises." They do not shake hands. She does not see him out.

**SCENE 4: The Minister**

21. Draco visits Shacklebolt later that week. The Minister is equally cagey — won't say what the project is. Confirms its importance: "She could change the lives of hundreds — thousands — for the better." Draco pushes: did she make you take a Vow of Secrecy? Shacklebolt raises his hands, not responding. Answer given.

22. The backhanded compliment: "I know you won't hesitate to get nasty, Malfoy, if any malicious individuals were to make a move against her." Draco accepts with a mock bow. Then Floo powder into the fire. "Cambridge." He's given her long enough.

**SCENE 5: The Penetration Test (Monday evening)**

23. Late Monday. King's Hall is quiet. Draco stands at the laboratory door. Before he casts a single spell, Granger rounds the corner — dishevelled, out of breath, Healer robes replacing the Muggle clothing. Her timely arrival is filed away: too clever for coincidence, but he detected nothing that should have alerted her.

24. She Transfigures a hairpin into a glossy wooden chair and perches to watch. Draco doesn't mind an audience — especially one he's about to systematically dismantle.

25. The door wards. Layer by layer: Caterwauling Charm ("Insulting"), basic intruder alarms, Salvio Hexia, Foribus Ignis, Custos Portae, a hair-trigger Confundus, a Blinding Hex ("decidedly mean"), a Balding Jinx ("unsportsmanlike"), and a concealed Confringo on the handle itself. Draco disarms the Confringo — touch and go, he breaks a sweat. Tells himself at least there's a Healer nearby if his face is blown off.

26. Four minutes. The door unlocks. Granger looks unimpressed. Draco swings it open — a stone wall. He's been dismantling an impeccable decoy. The real door is a metre down the corridor, behind a Finite Incantatem her undergrad staff can handle. "I needed my staff able to get in." His neck stiffens.

27. Laboratory interior. Sterile white, steel surfaces. Draco searches methodically: shelves, cupboards, organized Muggle literature. Words without meaning jump out — "cytokines, monoclonal antibodies, chimeric antigen receptors, T-cells." He waves his wand at everything. No magical traces, no hidden caches. Nothing useful unless you hold twelve doctorates.

28. "Gamma delta T cells. Antigens: MART-1, Tyrosinase, GP100, Survivin. All of magical provenance — but not otherwise noteworthy." Draco: "I see." Draco does not see at all. The false modesty: by "very few people" she means no one but herself.

29. The computers. The most Muggle corner of the laboratory — glowing boxes of light that Draco's instincts nearly skipped. She might as well have cast a Notice-Me-Not; his own pureblood habits did the work for her. He walks toward them. For the first time, Granger perks up — he's getting somewhere.

30. "Computers," says Draco, pulling up a distant memory from Muggle Studies. "Well done," says Granger, with the tone one takes to praise an especially slow child who has correctly identified a barn animal. He hasn't the faintest sodding clue where to go from here. "I'd need to bring in a Muggle-born." She suggests a "hacker." The violent term offers no self-explanation.

31. "What's to stop me from destroying the lot?" Granger shrugs. "It's all in the cloud." "The cloud." "Yes." He's stymied by Muggle infrastructure. The wards were an amusing puzzle. She knew there was nothing behind them. She wanted to see if he was as good as they say.

32. The pivot. "So no evidence of concealment, no written findings, computers, clouds... If I'm a baddie who needs information, what do I do next?" Granger looks at him inquiringly. "I go after you." He raises his wand. The spell hits her in the chest.
