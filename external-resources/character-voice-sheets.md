# Character Voice Dynamics: Tifa & Aerith

*How they interact. Read after the individual voice sheets (`voice-tifa.md`, `voice-aerith.md`).*

## The Dynamic: How They Talk to Each Other

The Draco/Hermione engine runs on **competence collision** — two people who are used to being the best at what they do, forced into a space where the other's expertise is undeniable. The friction is the draw.

### Mapped to Tifa/Aerith:

**What Tifa sees in Aerith:** Someone who moves through the world without armor. Physically open, throat exposed, no guard stance. This should read as weakness — it doesn't. Tifa can't figure out why. The disconnect between Aerith's apparent vulnerability and her actual capability is the hook.

**What Aerith sees in Tifa:** Someone who catalogs every threat in a room before she sits down. Wound tight, efficient, holding something back. Aerith recognizes the holding-back — she does it too, just differently. The shared concealment is the hook.

### Dialogue dynamics:

1. **Tifa leads with short, blunt statements. Aerith matches or redirects.**
   - Tifa: "You're not going alone."
   - Aerith: "I wasn't aware I'd asked for company."

2. **Aerith says something precise. Tifa responds physically (action beat, not words).**
   - Aerith: "The root system is compromised. This whole section will die within a week."
   - Tifa's jaw worked. She crouched beside the plant and pressed her thumb into the soil, like she could hold it together by force.

3. **When one drops a vulnerability, the other deflects for them.**
   - Aerith: "Sometimes I hear them. The flowers. Even when—" She stopped.
   - Tifa: "Bar's still open. I've got that whiskey you liked."

4. **Verbal sparring escalates through precision, not volume.**
   - Aerith: "You could try asking instead of deciding."
   - Tifa: "I could. I won't."
   - Aerith: "At least you're honest about it."
   - Tifa: "You say that like it's a compliment."
   - Aerith: "It wasn't."

5. **The grudging concession (the Hermione "I suppose I should thank you" move):**
   - Tifa: "...The tea thing. Earlier. That was — fine."
   - Aerith, a beat too late: "Fine?"
   - Tifa: "Good. It was good. Stop looking at me like that."

### What the dynamic is NOT:

- NOT bickering for its own sake. Every exchange has an undercurrent: attraction, territorial instinct, the desire to understand what the other is hiding.
- NOT one-sided expertise. Tifa knows things Aerith doesn't (combat, threat assessment, the underworld). Aerith knows things Tifa doesn't (magic, the planet, what grows in the dark). Neither defers to the other.
- NOT soft. Both characters have edges. Tifa's edges are visible (fists, blunt speech, the way she plants herself in a doorway). Aerith's edges are concealed (the smile that hardens, the redirect that feels like a wall, the silence that says more than the words did).

## Usage Notes for Writing Agents

1. **Before writing any scene with these characters**: Read this sheet. Internalize the voice patterns. Then write.
2. **The test for Tifa voice**: Would a fighter say this? Is this observation grounded in physical reality? If the sentence could belong to any character, it's wrong.
3. **The test for Aerith voice**: Is this precise enough? Does it conceal more than it reveals? If Aerith is being fully transparent, something's wrong — she always holds something back.
4. **Dialogue ratio target**: Scenes with both characters should be 50%+ dialogue. Their dynamic is VERBAL. Narration exists to ground the dialogue in bodies and space, not to replace it.
5. **Action beats, not tags**: "said" is invisible. Everything else is an action beat. Tifa cracks knuckles, rolls shoulders, adjusts gloves. Aerith touches plants, braids things, goes still. These beats replace dialogue tags entirely.
6. **Subtext rule**: If a character says exactly what they mean, you've made a mistake. Everything is filtered through competence, pride, or self-protection. The reader should always sense a gap between the words and the feeling.
