# Lordaeron's Palace

## Overview
Lordaeron's Palace sits in Royal palace complex inside Capital City, Lordaeron. and is current through chapter22.

## Sensory profile
Sight: White stone, narrow service arch shadow, hidden lock plate, and kitchen-side passages worn dark by ordinary labor beneath ceremonial power. Sound: Service-passage torch crackle, distant servant laughter, and the muffled hush of palace routine continuing over the blade’s wrongness. Smell: Cold stone, lye from the kitchens, and grease worn into service floors by years of unnoticed carts. Touch: Stone colder than morning warrants, as if Frostmourne’s presence is reaching through subfloor and wall rather than staying politely in one room. Atmosphere: The palace feels invaded before the protagonists ever see Arthas. Its service margins are still intact enough to enter, but the cold has already moved into the building’s bones.

## Story function
The outer wards at the palace are still the old peacetime lattice, which lets Antonidas and two Kirin Tor magi soften one service lock rather than fight a war at the gate. The blade’s wrongness can be felt through palace stone and even into the subfloor near the service arch.

## What happens here
Antonidas briefs the anti-blade plan at the eastern service arch, the Kirin Tor pair soften the lock, and Jaina and Sylvanas pass through the service door into the palace interior.

## People tied to this location
Antonidas, Cyndia, Jaina Proudmoore, Sylvanas Windrunner

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 expands Lordaeron’s Palace from covert entry problem into full catastrophe architecture. In chapter 22 the important fact was that its old peacetime service lattice could still be worked. In chapter 23 the important fact is what lies at the far end of that lattice: a throne wing where secrecy, monarchy, murder, and cursed magic all collapse into one visible room.

The palace remains a place of margins first. Service dark, hidden lock plates, and kitchen-side stone still matter because they are what let Jaina and Sylvanas reach the chamber in time to matter at all. But once the chamber opens, the palace stops behaving like operational terrain and starts behaving like evidence. White marble, broken columns, blood, crown, corpse, and blade all become part of the same story the room seems to tell to anyone who arrives late.

Future chapters should remember the palace not just as the site of Arthas’s fall, but as the machine that converts a partial rescue into a public accusation. It shelters the approach, magnifies Frostmourne’s cold through its bones, and then traps the victors inside the appearances of regicide the moment witnesses arrive.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 extends that trap deeper into the building. The palace is no longer only throne room and service passage. It becomes tribunal chamber, prison corridor, and evidence vault all at once. The important continuity fact is that these spaces are not magically separate. Frostmourne’s cold and intent leak through the stone from above the cells, so the palace continues acting like one contaminated body even after the court pretends the danger has been neatly contained.
