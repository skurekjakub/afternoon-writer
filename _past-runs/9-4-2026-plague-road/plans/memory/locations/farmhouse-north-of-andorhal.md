# Farmhouse north of Andorhal

## Core read

This farmhouse is important because it is recognizably ordinary. That is what makes it dangerous. The cult has not moved the work into some theatrical necromancer stronghold. It has written it into a kitchen, a threshold, a well, and a yard built for grain country routine.

## What happened here

Jaina gets the chapter's crucial partial answer in this room: the body is part of the lock, and the plague has been engineered to survive storage and baking. The place is also bait. The same stillness that makes the read possible makes the team vulnerable enough for the ambush to land.

## Carry-forward use

Future scenes should remember this site as the place where mechanism and cost were tied together: useful knowledge, a survivable wound, and the proof that fresh plague work can hide inside material that still looks like somebody's home.
