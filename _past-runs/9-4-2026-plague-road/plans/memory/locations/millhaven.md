# Millhaven

## Chapter 2-3 state

Chapter 2 introduced Millhaven as a seen-but-not-entered horror. Chapter 3 opens the place up and confirms that the silence was only the outer skin of the disaster. What makes Millhaven memorable is how intact it remains. No burned shell. No obvious massacre. A table still set for two. Dried stew in a pot. Shirts left on the line. A child's hoop by the ditch. The village looks less destroyed than interrupted, which is far worse. Life stopped here in the middle of itself.

The sensory profile should stay sharp for future use. Water still runs under the little bridge. Hooks knock in the wind. Loose boards shift. There is no animal answer, no dog, no goat, no kitchen noise, no human correction of any of those small sounds. The granary adds another layer: trapped dry heat, grain dust, burlap, mouse piss, and the wrong sweetness underneath everything. Behind the houses the earth itself tells the rest of the story — drag marks, heel gouges, churned ground, many barefoot exits, movement with no order until it found one pull and followed it north.

Millhaven is therefore not just the first fully claimed village. It is the first place where domestic detail, witness testimony, spoor, and magical saturation all become one fact. Mira survives here. The dead leave from here. The warning north begins here. For continuity purposes, remember Millhaven as the place where the plague stops being ominous spread and becomes a proven village-scale catastrophe.
