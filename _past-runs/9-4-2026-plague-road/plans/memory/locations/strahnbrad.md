# Strahnbrad

## Chapter 6 position
Strahnbrad enters the story as a war camp before it enters as a town. The prose gives mud, wet banners, horse lines, forge smoke, wounded men, and a map board ringed by polished plate. It is a place saturated by visible urgency, the sort of command environment where every detail pushes attention toward the threat already drawing blood. That is exactly why Jaina's plague warning lands so badly here.

The location matters because it clarifies Arthas's refusal without reducing him to simple cruelty. Strahnbrad is full of real pressure: Blackrock raids, troop movement, civilians in immediate danger, and Silver Hand presence answering the crisis in front of them. In story terms, it is the place where hierarchy, battlefield logic, and the invisible war in the grain collide.

## What chapter 6 establishes
Strahnbrad should be remembered as the military corridor the south feeder road was always going to cross, not a random stop. It is where Arthas hears the warning and chooses the visible war first. Future continuity should preserve both truths at once: Strahnbrad is genuinely urgent, and that genuine urgency is what makes the refusal so dangerous.
