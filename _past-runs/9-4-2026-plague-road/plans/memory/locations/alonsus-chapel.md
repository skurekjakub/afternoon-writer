# Alonsus Chapel

## Overview
Alonsus Chapel sits in Stratholme, near the central civic square and the routes feeding Market Row.. In chapter17, the location is defined by a holy landmark trapped inside civic desecration.

## Sensory profile
- Sight: Seen mostly at distance through smoke and square geometry, as a civic holy center still anchoring the city while soldiers and runners knot around it.
- Sound: Its bells carry across the opening of the Culling, trying to impose hour and order on a city already sliding into slaughter.
- Smell: Smoke, city ash, and the same bread-and-burning mixture that reaches the wall from the streets below.
- Touch: Felt indirectly through the tolling that reaches the witnesses before the chapel itself does.
- Atmosphere: A holy landmark trapped inside civic desecration.

## Established facts
- Alonsus Chapel is one of Stratholme’s major holy landmarks and the traditional birthplace of the Silver Hand. (chapter17)
- Its bells are still sounding when Arthas announces the Culling at the gate. (chapter17)
- The square around the chapel becomes one of the visible knots of reserve, runners, and controlled troop movement during the purge. (chapter17)

## Chapter 17 movement
- The bells of Alonsus Chapel ring over Arthas’s order until the city’s silence finally swallows them. (chapter17)

## Chapter 18 update
In chapter 18 the chapel matters through absence. Jaina no longer hears the bells. That missing sound becomes part of how the city is measured from the wall and then from the road. Alonsus Chapel remains a holy center in continuity terms, but the chapter's real use of it is to show how completely ordinary civic rhythm has been cut out of Stratholme.
