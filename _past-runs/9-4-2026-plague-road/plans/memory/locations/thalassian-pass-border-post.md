# Thalassian Pass Border Post

## Chapter 1-2 state

The border post begins as a pressure chamber and Chapter 2 shows how quickly it can become a launch point. It is still stone, ward-light, leather, control — a place built to contain a problem long enough for command to classify it — but now the memory of the place includes release as well as detention. Jaina's raw explanations, the confiscated papers, the ward-circle pressing on her magic, and Sylvanas's interrogation all happen inside a room whose whole architecture is designed to keep authority physically close.

What Chapter 2 adds is the mechanical reality of that authority. The post does not just hold prisoners; it can damp magic, release it again, assemble horses, and turn a border incident into a field operation in minutes. Cyndia works the anchor sequence. The ward pressure drops. The stable detail moves. The whole outpost changes shape from sealed chamber to armed hinge.

For continuity, this location should keep both halves of that identity. It is a place of blue-lit military containment, yes, but it is also the point where Jaina stops being a detained anomaly and becomes an escorted guide. The border post is where custody becomes motion.
