- Sight: The southern main gate choked with soldiers and horses, flour-white service lanes, smoke lifting in separate columns, torch lines cutting the street grid, and a city reduced from civic space to ordered districts under fire.
- Sound: Alonsus Chapel bells still trying to mark an ordinary hour, shouted orders clipped short under the gate, doors being broken in rhythm, screams spreading and then thinning, and finally the quiet of burning timber after human voices are worked out of the streets.
- Smell: Bread, wet burlap, horse piss, rank tallow, smoke, and the sweet-sour wrongness of the plague underneath every working urban smell.
- Touch: Crushed sacks in mud, rough service-stair stone, soot on the parapet, and city air hot enough to carry sparks up to the wall.

## Established facts
- Stratholme's southern main gate functions as both ceremonial threshold and public command stage, which is why Arthas's order lands as theatre as well as policy. (chapter17)
- The goods-side service lane can still carry civilians and scouts to an inner-wall stair above Market Row even after the main gate breaks into panic. (chapter17)
- Market Row is the city’s broad commercial spine and the first district Arthas assigns to Falric’s companies. (chapter17)
- From the inner wall, the city reads as separable districts with separate smoke columns and rotation lines rather than one uncontrolled riot. (chapter17)
- Alonsus Chapel and the square around it remain audible and tactically central during the opening of the Culling, with bells sounding until the city’s silence overtakes them. (chapter17)
- Arthas returns beneath the same gate on foot after the purge, washed clean enough to make the city look more stained by contrast. (chapter17)

## Chapter 18 update
Chapter 18 turns Stratholme into aftermath without letting it become abstraction. The riders leave by the south road, but the city does not release them cleanly. Smoke stays visible behind them as separate columns collapsing into one bruise on the horizon. More importantly, the city keeps teaching. Jaina's revised plague model comes from realizing that Stratholme's problem is deeper than contaminated grain alone: the bread-signature can thin, yet the answering pattern remains.

## Chapter 19 update
Chapter 19 proves that Stratholme remains a location even in absence. The city is no longer entered, climbed, or fought through, but its smoke still sets the sky over the south-road camp and keeps the chapter from pretending anyone has reached safety.

The northward decision is made under that sky. Stratholme therefore shifts from catastrophe-site to horizon witness: the burn behind them that strips away every excuse to keep working south as if more local proof would change the shape of the threat.
