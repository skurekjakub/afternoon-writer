# Eastweald

## Chapter 15 profile
In chapter 15 Eastweald stops reading as ominously occupied countryside and starts reading as a road already under civilian load. The child’s shoe in the mud does more work than any speech could. So do the loaded carts heading north, the broken wheel carried by hand, the fruit nobody had time to pick. This is still functioning country, but only in the way a body can still walk while already bleeding.

## What to remember
The crucial continuity point is that Eastweald remains materially ordinary right up to the edge of open disaster. That is why the plague is so dangerous here. Refugees, orchards, and trade routes are not aftermath; they are still-active systems feeding the next city.
