# Imperial Chamber

## Overview
The Imperial Chamber enters the memory ledger in chapter 23 as the place where private corruption becomes public history. It is the throne room of Lordaeron, but the chapter strips away any comforting sense of ceremonial distance. By the end of the scene it is battlefield, murder site, dynastic symbol, and legal trap all at once.

## Spatial character
The room matters because it is big enough to fight in and visible enough to condemn in. The open marble around the dais gives Sylvanas just enough room to draw Arthas, recover angles, and buy Jaina the slivers of time needed to work on Frostmourne. The dais concentrates power, which means every fall, body, and object placement reads like meaning. Terenas dies on the steps beneath the throne. Arthas dies there too. Frostmourne remains on the floor in plain view. Nothing in the room can be hidden once witnesses enter.

## Atmosphere and aftermath
Future chapters should remember the chamber as a place where victory immediately turns into accusation. The sword keeps singing even after Arthas is down. The marble keeps the cold. The first responders see corpses and intruders before they hear explanation. Uther’s command is the only thing that stops the chamber from becoming an even worse disaster in the same hour.
