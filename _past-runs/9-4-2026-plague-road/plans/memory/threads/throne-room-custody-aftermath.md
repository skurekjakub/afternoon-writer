# Throne-room custody aftermath

## Overview
Chapter 23 opens this thread by making victory look indistinguishable from guilt to anyone who arrives late. Terenas is dead. Arthas is dead. Jaina and Sylvanas are alive, armed, and standing in the wreckage. The room tells the wrong story first, and the story has enough visible evidence to be dangerous.

## What chapter 23 establishes
The crucial fact is that Uther does not solve the problem by personal authority alone. He believes enough to prevent immediate catastrophe and foolishness, but he still takes both women into custody. That means the aftermath moves into a space of testimony, legal framing, and political pressure rather than straightforward gratitude.

## Why it matters going forward
Future chapters should treat this as a live thread of institutional danger. The protagonists have already won the impossible fight. They may still lose the meaning of it.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 answers the first half of the question badly: the survivors are not believed enough. Custody hardens into tribunal, conviction, and imprisonment. The thread stays open because outside corroboration is finally beginning to gather, and the legal answer may not survive contact with the blade's worsening reality.
