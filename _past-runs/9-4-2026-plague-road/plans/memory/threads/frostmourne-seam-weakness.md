# Can the seam in Frostmourne’s field be turned into a usable weakness?

## Status
advanced

## What the reader knows
The seam is no longer just a promising irregularity. Chapter22 makes it into the story’s first actionable anti-Frostmourne plan, though one that demands lethal proximity and perfect timing.

## What the POV team knows
Jaina knows the weakness now has sequence and choreography; Sylvanas knows exactly what her own body will have to do to make the spell attempt possible.

## Latest evidence
The seam is not yet usable in combat, but Jaina keeps working it physically and mentally during the retreat, treating it as the one thing worth carrying out of the loss. On the march south, Jaina turns the seam from a felt hitch into a three-school framework: frost to force the turn, arcane to hold the seam open, then fire through the exposed gap. At Capital City the framework gains a live bodily condition as well: Sylvanas must buy Jaina three to five still seconds at sword range for the sequence to work at all.

## Notes
Advanced in chapter22 from technical clue to executable kill-room framework. The open question is no longer whether the seam is real, but whether the pair can survive long enough to exploit it.

## Chapter 23 resolution
Chapter 23 resolves the seam thread cleanly at the technical level. Jaina’s reading from the cave was correct. The crooked turn in Frostmourne’s field can be made usable. Frost can force the pattern. Arcane pressure can hold it open. Fire can drive through the breach. Once that happens, Arthas becomes mortal enough for the final shot to matter.

The resolution is important because it separates two questions the story had previously carried together. The first question was whether the seam was real. Chapter 23 answers yes. The second question was whether exploiting that seam would solve Frostmourne. Chapter 23 answers no.

Future chapters should therefore treat the seam problem as solved and the sword problem as newly transformed rather than ended.
