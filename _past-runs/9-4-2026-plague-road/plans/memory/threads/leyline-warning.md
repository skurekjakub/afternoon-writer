# Something is wrong in the leylines and the ward-stones can feel it

## Chapter 1-2 state

Chapter 1 plants the warning. Chapter 2 turns it into proof. By the end of the interrogation and ride south, nobody serious can treat Jaina's claim as abstract mage panic anymore. The wrongness is necromantic, the ward lattice is carrying it, the open land is carrying it, and infected animals are carrying it in their flesh.

What the thread still withholds is source. That matters. The chapter gives scale and mechanism one layer at a time — lines, then ground, then water, then bodies — but it does not yet answer who built this or how it first entered the countryside. So the thread has advanced dramatically without closing. It has moved from plausible anomaly to confirmed regional disaster.
