# Active Frostmourne aftermath

## Overview
This thread begins the instant the throne room should have become simple and refuses to do so. Arthas is down. The king is already dead. The protagonists are still standing. None of that solves the sword. Frostmourne remains active on the floor, which means the chapter’s apparent ending point is really the start of a new and worse custody problem.

## What chapter 23 establishes
The important continuity fact is that damaging Frostmourne is not the same thing as neutralizing it. Jaina’s method works against the bond to Arthas. It does not make the blade safe. The room itself recognizes this before the law does: Jaina fears the sword more than the chains, and Uther’s first serious command is that no one touch it.

## Why it matters going forward
Future chapters should treat Frostmourne as an active aftermath object, not a relic. Whoever controls the room, the testimony, or the prisoners still has to answer the question of what to do with the blade itself.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 escalates the thread from unresolved residue to active countdown. Jaina can feel the blade through the vault and into the cells below, and her estimate collapses the available time to days. That matters because it turns containment from a difficult future problem into the most urgent present one.
