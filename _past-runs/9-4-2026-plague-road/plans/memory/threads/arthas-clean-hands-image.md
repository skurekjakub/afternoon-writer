# What does Arthas’s clean-hands image mean for the chapters after Stratholme?

Status: **advanced**

## Reader knows
Stratholme hands the next chapter one unforgettable visual wound, and chapter 18 proves it stays active: Arthas has time to cleanse himself before returning to view, which makes the atrocity feel not only committed but absorbed.

## POV knows
Jaina and Sylvanas share the same image, but not the same history inside it. Chapter 18 makes clear that the sight is still acting on them, especially on Jaina, even after the city is behind them.

## Chapter 17 evidence
- After the purge, Arthas emerges beneath the gate on foot with his gauntlets off and his hands washed clean to the wrists. (chapter17)
- He looks up at the wall where Jaina and Sylvanas are standing, making the image consciously witnessed rather than accidental. (chapter17)

## Chapter 18 evidence
- The image is part of what freezes Jaina on the wall until Sylvanas gets her moving again. (chapter18)
- The relay that Arthas has sailed north with a chosen force makes the clean-hands image read as prelude to a colder next stage rather than as a sealed ending. (chapter18)

## Notes
Advanced in chapter 18. The image now functions as aftermath pressure and as part of the bridge toward Arthas's Northrend arc.
