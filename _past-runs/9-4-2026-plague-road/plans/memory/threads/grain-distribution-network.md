# How large is the grain distribution network, and who is running it?

Status: **advanced**

## Reader knows
The grain network has reached its terminal urban form, but chapter 18 shows that grain is no longer the whole answer. It delivered the pattern. Something deeper may still be activating it.

## POV knows
Sylvanas and Jaina know exactly where the network was vulnerable, and chapter 18 adds the harder truth that breaking stock does not break the entire design once contaminated bodies are already answering something farther away.

## Chapter 17 evidence
- Live handlers inside the city use whistle signals to coordinate or stiffen undead response, proving the distribution machine still includes active human command elements in the urban phase. (chapter16)
- Jaina can break preserved stock at warehouse level, which confirms the shell mechanism survives road, handling, and storage exactly as designed until deliberately disrupted. (chapter16)
- Stratholme’s service routes, Market Row, and inner districts show the network already fully distributed through a living city before Arthas enters in force. (chapter17)
- The Culling only becomes tactically legible because the grain system has already crossed from stores into kitchens, houses, and bodies. (chapter17)

## Chapter 18 evidence
- Chapter 18 lets Jaina separate shell from trigger: grain looks increasingly like the delivery mechanism rather than the full active source. (chapter18)
- The city's aftermath suggests bodies now retain a deeper pattern that can answer something farther away, implying the network extends beyond distribution into remote activation or relay logic. (chapter18)

## Notes
Advanced in chapter 18 by partially superseding the old model. Grain still matters, but now as one layer in a larger system rather than the final active mechanism.
