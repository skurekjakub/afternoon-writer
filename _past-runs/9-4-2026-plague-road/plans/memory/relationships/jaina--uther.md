# Jaina Proudmoore / Uther the Lightbringer

## Chapter 15 state
This relationship begins on the page in crisis and immediately matters. Uther does not waste time sentimentalizing Jaina or doubting her because of who she is. Once he hears the shape of her answer, he treats it as the one practical door still worth opening.

## Carry-forward
The key continuity note is simple: by chapter 15 Uther is one of the people materially helping Jaina try the smaller answer before Arthas’s larger one reaches Stratholme.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 deepens the Jaina-Uther relationship by putting it under the worst possible room conditions. Uther does not arrive in time to save Terenas, which means he cannot simply clear Jaina by personal witness. What he can do is more limited and still crucial: recognize her as the room’s best source of truth, stop the guards from compounding the disaster, and make sure Frostmourne is treated as the central danger rather than a trophy.

Jaina’s side of the relationship matters just as much. She does not waste time on pleading. She gives Uther the facts. Then she accepts custody from him because he is the only authority present who has any chance of holding both law and danger in the same frame.

Future chapters should remember them as allies under constraint. The trust is real. The exoneration is not yet available.
