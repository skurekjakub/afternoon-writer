# Jaina Proudmoore / Jandice Barov

This is the chapter's most dangerous relationship because it is built on genuine peer recognition. Jandice sees a mind capable of understanding the work. Jaina sees a woman disturbingly close to what she herself might have been with one different moral choice.

Their scenes should always carry two truths at once: attraction to intellectual speed and absolute opposition on what that speed has been used for. Jandice leaks because she wants to be recognized. Jaina keeps leaning in because the answer is finally close.

## Chapter 12 update

Chapter 12 shrinks the relationship to one brief threshold exchange and makes that brevity revealing. Jandice is still proud of Jaina's speed, still eager to report upward that her chosen guest was worthy. But the moment Kel'Thuzad takes command, Jandice is gone and Jaina passes into a different category of danger.

That is the relationship's new truth going forward. Jandice was never the destination. She was the academically seductive access route into a room where Jaina would meet the darker, more complete version of the same temptation.
