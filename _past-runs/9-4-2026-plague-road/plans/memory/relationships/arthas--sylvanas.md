# Arthas Menethil / Sylvanas Windrunner

## Overview
Chapter 21 finally gives Sylvanas direct contact with the version of Arthas the earlier chapters were moving toward.

## Chapter 21 update
What matters is not outrage. It is reclassification. Sylvanas goes into the cave ready to fight Arthas and comes out understanding that Arthas is now only part of the problem. Frostmourne is the larger fact.

That shift deepens the relationship while making it colder. Arthas is no longer a dangerous ally gone rotten or a prince she can judge from outside the room. He is a transformed enemy she has now seen fail ordinary death at close range.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 resolves the Arthas-Sylvanas line in the simplest and hardest way possible: once the blade can be made to fail him, Sylvanas is the one who finishes the room. That matters because chapter 21 already established that she had revised the file. Arthas was no longer just a fallen prince or brutal commander. He was a man carrying Frostmourne’s altered rules. Chapter 23 confirms that she understood the problem correctly.

The fight itself should be remembered as movement and attrition rather than clean duel form. Sylvanas buys time. She bleeds. She rises. She keeps Arthas’s attention moving off Jaina long enough for the seam to open. When it finally does, she does not hesitate. The final arrow is not emotional theater. It is disciplined completion.

Future chapters should remember that Sylvanas is the person who ends Arthas, but not the person who believes that ending him ends the blade. The chamber gives her victory and immediately forces her to stand inside its incompleteness.
