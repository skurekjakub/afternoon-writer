# Aldric Havel / Jaina Proudmoore

## Chapter 8 state
Chapter 8 gives this relationship a useful asymmetry. Jaina is the person who can say what the city means. Havel is the person who can say how to survive the city long enough for that meaning to matter. Neither function replaces the other.

The chapter therefore moves them past first-contact respect into something sturdier. Havel provides the target habits, the service doors, the fallback shelter, and the right grim distinction between a search and a net. Jaina provides the correction that makes all of that local work terrifying on a kingdom scale: the grain has already gone through the gates.

They do not become travel companions. That is the continuity point. Their partnership is strongest when it remains local-and-mobile, city man and field mage each taking the side of the aftermath that belongs to them.
