# Jaina Proudmoore and Sylvanas Windrunner

## Current state
Chapter 22 turns the partnership from defeat-tested trust into explicit interdependence. Jaina now has the only usable anti-Frostmourne framework, Sylvanas names herself as the person who must buy it time, and neither woman wastes energy pretending the answer belongs to only one of them.

## Power dynamic
Sylvanas still governs movement, entry, and bodily risk at sword range. Jaina still governs the magical read and the only possible break sequence. Chapter22 fuses those lanes so tightly that each woman becomes part of the other’s job rather than merely its support.

## Physical grammar
The chapter keeps pairing Jaina’s failing balance with Sylvanas’s near-interventions: a hand ready at Southshore, road spacing calibrated to catch without humiliating, and the final service-door entry taken as a two-body mechanism rather than a commander-plus-caster arrangement. By the palace approach their geometry is brutally explicit. Jaina braces on wall and rail to stay upright; Sylvanas prices every inch of that weakness and then volunteers her own body as the seconds the spell will need.

## Latest movement in the relationship
The return march from Baelgun’s camp turns their shared defeat into a live plan: Jaina derives the three-school sequence and Sylvanas instantly starts translating it into bodies, seconds, and palace-entry reality. At the eastern service arch Sylvanas ends the room’s hesitation by claiming the combat lane herself, which locks the partnership into explicit spell-and-spear interdependence before they enter the palace.

## Trajectory
Hostile containment -> operational pair -> damaged persistence -> chosen recommitment -> sea-crossing trust -> defeat-tested bond -> chapter 22’s explicit interdependence at sword range.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 is where the partnership stops being a plan and becomes fact. Jaina and Sylvanas do not merely coordinate in the throne room. They function as one mechanism with two kinds of cost. Jaina provides the only sequence that can wound Frostmourne at the bond. Sylvanas provides the seconds, the distraction, and the willingness to keep re-entering danger until that sequence can land. The chapter should be remembered as the place where their trust becomes bodily rather than theoretical.

That matters because the outcome is both total and partial. Together they end Arthas. Together they fail to save Terenas. Together they discover that Frostmourne remains active anyway. The relationship therefore advances not into comfort but into a harder kind of union: they now know they can do the impossible together and still inherit only wreckage from it.

The aftermath seals the shift. They go from chamber partners to chained witnesses in the same breath. Future chapters should remember that chapter 23 gives them not only shared triumph under fire, but shared legal and political exposure under the same set of irons.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 proves the bond survives separation. Jaina and Sylvanas are no longer moving in the same room or touching the same fight. They are behind walls, trading truth through stone, and still functioning as one unit the instant the question becomes how much time the blade has left before it finds another bearer.
