# Arthas Menethil / Jaina Proudmoore

## Overview
Chapter 21 puts them back in the same room and uses that proximity to close the last easy lie.

## Chapter 21 update
If earlier chapters let distance carry some residue of possibility, Frostmourne's cavern removes it. Jaina sees Arthas after the blade. She speaks into the room with all the old history still technically present. None of it reaches him in any way that matters.

That does not make the relationship irrelevant. It changes its function. Jaina no longer carries him as a person she might persuade. She carries him as proof of what Frostmourne does, and as the reason the blade must become a solvable problem rather than a fatal revelation.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 gives Jaina and Arthas their last shared room and refuses every easier version of that fact. There is no reconciliation beat waiting in the chamber. There is no speech that reaches the old prince in time. Jaina sees him clearly, but what she sees clearly is the bond between Arthas and Frostmourne, not a buried human self she can call back by force of feeling.

That is the crucial continuity update. Jaina’s final influence over Arthas is not romantic, moral, or persuasive. It is technical. She identifies what must break, holds long enough to break it, and creates the opening that lets Sylvanas finish what otherwise would not stay finished. Arthas dies only once Jaina has redefined the fight.

The emotional cost remains enormous. Terenas dies in front of her. Arthas dies in front of her. But chapter 23 closes the relationship as action rather than question. There is nothing left to save between them except whatever truth Jaina can still preserve about what he became.
