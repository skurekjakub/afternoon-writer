# Arthas Menethil / Terenas Menethil II

## Overview
This relationship enters the ledger only at the moment it ends. Chapter 23 gives Arthas and Terenas one final chamber exchange and makes it immediately clear that whatever family bond once existed has already been overwritten by Frostmourne, ambition, corruption, or all three at once.

## Chapter 23 dynamic
The important continuity fact is how ceremonial the violence is. The throne room should have been the place where inheritance, kingship, and father-son order still meant something. Instead it becomes the place where Arthas uses the language of kinship a heartbeat before destroying the man on the throne. Terenas has title but no practical leverage. Arthas has the blade and all the force that comes with it.

## Why it matters forward
Future chapters should remember Terenas’s death not only as a political event but as the final proof that Arthas has crossed beyond any ordinary filial or dynastic restraint. The murder also shapes the entire evidence profile that condemns Jaina and Sylvanas when they are discovered in the aftermath.
