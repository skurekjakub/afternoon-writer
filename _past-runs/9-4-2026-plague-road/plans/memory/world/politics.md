# Politics

## Overview
Continuity facts for politics through chapter22.

## Latest additions
By chapter22 Arthas has been back in Capital City for four days and the public story still treats him as the prince who ended the northern threat, showing that royal image management has outrun every private warning. Antonidas can reach the palace margins with two Kirin Tor magi but lacks the authority to force the throne-room problem through formal channels. Uther is physically present inside the palace sphere in chapter22 and still blocked by court protocol, confirming that even correct high-ranking opposition now lacks procedural access to the king in time.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 turns every earlier warning about institutional lateness into a full political crisis. Uther does arrive. That matters. But he arrives after the fatal beat, which means he can control the aftermath without being able to certify the beginning cleanly. The room therefore moves immediately into custody, appearances, and succession pressure rather than gratitude.

The death of Terenas and Arthas in the same chamber raises the stakes beyond ordinary blame. The throne room is no longer just evidence of one violent event. It is evidence in the middle of a dynastic break. That is why custody becomes necessary even for the people who actually saved the kingdom from something worse.

Future chapters should remember this as the moment when political truth and factual truth diverge completely in public view.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 proves that divergence can harden into state action. Lordaeron answers the throne-room catastrophe with tribunal, conviction, and imprisonment even while the magical emergency remains unresolved. At the same time, Silvermoon splits its posture between official disavowal and Lor'themar's practical intervention, which means politics in this phase is no longer one clean line of allegiance.
