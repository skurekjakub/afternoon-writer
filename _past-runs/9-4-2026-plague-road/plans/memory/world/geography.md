# Geography

## Overview
Continuity facts for geography through chapter22.

## Latest additions
Chapter22 binds the Northrend return route into one continuous chain: Baelgun’s camp below Muradin’s wall, Dragonblight’s exposed bone-country, Daggercap Bay, Southshore, the King’s Road, and finally Capital City’s palace service arch. Southshore is established as the first practical Lordaeron landfall from Northrend, functioning as immediate transfer point from sea chase to hard overland ride. Capital City’s eastern service approach provides covert palace access distinct from the public celebratory streets and main ceremonial entries.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 adds one crucial geographic layer to Capital City: the path from hidden service architecture into the Imperial Chamber itself. Up to now the palace was a covert-entry problem. Now it is a fully mapped political space. The protagonists can move through margin dark and arrive directly inside the kingdom’s ceremonial center, which means architecture becomes part of both the rescue and the accusation that follows it.

The Imperial Chamber also matters as lived combat geography. It is not a cramped assassination corner. It is large enough for movement, line-of-sight theft, and repeated redirection around the throne dais. That space is what lets Sylvanas buy Jaina the fractions of time the anti-blade sequence requires.

Future chapters should remember that the same building now contains both kinds of truth: service passages where reality can still be acted on, and bright marble rooms where reality can instantly be misread.
