# Military

## Overview
Continuity facts for military through chapter22.

## Latest additions
Baelgun’s camp response shows dwarven casualty doctrine under expedition conditions: immediate triage for the wounded commander, no false promises, and instant dismissal of still-viable fighters back toward the larger battlefield. Chapter22 proves the surviving field line can spend every warning channel available — pigeon, relay logic, ship turn, and personal ride — and still arrive behind the political clock. The final palace approach is a covert small-unit operation rather than a formal military intervention: service arch entry, minimum personnel, no public alarm, and no expectation of institutional reinforcement once inside.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 sharpens the story’s military logic around Frostmourne. The final answer is not a squad, a formation, or a heroic charge. It is an extremely small covert strike in which one fighter buys a few viable seconds for one caster. The chapter therefore confirms that the relevant unit of action against the blade is not an army. It is choreography.

The aftermath matters too. Once the room is won, command competence shifts instantly from combat to containment. Uther’s value is not that he arrives with enough force to solve the chamber. He arrives with enough authority to stop other armed men from mishandling the sword or the witnesses.

Future chapters should remember that chapter 23 turns endgame warfare into a problem of precision, not scale.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 adds a second military lesson: scale matters again once the fight has moved into prison and politics. Lor'themar's arrival with three hundred Farstriders does not undo the verdict, but it does alter the balance of what can happen next by putting disciplined force behind the magical warning.
