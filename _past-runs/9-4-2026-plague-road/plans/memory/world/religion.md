# Religion

Chapter 17 lets religion ring through the horror instead of standing outside it. Sacred architecture, paladin conscience, and public slaughter all occupy the same audible space.

## Key facts
- Priests and paladins at Hearthglen use prayer, holy fire, and the Light as ordinary emergency labor inside triage and corpse handling. (chapter15)
- The chapter establishes a practical devotional metric for survival work: a body cannot lie unburned longer than a prayer and a half without risk. (chapter15)
- Alonsus Chapel and its bells place the purge inside explicitly sacred civic space rather than at a remove from Lordaeron’s religious life. (chapter17)
- Uther’s refusal at Stratholme binds the ethics of the Silver Hand to disobedience rather than obedience when royal command demands atrocity. (chapter17)

## Chapter 18 update
Religion remains present through absence. The stopped bells of Alonsus Chapel become part of how Stratholme's desecration is remembered. The city is not merely burned civic space. It is sacred civic space whose ordinary sound has been cut out.
