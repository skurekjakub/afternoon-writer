# Magic

## Overview
Continuity facts for magic through chapter22.

## Latest additions
The anti-Frostmourne framework only functions if another person can create three to five still seconds at sword range, making bodily choreography part of the spell rather than external support. Jaina’s widened resonance channel cannot simply be turned off after Northrend; the return chapter shows it persisting as tremor, nosebleed, collapse risk, and the constant bread-warm taint even far from the original cave. Old peacetime Kirin Tor and palace warding can still be softened at a service lock by coordinated magi, but that access does not extend to clean authority over the room beyond.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 answers the story’s largest magical question so far with a split result. The anti-blade theory is correct. Jaina can reproduce the three-school sequence under live pressure and force Frostmourne’s seam open in real combat. That alone is a major world fact.

But the chapter is equally clear that the sword problem survives the proof. Arthas dies. Frostmourne does not go inert. Instead the blade remains active on the floor and throws out soul-echo violence as the bond tears. The magic therefore behaves less like a simple enchantment on a weapon and more like an active prison or engine whose user was only one layer of the system.

Future chapters should remember the distinction. The spell is proven. Neutralization is not.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 escalates that magical distinction into a countdown. Frostmourne remains active in vault custody, projects cold and intent through palace stone, and is strong enough that Jaina can feel it from the cells below. The additional important continuity fact is corroboration: other trained mages can now sense the blade too, which means the wrongness is no longer only one exhausted witness's testimony.
