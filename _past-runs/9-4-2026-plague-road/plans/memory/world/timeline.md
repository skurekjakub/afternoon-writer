# Timeline

## Chapter chronology
### chapter1
Single day, from morning patrol in Eversong to the beginning of Jaina's interrogation at the border post.
- Sylvanas kills the last Amani raider during a southeastern patrol and delays her return to the Convocation.
- A Farstrider runner reports a detained human mage inside the ward line, forcing Lor'themar north and Sylvanas south.
- At the Thalassian Pass border post, Cyndia briefs Sylvanas, Jaina Proudmoore's identity is confirmed, and Sylvanas opens the interrogation.

### chapter2
Direct continuation of the same day, from the interrogation at Thalassian Pass through evening arrival above Millhaven.
- Jaina explains the necromantic contamination, admits she crossed without authorization, and Sylvanas releases her only into armed escort.
- The party rides south out of the ward grid, where Jaina confirms the contamination has spread into the land itself and is tightening around Millhaven.
- Infected deer and wolves attack on the road, proving the necromantic pressure has entered living flesh.
- At dusk the riders reach the rise above Millhaven and see the village dark and silent below.

### chapter3
Direct continuation of chapter2 over the remainder of the same evening, from entry into Millhaven through the northbound dispatch and renewed southbound pursuit.
- Sylvanas's party sweeps Millhaven and confirms the village was interrupted rather than burned or cleanly evacuated.
- Mira is rescued from a cellar and confirms that villagers died, rose, and moved north.
- Spoor, witness testimony, and Jaina's read align on the north road, proving a significant northbound movement of dead.
- Neral leaves with Mira and the warning dispatch while Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel continue south.

### chapter4
Two days after Millhaven, over a single day from village arrival through evening departure toward Andorhal.
- The reduced quartet enters a still-living village and Jaina proves the plague is entering bodies through tainted grain and bread.
- Sylvanas matches the medical answer with supplier memory, delivery pattern, and price evidence, then the granary raid exposes Tomas, treated stock, and the first hard Andorhal route marks.
- Tomas dies by concealed poison before he can be turned into a witness.
- Jaina leaves triage and food instructions behind, Sylvanas orders the granary sealed, and the quartet rides southwest toward Andorhal.

### chapter5
Begins immediately after chapter 4's evening departure, runs through an overnight ride, a full day of ledgers and depots, then a late-night route analysis before dawn departure onto the south feeder road.
- The quartet turns inns, weigh houses, depot ledgers, and wagon marks into a regional map of the grain web, letting Jaina's changing contamination read redirect the pursuit in real time.
- At a stripped transfer depot, the party finds a staged overseer death, hidden necromantic residue, hurriedly erased records, and the first on-page Barov paperwork clue inside the Andorhal road system.
- Sylvanas and Jaina choose the south feeder road toward Strahnbrad and Andorhal after night map work confirms it as the live line still carrying both grain and military traffic.

### chapter6
Single day, from morning travel on the south feeder road through the Strahnbrad audience and onward to the same-day first sight of Andorhal.
- Jaina locks the grain-plague pattern into one coherent model while the quartet rides into Strahnbrad's military corridor.
- At Strahnbrad, Arthas hears the warning and still chooses the visible war first, giving only a token quartermaster response.
- The quartet rides on, the repeated farewell resurfaces, and they reach a still-working Andorhal that is carrying the plague in plain sight.

### chapter7
Single day and night inside Andorhal, from covert gate entry through the residue-room solve and into the launch window for a same-night capture.
- The quartet enters Andorhal under inspection cover and adds Corin and Aldric Havel as trusted local allies.
- Jaina and Corin prove the grain is treated during repack while Sylvanas and Havel map the embedded granary-district cell.
- The team merges both answers into a same-night live-capture plan aimed at cutting into the Andorhal machine before it reacts.

### chapter8
Direct continuation of chapter 7 across the same night in Andorhal, from the west-line snatch through the failed interrogation, the city-net escape, the Kel'Thuzad identification, and the sending-stone report to Antonidas.
- The field team captures a live operative on the west line and extracts partial command structure before a remote necromantic strike silences him.
- Jaina and Sylvanas survive a city response that closes around them with roof teams, street teams, and fallback pressure, proving the partnership now works as reflex under pursuit.
- In the shelter, the team proves Andorhal is only the throat of the rollout, identifies Kel'Thuzad as the architect north of the city, and sends the name to Antonidas before turning the hunt toward Eastweald.

### chapter9
Single day from the Eastweald approach through dusk regroup and a night split at the ridge.
- The four-woman unit investigates a fresh farmhouse ritual site north of Andorhal and Jaina salvages partial mechanism data while Sylvanas is wounded in the ambush.
- A larger compound perimeter kills Lyriel and proves the enemy is writing anti-scout wards into apparently clean terrain.
- At dusk Lor'themar intercepts the survivors with Silvermoon and Dalaran pressure, forcing the Barov and Hearthglen lines apart.
- Jaina rides east on the Barov thread while Sylvanas and Cyndia turn south-west toward Hearthglen and Arthas.

### chapter10
Single day into evening, from Jaina's ride through the Barov line to her descent below Scholomance's curated route.
- Jaina reaches the Barov estate, confirms the lead is live, and forces entry under Kirin Tor authority.
- Jandice Barov guides her across Darrowmere to Caer Darrow and through Scholomance's academic surface.
- Jaina reads Kel'Thuzad's logs, identifies resonance as the key mechanism concept, and fails to secure timely institutional extraction through Antonidas.
- Jandice wins permission from the master below, and Jaina descends alone toward the deeper answer.

### chapter11
Single day running parallel to chapter12, from the southbound Eastweald ride through Arthas’s mobilization and into the same-night northbound turn toward Caer Darrow.
- Sylvanas and Cyndia ride south through Eastweald, reading reduced formation, plague-administered villages, and grain-stink roads as proof that the crisis has outgrown ranger scale.
- At Hearthglen, Sylvanas forces access to Arthas, briefs him on the grain network, and watches him turn the warning into immediate mounted mobilization while Uther names the danger in his speed.
- Lor’themar’s dispatch confirms recall pressure and scrubbed evidence, then a Farstrider relay reports that Jaina entered Caer Darrow and did not return, sending Sylvanas and Cyndia north at once.

### chapter12
Runs parallel to chapter11 over a single sanctum conversation, from Jandice's threshold handoff to the instant Jaina recognizes Sylvanas's descent through the wards.
- Jandice leaves Jaina in Kel'Thuzad's inner sanctum and the plague's missing mechanism is completed in theorem language.
- Jaina and Kel'Thuzad shift from resonance theory into ethical, personal, and institutional argument, including Antonidas, preserved selfhood, and the continuity model.
- Kel'Thuzad names the Lich King and the Burning Legion as the larger chain above the plague.
- The sanctum begins receiving Sylvanas's approach through layered ward responses, setting the chapter 13 entry point.

### chapter13
Direct continuation from chapter 12 over one shoreline approach, one covert breach, one short sanctum fight, and the immediate southbound ride toward Stratholme.
- Sylvanas and Cyndia reach Darrowmere, confirm Jaina crossed, and use the east wall plus a side window to breach Caer Darrow covertly.
- Sylvanas captures Jandice in the upper levels, takes the old crypt route to the sanctum, and misreads the room in the fairest wrong way available to her.
- Jaina and Sylvanas contain and end Kel'Thuzad in seconds, then climb back out to Cyndia.
- A relay reports Hearthglen overwhelmed and Arthas moving toward Stratholme, sending the damaged three-woman line south at once.

### chapter14
Direct continuation from chapter 13, spanning late afternoon into deepening dusk on the south road from Darrowmere toward Hearthglen and Stratholme.
- Jaina briefs Sylvanas on the larger war, the plague mechanism, and Kel'Thuzad's expected return while withholding the sanctum's inner truth.
- Lor'themar delivers formal recall from Silvermoon and Antonidas separately asks Jaina to come home; both institutions are refused in favor of the mission.
- A south-track relay reports Hearthglen nearly failing again after dark and confirms Arthas has already driven the main body toward Stratholme.
- Jaina turns the completed mechanism into a narrow city-intervention plan, and the three-rider line drives south harder into the dark.

### chapter15
Single day, from the southbound approach through Eastweald and entry into Hearthglen to the immediate head-start ride toward Stratholme.
- Sylvanas, Jaina, and Cyndia reach Hearthglen through refugee-clogged Eastweald roads and see the town holding only by triage, pyres, and constant interruption of the newly dead.
- Uther gives the field truth, then Arthas receives the full plague picture and refuses to slow his march on Stratholme.
- Jaina names a narrow source-side plan built around granaries, bakehouses, and feed routes, and Sylvanas chooses to ride with that head-start lane instead of the main column.

### chapter16
Single day inside Stratholme and at its gates, from the head-start team's entry through the gate report before the army enters in force.
- Jaina, Sylvanas, and Cyndia penetrate Stratholme's service-side logistics spaces and ruin contaminated stock in multiple warehouses.
- The team learns the city is already too far gone for a clean rescue because bread has moved beyond storage into kitchens, houses, and bodies, while live handlers still coordinate the urban undead response.
- Arthas and Uther reach the main gate, and Jaina tells Arthas plainly that Stratholme cannot be saved cleanly.

### chapter17
One compressed stretch from the gate confrontation through the first visible hours of the purge.
- Arthas hears Jaina confirm that it is too late to save Stratholme cleanly and orders the Culling.
- Uther refuses publicly and withdraws his people; Jaina refuses as well, severing herself from Arthas in plain view.
- Sylvanas, Jaina, and Cyndia use a service lane to reach the inner wall and witness the purge take hold below them.
- Arthas reappears beneath the wall with his hands washed clean, giving the chapter its final image.

### chapter18
Immediate aftermath from the wall descent into the southbound road out of Stratholme.
- Jaina freezes in the wall aftermath until Sylvanas touches her shoulder and gets the group moving south.
- Below the wall, Cyndia has horses, water, and tack ready, letting the line reform without delay.
- On the road, Jaina revises the plague model: grain is the shell, contaminated bodies hold the deeper pattern, and some farther broadcaster or relay may be setting it off.
- Antonidas reaches Jaina by stone and offers extraction, but she refuses not yet.
- A Farstrider relay confirms Arthas is sailing for Northrend with a loyalty-selected force while Uther rides west for Capital City, leaving north as the chapter's live unanswered pressure.

### chapter19
Single day, from the field camp south of Stratholme through Vereesa's recall ride, Jaina's second stone call, and the line's turn north toward the coast.
- Sylvanas and Jaina turn post-Stratholme evidence into a working conclusion: south is aftermath, north is the real pursuit.
- Vereesa arrives with the Convocation's recall and Lor'themar's report that only thin mobilization and harbor relay support remain.
- Sylvanas refuses recall aloud and says the threat to Quel'Thalas is wherever Arthas goes next.
- Jaina places a second call to Antonidas and then closes the Dalaran line herself.
- The formation breaks camp and rides north toward the coast, carrying the pursuit beyond ordinary institutional cover.

### chapter20
Single continuous push from Seastone Harbor through the Wolfheart crossing to landfall at Daggercap Bay in Northrend.
- The pursuit reaches Seastone Harbor, buys passage with Captain Dagny Ironhull, and leaves Lordaeron by sea aboard the Wolfheart.
- Jaina's attempts to probe the source during the crossing draw supernatural headwinds and reveal that the resonance is now bidirectional.
- Sylvanas stops Jaina when magical effort tips into self-destruction, making the partnership's trust operationally explicit.
- The ship reaches Daggercap Bay, where the expanded docks prove Arthas landed weeks earlier and has already driven inland.

### chapter21
Single continuous push from dawn reading at Daggercap Bay through the inland march, Muradin’s briefing, the Frostmourne cave fight, and the wounded retreat back toward camp.
- At Daggercap Bay, daylight over the burned fleet proves Arthas spent ships and time alike to get inland first, while Dagny sets a hard return window.
- Sylvanas, Jaina, and Cyndia cross Dragonblight to Muradin’s camp, where Muradin names Frostmourne and briefs them on Arthas’s route.
- In Frostmourne’s cavern, Jaina identifies the blade as plague-kin, Muradin confronts Arthas, the fight fails cleanly, and Muradin is wounded.
- The retreat carries Muradin out alive while Jaina preserves the chapter’s one usable gain: the seam she heard in Frostmourne’s field.

### chapter22
Single relentless return push over roughly two days: cave aftermath and camp handoff in Northrend, forced march to Daggercap Bay, hard sea return, Southshore landfall, overland race to Capital City, and covert palace entry at dusk.
- Muradin is handed to Baelgun alive enough to keep, and the formation is pushed back toward the coast before rest can slow them.
- On the return through Dragonblight and across the Wolfheart crossing, Jaina turns Frostmourne’s seam into a three-school anti-blade framework while every warning channel is spent southward.
- The party reaches Southshore, rides the King’s Road to Capital City, learns Arthas has already been home for four days, and enters the palace through Antonidas’s service-side lane.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 is short in clock time and enormous in consequence. It covers one continuous palace movement: service entry, chamber breach, the murder arriving one beat before the rescue, the anti-blade sequence under live combat, Arthas’s fall, and the immediate custody aftermath.

Nothing in the chapter meaningfully resets between those beats. This is important for continuity. The same bodies carry the whole sequence. The same room carries the whole sequence. The same witnesses inherit its broken meaning before anyone can build a cleaner narrative around it.

Future chapters should treat chapter 23 as a single unbroken catastrophe-victory interval.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 follows immediately as aftermath compressed into one day: imprisonment below the vault, tribunal proceedings, conviction, and then the late arrival of outside corroboration. The important timing fact is Jaina's estimate that the next meaningful Frostmourne crisis is only days away, which compresses every future chapter decision.
