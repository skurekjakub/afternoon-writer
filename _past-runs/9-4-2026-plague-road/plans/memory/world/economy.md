# Economy

The chapter keeps proving that logistics decide fate first. By the time princes argue at the gate, the grain economy has already done its work in kitchens, carts, and bodies.

## Key facts
- In chapter 15 the decisive economic infrastructure is urban: granaries, bakehouses, warehouse lanes, and bread distribution inside Stratholme. (chapter15)
- Chapter 16 proves the last-mile food economy is the real disaster point: once bread has moved from warehouses into church kitchens, homes, and ordinary hands, the city has effectively consumed the network. (chapter16)
- Warehouse sabotage can still save portions of a city, but not the whole of it once normal household consumption has already begun. (chapter16)
- Grain carters, service routes, kitchens, and market access are all part of the same urban distribution web that makes Stratholme impossible to save cleanly once contamination is widespread. (chapter17)
- A fleeing grain carter can still give useful routing intelligence, showing that commercial movement remains the city’s most legible map even during collapse. (chapter17)
- The Culling only becomes thinkable because the contaminated grain economy has already crossed from warehouses into ordinary household consumption. (chapter17)
