# Ashcombe

## Overview
Prosecutor Ashcombe first appears in chapter24. He is the state's advocate in the tribunal over the throne-room aftermath.

## Presence on the page
Ashcombe reads as a courtroom specialist rather than a field actor. He belongs to papers, sequence, and the confidence of a man whose weapon is narrative framing.

## Voice and behavior
His voice is smooth, prosecutorial, and selective. He turns confusion into implication and damage into evidence of guilt. He matters because he gives the state's simpler story its sharpest language.

## Function in chapter 24
Ashcombe helps convert the aftermath of Arthas's death into a case against Sylvanas and Jaina. He does not disprove the blade's danger; he works around it by making motive, appearance, and suspicion feel easier for the room to carry.

## Current end-of-chapter state
At the end of chapter24, Ashcombe has won the verdict he wanted, but the prose leaves open that the blade's continued activity may outlast the usefulness of his courtroom victory.
