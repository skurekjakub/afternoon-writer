# Vereesa Windrunner

## Overview
Vereesa arrives in chapter 19 as both messenger and wound. The Convocation sends her because a sealed order would land harder in a younger sister's hands than in a magistrix's. The chapter knows that, and so does Sylvanas. What makes Vereesa matter is that she knows it too and rides anyway.

Dust, foam, and stiffness tell the story before she speaks. She has ridden hard enough that the mare is blown and one knee nearly fails on the dismount. Ink stains two fingers. The dispatch tube knocks at her hip. She is carrying chamber work in a ranger's body, and the mismatch hurts.

## How she sounds
Her first line is pure formality: rank, law, consequence. She can deliver the chamber voice cleanly. But the formal register does not hold. It cracks into family plea almost immediately, and that break is the heart of the scene. Vereesa can say "Ranger-General" and mean office; she can say "Lady Sun" and mean childhood, blood, and everything still left to lose.

She is not merely a courier. She argues. She reports what Lor'themar spent. She names the cost in family terms rather than political ones. The plea is not clever and it is not strategic. That is what gives it force.

## What chapter 19 establishes
Vereesa believes there is still a version of home that can be saved if Sylvanas turns back now. She also knows Lor'themar is close to spent. The chapter makes her the person who turns chamber arithmetic into something bodily: one hard ride, one sealed document, one exhausted voice saying come home before it is too late.

When Sylvanas refuses, Vereesa does not collapse or rebel. She folds the recall carefully, asks what answer she should carry, takes the mercy of truth, and rides back with it. That control matters. She is hurt, furious, and still functioning.

## Carry-forward
By the end of the chapter, Vereesa becomes the woman who bears the refusal home. She leaves knowing Sylvanas has chosen north, knowing Jaina is not turning back either, and knowing whatever remains between the sisters now has to survive distance, politics, and the story Silvermoon tells itself afterward.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 hardens Vereesa from family messenger into public witness. She reads the Convocation’s disavowal into the tribunal record, but the important part is what comes after: she stays in the city instead of letting the speech be her exit.

That means the sister bond is damaged but not abandoned. Vereesa is still capable of serving institutional language, yet the chapter leaves her unwilling to let that language be the last word on Sylvanas’s fate.
