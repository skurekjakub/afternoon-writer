# Terenas Menethil II

## Overview
Terenas enters chapter 23 as king, father, and the last living figure on the throne dais. He leaves it as the chapter’s first irreparable cost. The important continuity fact is timing: he is still alive when Jaina and Sylvanas reach the chamber door, which means the rescue is not late in principle, only one beat late in practice. That beat defines the whole room.

## Presence in the throne room
The prose gives him in fragments that matter. He is half-risen before the throne, crown already crooked, one hand clenched on the arm of the seat as if authority itself could still brace him. His voice is heard first through stone, older and thinner than Arthas’s, and then the scene closes the distance only to take speech away from him. When the blade goes in, the king ceases to be a ruling image and becomes a murdered body on white marble.

## Why he matters going forward
Future chapters should remember Terenas less as a speaking presence than as the fixed proof of what the protagonists did and did not save. His death guarantees that the throne room can never become a clean triumph. His body, his blood, and the crown rolling away across the seal are part of the evidence profile that condemns Jaina and Sylvanas even in the same hour they save the kingdom from something worse.
