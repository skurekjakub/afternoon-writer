# Sylvanas Windrunner

## Overview
Also known as Ranger-General, the Ranger-General. First appears in chapter1 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Chapter 20 puts her into shipboard command geometry: salt on her leathers, bow wrapped against spray, one hand on a shroud in the worst footing, and then a crouch under the stern ladder close enough to keep Jaina alive without softening the order. Chapter 21 keeps her in hard field economy under colder conditions: ash in the harbor wind, crouched over bootprints and sled grooves at Daggercap Bay, then later fighting with bow leather gone rigid under Frostmourne's ward and Muradin's harness strap burning a line into her palm on the retreat. Chapter 22 keeps Sylvanas in stripped pursuit and threshold geometry: Muradin’s weight burning through her grip on the descent to camp, then Lordaeron damp sitting wrong on her skin, and finally one hand near enough to catch Jaina at Southshore without stealing the last inch of pride.

## Voice and behavior
Chapter 20 turns her speech into deck-bone care: warnings about locked knees, dry shipboard jokes, and the one-word refusal that stops Jaina faster than the storm does. Chapter 21 pares her voice down to pursuit bone and then extraction bone: Move. Drink. Left shelf. Back. Carry. Count. The notable shift is not softness but reclassification; once Arthas appears with Frostmourne, her language starts treating the blade as the real problem. Her voice in chapter22 is all deployment bone: hard questions about whether Jaina can keep walking, military shorthand in the warning dispatches, and the flat decisive I do when the room stops pretending someone else will hold Arthas in place. Keeps appearing at Jaina's worst footing with a waterskin already in hand, then stands three paces off after the collapse so she can watch both the stern approach and the mage. Chapter 21 turns her body language into pursuit reading and extraction certainty: crouched over old tracks, dropping flat before ice-shard spray, catching Muradin’s harness with one hand, and never once wasting a motion on grief while bodies are still moving. Ranges half a pace ahead reading footing, takes tar-stiff rope in hand without seeming to notice when the plan’s cost becomes personal, and keeps appearing where Jaina’s balance is about to fail without turning the gesture into softness.

## What chapter 22 changes
Accepts Baelgun’s hard dismissal, sends every warning she can from Daggercap Bay and the Wolfheart, and keeps the formation moving south without allowing comfort to slow the return. Builds the throne-room plan around Jaina’s framework in practical field terms and tells Antonidas exactly how much help at the arch is actually useful. Names herself as the combat partner who will buy the spell its seconds and leads the formation through the service door first. Leaves the cave knowing Muradin is alive enough to hand off and Jaina has heard a repeatable seam in Frostmourne’s field worth carrying south. Now knows Arthas has been installed in Capital City for four days, that Northrend men are already on interior palace rotation, and that peacetime gate posture still holds around a corrupted prince. Knows the anti-blade framework can only be attempted if Jaina gets three to five still seconds at sword range, which makes Sylvanas’s own body part of the machinery now.

## Capabilities
Converts magical theory into a workable combat assignment by pricing bodies, timing, and movement instead of admiring the answer at abstract distance. Maintains command authority across camp handoff, sea return, overland race, covert contact, and palace infiltration without any institutional backing. Reads Jaina’s condition closely enough to pace the return around what the mage can still spend without losing the mission outright.

## Arc position
The working lie remains: Clear borders and obvious enemies are the threats a commander can truly solve first The deeper truth pressing against it is: The real answer may lie in who can still alter the road before force reaches the wall, even if that answer comes from compromised knowledge and not from command rank Right now the character stands at: Chapter 22 moves Sylvanas from co-investigator and protector into explicit sacrificial timing partner. The plan stops belonging to an abstract formation and lands in her own body the instant she says I

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 cashes out everything the partnership has been building toward. Sylvanas stops being the woman who says she will buy the spell its seconds and becomes the person who actually does it. She clears the approach, opens the throne room with violence rather than theater, keeps drawing Arthas onto herself every time Jaina needs a fraction more time, and turns the final opening into the shot that finally stays done.

The important continuity fact is how physical the cost becomes. This is not elegant archery at safe distance. It is column fragments, knife work, close passes under Frostmourne, blood on the temple, blood under the ribs, and the repeated decision to get up one more time because the mage still is not finished. Chapter 23 should be remembered as the chapter where Sylvanas makes her own body part of the anti-blade machinery.

The aftermath hardens rather than softens her. She survives, sees at once that the sword remains the room’s real danger, obeys custody only after Jaina signals it, and keeps enough command presence under restraint to unsettle the men touching her. She ends the chapter badly hurt, furious, and fully aware that saving the kingdom has put her in chains inside its palace.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 takes away the one thing Sylvanas trusts most: freedom of action. She is trapped in a cell, bruised, exhausted, and able to do almost nothing except listen through stone, tell the truth when the tribunal twists it, and wait for someone outside the prison to move fast enough to matter.

What matters for continuity is that imprisonment does not make her passive in mind. She inventories the cell, tracks Jaina through the wall, understands almost at once that the verdict is secondary to the blade, and pivots to Lor'themar the instant an external lever appears. The chapter leaves her in the form of helplessness she hates most: perfect comprehension with no room to act except through other people.
