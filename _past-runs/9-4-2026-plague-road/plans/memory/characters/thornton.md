# Thornton

## Overview
Tribunal Chair Thornton first appears in chapter24. He functions as the face of Lordaeron's legal process during the post-throne-room crisis.

## Presence on the page
Thornton is defined less by private physical detail than by courtroom position. He sits above the room in procedural authority and makes the tribunal feel like a machine already decided on what kinds of truth it will allow.

## Voice and behavior
His voice is cold, clipped, and procedural. He speaks in admissibility, order, and rulings. The important continuity point is that he treats urgency about Frostmourne as courtroom disruption rather than as the room's central danger.

## Function in chapter 24
Thornton holds the process together long enough to convert chaos into conviction. He is not written as a cackling villain. He is more dangerous than that: a man whose commitment to institutional order makes him smaller than the catastrophe unfolding around him.

## Current end-of-chapter state
At the end of chapter24, Thornton has preserved the tribunal's authority on paper, but that authority is already threatened by the growing evidence that the blade remains active and the case is not actually over.
