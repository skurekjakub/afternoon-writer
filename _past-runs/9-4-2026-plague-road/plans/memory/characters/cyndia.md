# Cyndia

## Overview
Also known as Ranger Cyndia. First appears in chapter1 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Chapter 20 shifts her from road-flank work to shipboard support and first-landfall readiness: sea-sick but functional, steadying what can be steadied on the Wolfheart and then hitting Northrend ground ready to move as soon as the gangplank drops. Chapter 21 keeps Cyndia on the useful edge of the formation even in Northrend: coming in at a trot with wall-smoke, holding the left and then the rear in the cave, hauling a wounded ranger clear by the harness, and helping drag Muradin home without letting the count break. Chapter 22 keeps Cyndia on the practical edge of every transition: ranging ahead and back through dragon bones to find a path, drifting closer whenever Jaina’s body starts failing, and taking the left side automatically once the road south and the palace approach both narrow.

## Voice and behavior
Chapter 20 keeps her speech spare and task-bent. Even at sea she stays in the register of useful observation and practical response rather than commentary, matching deck danger the way she once matched road danger. Chapter 21 keeps her in pure route-and-survival compression: wall smoke, status answers during the retreat, and no wasted language once the cave turns from assault to extraction. Her speech stays nearly erased into function in chapter22; the chapter uses proximity, route-taking, and silent intervention instead of dialogue to show her read of the danger around Jaina. At sea she contains discomfort instead of displaying it, staying tucked into necessary work and then resetting visibly once solid ground returns under her. Chapter 21 keeps her body language in pure field function: hand drifting to the bow at the gate challenge, cutting sideways to haul a ranger out of shardfall, and answering the final body count through exertion rather than commentary. Keeps Jaina in the corner of her eye like a bad treeline, crouches to wipe blood without ceremony, and repeats the same sweep pattern at the palace arch: door, corner, roofline, Jaina, back again.

## What chapter 22 changes
Finds the route to Baelgun’s camp through Dragonblight’s broken terrain and then quietly folds Jaina into her protected lane on the return march. Wipes Jaina’s blood herself on the Wolfheart and keeps close enough during the crossing and road push to catch the next collapse before it becomes a skull crack or a lost pace. Takes palace positions without being told, widening nearest Jaina once the service arch opens. Learns the sea itself is being pushed against them by the same hostile source pressure Jaina keeps probing, and that Daggercap Bay only proves Arthas is still far ahead rather than near enough to catch. Now knows Muradin’s camp is the last living foothold on the inland line, the cave fight cannot be won straight, and Muradin is alive enough to justify the carry back to surgery. Now knows Jaina’s physical state is deteriorating into a standing tactical variable and that the service-lane entry is likely the last quiet threshold before open palace danger.

## Capabilities
Maintains rear-guard and casualty-extraction discipline inside a collapsing cave fight without letting the retreat turn ragged. Extends ordinary Farstrider flank discipline into ad hoc close protection without losing the larger sweep of route, corner, and roofline. Keeps formation coherence across snow march, shipboard strain, overland sprint, and covert urban entry.

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 uses Cyndia the way the story has increasingly been using her: as the reliable outer edge of whatever impossible work the central pair are about to attempt. She is the hand-signal in the service passage, the ranger who keeps the occupied branch from becoming a surprise, and one more quiet piece of the protective shell around Jaina on the return south and the palace approach.

What matters for continuity is not chamber heroics but disciplined presence. Cyndia does not solve the blade problem. She solves the corridor problem. She keeps the service line readable, keeps the team’s flanks from collapsing while Jaina’s body is failing, and makes the palace approach feel like a real operation rather than two people walking into fate alone.

The chapter therefore advances her from practical support into a clearer form of active protection. Jaina is no longer just the mage in the group. In Cyndia’s spacing and readiness, she is now visibly part of what the ranger is guarding.
