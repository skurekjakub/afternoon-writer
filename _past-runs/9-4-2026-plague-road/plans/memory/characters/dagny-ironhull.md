# Dagny Ironhull

## Overview
Also known as Captain Dagny Ironhull, Captain Ironhull. First appears in chapter20 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Hard-weather harbor captain introduced in chapter20 as a woman built by sea labor and command presence, fully at home on a rough deck in ugly water. Chapter 21 keeps Dagny briefly on the page at Daggercap Bay as a fur-lined harbor captain moving carefully over ice and wreckage, still reading as someone more concerned with survival windows than ceremony. Chapter 22 brings Dagny back as pure deck authority: standing in the middle of the turnabout with both gloves tucked through her belt, reading Jaina in one captain’s glance, and later working the Wolfheart by the inch while every board in the ship shudders around her.

## Voice and behavior
Speaks in practical maritime authority: price, weather, ship handling, and hard limits stated without ornament or deference. Her chapter-21 speech stays in the same hard harbor register: two days, maybe three, break ice if you want the ship to matter when you come back. Her voice gets even harder under urgent return conditions: no blankets, no hammocks, short this time, not a corpse on my deck. She talks like a captain managing a dangerous engine she did not build but fully intends to survive. Reads as pure ship-command competence: planted stance, weather-balanced movement, and the kind of deck certainty that lets others borrow steadiness from her. In chapter21 that confidence shifts from deck to ice: careful footing, no wasted sympathy, and a face already calculating weather and freeze before anyone has finished the conversation. Plants herself at the center of hard deck labor, snaps orders without looking back for obedience, and measures Jaina in one flattening glance whenever the next push risks taking too much.

## What chapter 22 changes
Sets a hard waiting window at Daggercap Bay and makes clear that any return depends on crew help, broken ice, and time not yet spent. Turns the Wolfheart around immediately at Daggercap Bay, spends every usable warning lane before clearing ice, and forbids any version of comfort that would cost them time. Forces food, short-cast pacing, and deck discipline on Jaina throughout the crossing to keep ship-speed from becoming self-immolation. Knows Daggercap Bay and can bring the Wolfheart into it even after the voyage has turned punishing. Knows the bay will close quickly enough that the inland party gets two good days, maybe three with help, before the ship’s waiting cost becomes madness. Learns exactly how much speed can be bought from Jaina before the mage becomes more problem than engine, and adjusts the crossing around that moving limit.

## Capabilities
Reads the bay as a closing logistical window rather than a neutral harbor and sets realistic survival terms for a delayed departure. Can tune a ship’s movement around a mage’s intermittent power without surrendering seamanship to spectacle. Reads exhaustion, rigging strain, and the real margin before death more accurately than anyone else on deck.
