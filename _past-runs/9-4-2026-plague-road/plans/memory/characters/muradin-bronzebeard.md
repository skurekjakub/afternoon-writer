# Muradin Bronzebeard

## Overview
Also known as Muradin. First appears in chapter21 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Stocky dwarf commander cut from his own siege-worn camp: beard rimed with frost, iron rings clicking when he turns his head, and a hammer riding his back with the ease of long practice. Chapter 22 leaves Muradin as a body on the edge between witness and loss: blood thawed and frozen again in his beard, hauled on a hide stretcher through Baelgun’s camp and alive only in the hard practical sense.

## Voice and behavior
Blunt, practical, and warm in the dwarf way. He moves easily between threat, briefing, and rough humor, and cuts magical jargon short with demands like Speak dwarf. His one live verbal beat in chapter22 keeps the chapter-21 pattern intact — Speak dwarf — blunt enough to force plain sense out of magical horror even while he is already fading. Comes out of the shelter already striding hard, reads the newcomers in one sweep, and plants both hands on the table when Jaina finally gives him language for what Frostmourne is. His head rolls once toward Jaina on the stretcher and then the rest of the chapter treats him as weight, blood, and the uncertain work of keeping him alive.

## What chapter 22 changes
Lets Sylvanas’s party into the camp, spends his last useful intelligence on them immediately, and chooses to guide them himself instead of hiding behind the wall he built. Faces Arthas first in the cave and physically commits to the confrontation even after the evidence outside proves they are too late. Forces one last piece of useful translation into the chapter before collapsing fully, demanding plain speech about the sword from Jaina. Knows the cave route, the warning inscription on Frostmourne, and the local terrain well enough to guide a lighter force there quickly. Leaves the chapter having seen Jaina identify Frostmourne as kin to the plague, though he is unconscious before he can act on what that means next. Chapter 22 confirms Muradin got out of the cave alive enough to hand over to Baelgun’s healers, preserving the story’s last living witness to Arthas before and after Frostmourne.

## Capabilities
Compresses route, curse lore, and personality read into a briefing that gives the pursuers exactly the information they need and nothing decorative. Fights Arthas in close quarters long enough to prove the scale of the mismatch before the shard strike takes him down. Even at collapse, Muradin can still force a magical report into field-usable language.
