# Antonidas

## Overview
Also known as Archmage Antonidas. First appears in chapter1 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Not seen on page; the chapter presents him through reputation as the powerful archmage whose name immediately changes the weight of Jaina’s detention. Chapter 22 finally puts Antonidas bodily on the page again: travel-worn instead of tower-composed, under a cloak rather than formal robes, with service-passage torchlight and palace stone making him look thinner, older, and more frightened than distance ever allowed.

## Voice and behavior
Chapter 19 turns the voice into one last fragile tether: concern, authority, and extraction logic all still present, but no longer enough to keep Jaina on the line. Chapter 20 leaves Antonidas voiceless but present as remembered instruction: the mentor standard Jaina keeps testing herself against once the sea crossing proves endurance can become its own form of error. His voice in chapter22 alternates between stripped mentor urgency and forced operational plainness: only Jaina at first, then tell me the plan in words a judge could follow, then the quieter admission that he knows exactly what failure looks like. Keeps turning from torchlit passage to service door to Jaina’s face, putting fear away by focusing on logistics, locks, and the people still in front of him.

## What chapter 22 changes
Makes no live decision on page in chapter20, but his earlier teaching remains active enough to shape how Jaina judges the point where magical strain stops being disciplined and becomes reckless. Reaches Capital City in person with two Kirin Tor magi, calls Jaina over the stone from inside the city, and gives her the truth that all the warning lanes arrived too late. Arranges the palace-margin meeting, asks for the anti-blade plan in plain tactical language, and commits his magi to softening one service lock even though he cannot go farther in himself. By chapter20 his practical knowledge on page is indirect: Jaina still carries his standards for magical overreach and uses them while confronting the sea-crossing resonance. Now knows Arthas entered Capital City days earlier, public audiences have resumed under celebration, Northrend guards are already rotating inside the palace, and Uther’s formal attempts to intervene have failed. Learns Jaina’s anti-blade framework in full and understands that the question is no longer theory but who can buy the spell its seconds.

## Capabilities
His training remains durable enough to govern Jaina's self-assessment even in his physical absence and after the line to Dalaran is gone. Can still build a covert operational lane inside hostile political space while stripped of formal authority. Keeps strategic clarity under extreme emotional pressure, reducing palace access, magical sensing, and legal risk to the narrow facts that matter.

## Chapter 23 update
Antonidas barely exists on the page in chapter 23, and that is exactly the point. The blue-white light is already gone by the time the latch settles. He has opened the last service door he can open and then drops out of the action because the room beyond no longer belongs to institutions, mentors, or clean magical authority.

That absence matters for continuity. Antonidas is still part of the success, but only as the man who got them to the threshold. He cannot witness the chamber, cannot legitimize the outcome in real time, and cannot save Jaina from what the room will look like after the fact. Chapter 23 therefore preserves the same tragic limitation chapter 22 set up: even the right archmage, in the right city, with the right warning, can still arrive only to the margin.

Future chapters should remember him here not as a rescuer who failed at the last second, but as a mentor who gave the protagonists the only lane left and then had to let it close behind them.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 brings Antonidas back into view only to underline how powerless true expertise can be inside hostile politics. He is present, angry, and still trying to force the blade’s danger onto the record, but the tribunal keeps shrinking him into one more interested witness.

He should be remembered here as both mentor and institutional failure made human. He believes Jaina, understands the magical stakes, and still cannot stop the court from choosing procedure over comprehension.
