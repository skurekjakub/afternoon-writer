# Jaina Proudmoore

## Overview
Also known as Antonidas's apprentice, the mage, the prisoner. First appears in chapter1 and is current through chapter22.

## Presence on the page
Chapter 21 keeps the cost visible and specific: two breaths slower than she should be at Daggercap Bay, dwarven bread forgotten in her hand while she listens inland, lashes crusted white in Dragonblight, and bare shaking fingers still drawing the same invisible figure while Muradin is carried back to camp. Chapter 22 breaks the cost into plain visible pieces: bare hands in the killing cold tracing Frostmourne’s rhythm, a steady tremor that no longer waits for casting, the bread-warm taint sitting on her skin even in Dragonblight, and nosebleeds and collapse once the southbound push starts taking ship-speed directly out of her body. By the palace approach she is riding on near-empty reserves, bracing on rail, wall, or saddle whenever the next stretch of motion threatens to take her legs away.

## Voice and behavior
Chapter 20 gives her theorem-clean shipboard brevity: practical sarcasm with Dagny and Sylvanas, clean admissions when she is wrong, and one bare self-diagnosis — if she can still stand, she thinks she can still spend. Chapter 21 sharpens that brevity into field translation under pressure. She turns resonance theory into plain speech Muradin can use — the sword was made by whoever made the plague — and in the cave reduces herself to split-second tactical warnings and one grim answer about the seam: not yet. Her chapter-22 voice strips theory into field grammar: three schools, one exposed turn, three to five seconds at sword range. Even with Antonidas she speaks in brutal practical clauses because anything more ornate would lose the answer. Chapter 21 makes her body language all thought outrunning flesh: bread crushed in one hand while she listens inland, fingers writing invisible notation over Muradin’s table, one hand clamped around the other wrist to steady a failing cast, and bare shaking hands still tracing the seam while she walks. Keeps writing the pattern in the air with numb fingers, locks one wrist in the other hand to steady the tremor when revelation lands, and only stops moving once collapse physically forces her down. At the palace arch she braces on white stone rather than yielding the conversation, treating walls, railings, and saddles as temporary substitutes for a body running out of steadiness.

## What chapter 22 changes
Leaves Baelgun’s camp without stopping for warmth and keeps working the remembered hitch in Frostmourne’s field while marching through Dragonblight and across the sea. Builds the first complete anti-blade framework — frost, arcane, then fire — and admits aloud that it only works if someone else buys the opening. Spends every warning lane available, drives the Wolfheart south at ruinous personal cost, and still commits to the palace service approach the moment Antonidas offers it. Identifies Frostmourne as the plague’s sister-weapon, confirms that its field catches and throws direct spell force sideways, and carries out the first usable anti-blade clue: a repeatable seam in that deflection pattern. Now knows Arthas reached Capital City days before the pursuit, that the kingdom is celebrating him, and that even Antonidas and Uther can only work from the palace margins. Knows Frostmourne turns from taking to throwing, that the exposed seam lasts only seconds, and that a live attempt will require sword-range stillness under direct threat.

Supports a hard sea crossing through precise staged magical output instead of formless brute force. Translates arcane theory into plain field choreography fast enough for rangers, dwarves, and Antonidas to follow.

## Arc position
The working lie remains: Being right should be enough to force people to listen, if only she can make the case clearly enough The deeper truth pressing against it is: Knowledge matters only when it can be turned into action under hierarchy, shame, and shrinking time Right now the character stands at: Chapter 22 moves Jaina past raw discovery and into embodied deployment logic. She no longer imagines understanding the blade is enough; the answer now only exists if it can survive another person’s timing, body, and risk

## Chapter 23 update
Chapter 23 turns Jaina from planner of the anti-blade sequence into the person who actually scars Frostmourne from the inside. She enters the palace already near empty, follows Sylvanas through service dark, finds the seam at sword range, and keeps casting through nosebleed, concussion, numbness, and fear. The important continuity fact is that the answer works only because she is willing to spend herself past safety and because she trusts Sylvanas to buy the seconds she cannot make alone.

The victory is partial from the instant it lands. Jaina helps rupture Arthas’s clean bond to the sword and opens the line for Sylvanas’s final shot, but Frostmourne does not fall silent with him. It keeps singing on the marble. Jaina understands that before anyone else does, refuses to touch it, and immediately starts thinking in terms of containment rather than triumph.

The emotional residue matters just as much. Terenas dies in front of her. Arthas dies in front of her. Soul-echoes tear through her hard enough to drop her to the dais, and the first living witnesses still read the room as regicide and treason instead of rescue. By the time Uther reaches her, Jaina is coughing blood, short on magic, and still precise enough to report what happened in plain practical terms. She ends the chapter in irons, more afraid of the active sword than of custody, carrying the knowledge that she saved something and failed to save anything clean.

## Chapter 24 update
Chapter 24 leaves Jaina as an imprisoned alarm bell. She is physically wrecked, sleeping badly, hearing Frostmourne through stone, and forced to explain an ongoing magical emergency to a tribunal that would rather convict two survivors than admit the weapon remains active.

The key continuity point is that her understanding sharpens rather than blurs in captivity. She can feel the blade from below the vault, knows the timeline has collapsed to days, and hands that truth to Sylvanas and then to Lor'themar without cushioning it. The chapter ends with Jaina still powerless in law but newly less alone in knowledge, because other mages can finally feel what she has been trying to describe.
