# Grounding Gate Scratchpad — chapter25

## KEEP 1
- **Category:** opening material anchor
- **Excerpt:** "The blade beat through the white palace stone every fourteen seconds."
- **Defense applied:** Distributed grounding through architecture, timing, and bodily contact.
- **Why it survived:** The chapter opens on a countable physical signal tied to palace stone, not a mood statement. The countdown is immediately actionable and keeps the threat lodged in the room instead of hovering as abstract dread.

## KEEP 2
- **Category:** dialogue embodiment
- **Excerpt:** "\"It's sorting.\" ... \"Minds. It touches, tests, lets go.\""
- **Defense applied:** Dialogue stays pinned to wall, low voice, pauses, and chain-shift texture.
- **Why it survived:** The exchange never forgets the two-foot stone barrier or the cost of getting each sentence through it. The short lines preserve pressure and keep the prison geometry legible.

## KEEP 3
- **Category:** single-room legibility
- **Excerpt:** "The chain at her ankle checked her two paces from the door. She had measured that useless freedom in the first hour: cot, bucket, wall, two steps, iron."
- **Defense applied:** Spatial inventory with threshold clarity.
- **Why it survived:** This is exactly the kind of room-mapping the scene needs before the scale reveal arrives. It passes G5 cleanly because the actionable limits of the cell are explicit, measurable, and immediately reusable.

## KEEP 4
- **Category:** scale reveal grounded in matter
- **Excerpt:** "The visitor came through anyway. One moment the cell held only Sylvanas and the dark. The next it held another body, and the space between them shortened under its size. Weight settled into the flagstones. Air displaced."
- **Defense applied:** Impossible event carried by flagstones, ceiling, and displaced air.
- **Why it survived:** The reveal stays embodied instead of drifting into pure spectacle. The room remains the measuring instrument, so the demon's scale lands through pressure and fit, not wiki-like explanation.

## KEEP 5
- **Category:** exit beat holding to room
- **Excerpt:** "The dark held for a few heartbeats after it was gone, then thinned. Torchlight returned with a wet pop in the corridor bracket and laid itself back across the threshold."
- **Defense applied:** Re-entry to ordinary material reality after supernatural breach.
- **Why it survived:** Even after the visitor leaves, the prose uses bracket, torch, threshold, and timing to reassert the cell. The beat keeps the departure actionable and does not fail G5 on threshold clarity despite the scale reveal immediately preceding it.

## KEEP 6
- **Category:** closing leverage
- **Excerpt:** "Sylvanas sat on the cot at last. Chain pooled around her ankle and clicked once against the wood frame. ... Above her, Frostmourne thudded through the palace stone. It was still hunting. So was she."
- **Defense applied:** Closing thought defended by chain, cot, and blade pressure.
- **Why it survived:** The chapter earns its final line by keeping both hunts attached to prison matter instead of floating into sequel-slogan abstraction. The room is still present under the idea, which is why the close lands.
