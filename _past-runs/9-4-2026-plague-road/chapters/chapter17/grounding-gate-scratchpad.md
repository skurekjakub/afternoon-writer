# Grounding Gate Scratchpad — Chapter 17

## KEEP Decisions

### KEEP 1
**Category:** Sensory grounding  
**Excerpt:** "No. / Jaina's answer rang beneath Stratholme's southern crestwork, and the line under the main arch locked where it stood."  
**Defense:** The opening drops straight into physical space — the crestwork, the arch, the locked line. Grounded in architecture and body-position. No abstraction.  
**Survived because:** Concrete spatial anchoring from the first sentence.

### KEEP 2
**Category:** Physical detail chain  
**Excerpt:** "Falric moved before the sentence cleared the arch. Men ran for the chains. Others heaved a brewer's dray out of the lane. One guard looked at Uther, then at Arthas, then at Falric already moving, and chose motion over thought."  
**Defense:** Every clause is a physical action. The guard's decision is rendered through the sequence of his looking — three concrete targets — not through emotional labeling. "Chose motion over thought" is borderline but reads as Sylvanas's tactical inference, not narrator commentary.  
**Survived because:** Action chain grounds the chaos of obedience without abstracting it.

### KEEP 3
**Category:** Sensory friction  
**Excerpt:** "Handcarts jammed wheel to wheel under the wall. Dropped sacks bled flour into the mud. An open barrel lay on its side and people stumbled over it without seeing it. The smell was bread, wet burlap, horse piss, broken wood."  
**Defense:** Multi-sensory: visual (flour bleeding, barrel on its side), tactile (jammed wheels), olfactory (bread, burlap, piss, wood). Anglo-Saxon verb choices (jammed, bled, stumbled). Zero abstraction.  
**Survived because:** Exemplary sensory grounding. This is the target register.

### KEEP 4
**Category:** Dialogue grounding  
**Excerpt:** "'Don't go by the dye sheds,' he blurted. 'Service yard's gone bad there. Men dropping where they stand. East stair's still open if you take the tallow cut. Up past the wheelwright and the old feed crane. It'll put you on the inner wall above Market Row.'"  
**Defense:** The carter's route intelligence is delivered through place-names, landmarks, and movement verbs — not through abstraction. "Men dropping where they stand" is physical witness, not euphemism. The dye sheds, tallow cut, wheelwright, feed crane are all specific enough to navigate by. This is grounded civilian speech under pressure.  
**Survived because:** Named routes, concrete landmarks, actionable intelligence. Dialogue earns its information delivery.

### KEEP 5
**Category:** Physical witness at distance  
**Excerpt:** "Market Row first. Of course. Squads moved along its broad spine in clean intervals, one line driving inward while another fell back dragging bodies, sacks, and salvage. Fires choked exits, cleared doorways, burned stock still piled high enough to matter."  
**Defense:** The wall witness stays in what Sylvanas can see at distance: squad formations, movement intervals, bodies being dragged, fire placement. "Of course" is her tactical read, not narrator approval. The detail is spatial and military — rotation patterns, exit management, stock burning.  
**Survived because:** Distance-appropriate observation. Nothing claimed that can't be seen from a parapet.

### KEEP 6
**Category:** Tactical reading without emotional label  
**Excerpt:** "Correct. / Monstrous."  
**Defense:** These two one-word paragraphs are Sylvanas's judgment, not emotional labels. They arrive AFTER the concrete observation and function as her assessment of the evidence already presented. The chapter plan specifically requires that she hold tactical competence and moral horror simultaneously. Two words. No abstraction needed — the evidence is already on the page.  
**Survived because:** The assessment is earned by the preceding concrete paragraphs. Cut would lose the chapter's central tension.

### KEEP 7
**Category:** Physical detail / clean-hands reveal  
**Excerpt:** "His gauntlets were gone. His hands were bare. / Sylvanas saw that first. / Pale in the braziers' fire at the gate, the skin clean to the wrists. Too clean for accident. Too clean for proximity. Water found. Time made. Blood removed."  
**Defense:** This is the chapter's key image and it lands entirely through physical detail — gauntlets absent, bare skin, color in firelight, cleanliness at the wrists. The three fragment inferences ("Water found. Time made. Blood removed.") are Sylvanas's tactical deductions from physical evidence, not emotional labels. Each fragment names a concrete action Arthas must have taken.  
**Survived because:** Image-first, inference-second. The physical evidence carries the moral weight without a single emotional label.

### KEEP 8
**Category:** Body-part economy  
**Excerpt:** "On the stone beside Sylvanas, Jaina's hand stayed flat on the parapet, white at the knuckles, until even the last of the column had passed from sight."  
**Defense:** One body part — hand on stone. One detail — white knuckles. One temporal anchor — until the column passes. This is the closing image and it picks ONE physical detail to carry the whole emotional weight. No stacked descriptors, no body-part inventory.  
**Survived because:** Perfect body-part economy. Single detail carries the close.

### KEEP 9
**Category:** Tactile/olfactory grounding  
**Excerpt:** "They hit the tallow cut at a run. The smell arrived first, rank fat gone soft in the day's heat, vats stacked under a crooked awning. Two chandlers had abandoned a handcart half-loaded with candles. One still burned where it had tipped, wax spilling into the muck."  
**Defense:** Olfactory first (rank fat), then visual (vats, awning, handcart, burning candle, wax in muck). Named trade (chandlers). Specific material (tallow, wax). The burning candle in the muck is precise, mundane detail that grounds the lane as a real workspace abandoned mid-task.  
**Survived because:** Multi-sensory layering through concrete trade detail.

### KEEP 10
**Category:** Sound grounding  
**Excerpt:** "A scream. Then two streets of nothing. A roof beam giving way. Hooves. Someone retching on the lane below the wall. A captain's voice, steady, naming the next turn. Fire settling into its work."  
**Defense:** Sound inventory measured in distance ("two streets of nothing") rather than time. Each sound is concrete and source-identified. The mix of human (scream, retching, captain's voice) and material (roof beam, hooves, fire) creates a layered acoustic picture without abstraction.  
**Survived because:** Sound-as-distance is a grounding move. Every noise has a physical source.
