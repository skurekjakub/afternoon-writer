# Grounding Gate Scratchpad — chapter22 iteration 2

Verdict: FAIL

## KEEP decisions

### KEEP 1 — Baelgun's camp still reads as a real casualty yard
- **Category:** place / handoff infrastructure
- **Excerpt:** "No carved gate. No challenge trench. Just a yard stamped hard by dwarf boots and stretcher drags..."
- **Defense applied:** Working-place materiality
- **Why it survived:** The opening camp is built from labor pattern, weather exposure, and triage equipment rather than from generic snow-camp shorthand. Forge trench, hide roofs, stretcher drag, beard clasps, and immediate treatment pressure all keep the place physically dwarven.
- **G5 check:** The beat does not rely on Baelgun's name alone; yard wear, treatment flow, and cold-exposed structures make the threshold actionable and legible.

### KEEP 2 — Jaina's seam work stays bodily more often than not
- **Category:** embodied magic
- **Excerpt:** "Take. / Throw. / A hitch she could count through clenched teeth."
- **Defense applied:** Motion before theory
- **Why it survived:** The chapter's best mechanism beats are still carried in teeth, breath, fingers, shake, and repeated counting. Even with residual conceptual drift elsewhere, the core discovery usually remains tied to body and timing.

### KEEP 3 — Daggercap returns as damaged infrastructure, not nostalgia
- **Category:** callback place fidelity
- **Excerpt:** "Daylight laid the harbor bare again: black wreckage frozen into the edge, hacked docks, ropes gone stiff as iron..."
- **Defense applied:** Place continuity through fresh physical evidence
- **Why it survived:** The bay reads as a worked, damaged harbor with scars from Arthas's passage still on it. Wreckage, ropes, burn scars, handspikes, curses, and cracked ice all keep the return anchored in matter instead of sentiment.
- **G5 check:** The passage would still work without the Daggercap label; harbor damage, labor, and smell-profile make the location actionable on page.

### KEEP 4 — The crossing pays for speed in deck labor and body cost
- **Category:** travel / ship materiality
- **Excerpt:** "Nosebleed by dusk. Again by moonrise. Again the next morning. The blood ran bright, then went black where salt dried it into the grain of the boards."
- **Defense applied:** Cost distributed through machinery and bodies
- **Why it survived:** The southbound rush is bought in sail work, fish shoved into Jaina's hand, blood on planks, rag cleanup, and crew discipline. The voyage does not float off into pure urgency for long; it keeps remembering ship, deck, and body.

### KEEP 5 — Lordaeron's false calm is still socially embodied
- **Category:** civic texture
- **Excerpt:** "Goldenrod garlands looped from fence post to fence post. Someone had tied blue ribbons around the square pump. A painted board outside the tavern showed a blond prince in impossible armor..."
- **Defense applied:** Public-normalcy grounding
- **Why it survived:** The lie lives in visible objects and ordinary people, not in pure exposition. Garlands, ribbons, stick-figure triumph art, washerwoman, runner, baker, and bells all keep the kingdom's ignorance inhabited.
- **G5 check:** The passage does not fail on route clarity or threshold legibility; square pump, tavern front, road traffic, and village business make the place usable even without named geography.

### KEEP 6 — The palace side-lane remains physically usable
- **Category:** threshold / service architecture
- **Excerpt:** "Antonidas waited where he said he would, under a narrow service arch off the eastern court... Blue-white Kirin Tor light sank into old metal..."
- **Defense applied:** Service-architecture specificity
- **Why it survived:** The infiltration margin is built from arch, lock plate, cold mortar, kitchen wear, and single-file entry mechanics rather than ceremonial blur. The lane reads as a real back-of-house route.
- **G5 check:** The beat does not lean on palace or Kirin Tor labels alone; arch geometry, worn stone, and service-door mechanics keep the threshold concrete and actionable.
