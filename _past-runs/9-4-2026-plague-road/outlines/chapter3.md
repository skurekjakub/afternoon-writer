# Chapter 3: The Dead Village

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 2's final approach to Millhaven.
**Open location:** Millhaven, just south of the Quel'Thalas borderlands.
**Transport:** Mounted approach on Thalassian horses; on foot during the village sweep.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, one unnamed escort ranger.
**Immediate objective:** Read Millhaven, find out what happened to its people, and decide whether the danger is really moving toward Quel'Thalas.

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- The contamination is real, physical, and big enough to matter beyond one village
- Jaina's readings are useful even when her sense of the road is poor
- Millhaven is the first place on the south road where the wrongness knots tight

**What Sylvanas does NOT know at open**
- How it first got into the village
- Whether the dead are organized
- Whether any living witnesses remain
- How large the threat is beyond this village

**Must not be implied yet**
- No grain confirmation
- No organized cult proof
- No assumption that the old trader is anything but a local source

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Millhaven is the first fully claimed village on this road
- The survivor girl's testimony plus the follow-up tracking pass confirm the dead marched north toward Quel'Thalas
- Neral rides north with the girl and warning, leaving only Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel to keep riding south
- The newer grain-seller mention enters the file as a weak unresolved clue

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** This is still border defense to read, report, and answer, not a human catastrophe Sylvanas has to own on its own terms.
- **Surface objective:** Read Millhaven cleanly enough to turn shock into a warning she can send home before the road worsens again.
- **Pressure source:** The village is not a rumor or a bad patrol report. It is a whole settlement gone wrong, with missing dead, one living witness, and a kind of wrongness Sylvanas cannot price without Jaina.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Sylvanas keeps treating Jaina as a specialist she can consult when needed, not as half of the field read.
- **Chapter test:** Millhaven forces her to weigh tracks, corpses, silence, witness testimony, and magic together. None of it resolves cleanly if she cuts out Jaina's side.
- **Forced choice:** Keep Jaina on a short leash as a useful prisoner, or start letting her read shape what the party does next.
- **End-state shift:** By chapter close Sylvanas still does not trust Jaina, but she stops treating the mage's read as optional color. It is part of the map now.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 4 opens with Sylvanas expecting Jaina to speak when the wrongness turns strange, even if authority still stays in Sylvanas's hands.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** The only woman here who can tell how deep the wrongness goes and which parts of it matter.
- **POV misread at open:** Useful, but still a human mage who might see the magical half more clearly than the ground under their boots.
- **Correction earned here:** Jaina does not just name the wrongness. She helps separate what is loud from what matters, which keeps the field read from collapsing into blind horror.
- **Interaction rule:** Sylvanas still withholds warmth. Jaina earns more room to speak, not comfort.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Custody ride under pressure. Useful prisoner, armed escort, no trust.
- **Operational change this chapter:** The village makes side-by-side reading unavoidable. By the end, they are building one picture out of two kinds of evidence.
- **Naming rule:** No easy softening yet. If Sylvanas uses Jaina's name in her own head, it should land because the mage's read is doing real work, not because the mood warmed.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** This is first proof of usefulness, not rapport.

## Cast and handoff rules

- **Cyndia:** Remains with the party and is the ranger Sylvanas trusts most on the ground.
- **Lyriel:** Stays with the party into Chapter 4.
- **Neral:** Present at chapter open. She stays through the cellar rescue and the follow-up tracking pass; once the girl's testimony and the ground read together show the risen dead are likely mustering toward Quel'Thalas, she is sent north with the warning dispatch and exits the traveling party this chapter.
- **Unnamed escort ranger:** Present at chapter open as the spare fourth rider from Chapter 2. She leaves north with Neral and the survivor so the southbound group reduces cleanly to the four named leads.
- **Survivor girl:** Found in a cellar this chapter. She does not travel with Sylvanas and Jaina afterward; Neral escorts her north on horseback once the warning dispatch leaves.
- **Old grain seller:** First Mal'Ganis disguise. Must read as useful, local, unremarkable, and not yet suspicious to the POV.

**Chapter handoff target:** Chapter 4 opens after a two-day southbound move. By then the traveling party is Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel only.

## Scene 1: Millhaven

**Scene function:** Show the first village the plague has truly taken, and make the missing dead a real problem.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, escort
**Knowledge at scene start:** The contamination is real and physical; how it got here is still unclear

### Beat 1 — Scene: Arrival into the silence
- **Goal:** Enter the village carefully and assess whether it is occupied, trapped, or abandoned
- **Conflict:** Millhaven is intact but acoustically dead; every normal village sound is missing
- **Outcome:** **no-and** — the party gains entry, but the absence itself is worse than visible damage
- **Value shift:** approach -> dead zone
- **New on-page information:**
  - Millhaven was not burned, sacked, or visibly fought over
  - The wrongness is recent enough that ordinary life still seems interrupted rather than erased
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Where the people are
  - Whether anything inside is still living
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Cold stove, swinging door, no livestock noise
  - Dust on places that should have been touched today
  - Horses reacting to the silence
- **Transition intent:** Sylvanas orders a real sweep instead of leaving the whole village to Jaina's magic

### Beat 2 — Scene: The sweep
- **Goal:** Read the village for timeline and population fate
- **Conflict:** Every building says "left in the middle of living" while offering no bodies and no obvious kill site
- **Outcome:** **no-and** — the sweep produces evidence of staggered collapse but no explanation for the vanished population
- **Value shift:** dead zone -> impossible absence
- **New on-page information:**
  - The collapse took days, not a single sudden event
  - Domestic life froze in place before emptying out
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the missing population fled, was taken, or rose
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Food gone stale on tables
  - Child's toy left where it fell
  - Cat as the only casual living presence
- **Transition intent:** Jaina tests the contamination inside the most saturated structures

### Beat 3 — Scene: Contamination everywhere
- **Goal:** Let Jaina test whether the village gives up how this started
- **Conflict:** The whole place reads wrong: well, soil, root cellar, granary, trough
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Jaina proves the whole village is soaked in it, but she still cannot say what first brought it in
- **Value shift:** impossible absence -> the whole village is soaked, but the cause is still slippery
- **New on-page information:**
  - The granary is the strongest single read, but Jaina refuses to mistake the loudest layer for the whole answer
  - Sylvanas is starting to treat Jaina as the one kind of reader she does not have
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether it came through grain, water, soil, or some slower layering
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Jaina kneeling in grain while Sylvanas clears the room behind her
  - Normal-looking stores with invisible wrongness
  - Stale dust and closed-space heat
- **Transition intent:** Sylvanas takes the search outside and puts her hands on the ground herself

### Beat 4 — Scene: Too many bodies missing, not enough clean track
- **Goal:** Determine what happened to the bodies
- **Conflict:** The ground behind the village is too churned and partial to call cleanly; there are drag marks, broken crust, and trampled exits, but not yet a direction Sylvanas will stake a border warning on
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Sylvanas confirms many of the dead left Millhaven on their feet, but the ground alone does not yet prove where they went
- **Value shift:** the whole village is soaked, but the cause is still slippery -> too much danger, not enough proof
- **New on-page information:**
  - A large number of bodies are missing from the village
  - The dead did not simply collapse in place and stay there
  - Sylvanas needs either a witness or a cleaner line of spoor before she commits a warning north
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the dead moved toward Quel'Thalas or elsewhere
  - Who is directing them
  - Where the rest of the villagers are
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Broken dirt where too many feet dragged through the same lane
  - Sylvanas crouched over muddied sign she refuses to overcall
  - Cyndia frustrated by counts without a trustworthy line
- **Transition intent:** The ground is not enough; the chapter now needs a living witness or cleaner evidence

## Scene 2: The Survivor and the Proof

**Scene function:** Put a living child inside the disaster, then use her to turn guesswork into warning.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, survivor girl
**Knowledge at scene start:** The contamination is real, many bodies are missing, and the ground alone has not yet proved where the dead went

### Beat 5 — Scene: The girl in the cellar
- **Goal:** Find any living witness left in Millhaven
- **Conflict:** They locate an alive but traumatized girl hidden in a cellar, dehydrated, terrified, and unreliable in sequence
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — they get a witness, but the testimony is fragmentary and emotionally broken
- **Value shift:** mobilization risk without proof -> living testimony at last
- **New on-page information:**
  - At least one living person survived inside the village
  - Neral is the first ranger the girl does not recoil from, which makes her the natural escort if the child has to be moved
  - The girl confirms death, rising, and night movement rather than simple disease collapse
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Exact timeline
  - Direction of the dead
  - How it first got in
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Cellar damp
  - Child's whisper gone hoarse
  - Jaina lowering herself into the dark while Sylvanas controls the space above
  - Neral crouching to the girl's height instead of looming over her
- **Transition intent:** The girl can talk, but only in broken pieces; the party must extract what is useful without pretending she is reliable

### Beat 6 — Scene: Fragmented testimony
- **Goal:** Pull usable intelligence from the survivor
- **Conflict:** The girl remembers in images, not order: sweetness on people's breath, neighbors getting sick, bodies rising, dead shuffling out toward the north road at night, adults telling children to stay hidden, and complaint about a newer grain man on the route
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — Sylvanas gains confirmation that the dead rose and moved with direction, and hears one early oddity in who had been selling grain, but the child's memory still needs the ground to back it
- **Value shift:** living testimony at last -> horror with direction implied
- **New on-page information:**
  - Some risen dead came back for the sick who remained
  - The girl remembers movement toward the north road rather than blind milling
  - There was talk of a newer grain seller before the collapse
  - The girl is too unstable to build a full timeline from alone
  - Jaina is the one who can pull the few useful pieces out of the girl's broken story, which Sylvanas notices
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the northbound memory matches the physical trail cleanly enough to warn Quel'Thalas
  - Whether the grain clue matters or is incidental
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Girl's fingers locked around a blanket scrap
  - Jaina hearing the tremor in every remembered detail
  - Neral keeping the girl steady with quiet, practical questions instead of orders
- **Transition intent:** The testimony points north, but Sylvanas still needs the ground to agree before she spends riders on warning

### Beat 7 — Scene: The tracking pass
- **Goal:** Verify whether the girl's broken memory matches the ground outside Millhaven
- **Conflict:** The road cut north of the village is damaged by wagon ruts, scavengers, and too many overlapping exits, so the read has to be made from scraps instead of a clean trail
- **Outcome:** **no-and** — Sylvanas and Cyndia finally confirm a north-northeast mass movement toward Quel'Thalas, and the answer immediately widens the strategic emergency
- **Value shift:** horror with direction implied -> the dead marched toward home
- **New on-page information:**
  - The girl's testimony and the physical spoor now support each other
  - At least sixty reanimated bodies moved north in rough formation
  - No idea what prompted it
  - Jaina's magical read helps strip ordinary road damage out of the spoor, which Sylvanas files as another kind of tracking whether she likes the comparison or not
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the dead are bunching up on their own or being pushed into line by living hands
  - Whether the warning will arrive in time to matter
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Parallel grooves finally clean enough to trust at the north road cut
  - Sylvanas reading the ground with her fingertips and committing to the call
  - Cyndia's count turning from doubt into alarm
- **Transition intent:** The proof is finally good enough to justify a warning north, and the survivor now has to leave with it

## Scene 3: The Grain Seller on the Road

**Scene function:** Turn proof into action, bring in the old trader as a useful local, send Neral north with the girl, and close on the smaller group riding south.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, Lyriel, Neral, old grain seller, survivor girl
**Knowledge at scene start:** The girl and the ground together have confirmed the dead are moving north; the child cannot stay with the investigation; Neral is the obvious dispatch rider; "new grain man" exists only as one witness fragment

### Beat 8 — Sequel: Dispatch north
- **Emotion:** Urgency with no time to indulge it
- **Dilemma:** Keep Neral for the road south, or cut her loose now so Quel'Thalas gets warning and the child gets out
- **Decision:** Put the warning in Neral's hands, send her north with the survivor and the unnamed escort ranger, and keep Cyndia and Lyriel on the southbound ride
- **Value shift:** the dead marched toward home -> one rider gone, danger larger
- **New on-page information:**
  - Neral is the best rider to carry a warning north in a hurry
  - Neral is unexpectedly good with frightened children, which makes the choice cleaner than a purely tactical handoff
  - The unnamed fourth rider folds into Neral's northbound escort, which leaves the southbound group at the four named leads
  - Sylvanas is prioritizing the border threat even while continuing south
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the warning will arrive in time to matter
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Written message sealed in haste
  - Dispatch tube strapped at Neral's saddle
  - Neral calming the girl with matter-of-fact talk about safe stables, sweet feed, and the horned white "unicorn" mounts children in Quel'Thalas grow up dreaming about
- **Transition intent:** The decision is made; one last road contact can still sharpen the picture before the party splits directions
### Beat 9 — Scene: The old man with the empty wagon
- **Goal:** Gather route intelligence and see how far the outbreak has progressed beyond Millhaven
- **Conflict:** An old grain seller on the road north has exactly the kind of unforced local knowledge a field commander values, and finding Millhaven totally gone catches him visibly off guard instead of confirming anything he expected
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — he gives useful road talk, and his surprise makes it clear Millhaven shocked him too, but the party still has to trust a stranger's read of the country ahead
- **Value shift:** one rider gone, danger larger -> the road picture gets clearer
- **New on-page information:**
  - Settlements ahead are still trading and only lightly touched by sickness
  - Millhaven is the first village on this road fully claimed by the plague
  - The trader's empty wagon is heading north, and he clearly did not expect to find the village silent
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - That the trader is linked to the contamination itself
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Grain dust in the slats of the empty wagon
  - Surprise breaking through his otherwise forgettable face
  - Girl settling in front of Neral's saddle while the horse sidesteps
- **Transition intent:** The exchange sharpens the road picture just before the party splits north and south

### Beat 10 — Scene: The parting
- **Goal:** Complete the parting cleanly and move on
- **Conflict:** The trader's farewell lands just oddly enough to earn Sylvanas a brief glance back, but the moment is too thin to matter more than the dead and the road south
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** — the parting completes cleanly, though Sylvanas gives him one second look before letting the matter go
- **Value shift:** the road picture gets clearer -> parting complete, eyes south again
- **New on-page information:**
  - The survivor girl leaves the chapter here under Neral's escort, headed north on horseback
  - Sylvanas gives the trader a brief look back before turning south again
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - The trader's real identity
  - Whether the trader matters at all beyond this practical handoff
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Sylvanas's brief glance back
  - Wagon wheels starting north again after that startled pause
  - Girl silent against Neral's saddle as the horse steps out
- **Transition intent:** With Neral escorting the girl north, the road south narrows to Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel

### Beat 11 — Sequel: Deeper south
- **Emotion:** Forward pressure held in check
- **Dilemma:** Turn back after warning the border, or keep following the stronger readings and the live-settlement trail
- **Decision:** Keep moving south toward the next still-living village
- **Value shift:** parting complete, attention pulled south again -> committed pursuit
- **New on-page information:**
  - Only Sylvanas, Jaina, Cyndia, and Lyriel are riding south now
  - They are committing to a multi-day push deeper into Lordaeron
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Grain as the thing carrying it
  - The organization behind the plague
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Road opening into broader farmland
  - Stronger readings ahead
  - One fewer escort horse in the party after Neral's dispatch
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 4 opens two days later at the next village, still alive and still misunderstanding what is happening to it
