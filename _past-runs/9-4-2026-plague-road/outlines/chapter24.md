# Chapter 24: The Condemned

**POV:** Sylvanas Windrunner (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Opens hours after Chapter 23's arrest and closes after the verdict with Lor'themar outside the cell.
**Open location:** Capital City palace dungeons.
**Transport:** Static imprisonment -> escorted movement in chains to the tribunal hall -> return to cells -> corridor-side contact through bars.
**Active cast at open:** Sylvanas, Jaina in adjacent cell, palace guards, tribunal officials.
**Immediate objective:** Survive the legal machinery long enough to keep the blade intelligence alive while the kingdom misreads the room above them.

## Knowledge ledger

**What Sylvanas knows at open**
- The pair saved the kingdom in the chamber and still left the room with the worst possible evidence profile.
- Jaina is alive but deeply depleted in the adjacent cell.
- Frostmourne sits in custody above them, no longer riding through Arthas but still tethered north and still active.
- Antonidas reached Capital City before the murder and still missed the room by the same brutal minutes that beat Uther.
- Uther arrived too late to witness the murder itself.
- Every warning the investigation ever sent can now be reframed as premeditation.

**What Sylvanas does NOT know at open**
- Whether Antonidas defies Dalaran publicly or obeys the line it draws around the tribunal.
- Whether Silvermoon stands behind her or cuts her loose completely.
- How quickly Frostmourne is seeking another mind through the palace.
- Whether Lor'themar or any outside ally reaches the city in time to affect anything.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No cosmic visitor yet; that belongs to the next chapter's night.
- No promise of prison break, acquittal, or even competent containment.
- No certainty about who in the palace is already vulnerable to the blade's call until Jaina starts counting the rhythm through stone.
- No softening of the legal mechanism into simple corruption; it has to work cleanly and still produce the wrong answer.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- The tribunal convicts both women on every major count and commutes death to indefinite imprisonment.
- Antonidas comes with two Kirin Tor magi, but no sanctioned Kirin Tor witness takes the stand.
- Uther testifies clearly and is dismissed as a wounded former commander.
- Vereesa delivers the Convocation's disavowal in person and stays in the city afterward.
- Jaina clocks the blade's acceleration as days rather than weeks.
- Lor'themar arrives with three hundred Farstriders and mages who can independently feel the blade's wrongness.

## Arc position

### Sylvanas
- **Current stance at open:** The battlefield is gone, but the need to read what is happening has not gone with it.
- **Surface objective:** Keep Jaina reachable through stone, keep the truth in circulation as long as possible, and find any remaining tactical surface inside a system built to deny one.
- **Pressure source:** The cold from the vault, Jaina's condition through the wall, the tribunal's elegance, and the certainty that Sylvanas can see each move coming without being able to stop it.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Some part of Sylvanas still expects clean reading to generate leverage, even in a courtroom designed to leave the accused none.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can endure perfect comprehension without action - the form of helplessness most poisonous to her.
- **Forced choice:** Keep speaking the truth even as it arms the prosecution, or let silence cede the record entirely.
- **End-state shift:** Sylvanas moves from total abandonment to the first outside reinforcement, but only after the verdict has already locked.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 25 opens with the pair still imprisoned, the blade seeking faster, and the larger board about to walk through the wall.

### Jaina through Sylvanas's POV
- **Visible function:** Adjacent voice, resonance instrument, and the only person in the palace who can hear how close the blade is to finding another hand.
- **POV pressure:** Sylvanas reads Jaina through breath, smell, tapping, and the body language of a woman forced to sit near the weapon she already scarred.
- **Interaction rule:** No interiority. Jaina's pain and calculation stay external.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Post-peak and reduced to what survives stone.
- **Change this chapter:** The partnership stops being combat choreography and becomes shared endurance plus information transfer.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** Let the wall, the silence, and the chain of outside failures prove the bond. Do not overwrite the institutional brutality with sentiment.

- **Antonidas:** Present in the hall, exhausted, furious, and politically muzzled. He gets to be seen and still not get to save her.

## Scene 1: The Cells

**Scene function:** Reframe the triumph as helpless imprisonment and establish Frostmourne as the second, more urgent prison above them.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina through the wall, guards, interrogators
**Knowledge at scene start:** The room above them proved innocence only to people who were not there to see it.
**Arc pressure:** Keep stone, cold, bread-smell, and the limited acoustics active so the chapter stays bodily and close.

### Beat 1 - Sequel: The Stone Between
- **Emotion:** Helplessness sharpened into habit.
- **Dilemma:** Sylvanas can map the cell and listen to Jaina through the wall, but neither action opens the door or reaches the blade.
- **Decision:** She listens anyway because inventory is the only useful discipline left.
- **Value shift:** aftermath -> imprisonment understood
- **New on-page information:**
  - The cell is small enough to map in paces and still useless once mapped
  - The cold rising through the floor is wrong in direction and intensity, which means the blade is already affecting the building
  - Jaina's breath and bread-smell through stone become the only functioning proof that the partnership still exists inside the system
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How quickly the blade's pressure worsens
  - Whether the tribunal reads the room as murder, conspiracy, or something even simpler
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Thin torch bars on the wall through the grate
  - Frost smell replacing old dungeon damp
  - Jaina's uneven breathing carrying through masonry to elven ears
- **Transition intent:** Once the cells are understood, the chapter can let the legal machine come inside them.

### Beat 2 - Scene: The Interrogation
- **Goal:** Put the investigation's full truth into the official record.
- **Conflict:** The truth is too large for the officers taking it down and will read as invention more cleanly than it reads as reality.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Sylvanas tells the entire truth and the listeners visibly stop believing well before she finishes, which means the transcript itself becomes ammunition.
- **Value shift:** imprisonment understood -> truth enters the wrong record
- **New on-page information:**
  - The prosecution's questions were written before the interrogator entered the cell
  - Sylvanas can watch belief fail in real time as the scale of the truth grows
  - The entire investigation, stated plainly, sounds like the defense a guilty person would invent if she needed something too large to rebut
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any judge or clerk reads past the implausibility of the content
  - Whether Jaina hearing the whole account through the wall helps or only hurts
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Clerk's pen scratching quickly across a board balanced on knees
  - Guard-captain posture shifting from attention to endurance
  - Iron lock turning again after the truth is filed away as fantasy
- **Transition intent:** Once the truth is in the record, the chapter can let the blade remind them the trial is not even the deepest problem.

### Beat 3 - Sequel: The Cold from Above
- **Emotion:** The law is one prison; Frostmourne is another and hungrier one.
- **Dilemma:** The blade is active and looking, but the only people who can interpret what that means are in separate stone boxes.
- **Decision:** Sylvanas speaks through the wall and starts treating Jaina's attunement as the only live intelligence source left.
- **Value shift:** truth enters the wrong record -> the blade becomes the urgent clock
- **New on-page information:**
  - The dungeon is getting colder because the blade is pulling heat through the foundations
  - Jaina can hear the weapon actively looking for a next mind
  - The partnership's wall-language now includes live threat reporting, not just endurance
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Which people in the palace are most vulnerable first
  - Whether anyone outside the cells can be made to understand the danger before it chooses a new hand
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Breath-clouds thickening in a cell that should not be this cold
  - Jaina's voice through stone saying one terrible verb: looking
  - Wool blanket on the cot smelling of frost instead of damp
- **Transition intent:** Once the blade becomes a clock, the chapter can carry that clock up into the hall where the verdict gets written anyway.

## Scene 2: The Trial

**Scene function:** Show the system working well enough to condemn the wrong people and let the blade become evidence against its only readers.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Thornton, Ashcombe, palace mages, Uther, guards, gallery
**Knowledge at scene start:** The blade is active, the accused are right, and the room above them is built to convert their competence into guilt.
**Arc pressure:** Keep chains, lead-lined chest, dust-light, and Uther's hands concrete so the trial never drifts into legal abstraction.

### Beat 4 - Scene: The Prosecution's Case
- **Goal:** Establish the prosecution's narrative and its control of the room.
- **Conflict:** The narrative is elegant because it is built from real facts and only reverses the direction of causation.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Ashcombe builds a clean conspiracy from the investigation's own paperwork, and Sylvanas can see every move while having no way to break the sequence.
- **Value shift:** the blade becomes the urgent clock -> the legal cage closes
- **New on-page information:**
  - The prosecution treats months of correct investigation as months of deliberate preparation
  - Scholomance knowledge, Northrend travel, relay work, and warnings all become "proof" of design instead of response
  - Thornton's competence matters because the courtroom is not lawless; it is precise
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any witness can puncture the prosecution's causation chain
  - Whether the tribunal sees the pair as traitors, spies, or something worse
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Light columns catching courtroom dust
  - Lighter courtroom chains that still say the system already won
  - Two-second look between Jaina and Sylvanas across a floor too wide to cross
- **Transition intent:** Once the prosecution establishes the frame, the chapter can bring the actual blade into the room and let fear help that frame.

### Beat 5 - Scene: The Blade as Evidence
- **Goal:** Present Frostmourne and hear how the court interprets its presence.
- **Conflict:** The blade is genuinely horrifying, but the horror is being used to prove the accused knew too much to be innocent.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the room feels the blade's wrongness, and the prosecution turns that wrongness directly into an argument against the only two people who understand it.
- **Value shift:** the legal cage closes -> the predator enters the courtroom in a box
- **New on-page information:**
  - Even lead, wards, and oak only muffle the blade; they do not quiet it
  - Palace mages can describe the weapon's effects without understanding its will or its history
  - Jaina is actively harmed by the exhibit while the court mistakes her knowledge for complicity
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How much faster the vault wards degrade for every minute the blade spends in open hearing space
  - Whether anyone in the room notices Jaina's reaction for what it really is
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Lead-lined chest on a center table drawing warmth out of the room
  - Fogging breath above the clerk's notes
  - Jaina gripping chair arms hard enough for the knuckles to whiten
- **Transition intent:** Once the blade itself is on the floor between them, the chapter can spend Uther's testimony and show why even that is not enough.

### Beat 6 - Scene: The Lightbringer Dismissed
- **Goal:** Let Uther give the cleanest truth available to the room.
- **Conflict:** The prosecution does not have to prove Uther wrong; it only has to prove him wounded and therefore interpretable.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - Uther's testimony lands with full factual force and is then neutralized by cross-examination built around his humiliation at Stratholme and his lateness to the actual murder.
- **Value shift:** predator in a box -> hope enters and is professionally cut down
- **New on-page information:**
  - Uther can describe Hearthglen, Stratholme, and Arthas's deterioration in a way that should dismantle the case
  - The court only needs the fact that he was stripped of command and arrived after the act
  - Twenty minutes of architectural bad timing become the difference between witness and hearsay
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any later witness is stronger than Uther (none are)
  - How Jaina is physically holding up under the blade's continued presence
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Uther's hands closing into fists while the prosecutor reframes him
  - Formal paladin surcoat where armor should have been
  - Tribunal room going quieter because everyone feels the witness should matter more than the court lets him matter
- **Transition intent:** Once Uther is cut down, the chapter can move into the deeper betrayals the verdict still needs.

## Scene 3: The Betrayals

**Scene function:** Let every outside system fail in its own style before the law says the word guilty.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina, Antonidas, two Kirin Tor magi, Vereesa, Thornton, Ashcombe, gallery
**Knowledge at scene start:** The strongest witness in the kingdom just failed to change the frame.
**Arc pressure:** Keep Antonidas in the gallery, the witness chair, Vereesa's face, and Thornton's steady procedure concrete so the scene lands through bodies, not only concepts.

### Beat 7 - Sequel: The Archmage at the Wall
- **Emotion:** Worse than absence - the mentor arrived and still hit the same wall.
- **Dilemma:** Antonidas is in the hall with two Kirin Tor magi, but any sworn testimony or direct blade examination would publicly expose Dalaran's prior warning and failure.
- **Decision:** Sylvanas reads the line for what it is and does not waste breath pretending presence is the same thing as help.
- **Value shift:** hope professionally cut down -> presence without aid
- **New on-page information:**
  - Antonidas came in person and is allowed to observe, object, and ask for continuance, but not to turn himself into official Kirin Tor evidence
  - The tribunal treats unsanctioned Kirin Tor examination of Frostmourne as tampering rather than rescue
  - The witness chair still stays empty, and that emptiness now lands harder because blue Kirin Tor robes are visible in the room
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Antonidas's conscience breaks later into open defiance
  - Whether the tribunal reads his constrained presence as caution, guilt, or political theater
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Antonidas standing in the gallery instead of the witness box
  - One Kirin Tor mage going pale when the lead-lined chest is opened
  - Jaina not looking toward her mentor until the objection is denied
- **Transition intent:** Once the mentor is reduced to visible uselessness, the chapter can bring in the sister carrying official severance.

### Beat 8 - Scene: The Disavowal
- **Goal:** Hear Silvermoon's formal position on Sylvanas in the aftermath of Stratholme and the palace killings.
- **Conflict:** The Convocation's words are political, but the voice reading them belongs to Sylvanas's sister.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the diplomatic shield dissolves completely, and the family bond is made into the instrument that delivers the cut.
- **Value shift:** one more ally chooses not to arrive -> family becomes the state's weapon
- **New on-page information:**
  - The Convocation claims Sylvanas acted without authorization and with no sovereign protection behind her
  - Vereesa was chosen as courier precisely because the words would hurt more in her voice
  - Vereesa stays in the hall afterward, which means the clean narrative is already cracking around the edges
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Vereesa is still acting wholly as the Convocation's instrument by the chapter's end
  - Whether any of Silvermoon's political cost can be turned back toward leverage later
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Windrunner crest pinned to the cloak of the woman reading the disavowal
  - Gold wax seal broken in a room full of strangers
  - One brief look between sisters carrying more than the formal text does
- **Transition intent:** Once the state has spoken through family, the verdict can fall with nothing left in the way.

### Beat 9 - Scene: The Verdict
- **Goal:** Hear the tribunal's official answer to everything the investigation did.
- **Conflict:** The system is competent, the evidence is real, and the truth exceeds the legal frame offered to hold it.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - guilty on every major count, death commuted to indefinite imprisonment because politics prefers living prisoners to executed heroines.
- **Value shift:** family becomes the state's weapon -> the wrong answer becomes law
- **New on-page information:**
  - Thornton's ruling follows the prosecution's causation chain all the way to its intended end
  - Commutation is political management, not mercy
  - The kingdom now has an official history in which its saviors murdered its royals
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the blade claims another mind before any appeal fiction matters
  - Whether any outside force now has enough leverage to reopen what the court just closed
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Thornton's pen steady in the same hand that handled every earlier procedure
  - Jaina's tremor stopping briefly in shock rather than recovery
  - Guards tightening chains only after the word guilty lands
- **Transition intent:** Once the law closes, the chapter can return to the colder problem waiting below it.

## Scene 4: The Aftermath

**Scene function:** Make the verdict feel instantly secondary to Frostmourne's acceleration and bring the first outside reinforcement to the door too late.
**Cast in scene:** Sylvanas, Jaina through wall, Lor'themar, guards
**Knowledge at scene start:** The verdict is final on paper and useless against the blade above them.
**Arc pressure:** Keep cell acoustics, tapping, grate-bars, and corridor cadence concrete so the shift from law to countdown feels grounded.

### Beat 10 - Sequel: The Seeking
- **Emotion:** The verdict settles and immediately gets displaced by a colder urgency.
- **Dilemma:** They now know the blade's timetable is collapsing, but the only people who know it are still behind locked doors.
- **Decision:** Sylvanas starts treating elven sensitivity as the first corroboration path that might exist outside the cells.
- **Value shift:** the wrong answer becomes law -> the real clock reasserts itself
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina's tapping through the wall is how she counts the blade's accelerating cycle
  - The timeline has shrunk to days, not weeks
  - Elven mages near the vault might independently feel enough wrongness to corroborate what Jaina hears precisely
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether any corroboration can move fast enough to beat the blade
  - Which palace minds are already nearest the call
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Fingertip taps through stone counting the blade's heartbeat
  - Air in the cell getting colder after the verdict instead of warmer
  - Sylvanas holding a possible plan warm in her head because that is all she can physically hold
- **Transition intent:** Once the corroboration thought exists, the chapter can bring Lor'themar to the grate and give it an outside surface.

### Beat 11 - Scene: The Arrival
- **Goal:** Bring Lor'themar to the cell and establish what outside force is actually in play now.
- **Conflict:** He arrived after the verdict, which means his presence changes the landscape but cannot undo what just happened.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Lor'themar brings three hundred Farstriders and mages who already felt the blade in the palace foundations, but the cell remains shut and the blade's timeline remains brutally short.
- **Value shift:** the real clock reasserts itself -> reinforcement without release
- **New on-page information:**
  - Lor'themar won just enough in Silvermoon to move a real force into Capital City
  - The elven mages can independently feel the vault's wrongness from outside the cells
  - Sylvanas is no longer alone politically, only still trapped physically
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Lor'themar can turn perception into leverage before the blade chooses
  - Whether Vereesa's continued presence helps or hinders that move
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Familiar Farstrider cadence in dungeon footsteps before the face appears
  - Road-dust on Lor'themar where court polish should be
  - Two hands meeting across cold iron bars for the only contact the chapter allows
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 25 opens at night with the cells still closed, the blade still seeking, and the larger board about to show one corner of itself.
