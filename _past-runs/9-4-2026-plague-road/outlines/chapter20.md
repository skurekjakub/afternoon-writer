# Chapter 20: The Voyage

**POV:** Jaina Proudmoore (limited third, absolute)
**Timeline position:** Directly follows Chapter 19. Opens at the northern coast after the ride out of Lordaeron's interior and closes with landfall at Daggercap Bay in Northrend.
**Open location:** Northern Lordaeron harbor road and quay.
**Transport:** Horseback arrival -> harbor work on foot -> schooner crossing aboard the *Wolfheart* -> dockside landfall.
**Active cast at open:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Captain Dagny Ironhull and the *Wolfheart* crew.
**Immediate objective:** Secure passage north and keep the pursuit alive across open water after both institutions have fallen away.

## Knowledge ledger

**What Jaina knows at open**
- North is no longer a theory lane; Sylvanas chose it over recall and Jaina chose it over Dalaran.
- Arthas already has a sea lead and a hand-picked force that followed him through Stratholme.
- The resonance thread grows stronger toward the coast, which means the source really does lie across the water.
- The relay network dies at the harbor; once they sail, no institution is carrying them.
- The pursuit now depends on money, a willing ship, and whatever Jaina can still hear.

**What Jaina does NOT know at open**
- Whether any captain will accept a Northrend run at all.
- How directly the source can answer her probing once she is on open water.
- What the sea crossing will cost her body if she has to force it.
- What Arthas is chasing in Northrend besides the source itself.

**Must not be implied yet**
- No Muradin briefing or Frostmourne specifics yet.
- No full explanation of the source as a named cosmic hierarchy.
- No promise that arrival means they can still catch Arthas before he acts.
- No assumption that Jaina can push her Frostmourne sensitivity wider and then simply settle it again.

**What the cast knows leaving the chapter**
- Captain Dagny's *Wolfheart* can get them to Northrend if Jaina pays the crossing cost in power.
- The source opposes them directly through supernatural headwinds once the ship is committed.
- The forced stillness of the voyage opens a new honesty lane between Jaina and Sylvanas.
- Jaina can push through the opposition, but doing so sharpens her sensitivity and makes the bread-taste part of her baseline.
- Sylvanas will stop Jaina when Jaina will not stop herself.
- Daggercap Bay proves they arrived, but it also proves Arthas is still weeks ahead in practical terms.

## Arc position

### Jaina
- **Current stance at open:** If she can keep north in reach, being right can still matter.
- **Surface objective:** Turn the chosen pursuit into physical arrival before Arthas's lead hardens into something unrecoverable.
- **Pressure source:** Arthas's sea lead, the dead sending-stone in her pocket, the open water ahead, and the knowledge that only Sylvanas is left to check her when she starts spending herself like fuel.
- **Misbelief manifestation:** Jaina still treats willpower and correct analysis as if they can bridge any distance if she simply keeps pushing.
- **Chapter test:** The chapter asks whether she can let another person define her limit without reading that limit as surrender.
- **Forced choice:** Preserve herself for Northrend, or spend herself just to arrive.
- **End-state shift:** Jaina reaches Northrend, but the crossing proves the chase is no longer only on the map; it is in her body now.
- **Carry-forward residue:** Chapter 21 opens with Northrend underfoot, Arthas still ahead, and Jaina already altered by the voyage that got them there.

### Sylvanas through Jaina's POV
- **Visible function:** Commander, counterweight, and the only person on the ship whose authority can still cut through Jaina's overreach.
- **POV pressure:** Jaina reads care through orders, water, presence, and timing rather than through spoken softness.
- **Interaction rule:** No interiority. Everything comes through tone, movement on deck, and the fact that Sylvanas sees the cost before Jaina will admit it.

### Partnership / team
- **Operating mode at open:** Chosen over institutions, stripped down to travel logistics and the two women still carrying the same direction.
- **Change this chapter:** The sea forces stillness, and the stillness gives the partnership room to become more than tactical shorthand.
- **Do-not-overstate rule:** Let the waterskin, the one-word command, and the deck silence carry the turn. Do not name the bond ahead of the scene.

## Cast and handoff rules

- **Dagny:** Pure pragmatist. She prices danger and handles seawork cleanly. No mystical register, no sailor superstition doing story work for her.
- **Cyndia and the rangers:** Shipboard perimeter, load discipline, and visible proof that the formation still functions even when the fight is weather rather than steel.
- **Antonidas:** Present only through Jaina's dead stone, remembered training, and the severed line she keeps touching even though it is over.

## Scene 1: The Harbor

**Scene function:** Turn the north choice into literal departure before the coast can reclaim either woman for an institution that already failed them.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, three Farstrider rangers, Dagny, harbor hands
**Knowledge at scene start:** North is chosen, the relay network ends here, and the chase dies if they cannot buy passage.
**Arc pressure:** Keep horses, quay mud, stripped saddles, tide timing, and the dead stone active so departure feels material instead of abstract.

### Beat 1 - Scene: The Harbor
- **Goal:** Secure a captain and a vessel willing to sail for Northrend.
- **Conflict:** Northrend is a bad run under the best conditions, and this passage offers no institutional backing - only urgency, money, and risk.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Dagny agrees to carry them on the *Wolfheart*, but she agrees as a captain pricing danger, not as an ally stepping into their cause.
- **Value shift:** chosen pursuit -> paid departure
- **New on-page information:**
  - The *Wolfheart* is a hard-water schooner already working northern routes
  - Dagny accepts that the job is dangerous and raises the price instead of refusing the run
  - The coast is the last place where Farstrider or Dalaran infrastructure can still touch this pursuit at all
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the sea itself will let them through
  - Whether the captain's weather clause becomes more than a bargaining point
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Salt wind and gull noise over tarred rope
  - Horses steaming at the quay after the hard northbound ride
  - Dagny looking at the weather and at Jaina as if pricing both together
- **Transition intent:** Once the berth is bought, the chapter has to show what departure costs before the ship even moves.

### Beat 2 - Scene/Sequel: The Ship
- **Goal:** Board, settle the formation into shipboard roles, and convert "north" into a vessel, a deck, and a tide window.
- **Conflict:** The practical work is easy; the emotional weight is not. Jaina has to cross the last edge of land carrying a sending-stone that is already dead.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the formation boards cleanly and the ship is ready on the tide, but the transition makes the institutional severance feel heavier rather than lighter.
- **Value shift:** paid departure -> the old world starts falling away
- **New on-page information:**
  - The horses and land gear stay behind; from here forward the chase belongs to the ship
  - Jaina still keeps the cold sending-stone instead of discarding it
  - Sylvanas and Cyndia fall into shipboard discipline as fast as they fall into road discipline
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Jaina can keep the resonance thread useful without making herself visible to the source
  - What the crew will do when the crossing turns unnatural
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Wet planks under boots
  - Saddles and road dust left on the quay instead of taken aboard
  - The dead weight of the stone in Jaina's coat pocket
- **Transition intent:** Once the ship is loaded, the chapter has to close the last land-side lane and push the coast below the horizon.

## Scene 2: The Departure

**Scene function:** Kill the last easy routes home and let the coastline become absence.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Knowledge at scene start:** Passage is secured, but every message or support lane that matters still belongs to land.
**Arc pressure:** Keep relay endings, the dead sending-stone, and the visible coastline on page so leaving actually feels irreversible.

### Beat 3 - Scene/Sequel: The Last Relay
- **Goal:** Hand off the last shore-side instructions and accept that everything beyond this harbor runs on competence and guesswork.
- **Conflict:** The relay network can carry route facts, not rescue, and Jaina gets nothing from the stone except the proof that she already cut herself loose.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - Cyndia leaves a clean final relay trail and the formation departs with its route known, but the real help line is gone before the ship clears the breakwater.
- **Value shift:** old world starts falling away -> silence becomes the operating condition
- **New on-page information:**
  - Cyndia leaves precise harbor and destination notes with the last usable Farstrider contact
  - Jaina tests the stone only enough to prove it stays cold and mute
  - No escort, no portal, and no institutional correction arrives before departure
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether anything sent from shore can matter before Arthas acts
  - Whether Jaina's next contact with the source will be observation or retaliation
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Brass relay cylinder warm from a runner's hand
  - The stone staying dark even under Jaina's touch
  - Harbor bells marking the tide while nobody calls them back
- **Transition intent:** The next beat lets the coast vanish in stages and leaves only ship, sea, and signal.

### Beat 4 - Scene: The Departure
- **Goal:** Clear the harbor and commit the pursuit to open water.
- **Conflict:** The ship moves exactly as it should, which makes the emotional cost land harder; leaving is technically smooth and personally absolute.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the *Wolfheart* gets cleanly to sea, but the disappearing coast makes the loss of ordinary world, rank, and safety newly concrete.
- **Value shift:** silence becomes the operating condition -> the pursuit is alone
- **New on-page information:**
  - The Eastern Kingdoms vanish in stages rather than all at once
  - Shipboard life immediately reduces the formation to watch rotations, bunks, and deck lanes
  - Jaina feels the north thread more clearly once nothing landbound is crowding it
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether arrival is still possible on usable time
  - Whether the sea is empty or actively hostile
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Coastline becoming first a cliff line, then a grey strip, then nothing
  - Rigging strain replacing road noise
  - Sea-cold taking over where harbor smells used to be
- **Transition intent:** Once land disappears, the chapter can let the source answer back.

## Scene 3: The Crossing

**Scene function:** Put Jaina's gift and the source in direct contest, then turn forced stillness into the partnership beat the road kept deferring.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Knowledge at scene start:** They are at sea, northbound, and fully beyond land support.
**Arc pressure:** Keep deck labor, weather, the captain's math, Jaina's body, and Sylvanas's interventions visible so the crossing never drifts into summary.

### Beat 5 - Scene: The Headwinds
- **Goal:** Identify the new resistance and decide whether the voyage can continue through it.
- **Conflict:** The headwinds are not weather. They carry the same frequency family as the plague, which means the source has noticed them and is pushing back.
- **Outcome:** **no-and** - the ship's ordinary progress collapses, and Jaina realizes her own northward probing is what made them legible to the thing opposing them.
- **Value shift:** open sea -> direct opposition
- **New on-page information:**
  - The headwinds behave like intention rather than weather - constant, directional, and timed
  - Jaina's resonance sensitivity is bidirectional; every probe north announced her
  - Dagny does not break the contract when she hears the truth; she recalculates what Jaina can buy with her body
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether Jaina can force them through without ruining herself
  - Whether the source's opposition has a limit
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - A gale that feels like a door slammed shut
  - Sailcloth snapping against a wind that never offers a natural gap
  - Jaina reaching into the wind and feeling something reach back
- **Transition intent:** Once the opposition is known, the ship has to slow enough for the talk the road never allowed.

### Beat 6 - Sequel: The Dialogue
- **Emotion:** Forced stillness. The ship can only claw forward in bursts, which leaves the partnership with hours it cannot fill by marching or fighting.
- **Dilemma:** How do duty and certainty work once the institution defining them becomes an obstacle instead of a frame?
- **Decision:** Neither woman backs away from the question, even when the question starts sounding too much like Arthas and Kel'Thuzad from another angle.
- **Value shift:** working silence -> exposed honesty
- **New on-page information:**
  - Sylvanas stopped treating the Convocation as the relevant variable long before she openly defied it
  - Jaina recognizes that her Dalaran severance followed the same shape - action first, spoken break second
  - The first outline of the certainty parallel surfaces: they are chasing a man destroyed by conviction while sailing north on conviction of their own
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether their certainty is different in kind or only in consequence
  - What the partnership becomes once there is no institution left around it
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Mainmast cold against Jaina's back
  - Ship creak and contested water under a conversation with no witnesses
  - Sylvanas settling across from her without asking permission because that part is already solved
- **Transition intent:** After the honesty beat, the chapter has to show what Jaina does with the captain's math.

### Beat 7 - Scene: The Push
- **Goal:** Recover the voyage timeline before provisions and distance make the crossing impossible.
- **Conflict:** Jaina can match the headwinds only by sustained channeling that turns her body into the second engine of the ship.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - the *Wolfheart* regains lost distance and crosses the halfway point, but the recovery cost is Jaina's reserves, her blood, and a channel she may not be able to narrow again.
- **Value shift:** opposition -> progress bought with the body
- **New on-page information:**
  - The crossing becomes a raw endurance contest between Jaina's sustained output and the source's resistance
  - The bread-taste stops being an intermittent symptom and becomes a constant companion
  - The channel widens far enough that Jaina can feel not just a point in the north but a cold structure answering back
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether recovery after arrival is possible before the next fight
  - Whether the widened channel is damage, asset, or both
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Blood drying on the back of Jaina's hand while her hands never lower
  - The hull shuddering between two opposed winds
  - Dagny watching her like a captain watching an engine burn too hot
- **Transition intent:** Once the progress is recovered, the chapter needs the person who can tell Jaina when enough is enough.

### Beat 8 - Sequel: Stop
- **Emotion:** Jaina's will outlasts her body's tolerance.
- **Dilemma:** If the ship can still move, who gets to decide when the cost is too high - the person pushing, or the person watching what the push is doing to her?
- **Decision:** Sylvanas decides for her. One word. No debate.
- **Value shift:** progress bought with the body -> surrender that reads as trust
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina will not stop herself once arrival becomes mathematically possible
  - Sylvanas's authority still reaches the part of Jaina that obeys competence
  - The care lands through command, water, warmth, and shared silence rather than through explanation
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether the widening channel will narrow again once the push ends
  - What the source has done to Jaina by holding that line open for so long
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - One word crossing a noisy deck and landing harder than any shouted order
  - Leather waterskin warm from Sylvanas's cloak
  - The natural wind reappearing under the killed channel as if there had always been weather beneath the contest
- **Transition intent:** Once Jaina has been stopped, the chapter can let the mind do what the body no longer can - ask what kind of certainty carried her here.

## Scene 4: The Approach

**Scene function:** Turn shipboard cost into philosophical pressure, then into ice, coast, and hard proof that the chase is still late.
**Cast in scene:** Jaina, Sylvanas, Cyndia, Farstrider rangers, Dagny, *Wolfheart* crew
**Knowledge at scene start:** The crossing will succeed, but Jaina paid for that success visibly.
**Arc pressure:** Keep bunk, stars, ice-poles, rigging frost, and Northrend shorework concrete so arrival feels earned rather than summarized.

### Beat 9 - Sequel: The Kel'Thuzad Echo
- **Emotion:** Exhausted clarity in the dark.
- **Dilemma:** If Kel'Thuzad, Arthas, and Jaina all broke from their institutions because they were certain they were right, what actually separates Jaina from the men whose certainty destroyed everything around them?
- **Decision:** No answer yet. The question is planted and carried forward unresolved.
- **Value shift:** exhausted effort -> doubt enters certainty
- **New on-page information:**
  - Jaina can now frame the parallel cleanly enough to frighten herself with it
  - Being right is not the differentiator; Kel'Thuzad and Arthas were right about parts of their problems too
  - The most dangerous thing happening to Jaina is not failure but repeated validation
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Whether friction from another person is the missing difference or only a partial answer
  - What that doubt will demand when she has to act fast again
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Narrow bunk rocking under a body too tired to sleep well
  - Bread-taste and sea-sway sharing the same night
  - Cold stars over a ship still moving north
- **Transition intent:** The chapter has asked the question; dawn now has to turn theory back into environment.

### Beat 10 - Scene: The Ice
- **Goal:** Survive the last approach through Northrend waters and translate source-pressure into a place.
- **Conflict:** The headwinds ease only because the ship has entered a colder, harsher problem - ice, ambient resonance, and a coast that does not care whether they survive it.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they enter Northrend waters and keep advancing, but the resonance stops being something Jaina reaches for and becomes something that reaches for her.
- **Value shift:** doubt in the bunk -> proximity made physical
- **New on-page information:**
  - Northrend's cold is a place-pressure, not just a weather pressure
  - The source's signature is ambient this close; Jaina no longer has to extend to hear it
  - The contamination echo in Jaina aligns harder under proximity rather than fading
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - How much of Jaina's altered perception will remain once they leave the source's immediate radius
  - Whether the coast offers a usable lead or only proof of lateness
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Iron-tipped poles ringing off ice floes
  - Frost webbing the rigging at dawn
  - The cold changing from wind-bite to something that presses behind the eyes
- **Transition intent:** Once the sea has turned to ice and pressure, the chapter can let the coast itself answer whether they are in time.

### Beat 11 - Scene: The Coast
- **Goal:** Make landfall at Daggercap Bay and read Arthas's lead in the traces he left behind.
- **Conflict:** Arrival is real, but arrival is not rescue. The harbor already shows weeks of military use and departure.
- **Outcome:** **yes-but** - they reach Northrend alive, but the camp traces, docks, and abandoned signs say Arthas is still well ahead and fully inland now.
- **Value shift:** crossing -> arrival that becomes a chase
- **New on-page information:**
  - Daggercap Bay carries fresh signs of Arthas's expedition - expanded docks, caches, bootprints, and empty camp traces
  - The source stops opposing the crossing once they enter its domain
  - The pursuit now changes from sea arithmetic to inland tracking
- **Still unknown after beat:**
  - Who, if anyone, is still alive inland who can tell them what Arthas found
  - How much farther north the real answer sits beyond the harbor
- **Sensory anchors:**
  - Frozen mud under boots after four days of deck motion
  - New timber on dwarven trade docks
  - The resonance everywhere at once instead of only at the edge of thought
- **Transition intent:** Chapter 21 opens on that harbor intelligence and the first inland ally.
